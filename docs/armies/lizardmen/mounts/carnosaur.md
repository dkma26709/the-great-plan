# Carnosaur


> The apex predator. Everything in Lustria learned to fear the Carnosaur before it learned anything else. Fast for its size, terrifyingly strong jaws, and a predator's instinct to go for the throat. Where the Stegadon is a wall, the Carnosaur is a spear — a surgical killer that happens to be enormous. Ridden into battle only by Saurus Oldbloods and Saurus Scar-Veterans, whose own apex-saurian nature is the one thing a Carnosaur respects.

- **Special rules:**
  - Cold-Blooded (see §8)
  - **Predatory Fighter** (see §8) — Saurus species rule; the Carnosaur's natural attacks (Massive Jaws, Raking Claws, Sweeping Tail) trigger extra attacks on unmodified to-hit rolls of 6 (no further cascade)
  - **Terror** (see §8)
  - **Apex Predator** *(identity rule)* — the Carnosaur's Massive Jaws gain **+1 to hit and +1 to wound** when targeting any model with the **Monster**, **Monstrous Infantry**, **Monstrous Cavalry**, or **Warbeast** keyword. Represents the Carnosaur's lore-canonical instinct to go for the biggest prey: devastating against peers and large beasts, no bonus against rank-and-file infantry or cavalry
  - **Pinned Prey** *(unit identity rule — same beast, same biology as Wild Carnosaur §10 Rare entry)* — when this Carnosaur attacks in melee, **resolve Raking Claws attacks first**. If **both Raking Claws score hits against the same enemy model** (single-model unit, Character, Champion, or any specific rank-and-file model), that model is **pinned** — for the rest of this Combat phase, this Carnosaur's Massive Jaws attacks **against that pinned model** gain **+1 to-Hit and +1 Damage**. Effectively only matters against multi-W targets — the +1 Damage Jaws bonus is wasted on W 1 rank-and-file (a single hit kills regardless). Captures the AoS-iconic predator pattern + direct lore: "they begin to prefer ambush tactics, often using their clawed forelimbs to pin their prey down, then ripping into their meals with their powerful jaws." Stacks with Apex Predator vs Monster + Pinned: Massive Jaws strike at +2 to-Hit (capped at 2+), +1 to-Wound, AP -3, D D3+1 — devastating but conditional on landing both Claws on the same model.
  - **Bloodroar** — in addition to the Terror aura, at the start of each Combat phase every enemy unit with any model within **12"** of the Carnosaur immediately gains **+1 stress**. The roar carries the weight of a tyrannosaur's lungs — the sound alone has broken formations
  - **Pursuit Predator** — when a unit falls back from combat with the Carnosaur, the Carnosaur **must** follow up (no hold option). Additionally, if the Carnosaur wipes out a unit in melee, the combined model may immediately make a free move of up to **6"** toward the nearest enemy unit. The kill instinct does not stop
  - **Bloodlust** *(Carnosaur identity rule)* — once this Carnosaur scores at least one unsaved wound in melee, it gains the **Frenzy** special rule (see §8) for the rest of the battle. The killing fury, once kindled, never abates — Frenzy cannot be lost by losing a combat round, by stress thresholds, or by any other rule that would normally strip Frenzy. Lore-direct: "having tasted blood, a Carnosaur is at its most deadly, for it enters a state of savage bloodlust."
  - **Locked Jaws** *(Lizardmen biology — soft inherit from Saurus species pass 2)* — if this Carnosaur is reduced to 0 W *during the Combat phase*, before being removed it makes **1 final Massive Jaws attack** against the model that scored the killing blow (or the closest enemy model in base contact, if the killing attacker is out of base contact). Single attack — no Predatory Fighter cascade on the death-bite, no Pinned Prey or Apex Predator interaction (the dying beast manages one last reflexive snap, not a calculated bite). Lore: severed reptilian heads keep snapping; even in death the apex predator's jaws still close on the killer.
  - **Degrading** — 3-tier damage table below

> **Lore notes:** The apex predator as a character mount — same beast biologically as the Wild Carnosaur, ridden by Saurus Oldbloods or Scar-Veterans whose own apex-saurian nature is the one thing a Carnosaur respects. Carnosaur is deliberately the mirror of the Stegadon — both Lord-tier Monsters, different combat niches. Stegadon = mass, slow, armoured, howdah platform. Carnosaur = precision, fast, surgical killer. **Apex Predator** drives the hunt-the-biggest instinct (+1 to-Hit / +1 to-Wound vs Monstrous keyword targets). **Pinned Prey** is the lore-explicit hunt pattern (claws-then-jaws — "use forelimbs to pin prey down, then rip with jaws"); stacks with Apex Predator vs Monsters for devastating but conditional output. **Pursuit Predator** forces relentless follow-ups — guaranteed pressure on broken enemies, the kill instinct does not stop. **Bloodlust** locks Frenzy in on the first unsaved wound (parallel to Aggradon Lancers, biology-consistent saurian fury). **Locked Jaws** is the soft Lizardmen biology inherit — even in death, one final reflexive snap.
