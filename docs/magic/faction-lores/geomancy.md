# Lore of Geomancy


> The ancient stone-shaping magic of the Old Ones, passed to the Slann and inscribed in every temple-city of Lustria. Where the general lores reach for the elements and the heavens, Geomancy reaches **inward and downward** — into the bedrock, the bones of the world, the tectonic memory of a planet that once danced to the Old Ones' will. A Slann mage-priest does not *ask* the earth; the earth remembers whose children they are. The lore specialises in: terrain manipulation (the only lore that can move, summon, *and* remove terrain features), mobility denial (the Earth Tremors attribute layered with Fissures and Entomb), and catastrophic area damage at Master tier (the Great Leveller, Ruination of Cities). Flavour register: slow, geological, massive — the patience of stone before the crack.

**Lore Attribute — Earth Tremors:** whenever this wizard **successfully casts a Geomancy spell**, or whenever a Geomancy spell inflicts **one or more unsaved wounds** on a target unit, that unit suffers **-1 M** (minimum 1) until the start of the caster's next Magic phase. **No effect on units with the Fly or Strider special rules.** The land itself rejects the enemy's steps.

**Signature Spell — Earth Line**

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 7 | Special (line) | Direct Damage |

The caster selects a direction. A narrow line **18"** long extends from the caster's base. Each **enemy model** touched by the line must pass a **Toughness test** (roll a D6 — equal to or less than the model's T passes). On a **failure**, that model suffers **1 wound with no armour save allowed** (ward saves permitted). **Magical Attacks.**

Classic WAP Lore of Geomancy signature, adapted. A sudden fissure opens along the ground — the tougher-bodied cross it, the weaker fall in. Mechanically parallel to Shadow's Pendulum of Shadow but differentiated: Pendulum auto-hits with D3 damage; Earth Line filters via T tests with flat 1 damage.

#### Basic (Tier 1) — Lore Access 1+

**Empowered Obsinite**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 7 | 18" | Augment | Until start of next turn |

Target friendly unit's **ranged attacks gain +1 AP** (AP 0 → AP -1; AP -1 → AP -2, etc.) until start of next turn. Stone-dust and obsinite-flakes line every arrow and javelin — edges meant for cutting flesh now bite through plate. Deliberately ranged-only (Metal's Enchanted Blades covers melee +1 AP) — preserves the lore identity distinction.

**Tectonic Shift**

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 8 | 24" | Direct Effect |

Target one piece of terrain within 24". The caster rolls **2D3** — move the terrain that many inches in any chosen direction (facing preserved). Stops 1" short of another terrain piece, model, or the battlefield edge. Units on or under the terrain **move with it** (stopping 1" short of any model). Affected units are subject to **Earth Tremors** (-1 M until start of caster's next Magic phase).

Uniquely Geomancy-flavoured — the only spell in the ruleset that *moves* existing terrain. Tactical uses: drag an enemy regiment out of position, shift a cover piece to protect a friendly flank, push a hill into a charge lane.

**Earthen Ramparts** *(RIP)*

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 9 | 18" | Augment, Remains In Play |

While this spell is in play, the target friendly unit suffers **-1 to hit from all enemy attacks** (melee, ranged, spell) and **charging enemy models suffer an additional -1 to hit** in the turn they make contact. The spell **ends immediately** if the target unit moves for any reason (voluntary or involuntary — Advance, March, Charge, Flee, Push-back, Fall Back, etc.).

Stone walls rise and close around them; any movement collapses the fortification. Self-limiting RIP — powerful defensive effect, but anchors the unit in place.

**Fissures**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 8 | 24" | Hex | Until start of next turn |

Target enemy unit treats **all terrain as Difficult Terrain** until start of next turn. The ground opens in hairline cracks ahead of them — each step threatens to turn an ankle. Layers with **Earth Tremors** (-1 M from the attribute) for a total mobility shutdown on movement-reliant enemies.

#### Intermediate (Tier 2) — Lore Access 2+

**Entomb** *(RIP)*

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 11 | 24" | Hex, Remains In Play |

Target **unengaged** enemy unit. While this spell is in play, the target:
- **Cannot move** (Advance, March, Charge, Wheel, Flee — all denied; Rally permitted)
- **Cannot use ranged attacks**
- **Cannot be damaged by any source except Lore of Geomancy spells** — wards, armour saves, etc. are irrelevant; other damage is simply not applied
- **Is treated as impassable terrain** for all movement, LOS, and template purposes

The unit is swallowed by rising stone — locked, untargetable, but also untouched. A double-edged spell: can take a crucial enemy unit out of play, but also temporarily protects them from other threats.

**Uproot the Mountains** *(Summoning)*

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 12 | 18" | Terrain, Summoning |

The caster chooses **one** of:
- **Summon:** place a **hill terrain feature** of up to **6" diameter, one level high**, anywhere within 18" of the caster. Placement must not overlap existing terrain or models at the moment of placement.
- **Remove:** target an existing hill within 18" and remove it from play.

Any unit on or under the affected hill at the moment of the cast is subject to **Earth Tremors** (-1 M until caster's next Magic phase). Per Summoning rules (§7.3), the effect is **permanent** after the cast-turn dispel reaction.

Companion to Life's Awaken the Wild (forest): Geomancy summons hills where Life summons forests. Different tactical uses — hills grant elevation and cover but block LOS less.

**Seismic Upheaval**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 10 | 24" | Hex | Until start of next turn |

Target enemy unit suffers **D6 hits at S4, AP 0, D1** immediately. Additionally, until start of next turn, the unit suffers **-1 to hit** on all melee and ranged attacks. The ground buckles and rolls beneath them — footing fails mid-stride, blades miss, arrows fly wide.

Combines modest damage with a meaningful combat debuff at long (24") range.

#### Advanced (Tier 3) — Lore Access 3+

**Cavernous Crevasse**

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 13 | 18" + 12" line | Direct Damage |

The caster selects a point within 18". A line extends **12"** from that point in a chosen direction. Each **enemy model** touched by the line: roll a **D6 for each Wound on the model's profile** — on each **4+**, the model suffers **1 wound with no armour save allowed**. **Regeneration does not apply against this spell** (other ward saves still apply). **Magical Attacks.**

Distinct from Earth Line (signature, T-test-based): Cavernous Crevasse is auto-hit per W-roll, meaning **multi-wound models are disproportionately vulnerable** (a 3-W monster rolls 3 dice; ~1.5 wounds expected). Anti-monster, anti-hero. A chasm opening under heavy targets drinks them first.

**Telepathic Summons** *(Conveyance)*

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 13 | 24" (target) | Conveyance, Instant |

Target friendly unit within **24"** of the caster. The unit is immediately **removed from the battlefield** and **placed anywhere within 12" of the caster**, in a legal formation, **more than 1" from any enemy model**, not inside impassable terrain. The unit **cannot declare a Charge** on the turn it arrives.

The Slann's mind reaches across the field and draws the chosen unit to a new position. Same-turn redeployment — different from Life's Paranoth's Pathway (which takes a turn but allows placement anywhere on the field). This one is instant but constrained: the unit must end near the Slann.

**Gravitic Redirection**

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 13 | 12" | Direct Damage, Instant |

Target enemy unit. Roll a **D6 for each model** in the unit — each **4+** result inflicts **1 wound at AP -3, damage 1** on a model in the unit (target player allocates). Armour saves apply with the AP modifier; ward saves apply normally. **Magical Attacks.**

Gravity doubles on the target zone; models stagger, fall, and are crushed from within. Scales punishingly with unit size — a 20-strong regiment averages ~10 wounds before saves, a 5-strong elite unit averages ~2.5. Short-range (12") means the Slann must push forward to unleash it, creating tactical risk.

#### Master (Tier 4) — Lore Access 4+

**The Great Leveller**

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 18 | 12" (aura) | Direct Damage |

**All units (friendly and enemy) with any model within 12" of the caster** — excluding the caster's own unit — suffer **3D6 hits at S5, AP -2, D1**. **Magical Attacks.** Applies even to units engaged in close combat.

The earth itself rises and falls in a single catastrophic wave. A doomsday spell — indiscriminate, devastating, and as likely to bury the Slann's army as the enemy's. Use when you're losing badly and need a reset, or when the enemy has massed too close and you're willing to pay in friendly blood.

**Ruination of Cities**

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 17 | 18" | Direct Damage |

Target enemy unit suffers **2D6 hits at S5, AP -2, D1**. **Magical Attacks.** A unit that suffers **any wounds** from this spell has its Movement **halved** (rounding up) until start of caster's next Magic phase — the survivors stagger and retch, their legs failing from within.

If the target is **inside a building**, the spell inflicts **3D6 hits** instead (the building collapses around them). Anti-regiment doomsday — pairs with Earth Tremors to create a total mobility shutdown on the target.

**The Worldshaper** *(Summoning)*

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 17 | 24" | Terrain, Summoning |

The caster permanently reshapes a section of the battlefield. Choose **one**:
- **Rift:** place a line of **impassable terrain** up to 12" long and 1" wide anywhere within 24" of the caster. Cannot be crossed by any ground-based movement. Must not pass through models at the moment of placement.
- **Plateau:** summon a **hill feature** of up to 8" diameter and **two levels tall** (elevation bonuses per §7.2 Shooting phase — pending formalisation). Counts as Difficult Terrain for enemies; friendly units treat it as Open Terrain.

Per Summoning rules (§7.3), the effect is **permanent** after the cast-turn dispel reaction. The only spell in the game that can permanently split the battlefield in two — a massive terrain-level decision, capstone to the entire geomantic tradition.

> **Design note on Geomancy.** Geomancy is the Slann's *earth* lore — the terrestrial half of their faction identity. Its uniqueness: three terrain manipulation spells (Tectonic Shift, Uproot the Mountains, The Worldshaper), two WAP-faithful mass damage capstones (Great Leveller, Ruination of Cities), and the Earth Tremors attribute that layers -1 M onto any unit the lore touches. The Master tier (Lore Access 4+) pushes Geomancy into spells no general lore can match — indiscriminate area destruction and permanent battlefield reshaping that only a Slann Mage-Priest's foresight can justify wielding. Paired with the Slann's second faction lore (Cosmic Architecture, below), Geomancy gives Seraphon armies the strongest magic presence in the game when a Slann is on the field.
>
> **Calibration pass.** Range identity is **grounded mid-range** — earth magic stays close to the bedrock and doesn't reach into the sky. 18" baseline for spell effects, 24" for hex placements and terrain summoning, 12" for intimate gravity work (Gravitic Redirection) and the Master self-aura (Great Leveller's doomsday catches friend and foe near the caster). CV calibration after this pass: Sig 7 (T-test line, kept reliable). Basic 7-9 with Earthen Ramparts at the top of band as a near-Intermediate-strength defensive RIP. Intermediate 10-12 with Uproot the Mountains' permanent terrain summon at the cap. Advanced 13 across the board (Cavernous Crevasse, Telepathic Summons, Gravitic Redirection all calibrated to the high end of Adv since Geomancy's Adv-tier spells are uniformly strong). Master 17-18: **The Great Leveller** (CV 18, doomsday self-aura), **Ruination of Cities** (CV 17, anti-regiment), **The Worldshaper** (CV 17, permanent terrain split). Three Master spells (vs the general lores' two) reflect Geomancy's faction-lore depth — a Lord-tier Slann ideally takes one or two as situational capstones. Lore Attribute (Earth Tremors) remains unchanged.
