# Dryads

> *"Dryads are spiteful creatures with hearts akin to shards of ice."* The Dryads are the rank-and-file of the **Forest Spirit** sub-roster — bark-bodied tree-spirits born of Athel Loren's primeval magic, **bound to the living forest** long before the Asrai ever came to walk beneath its boughs. They are the embodiment of nature's duality: a Dryad in repose is a slender, near-elven figure of woven branches and silver bark; a Dryad roused to wrath is a *thing of teeth and thorns*, snapping branches into talons and tearing intruders apart with claws of root and bramble. They lure the unwary off forest trails — *the path you walked an hour ago is no longer where it was* — and when the intruder is alone in the deep wood, the Dryads emerge from the trees themselves. **Spite-cloaked claws** rake bark-grooves into armour; **Forest Spirit** wreathes the unit in primeval magic; **Hatred** drives every blow into the body of any creature who has set foot in Athel Loren uninvited. The fingerprint: an elf-sized Forest Spirit Infantry unit with apex melee output for the points (A 2 Spite Talons, S 4, T 4 / W 2, NA 6+, Magical Attacks, Imm Psych, Fear, Hatred). The wood-body is wood — not gromril plate — so the natural armour is *modest* (NA 6+); the unit's real durability comes from T 4 (better than elves), W 2 (double the rank-and-file elf body), and the conditional Forest Spirit Ward (5+ in forest, 6+ outside) — strong in the woods, merely solid in the open — set against the perennial trade-off: **Flammable**, the great Forest Spirit weakness. The Wood Elf player wants Dryads in the trees; the opposing player wants a brazier and a torch.

## Special Rules

- **Forest Spirit** *(Forest Spirit sub-roster faction trait — Dryads / Tree Kin / Treemen)*. Forest Spirits are bound to Athel Loren's primeval magic. The bundle:
  - **Fear** *(per §8 Psychology)*. At LiS 2, the Fear aura projects to **(6 + 2)" = 8"**. A pack of bark-bodied tree-spirits emerging from the treeline registers as primal terror on any battlefield.
  - **Magical Attacks** — all melee attacks made by this unit (Spite Talons) count as **Magical Attacks** for the purpose of damaging Ethereal models and bypassing Ward saves that protect only against non-magical damage. The Dryad's claws are wreathed in Athel Loren's magic; they cut wherever a mundane weapon would skip off.
  - **Flammable** *(per §8 — see Defence)*. Flaming Attacks against this unit **reroll failed to-Wound rolls**. The bark-and-thorn body is literal tree-flesh; fire takes hold readily. *Lore-true*: an Athel Loren Forest Spirit walks through arrows but burns like dry kindling. The Forest Spirit's defining weakness.
  - **Forest Spirit Ward** *(conditional)* — this unit has a **Ward 5+** while **wholly within a forest terrain feature**, or **Ward 6+** while outside forest terrain. The Ward stacks with the natural armour 5+ per the standard Ward-save sequence (failed armour save → Ward save). The bond to Athel Loren strengthens the Forest Spirit when fighting beneath the boughs; out in the open, the bond attenuates and the Ward weakens.
  - **Forest Strider** — this unit treats **forest terrain** as Open ground; no movement penalty, no Dangerous Terrain test, full M 5 advance through dense undergrowth. The forest does not impede the Forest Spirit any more than it impedes the Asrai. *(Mechanically equivalent to Forest Stride; named Forest Strider on Forest Spirit profiles to distinguish the Forest-Spirit-biological inheritance from the elven Forest Stride biological-inheritance.)*
  - **Immune to Psychology** *(per §8)* — Forest Spirits are bound to Athel Loren's will, not mortal sensibility. Stress triggers and behavioural impositions from Psychology-keyword rules do not apply. The unit cannot itself be Feared / Terrored / panicked-out-of-position.
- **Hatred (Intruders)** *(Dryad unit identity rule)*. **Hatred** (per §8) against **all enemy units** — Dryads despise every creature that has not been permitted into Athel Loren. Reroll failed to-Hit rolls in the first round of combat against any enemy. The lore-canonical *"hearts of ice"* spite, given mechanical form. (Other Forest Spirit units in the sub-roster may carry different Hatred scopes; the Dryad scope is broad reflecting the spite-of-the-forest baseline identity. Even other Forest Spirits *outside Athel Loren* could theoretically be intruders — but the keyword filter excludes friendly Wood Elf and Forest Spirit units, so the rule never fires on faction-mates.)
- **Asrai Way of War** *(Wood Elf faction-wide identity rule — see FACTION.md)*. While in **Loose** state (per §6), this unit gains the **benefits of the Skirmisher special rule** (per §8): **Coherency 2", +1 Movement, Fire on the move, Free pivots**. The "Cannot Form" clause does NOT apply — Dryads may freely toggle between Formed (rank-discipline, Steady Ranks, depth-Resolve) and Loose (skirmisher-tier mobility, dispersion -1 to-Hit from incoming ranged, no-flank-or-rear-vulnerability). For Dryads, the Fire-on-the-move clause is moot (no ranged kit), but the +1 M (effective M 6 in Loose, paired with M+2D6 charge range) and Dispersed (mitigates ranged fire that would otherwise exploit Flammable) are both meaningful. *(Lore-canonical: Dryads emerge from the trees in dispersed packs, surrounding intruders before striking — the Loose state is closer to their natural battle-shape than Formed regimentation.)*

## Options

**No equipment options.** Forest Spirits do not wear armour, carry shields, or wield manufactured weapons. The bark-and-thorn body is the entire kit; the Spite Talons are part of the body, not an item.

**Command options:**
- **Branch Nymph (Leader)** (+5 pts) — older Dryad with a longer-lived bond to Athel Loren; sharper-edged talons, deeper-rooted Hatred.
- **No Standard Bearer.** Forest Spirits do not carry banners — Athel Loren itself is the standard the Dryad fights beneath; no mortal symbol could add to the wood's own claim on the spirit.
- **No Musician.** Forest Spirits do not march to drums or horns — the rustle of branches and the whisper of the wood through bark *is* the cadence the unit moves to.
