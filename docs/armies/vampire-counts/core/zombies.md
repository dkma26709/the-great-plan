# Zombies

> The crudest tier of Undead. Reanimated corpses in various stages of decay, no weapons beyond rotting fists, no armour, no coordination. Necromancers raise zombies as the lowest-effort animation — a horde of shuffling dead that overwhelms by mass and tirelessness rather than skill. Zombies are slower than Skeletons in lore, dimmer (the necromantic binding is loose, just enough to drive forward and bite). Where Skeletons are *trained* dead (former soldiers reanimated with their drilling intact), Zombies are *just dead* (any corpse will do).

## Special Rules

- Undisciplined
- **Undead** (see §8)
- **Numberless** *(identity rule)* — when **Invocation of Nehek** (or any Necromancy spell that restores Undead models) targets a Zombies unit, the spell restores **+2 wounds beyond the listed amount** (e.g., D6+2 wounds for Invocation of Nehek's D6 baseline). Represents zombies as the cheapest resurrection target — any corpse will do, and the necromancer can flood the table with shambling dead by a fraction of the effort required for a Skeleton
- **Tide of the Restless Dead** *(identity rule)* —
- **Spreading mass.** This unit may use **2" coherency** (Skirmishers' value) — the Zombie tide doesn't keep ranks; it spreads loosely and wraps around enemy formations. The unit is *not* a Skirmisher (no Skirmisher rules apply); only the coherency value is loosened.
- **Bogging mass.** Enemy units in base contact with this Zombie unit are treated as **Loose** for the duration of the contact, regardless of their actual geometry. Formation discipline collapses against the dead's grasping mass — the engaged enemy unit forfeits all Formed benefits (charge resistance, +1 Res on stress tests, CR absorb-2) while in contact. Effect ends as soon as the contact ends.
- **Enveloping mass.** While the engaged enemy unit has **fewer current models than this Zombie unit**, the enemy cannot voluntarily Disengage, Fall Back, or Flee from contact with this Zombie unit. The corpses have wrapped around — there is no clean way out.
- **Clambering mass.** At the **end of the Combat phase**, after all strikes have resolved and combat resolution has been computed (but before consequences fire at End of Turn), if this unit was engaged with an enemy unit during the phase, each model may make an individual repositioning move of up to **2"**. Constraints:
- The unit must end the repositioning **in coherency** (per its 2" value above).
- The unit must end with at least one model **in base contact with an enemy unit it was engaged with this phase** — the dead don't let go.
- Otherwise unrestricted: models may move in any direction, including around the enemy's flanks or rear. Subsequent Combat phases resolve with the new arc geometry — a Zombie unit that climbs around to the rear over two phases will, on the third, be striking the rear arc with all the directional consequences that implies.
- **No attacks resolve** from this movement. No reactions, no Stand-and-Shoot, no charge bonuses — this is structural shuffle only, the dead climbing over their own and the enemy's fallen to find new grips on the prey.
- *The dead don't break formation because they have none. They don't tire because they cannot. They keep coming until your line collapses or the binding fails.*
