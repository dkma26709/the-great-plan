# Vampire Counts — Test Profiles


> **Design principle:** Cross-faction demonstration roster — third non-Seraphon faction after Empire and Ogres. Where Empire stress-tested rank-and-flank human infantry and Ogres tested Monstrous-Infantry-as-whole-army, Vampire Counts test the **Undead paradigm** — entire armies that don't break, don't rout, dissolve when overwhelmed (per the §8 Undead keyword and Crumble mechanic). Tests our resurrection / model-restoration framework via Necromancy spells, our keyword-stacking via Undead + Skeleton + Wight + Vampire sub-keywords, and our combat-and-caster hybrid pricing via the Vampire Lord. See `cross-faction-reference-notes.md` at project root for design-prep research.

> **Heft calibration note for Vampire Counts.** Two distinct heft tiers within the faction: **rank-and-file Undead** (Skeletons, Zombies, Grave Guard) keep WFB reference stats with **no +1 Res bump** — their low Res (4-6) is *intentional*. Res in our stress system controls when Crumble triggers, and rank-and-file Undead are meant to be brittle under sustained pressure (the lore is dissolution under stress, not resilience). The counterbalance is **Necromancy resurrection** — rank-and-file Undead are kept on the table by spell support, not by intrinsic durability. **Vampire characters** receive Lord/Hero-tier wound bumps (Vampire Lord W 3 → 5; Vampire Hero W 2 → 4) reflecting supernatural toughness; their Res stays at reference (10 / 9, already at the high end). **Necromancers** are mortal humans, not Undead — they take Hero-tier heft (Res +1 to 8 per stress-system standard, W bumped from reference 2 to 3 per the Hero-tier wound convention applied across §16 characters).

> **Faction-wide rule reminders.** Skeleton Warriors, Zombies, Grave Guard, Vampire Lord, and Vampire Hero all carry the **Undead keyword** (see §8). **Necromancer is mortal — no Undead keyword** (he can be Broken, can Rally, takes normal stress). The Necromancer's role is *keeping the Undead on the table* via spells, particularly **Invocation of Nehek** (signature of the Lore of Necromancy — see §11 Faction Lores, currently drafted as a stub; full lore expansion pending). Without Necromancy resurrection, rank-and-file Undead bleed off the table fast under sustained Crumble pressure — this is the deliberate balance pivot of the Undead-army paradigm.
