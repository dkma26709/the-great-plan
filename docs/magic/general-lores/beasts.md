# Lore of Beasts


> Primal, savage, transformative. Where Heavens is celestial and cerebral, Beasts is physical and instinctive — the magic of feral empowerment, of the predator's hunt, of fang and claw and bark-skin. A Beasts wizard does not throw thunderbolts. They wake the animal in their allies and send it loose.

**Lore Attribute — Pack Senses:** A Beasts caster may cast spells that require line of sight through friendly units, treating those friendlies as not blocking LOS. The wizard sees through the eyes of drilled beasts and the instincts of nearby creatures — a natural advantage low-LiS casters benefit from more than high-LiS ones, since the latter already see over their own lines.

**Signature Spell — Mark of the Beast**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 6 | 18" | Augment | Until start of next turn |

Target friendly unit's melee attacks gain **Poisoned Attacks** until start of next turn. Claws, fangs, and blades run with bestial venom.

#### Basic (Tier 1) — Lore Access 1+

**Flock of Doom**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 8 | 24" | Direct Damage | Instant |

Target enemy unit suffers **2D6+2 hits at S2, AP 0, D1, Poisoned Attacks**. A cloud of crows, bats, ravens, or worse descends on the target. Individual pecks do little, but the filthy beaks carry venom and there are a lot of them. A swarm-volume spell — devastating to infantry, shrugged off by monsters (whose durability is what the Amber Spear is for). Strong-for-tier hit volume: averages ~9 hits with Poisoned auto-wounding on natural 6s, so the CV is held high in Basic.

**Pann's Impenetrable Pelt**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 7 | 18" | Augment | Until start of next turn |

Target friendly unit gains Natural Armour (5+) until start of next turn. If the unit already has Natural Armour better than 5+, it retains its own value — the spell does not worsen anything. A thick coat of bristle and hide grows over the target; temporary bestial armour.

**Curse of Anraheir**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 8 | 24" | Hex | Until start of next turn |

Target enemy unit suffers **-1 to hit** with all melee attacks. The unit also treats all terrain as dangerous (resolved per the terrain rules in §6 / §12 when formalised — for now, impose -1 M when moving through any terrain feature in addition to the to-hit penalty). The curse snares limbs, twists judgement, makes the ground betray them. Dual-effect hex (combat debuff + movement penalty) — strong-for-tier, so CV held high in Basic.

**Savage Vigour**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 8 | 18" | Augment | Until start of next turn |

Target friendly unit gains **Frenzy** until start of next turn. When the duration ends, the unit reverts to its normal state — it does not retain Frenzy (nor does it lose any Frenzy it already had). Frenzy's normal rules apply during the duration: +1 A, must charge the nearest visible enemy, cannot disengage from combat, Immune to Psychology.

#### Intermediate (Tier 2) — Lore Access 2+

**The Amber Spear**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 10 | 36" | Magic Missile | Instant |

**D3 hits at S8, AP -3, D3.** A crystalline spear of pure animal fury lances out from the caster. The anti-large tool of the lore — designed to crack open Monstrous Infantry, Cavalry, and Monsters. A direct hit on a Dragon is genuinely dangerous. The lore's longest-reach spell — a thrown projectile rather than a summoned animal — and the only Beasts answer to high-T high-W targets, so CV reflects the niche-defining power.

**Hunter's Mark**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 10 | 24" | Hex | Until start of next turn |

Target enemy unit becomes **Hated (this army)** until start of next turn — every friendly unit in the caster's army gains the Hatred (see §8) benefits against the marked target for its first round of engagement. The beast senses the wounded one; the whole pack zeroes in. Army-wide setup hex — every friendly unit benefits, so the CV is held at the high end of Intermediate.

**Bind the Prey**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 9 | 24" | Hex | Until start of next turn |

Target enemy unit suffers **-D3 Movement** until start of next turn. Phantom roots, brambles, and treacherous soil tangle their feet — limbs catch where they never did a moment ago. Pairs with The Hunt: friendlies speed up, enemies slow down, the gap closes.

**Savage Beast of Horros** *(Character-only augment)*

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 10 | 18" | Augment | Until start of next turn |

Target friendly **Character** gains **+2 Strength**, **+2 Attacks**, and **Frenzy** (see §8) until start of next turn. The hero swells with primal fury — for a single round, they fight as a beast-lord of legend, all snarling teeth and hammering blows. Champion-tier augment that pushes one chosen Character to monstrous combat output for a single decisive turn. Stacks deliberately with Wyssan's Wildform on the same Character's unit (Wyssan's targets the unit, Savage Beast targets the Character) for a peak two-cast combo. Frenzy's autonomy cost (must charge nearest enemy, cannot disengage) is the offset for the raw combat boost — a buffed Character may end up locked into a fight the player would rather avoid.

#### Advanced (Tier 3) — Lore Access 3+

**Wyssan's Wildform**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 12 | 18" | Augment | Until start of next turn |

Target friendly unit gains **+1 Strength and +1 Toughness** until start of next turn. The iconic Beasts transformation — the unit becomes a more bestial version of itself, hitting harder and absorbing more punishment. Cornerstone of a Beasts-buff strategy. Two-axis stat boost on a unit is among the strongest augments in the ruleset, so CV held at the high end of Advanced.

**The Hunt**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 12 | 18" | Augment | Until start of next turn |

Target friendly unit gains **+1 Movement and Swiftstride** until start of next turn. The pack runs together — faster, more reliable in the chase. Committed mobility; no damage rider. Paired with Bind the Prey on the same target, the speed differential is overwhelming.

**Regrowth of the Wild**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 12 | 12" | Augment | Until start of next turn |

Target friendly unit gains **Regeneration (4+)** until start of next turn. Bark, hide, or fur closes over wounds in real time — the body remakes itself against the damage it takes. Range held tight at 12" — Beasts healing is intimate, hand-on-hide rather than reaching across the field.

**Beast Ward**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 13 | 12" | Augment | Until start of next turn |

Target friendly unit gains **Ward vs Spells (5+)** and **+1 Toughness** until start of next turn. The primal energy of the earth shelters them — foreign magic finds no purchase, and their flesh hardens against physical harm. A dual-protection capstone. Two effects (anti-magic Ward + stat boost) — strong-for-tier, so CV held at the top of Advanced.

> **No Transformation.** Traditional WHFB/WAP Beasts included caster-to-monster transformation spells (Transformation of Kadon). These are deliberately omitted — they create mechanical awkwardness (replacing the model mid-game, swapping stats, mount interactions) without adding to the lore's core identity. Bestial power in this Lore of Beasts expresses through augmentation of existing troops, not the caster becoming a beast themselves.

#### Master (Tier 4) — Lore Access 4+

**The Wild Hunt**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 18 | 24" | Augment | Until start of next turn |

All friendly units with the **Warbeast**, **Cavalry**, or **Monstrous Cavalry** keyword within **24"** of the caster gain: **Frenzy** (see §8), **+1 S on the charge** (stacking with any existing charge bonuses), and **+1 Attack on their mount or natural-weapon profile** for the duration.

The primal hunting instinct surges across every beast and rider in the army. Saurians, horses, drakes, wolves — all remember what they are beneath harness and saddle, and become apex predators for a single wild turn. When the spell ends, the beasts remember their trainers and the hunt is over; the blood cools and discipline returns. Three-axis army-wide augment scoped to mounted units — apex offensive capstone, so CV holds high in Master.

**Mantle of the Beasts** *(RIP, aura)*

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 16 | 12" (self-radius) | Augment (aura), Remains In Play |

*Remains In Play.* While in play, all friendly units wholly within **12"** of the caster gain **Frenzy** (see §8). The wizard becomes the heart of a wild animal-host — bestial fury radiates outward in a tight bubble, infusing every fighter near her with the predator's instinct. Persistent army-wide Frenzy, distinct from Wild Hunt: where Wild Hunt is a one-turn spike scoped to Warbeast / Cavalry / Monstrous Cavalry with stacking charge and mount bonuses, Mantle is RIP and applies to *any* friendly unit type within the bubble. The 12" radius (vs Reading of the Stars' 18") reflects Frenzy's heavier autonomy cost — the caster must commit aggressively to the front, and the bubble's units lose the choice of when to charge for as long as the spell holds. Frenzy compels charges into the nearest visible enemy and prevents disengagement, so the spell scopes the bubble's tactical autonomy as well as its offensive output.

> **Design note on Lore of Beasts.** Range identity is **closer reach** than Heavens — the wizard works *with* nearby creatures and units rather than calling fate from afar, so most augments sit at 12-18" (Regrowth of the Wild and Beast Ward at 12" reflect the most intimate "hands on hide" magic, while standard augments stay at 18"). The lone 36" outlier is The Amber Spear, a *thrown projectile* of crystallised animal fury rather than a summoned creature — Beasts' single long-reach option, deliberately the lore's heaviest single-shot damage to compensate for the lack of other long-range tools. CV calibration after this pass: Sig 6 (must be reliable), Basic 7-8 with strong-effect spells (Flock of Doom, Curse of Anraheir, Savage Vigour) at the top of band, Intermediate 9-10 (the multi-effect hexes Hunter's Mark and the new champion-buff Savage Beast of Horros at the top), Advanced 12-13 across the board, Master 16-18. Master tier offers a binary capstone choice paralleling Heavens: **The Wild Hunt** for one-turn cavalry / mounted spike, **Mantle of the Beasts** for persistent army-wide Frenzy. A Lord-tier Beasts caster ideally takes both — they cover the burst-offensive and sustained-offensive axes respectively. Lore Attribute (Pack Senses) and the explicit "No Transformation" exclusion both remain unchanged.
