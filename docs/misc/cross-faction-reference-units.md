# Cross-Faction Reference Units


> **Design principle:** Single representative units drawn from factions outside the four full demonstration rosters (Empire, Ogres, Vampires, Bretonnia, plus the Lizardmen original). Each unit is a *single profile + identity rule + design note*, not a faction commitment — no full mechanic suite, no army-wide lore, no command structure beyond the standard Leader/Musician/Standard pattern. The purpose is **calibration data** — having more cross-faction comparison points helps validate stat heft, point cost, and identity-rule density without requiring a dedicated faction draft for each. Drafted 2026-04-28 to widen the reference set before further Lizardmen passes; lore informs identity throughout, since flavour and mechanics are inseparable.

## Orc Boyz *(Orcs & Goblins)*

> The rank-and-file warrior of every Waaagh!. Common Orcs are large, brutish, and aggressive — physically similar in size to Saurus Warriors and Chaos Warriors, beaten by neither in the brawl-from-the-front role. The fungal-tinged Orc physiology gives them T 4 resilience and **W 2** (Pass-4 species-identity scaling, capturing fungal pain-tolerance), and at S 4 an Orc swing genuinely hurts. They wear basic furs and crude scrap-armour, wield whatever **choppa** they could find or steal — the iconic Orc weapon, any heavy edged thing they can hack with, cleaver-weight enough to bite through light armour (**AP -1** baseline) — and follow whichever Orc shouts loudest until a louder one comes along. Without immediate enemies to fight, an Orc unit's natural aggression turns inward — squabbling, brawling, sometimes breaking ranks to charge at random just to release tension. This is the famous **Animosity**: the cost of fielding Orcs is that you don't entirely command them. The flip side: an Orc unit *in* combat thrives on it. **Loves a Scrap** captures this — every round of melee actively bleeds stress off the unit, AND once the Boyz have been brawling for a turn the momentum makes them hit harder (sustained-combat +1 attack from round 2 onward). The Orc unit warms up to the fight; a player who keeps the Boyz engaged keeps them functional indefinitely AND increasingly dangerous.

## High Elf Spearmen *(High Elves)*

> The standing levy of Ulthuan — the great island-realm of the High Elves. Every adult elf takes up arms at need; the Spearmen are the citizen-soldier core, drilled in formation phalanx since youth and ready to march at the call of their Prince. Ten thousand years of perpetual war against Druchii, Daemons, and the countless lesser threats of the world have honed their tactics to a perfection no younger race can match. Spearmen are not the elite — those are the warrior-cults of Swordmasters, White Lions, Phoenix Guard. But Spearmen are drilled to a standard that puts them above human State Troops and on par with Bretonnian Knights in steadiness. **Elven Grace** captures the iconic elven trait: a sixth-sense awareness of incoming blows that slows nothing and dodges everything within the elf's reaction window. **Martial Prowess** is the millennium-of-training reflex — every blow finds its target unless the elf himself fumbles. **Defensive Formation** is the spear-phalanx drill: locked shields, braced spears, every charging enemy meets a wall of points held at exactly the right height.

## Swordmasters of Hoeth *(High Elves)*

> Warrior-scholars of the White Tower. Cult-trained at Hoeth, the Asur god of wisdom — every Swordmaster has spent decades or centuries under the Loremasters, learning the thirty basic exercises of the order before progressing to forms that "would be impossible without decades of training and a mastery of one's own body." Founded by Phoenix King Bel-Korhadris to guard the Tower's scholars; weaponised by Aethis against the Cults of Excess and their Slaaneshi corruption — a secret war that continues to this day. When Ulthuan marches to war, none go more eagerly, "for only in war can they truly unleash the full extent of their deadly art." The Hoeth-forged ithilmar greatsword is balanced to perfect lightness despite being as long as an elf is tall, its edge never dulling, its enchantments enduring through helms and skulls alike. The cult vow: *On the last shore, a sword in my hand. Ulthuan shall never fall.* **MA / MD 7** and **I 7** mark the apex of non-character melee skill in the ruleset — Swordmasters are the best swordsmen alive, individually superior to almost anything they will face. **The Swordmaster identity is precision, not brute force.** A sword — even an ithilmar one — does not shred full plate; it *finds the gap*. The Hoeth-Forged Greatsword expresses this directly: low armour penetration (AP -1) but devastating depth (D 2) when the cut lands, and on the perfect strike (natural 6 to-Wound) **Touch of the Master** delivers 4 damage in a single blow — enough to one-shot a Lord-tier W ≤ 4 character. Lore-true: "the greatest masters of the order can slay a foe with but a touch, or kill with a single precisely-pitched whistle." The cult's combat fingerprint is the character / monster / elite-infantry hunter, not the plate-shredding grinder — the historical truth of the swordsman, expressed in mechanical form.

### Special Rules

- Disciplined
- **Elven Grace** *(faction-wide for elves — Ward 6+ in melee vs attacks at the elf's I or lower; see §8 Defence)*. At I 7, Swordmasters trigger the Ward against essentially everything in the ruleset — only apex-Initiative characters and a handful of monsters reach I 8+ and bypass it.
- **Martial Prowess** *(faction-wide for elves — reroll to-Hit 1s in melee; see §8 Combat)*. At MA 7, the unit is hitting on 2+ against most defenders, so Martial Prowess catches only the natural-1 fumbles; the gain is small but consistent.
- **Touch of the Master** *(Swordmasters unit identity rule)* — functions as **Killing Blow (Any, 4)** per §8. On an unmodified to-Wound roll of **6** in melee, the Swordmaster's attack deals **4 damage** instead of 2; excess damage does not carry between models (per §8 Killing Blow). The greatest masters can slay a foe with a touch, or kill with a single precisely-pitched whistle — the perfect strike finds the gap, drives through, and severs what cannot be re-knit. One-shots W ≤ 4 targets; meaningfully wounds anything bigger.
- **Swordmasters** *(Swordmasters of Hoeth unit identity rule)* — captures the apex cult discipline. Two effects:
  - **Blade-veil deflection.** Swordmasters gain **Ward Save (5+)** against unsaved wounds caused by **non-template ranged attacks** (arrows, bolts, bullets, javelins — anything resolved against a single-model target with line of sight that the elf can physically see in flight). The Ward does **not** apply against template attacks (cannonballs, mortar shells, breath weapons, flame templates, scatter weapons), against spell-source wounds (the §8 *Ward vs Spells* mechanic covers that ground separately), or against melee attacks (Elven Grace covers that ground). Lore-true: "blades blur and weave, knocking aside arrows in mid-flight" and the meditation ritual's "impenetrable web of ithilmar that swoops gracefully around the swordmaster." Per Ward Save Stacking (§8 Defence), this Ward stacks with other Ward sources under the standard step-up-to-4+-cap rule.
  - **No Initiative penalty from great weapons.** A Swordmaster suffers **no Initiative penalty** from wielding a great-weapon-class melee weapon. The Hoeth-forged greatsword is balanced to perfect lightness in cult-trained hands; the elf strikes at the bearer's full I 7 regardless of the weapon's class. *(Forward-compatibility clause: §8 imposes no great-weapon Initiative penalty by default; this entry ensures that if such a penalty is introduced in a future system pass, Swordmasters are exempt by their cult-training identity.)*

The mechanical fingerprint that emerges: low save-stripping, high per-wound depth, exceptional on the lucky-strike via Touch of the Master. `[2H Reach Magical]`

## War Lions *(High Elves)*

> Apex predators of the Chracian mountains, drawn into Asur service. Wild white lions of Chrace "stand as tall at the shoulder as a horse," and "a swipe of their claws is enough to shatter a spine" — they are amongst the fiercest creatures of the mountain kingdom, hunted in single combat as the rite of passage that turns a young Chracian Elf into a White Lion of the Phoenix Guard. The lions that march to war alongside Asur armies come from one of two paths. Most are **cub-raised** — taken as kits by veteran White Lion warriors and reared into "fierce but loyal war beasts," loyal to the regiment as the regiment is loyal to the pride. The rest are **mage-bound** — wild lions captured by Elven Wizards and held by Saphery-tier magical compulsion. The mage-bond is faster and riskier: "the white lions of Chrace are intelligent and patient beasts that are not easily controlled," and a bond that frays under battle pressure can turn the pride on the army that summoned it. Either way, what arrives on the field is a pride of pack-hunters: fast, fearless, devastating against soldiers on foot. **Pride Hunter** is the cult-of-Chrace bond expressed mechanically: the lions specialise in *infantry prey*. Against soldiers they read body language, isolate the weakest, and strike together. Against cavalry, monsters, or other beast prides, they remain competent threats — but the pack advantage is lost. Battlefield role: flanking strike force. Send them around the line, into the back of an infantry block, and watch the bone-breaker claws unmake whoever the cult sergeants point them at.

The pelt provides **no save in melee** — close-quarters strikes have leverage and force that arrows do not, finding gaps and gripping into the hide. *(Lore-stretch flagged in design.md: a pelt biologically resistant to arrows would also resist some melee, but the design framing scopes this rule strictly to ranged for mechanical distinctness.)*

## Dwarf Warriors *(Dwarfs)*

> The clan-warriors of the dwarf holds — every adult male dwarf is one, trained in axe and shield from boyhood. They wear well-crafted armour (every dwarf can make his own), wield well-forged weapons (every weapon a tool used as a weapon when needed), and march in solid formation under their thane's banner. Dwarfs do not seek glory — they seek to **repay grudges**, to hold the line, to grind the enemy down through patience and superior craftsmanship. The Book of Grudges remembers every offense ever committed against dwarfkind across millennia, and a dwarf in battle is reading from that book in real time. **Ancestral Grudge** captures the faction-wide background hatred — Hatred reserved for the specific traditional foes (Greenskins, Skaven, Trolls, Beastmen) who have wronged dwarfkind through history. **Stubborn** is the iconic dwarf morale: they ignore the indignities of Shaken and Wavering, fighting at full effectiveness until they finally shatter. **Resolute** is the morale-floor on top — when the line falters, dwarfs hold a half-step longer than other races would. And **Grudgebearer** is the Book of Grudges made manifest at the unit level: a clan-warrior reads a name from the book before the battle begins, and that name does not leave their mind until the bearer of it is dead.

### Special Rules

- Disciplined
- **Stubborn** *(Dwarf faction-wide identity rule — see §8)* — Dwarf units ignore the state effects of **Shaken** (-1 to-hit) and **Wavering** (cannot-charge). Stress still accumulates normally and the **Broken** threshold still applies; the unit fights at full effectiveness until it finally shatters. Iconic dwarf stoicism — the underground city's iron resolve made manifest in every clan-warrior. Faction-wide for Dwarfs (vs Empire's Greatswords-only application) reflects WFB lore: every dwarf is stoic at heart, and Hammerers / Ironbreakers / Longbeards differentiate above this baseline via *other* rules (Killing Blow, heavy plate, etc.) rather than via Stubborn itself.
- **Ancestral Grudge** *(Dwarf faction-wide identity rule)* — Dwarf models gain **Hatred** (see §8) against units with the **Greenskin**, **Skaven**, **Troll**, or **Beastmen** keyword. Hatred lets the unit reroll failed to-Hit rolls in the first round of combat against Hated targets. The Book of Grudges made manifest as a faction-wide background — dwarfs strike harder against the races that have wronged them across millennia.
- **Resolute** *(Dwarf-wide faction trait — see §8 Psychology)*. -1 to combat-resolution-loss-to-stress conversion (minimum 0). The phalanx absorbs the indignity of losing slightly better than other races.
- **Magic Resistance (1)** *(Dwarf faction-wide species trait — see §8 and §1.5 Dwarfs species reference)* — Dwarfs are **naturally resistant to magic**. Enemy spells targeting or affecting any Dwarf unit suffer **-1 to the casting roll**. The dwarven body and soul reject the Winds; spells skip off them more readily than they would mortal humans. Faction-wide species baseline; every drafted Dwarf unit carries this rule (elite tiers may scale to MR 2 if lore warrants).
- **Grudgebearer** *(Dwarf Warriors unit identity rule)* — at the start of each game (during deployment, before the first turn), the Dwarf player **nominates one enemy unit as a recorded grudge**. Dwarf Warriors gain **Hatred against the nominated unit for the entire game** — not just the first round of combat (per standard Hatred), but **every round** in which they fight that unit, until the nominated unit is destroyed. The Book of Grudges made manifest at the unit level: a clan-warrior reads a name from the book before the battle begins, and that name does not leave their mind until the bearer of it is dead. Tactical decision at deployment: the Dwarf player picks the enemy unit they most want to grind down — could be a non-traditional foe (Bretonnian Knights, Empire Knights, Slann) where Ancestral Grudge wouldn't apply, or doubles down on a Greenskin/Skaven Hated target for sustained reroll-to-Hit across the whole game.

## Slayers *(Dwarfs)*

> *"I am a Dwarf! My honour is my life and without it I am nothing. I shall become a Slayer. I shall seek redemption in the eyes of my ancestors. I shall become as death to my enemies, until I face he that takes my life and my shame."* — the Slayer Oath, sworn at the Shrine of Grimnir at Karak Kadrin, the **Slayer Keep**. Slayers are Dwarfs whose honour has been irreparably stained — by failure, by loss of family or treasure, by personal tragedy — and who have sworn to fight until killed in glorious combat. - **Quell Animosity** *(Black Orcs unit identity rule)* — *"Quit yer squabblin'!"* Friendly **Greenskin** units with any model within **6"** of this unit at the start of the Movement phase **automatically pass their Animosity roll this turn** (treat the D6 as a 2-6 result — no squabble triggered). The Black Orc's presence bellows orders at the lesser Orcs and Goblins, keeping their small minds on the enemy ahead. **Range 6"**; **Black Orcs cannot suppress their own Animosity via this rule** (they still roll Animosity for themselves per §8). The lore-defining mechanical contribution Black Orcs make to a Greenskin army: a single Black Orc unit anchors several nearby Orc / Goblin / Snotling units against the Animosity tax.
- **Armed to da Teef** *(Black Orcs unit identity rule)* — Black Orcs carry the **Black Orc Choppa + Shield**, the **Pair of Black Orc Choppas**, AND the **Great Weapon** simultaneously, "armed to da teef" per lore. At the **start of each Combat phase**, the controlling player declares which of the three weapon profiles is active for this unit *for that Combat phase*. The active profile determines the unit's Attacks, Strength bonus, AP, and shield availability for the duration of that phase. The Black Orc can switch profiles between Combat phases — no commitment, no penalty — letting the unit adapt to whatever is in melee range each round.

### Options

**No equipment swap options.** The full "armed to da teef" kit (Choppa+Shield / Pair of Choppas / Great Weapon) is baked into the base profile via Armed to da Teef; players don't choose at deployment — they choose each Combat phase.

**No worn armour options.** Heavy armour is the cult-elite kit; the Black Orc identity does not bend to list-build downgrade.

## Orc Boar Boyz *(Orcs & Goblins)*

> *"'Ere we go, 'ere we go, 'ere we go!"* — Orcs who, "through sheer guts and determination, have managed to tame and ride the vicious War Boar into battle." The War Boar is "an extremely tough animal" of the Badlands deserts, "notoriously bad-tempered, loudly flatulent, wholly dangerous and very unpredictable" — "almost as large as an Imperial warhorse, but considerably bulkier." The boar kills many would-be riders before accepting one; the survivors form **the prestigious ranks of the Boar Boyz**, the heavy shock cavalry of any Orc warlord's army. **Tusker Charge** is the unit signature: "the impact of the Boar Boyz charge shatters bones and sends their victims flying dozens of feet into the air." It takes some distance to build speed, but "the War Boar's churning legs get the beast moving at a rate wholly unexpected from such a lumpen mass," and the ensuing ground-shaking charge delivers D3 Impact hits per model at S 5 (the mount's S 4 brawn + charge momentum) AP -1 (tusks). The boar's **tough hide** (NA 6+) lets the unit "shrug off hails of missile fire" — the natural-armour layer compensates for the bare-Orc-rider baseline. Combat fingerprint: midt-tier Greenskin cavalry, devastating on the charge round, lighter in sustained melee than the price-matched apex chivalric or saurian cavalry tiers. The Tusker Charge is the play — bring the unit into shock range, charge, deliver Impact carnage, then hold the position with the surviving Boar Boyz' melee output.
