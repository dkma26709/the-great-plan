# The Fay Enchantress

> The most senior of the Lady's chosen — the Lady's voice incarnate, born in the Forest of Loren and raised in the secret groves of the fae. The Enchantress is older than any Bretonnian noble, possibly older than the chivalric order itself; her hair is silver, her eyes the colour of the lake at dawn, and the air around her shimmers with the Lady's grace. She does not ride out lightly — a duke must petition her at the Royal Court, and she chooses whether to attend his campaign. When she does, the battle's outcome is essentially decided in her favour. She knows every spell of the Lady's lore by heart, blesses the chivalric line with Wards that even Grail Knights envy, and walks beneath a personal aegis that turns aside every mortal blow. Her presence is the Lady's full presence on the battlefield; no Bretonnian army has ever lost a battle the Fay Enchantress chose to attend.

> **Named character.** Per §17 named-hero convention, the Fay Enchantress is a unique Lord-tier Lady's Chosen character. Only **one Fay Enchantress** may be fielded per army. **May not be fielded alongside the Prophetess of the Lady** — the two represent overlapping apex-caster roles (the Prophetess is the duchy's senior Damsel; the Fay Enchantress is the Lady's voice incarnate, of which there is only one in Bretonnia at a time).

## Special Rules

- Disciplined
- **Unique** *(per §17 named-hero convention)* — only one Fay Enchantress may be fielded per army; may not be fielded alongside the Prophetess of the Lady
- **Lady's Chosen** (intrinsic — see §17 Faction Mechanics) — counts as a vow-keyword for all rule interactions
- **Blessing of the Lady** (intrinsic via Lady's Chosen — see §17) — Ward Save 6+ vs S ≤ 4, 5+ vs S ≥ 5. Lost on flee from combat. **The Silver Pelt's 4+ Ward supersedes the Blessing at the personal level per §8 Ward Save Stacking** — the Enchantress effectively walks at Ward 4+ vs anything. The 6+/5+ Blessing remains the relevant value for the *mount* (Silvermane is not covered by the Silver Pelt)
- **Wizard (LA 4, Lore of the Lady, Loremaster)** — see Magic Profile. **Loremaster of the Lady** means the Enchantress knows **all 11 Lady spells** by heart (Signature + 4 Tier 1 + 3 Tier 2 + 3 Tier 3 + 2 Master). She does not roll for spells; she has them all. Mechanically equivalent to the Slann's *Focus of Mystery* Discipline, scoped to the Lady's lore
- **Inspiring Presence (12")** *(intrinsic — see §8)* — the Fay Enchantress may be nominated as the army's General. When she is, friendly Bretonnia units within 12" treat their Res as the Enchantress's (10), enables rally without Leader, allows rally in combat, may reroll recovery / rally rolls. (She is *typically* the General when fielded; the Bretonnian Lord may keep that role if the player prefers the chivalric command archetype, in which case the Enchantress's Inspiring Presence still fires but the Lord's superior position-on-the-front-line is the army's nominated General.)
- **The Silver Pelt** *(identity rule — personal item, intrinsic)* — the Enchantress wears a single garment woven in the Lady's groves: a silver fae-cloth that turns aside mortal harm. She has a personal **Ward Save (4+)** against any incoming attack (melee, ranged, magical). The Silver Pelt is *intrinsic* — it is not a magic item, cannot be removed, does not stack with magic-item Ward sources per §8 Ward Save Stacking (the Silver Pelt's 4+ supersedes the Blessing's 6+/5+ at the personal level). **Mount is not protected** — the Silver Pelt is the Enchantress's body alone; Silvermane saves on her own Blessing 6+/5+ if attacked
- **Magic Resistance (3)** (see §8) — enemy spells targeting or affecting this character suffer **-3 to the casting roll**. Stronger than the Prophetess's MR(1); the Enchantress is the Lady's voice, and hostile magic finds its hardest test against her
- **Aura of Beguilement** *(identity rule — area effect)* — enemy units in melee combat with the Enchantress or with any unit she is attached to (per §1 Character Attachment Exception, §6) suffer **-2 MA** (minimum MA 1) for the duration of the engagement. The fae-charm reduces enemy combatants to half-strikes; the Enchantress's presence and the Lady's protective grace unbalance the would-be attacker's lance and sword arm. Cumulative with other MA penalties up to the MA 1 minimum
- **Voice of the Lady** *(identity rule — once per battle, army-wide buff)* — once per battle, at the start of the friendly Magic phase, the Enchantress may invoke the Lady's voice. **Every friendly Bretonnia unit** on the battlefield has its **Blessing of the Lady Ward improved by one step** for the remainder of the current Battle round:
  - Knight's Vow / Crusader's Vow / Questing Vow Blessing 6+/5+ → 5+/4+
  - Grail Vow Blessing 5+/4+ → 4+/3+ (Strength-variable improvement caps at 3+ via §8 Ward Save Stacking)
  - Lady's Chosen Blessing (Damsel, Fay Enchantress herself via her Blessing) 6+/5+ → 5+/4+
  - Grail Reliquae's Blessing 6+/5+ → 5+/4+
- **Peasants without an intrinsic Blessing are unaffected** — Voice of the Lady can only *improve* an existing Blessing, not grant one. The Reliquae's Pilgrims have the Blessing intrinsically and benefit; ordinary peasant units (Men-at-Arms, Longbowmen, Bidowers, Mounted Yeomen, Field Trebuchet Crew) do not. The Lady speaks through her chosen; her voice falls on the chivalric host like a bell across the battlefield
- **Lance Formation eligible** (when attached to a KotR / Knights Errant / Grail Knights unit per §1 Character Attachment Exception, §6) — counts as Lance per §17 Faction Mechanics
- **Class Barrier** *(clause of Peasant's Duty — interaction)* — Lady's Chosen keyword. Cannot effectively attach to peasant units (Men-at-Arms, Longbowmen, Bidowers, Mounted Yeomen, Field Trebuchet Crew, Grail Reliquae Pilgrims) — loses Character Attachment shielding when within their coherency. Attaches normally to vow-bearing units (KotR, Knights Errant, Questing Knights, Pegasus Knights, Grail Knights, Foot Knights, Lord / Paladin / Damsel)
- **Silvermane the Unicorn** *(mount profile note)* — the Enchantress's unique fae-mount; the *original* Unicorn from which the rest of the species descend. M 14 (fastest non-flying mount in any drafted faction), MA 5 MD 5, S 4, T 4, W 3, I 6, Res 9. **Magical Attacks** on its strikes (the Unicorn's horn is woven fae-iron). **Magic Resistance (1)** intrinsic to the mount — non-stacking with the Enchantress's MR(3) (MR is rider-specific per §8; the Enchantress walks at MR(3), Silvermane defends at MR(1) when she is the spell's target). Silvermane does not take barding; the Unicorn is fae, not bred for war-armour
