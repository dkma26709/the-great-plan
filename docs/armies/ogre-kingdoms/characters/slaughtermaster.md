# Slaughtermaster


> The fattest Ogre in the army — the spiritual leader of the tribe. A Butcher who has consumed enough enemy meat, blood, and fat to ascend to Lord status; tattooed in tribal sigils, blood-streaked, gut-pot strapped to his shoulders. The Slaughtermaster channels Gut Magic — the Lore of the Great Maw — drawn from the cosmological hunger of the Great Maw itself, the primordial hole-in-the-ground worshipped as a god. Beasts magic flows naturally to Ogres alongside Maw — savage, animal magic suits Ogre cosmology where most other Winds do not. The Tyrant rules through dominance; the Slaughtermaster rules through spiritual gravitas (and being too fat to easily kill).

> **Lore notes:** The fattest Ogre in the army — spiritual leader, gut-pot strapped to his shoulders, channelling the Lore of the Great Maw. **Dual-lore intrinsic access (Great Maw + Beasts)** is the structural identity — the only fixed-dual-lore configuration in the ruleset. Beasts magic flows naturally to Ogres alongside Maw — savage, animal magic suits Ogre cosmology where most other Winds do not. Wears no armour over the gut-plate — *the bulk is the protection, and the flesh is the spell-focus*. **Bloodgreed** is the identity rule: on personal melee kills (own attacks only — not allies, not magic, not ranged), regenerate 1 W. With Bloodgreed regen, the Slaughtermaster who commits to melee can sustain damage over a battle, but only at the cost of personal risk and the absence of armour.
