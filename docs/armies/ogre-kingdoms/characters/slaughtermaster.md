# Slaughtermaster


> The fattest Ogre in the army — the spiritual leader of the tribe. A Butcher who has consumed enough enemy meat, blood, and fat to ascend to Lord status; tattooed in tribal sigils, blood-streaked, gut-pot strapped to his shoulders. The Slaughtermaster channels Gut Magic — the Lore of the Great Maw — drawn from the cosmological hunger of the Great Maw itself, the primordial hole-in-the-ground worshipped as a god. Beasts magic flows naturally to Ogres alongside Maw — savage, animal magic suits Ogre cosmology where most other Winds do not. The Tyrant rules through dominance; the Slaughtermaster rules through spiritual gravitas (and being too fat to easily kill).

> **Lore notes:** Stats per heft calibration — WS 4 / BS 2 / S 4 / T 5 / I 3 / A 4 reference unchanged. **W 5 → 8** (Lord-caster wound bump; **parity with Slann W 8** — both are Lord-tier corpulent caster characters whose role is spiritual / magical leadership, not personal warlord apex. Slaughtermaster sits **below the Tyrant's W 10** because the Tyrant is the *combat* Lord of the Ogre roster and the Slaughtermaster is the *spiritual* Lord — different role-axes, different bulk profiles. Per the "no artificial wound ceiling — unit identity drives the wound count" principle: Slaughtermaster lands at W 8 not because Slann sits there, but because the Slaughtermaster-as-spiritual-leader identity calibrates similarly to other Lord-caster identities). **Ld 8 reference unchanged** (no +1 bump per Ogre heft note). Combined **6+ save baseline (gut-plate alone)** — no armour option per "Slaughtermasters wear no armour over the gut" lore call; the bulk is the protection. Magic Profile is the Lord-caster ladder — Lvl 3 (LA 3) at CaB 3 / DiB 2 / Ch 2 / CB +2 / DB +1 / DR 18" — Channels and DR both step down from Wizard Lord Lvl 3's CaB 3 / DiB 2 / Ch 3 / CB +2 / DB +1 / DR 24" because Slaughtermaster trades single-spell throughput for dual-lore breadth. **Lvl 4 upgrade (+35 pts)** bumps CaB +1, Ch +1, CB +1, DB +1, unlocks LA 4 — at Lvl 4 the Slaughtermaster reaches Master tier in *both* lores (when Master content exists). **Lores Known** is the structural identity — fixed dual-lore (Maw + Beasts) is the only such configuration in the ruleset to date; sits between single-lore casters (Skink Priest, Empire wizards) and Slann's all-Winds Mastery. The 4-spells-known total (2 Sig + 2 additional) is twice what a single-lore Lord-caster knows; books in part of the 270 / 305 pt cost. **Bloodgreed** is the identity rule — self-only W regeneration on personal melee kills. Per design call (2026-04-25 session), only the Slaughtermaster's own kills count — *not* allies, *not* magic, *not* ranged. Captures the Butcher's literal lore (consume the slain). Pattern: combat-axis identity, no morale-axis interaction. With W 8 baseline + Bloodgreed regen, the Slaughtermaster who commits to melee can sustain damage over a battle — but only at the cost of personal risk and the absence of armour. **Lore of the Great Maw is pending separate §11 drafting** — Slaughtermaster ships with the magic profile, lore-access mechanic, and Bloodgreed locked, but the actual Maw spells are deferred. Note: the **Great Mawpot** army-buff magic item, traditionally carried by the Slaughtermaster in WFB reference, is also pending the lore drafting (will be drafted alongside the Maw spell list as a magic-item upgrade slot).
