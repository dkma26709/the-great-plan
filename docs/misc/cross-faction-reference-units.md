# Cross-Faction Reference Units


> **Design principle:** Single representative units drawn from factions outside the four full demonstration rosters (Empire, Ogres, Vampires, Bretonnia, plus the Seraphon original). Each unit is a *single profile + identity rule + design note*, not a faction commitment — no full mechanic suite, no army-wide lore, no command structure beyond the standard Leader/Musician/Standard pattern. The purpose is **calibration data** — having more cross-faction comparison points helps validate stat heft, point cost, and identity-rule density without requiring a dedicated faction draft for each. Drafted 2026-04-28 to widen the reference set before further Lizardmen passes; lore informs identity throughout, since flavour and mechanics are inseparable.

### Orc Boyz *(Greenskins / Orcs & Goblins — Common Orcs, Core)*

> The rank-and-file warrior of every Waaagh!. Common Orcs are large, brutish, and aggressive — physically similar in size to Saurus Warriors and Chaos Warriors, beaten by neither in the brawl-from-the-front role. The fungal-tinged Orc physiology gives them surprising T 4 resilience, and at S 4 an Orc swing genuinely hurts. They wear basic furs and crude scrap-armour, wield whatever **choppa** they could find or steal (the iconic Orc weapon — any heavy edged thing they can hack with), and follow whichever Orc shouts loudest until a louder one comes along. Without immediate enemies to fight, an Orc unit's natural aggression turns inward — squabbling, brawling, sometimes breaking ranks to charge at random just to release tension. This is the famous **Animosity**: the cost of fielding Orcs is that you don't entirely command them. The flip side: an Orc unit *in* combat thrives on it. Brawling is what they're for. **Loves a Scrap** captures this — every round of melee actively bleeds stress off the unit, and a player who keeps the Boyz engaged keeps them functional indefinitely.

- **Keywords:** Greenskin, Orc, Infantry, Core
- **Unit type:** Infantry
- **Unit size:** 10+
- **Points:** 7 per model
- **Natural Armour:** —
- **Equipment (default):** Choppa (hand weapon)
- **Worn armour options:** Light armour (+1 pt/model) → 6+ save
- **Worn armour additive:** Shield (+1 pt/model) — combines with armour for one step per source (per §8 Armour Save Stacking)
- **Equipment options (pick one):**
  - **Additional Choppa** (+1 pt/model) — second hand weapon; cannot pair with shield
  - **Spear** (+1 pt/model) — 2" reach; may pair with shield
  - **Great Choppa** (+2 pts/model) — replaces hand weapon, no shield, two-handed
- **Command options:**
  - **Boss (Leader)** (+5 pts) — champion
  - **Standard Bearer** (+10 pts, may take Magic Standard up to 25 pts)
  - **Musician** (+5 pts)

**Special rules:**
- Undisciplined (see §8)
- **Animosity** *(Greenskin-wide identity rule)* — at the start of each Movement phase, roll a **D6** for the unit. On a **1**, the unit's natural aggression breaks discipline:
  - If any enemy unit is within charge range (per §6 Charges) and visible, the unit **must declare a charge against the nearest such enemy** this Movement phase
  - If no charge is possible (no visible enemy within charge range), the unit instead gains **+1 stress** and may not Advance or March this Movement phase (squabbling among themselves)
  
  Iconic Greenskin unpredictability — the unit might surge forward when the player wanted them to hold, or stand bickering when the player wanted them to flank. The cost of fielding Greenskins: a player commits to roughly 1-in-6 per unit per turn losing positional control.
- **Loves a Scrap** *(Greenskin-wide identity rule)* — at the end of each **Combat phase** the unit was engaged in melee, the unit **removes 1 stress**. Greenskins thrive on the brawl — every round of combat physically calms them, and the squabbling-Animosity disappears the moment there's a real fight to have. Pairs thematically with Animosity: an Orc unit that *isn't* fighting accumulates problems (Animosity squabbles, slow stress creep), while one that *is* fighting bleeds stress off naturally. Player incentive: keep the Boyz engaged.
- **Boyz Will Be Boyz** *(Orc Boyz unit identity rule)* — when armed with one or two hand weapons (Choppa or Pair of Choppas — **NOT** spear, **NOT** great weapon), the unit gains **+1 Attack** on the turn it charges. The iconic Orc brawl-rush: throw yourself at the enemy with both arms swinging. Restricting to hand weapons preserves spear+shield as a genuine defensive tradeoff and prevents great-weapon Orc charges from becoming overpowering.

**Weapon profiles:**

- **Choppa** *(default, 1H — may pair with shield)* — 1" reach | **A 1** | S 4 | AP 0 | D 1 — any heavy edged thing the Orc can swing `[1H Blade]`
- **Pair of Choppas** *(Additional Hand Weapon — 2× 1H — cannot pair with shield)* — 1" reach | **A 2** | S 4 | AP 0 | D 1 — second hand weapon doubles the swings; loses the shield slot `[Paired]`
- **Spear** *(option, 1H — may pair with shield)* — 2" reach | **A 1** | S 4 | AP 0 | D 1 — improvised spear; the 2" reach lets the second rank stab past the first. No charge-defence bonus (unlike Celestite Spear) — Orcs are not drilled phalanx fighters `[1H Reach]`
- **Great Choppa** *(option, 2H — no shield, +2 pts/model)* — 1" reach | **A 1** | S+2 (= S 6) | AP -1 | D 1 | **Two-Handed** | **-1 Initiative when used** — heavy two-handed cleaver; pure brute damage. The wielder swings high and hard, but the bulk of the weapon slows their reactions: -1 I makes Orc Great Choppa swings effective I 1 (base I 2 minus 1) — striking later than almost anything in the game. The +1 I from charging brings it back to I 2 on the charge turn, but on the receiving end of a charge or in a sustained brawl, the Great Choppa Orc takes hits before swinging back. No Boyz Will Be Boyz bonus on the charge `[2H]`

> **Lore notes:** **S 3 → 4** (Orcs are physically large infantry — same size class as Saurus and Chaos Warriors; strength baked into the base profile rather than via Mighty Blow). **T 4** unchanged from WAP. **Res 7** unchanged (deliberate cross-faction +1 waiver — Greenskins love combat but aren't brave; the +1 Res convention applies to disciplined factions like Empire / Bretonnia humans, not Greenskins). **I 2** unchanged. **A 1** baseline (single hand weapon = A 1; +Additional Hand Weapon = A 2; +charge with Boyz Will Be Boyz = A 2 → A 3 with hand weapons). **Animosity** translated from WAP "D6 each turn → behaviour table" to "D6 each turn → charge-or-stress" — preserves iconic unpredictability without requiring a behaviour-table sub-system. **Loves a Scrap** is the Greenskin morale signature — bleeds stress in melee, rewards aggressive play, punishes holding the Boyz in reserve. **Boyz Will Be Boyz** is the unit-specific identity (distinct from the Greenskin-wide rules) — +1 attack on the charge captures the iconic Orc brawl-rush and creates room for **Big'uns** above (next tier: same S 4 / T 4 base, +1 A always-on rather than charge-only, possibly +1 W, heavier armour, ~10-12 pts). Great Choppa pricing tightened from a free swap to +2/model with -1 I and AP -1 (was -2) to ensure it's not the auto-pick — each weapon option (default + shield, Additional Choppa, Spear + shield, Great Choppa) now has a distinct tactical niche. Points 7 per model — uplifted from WAP 5 to reflect S 4 base + Loves a Scrap relief mechanic, while Animosity downside stops it climbing to 8-10.
>
> **Greenskin design philosophy** *(extracted for future Goblin / Big'un drafts)*: fine combat stats (S 4 / T 4 for Orcs; smaller and weaker for Goblins), low Initiative (I 2 Orcs / probably I 3 Goblins), low Resolve (Res 7 Orcs / Res 5-6 Goblins — never the +1 cross-faction bump), love of combat for Orcs (Loves a Scrap stress relief in melee — Goblins likely get a different morale-mechanic since Goblins *don't* love fighting), Animosity faction-wide (scales: Orc squabbles, Goblin abject panic). Saurus / Skink-style intra-faction differentiation — Orcs are the strong-tough core, Goblins are the cheap-disposable horde, with full mechanical separation.

### High Elf Spearmen *(Asur / High Elves — Core)*

> The standing levy of Ulthuan — the great island-realm of the High Elves. Every adult elf takes up arms at need; the Spearmen are the citizen-soldier core, drilled in formation phalanx since youth and ready to march at the call of their Prince. Ten thousand years of perpetual war against Druchii, Daemons, and the countless lesser threats of the world have honed their tactics to a perfection no younger race can match. Spearmen are not the elite — those are the warrior-cults of Swordmasters, White Lions, Phoenix Guard. But Spearmen are drilled to a standard that puts them above human State Troops and on par with Bretonnian Knights in steadiness. **Elven Grace** captures the iconic elven trait: a sixth-sense awareness of incoming blows that slows nothing and dodges everything within the elf's reaction window. **Martial Prowess** is the millennium-of-training reflex — every blow finds its target unless the elf himself fumbles. **Defensive Formation** is the spear-phalanx drill: locked shields, braced spears, every charging enemy meets a wall of points held at exactly the right height.

- **Keywords:** Asur, High Elf, Infantry, Core
- **Unit type:** Infantry
- **Unit size:** 10+
- **Points:** 10 per model
- **Natural Armour:** —
- **Equipment (default):** Asur Spear + Light armour + Shield (combined save **5+**)
- **Worn armour options:** Medium armour (+1 pt/model) — upgrades light armour to medium (combined with shield = **4+** save)
- **No equipment swap options** — Spearmen are spear-specialists; no great-weapon or additional-weapon options. Players who want elf elite-melee take Swordmasters or White Lions.
- **Command options:**
  - **Sentinel (Leader)** (+5 pts) — phalanx champion
  - **Standard Bearer** (+10 pts, may take Magic Standard up to 25 pts)
  - **Musician** (+5 pts)

**Special rules:**
- Disciplined (see §8)
- **Elven Grace** *(High Elf faction-wide identity rule)* — High Elf models gain **Ward (6+) in melee** against attacks made at the elf's Initiative value **or lower**. Against attacks made by models with **higher Initiative than the elf**, Elven Grace does not apply — the strike lands faster than the elf can react. With High Elf Spearmen at I 5, most opponents (humans I 3, Orcs I 2, Saurus I 1, dwarfs I 2) trigger the Ward; only high-I attackers (some elf-tier characters, fast monsters, Witch Elves at I 6) bypass it. Iconic elven trait — they *are* harder to hit, but only as long as nothing strikes faster.
- **Martial Prowess** *(High Elf faction-wide identity rule)* — High Elf models may **reroll all to-Hit rolls of 1** in melee. The millennium-of-training reflex — every blow finds its target unless the elf himself fumbles.
- **Defensive Formation** *(High Elf Spearmen unit identity rule)* — when defending against a Charge declared by an enemy unit, this unit gains **+1 to-Hit** in the resulting Combat phase against the charging unit (and only against that unit, for the round of combat resulting from that charge). The drilled phalanx of Lothern: spears braced, shields locked, every charging enemy meets a wall of points held at exactly the right height. Captures "elf phalanx is harder to charge than to charge at" — a player who wants Spearmen to be effective should hold them in defensive formation, not push them forward to make the first strike.

**Weapon profiles:**

- **Asur Spear** *(default, 1H — paired with shield)* — 2" reach | **A 1** | S 3 | AP 0 | D 1 — drilled elven spear; the 2" reach lets the second rank stab past the first as part of the formation drill. No anti-charge bonus baked into the weapon itself — that bonus is the unit's Defensive Formation identity rule rather than a weapon trait, so it scopes cleanly to the unit (a Hero with a spear from the Armoury wouldn't gain Defensive Formation just by holding a spear). `[1H Reach]`

> **Lore notes:** Stats unchanged from WAP reference except **Res 8 → 9** per cross-faction +1 convention applied to disciplined factions (Empire / Bretonnia humans, High Elves — *not* Greenskins). Res 9 puts Spearmen at Imperial Knight / Greatsword morale tier — appropriate for ten-millennia-trained citizen-soldiers; the spear-levy-but-elite-by-human-standards positioning. **T 3 / W 1** unchanged — elves are slender; what makes them resilient is high I + Elven Grace + Martial Prowess, not stat heft. **M 5** unchanged — elves are quick. **I 5** unchanged — the iconic elven reflex. **A 1** baseline. **Elven Grace and Martial Prowess** are faction-wide rules (would apply to most other High Elf units we'd later draft — Sea Guard, Swordmasters, Phoenix Guard, etc.). **Defensive Formation** is the unit-specific identity, distinct from the faction-wide rules — captures "Spearmen are best when receiving charges, not making them." **Valour of Ages** (WAP re-roll Panic vs Dark Elves) skipped — our system uses stress, not Panic tests, and the Dark Elf-specific scope is too narrow to translate cleanly. Points 10 per model — uplifted from WAP reference (~9-11) to reflect Res 9 bump and the Defensive Formation identity. Sits between Empire Halberdier (6 pts, T 3 S 3 WS 3 Res 8, no faction-wide buffs) and Saurus Warrior (10 pts, T 4 S 4 + Predatory Fighter): same price as Saurus despite worse defensive stats, but compensated by I 5 + Elven Grace + Martial Prowess + Defensive Formation — the elf model is *individually* superior in skill but dies to one wound. Direct trade-off between durability and skill.
>
> **High Elf design philosophy** *(extracted for future Asur drafts)*: high WS / BS / I (4 / 4 / 5 baseline), high Res (+1 cross-faction bump applied), low T / W (3 / 1 — slender, fragile), faction-wide Elven Grace (Ward 6+ in melee vs equal-or-lower I) and Martial Prowess (reroll to-Hit 1s in melee). Skill-and-discipline identity, distinct from Greenskin "fight hard but unreliable" and Saurus "tough and disciplined." Elf elites would scale via more attacks, higher WS, sometimes Killing Blow or other one-hit mechanics — never via T or W increases.

### Dwarf Warriors *(Karaz Ankor / Dwarfs — Core)*

> The clan-warriors of the dwarf holds — every adult male dwarf is one, trained in axe and shield from boyhood. They wear well-crafted armour (every dwarf can make his own), wield well-forged weapons (every weapon a tool used as a weapon when needed), and march in solid formation under their thane's banner. Dwarfs do not seek glory — they seek to **repay grudges**, to hold the line, to grind the enemy down through patience and superior craftsmanship. The Book of Grudges remembers every offense ever committed against dwarfkind across millennia, and a dwarf in battle is reading from that book in real time. **Ancestral Grudge** captures the faction-wide background hatred — Hatred reserved for the specific traditional foes (Greenskins, Skaven, Trolls, Beastmen) who have wronged dwarfkind through history. **Stubborn** is the iconic dwarf morale: they ignore the indignities of Shaken and Wavering, fighting at full effectiveness until they finally shatter. **Resolute** is the morale-floor on top — when the line falters, dwarfs hold a half-step longer than other races would. And **Grudgebearer** is the Book of Grudges made manifest at the unit level: a clan-warrior reads a name from the book before the battle begins, and that name does not leave their mind until the bearer of it is dead.

|---|----|----|----|---|---|---|---|----|-----|----|
| 3 | 4 | 4 | 3 | 3 | 4 | 1 | 2 | 10 | 1 | 1 |

- **Keywords:** Dwarf, Karaz Ankor, Infantry, Core
- **Unit type:** Infantry
- **Unit size:** 10+
- **Points:** 12 per model
- **Natural Armour:** —
- **Equipment (default):** Dwarf Axe (hand weapon) + Medium armour + Shield (combined save **4+**)
- **Equipment swap option (free, replaces shield):**
  - **Great Weapon** — 2H, no shield (combined save 5+ from medium armour alone)
- **Worn armour upgrade option:**
  - **Heavy armour** (+2 pts/model) — upgrades medium to heavy (combined save 3+ with shield, or 4+ with great weapon)
- **Command options:**
  - **Veteran (Leader)** (+5 pts) — clan champion
  - **Standard Bearer** (+10 pts, may take Magic Standard up to 25 pts)
  - **Musician** (+5 pts)

**Special rules:**
- Disciplined (see §8)
- **Stubborn** *(Dwarf faction-wide identity rule — see §8)* — Dwarf units ignore the state effects of **Shaken** (-1 to-hit) and **Wavering** (cannot-charge). Stress still accumulates normally and the **Broken** threshold still applies; the unit fights at full effectiveness until it finally shatters. Iconic dwarf stoicism — the underground city's iron resolve made manifest in every clan-warrior. Faction-wide for Dwarfs (vs Empire's Greatswords-only application) reflects WFB lore: every dwarf is stoic at heart, and Hammerers / Ironbreakers / Longbeards differentiate above this baseline via *other* rules (Killing Blow, heavy plate, etc.) rather than via Stubborn itself.
- **Ancestral Grudge** *(Dwarf faction-wide identity rule)* — Dwarf models gain **Hatred** (see §8) against units with the **Greenskin**, **Skaven**, **Troll**, or **Beastmen** keyword. Hatred lets the unit reroll failed to-Hit rolls in the first round of combat against Hated targets. The Book of Grudges made manifest as a faction-wide background — dwarfs strike harder against the races that have wronged them across millennia.
- **Resolute** *(Dwarf faction-wide identity rule)* — when this unit would convert **combat resolution loss to stress** (per §5 — losing combat causes the loser to take stress equal to the CR difference), the unit **subtracts 1** from the converted total (minimum 0). The dwarf phalanx absorbs the indignity of losing slightly better than other races. Translates the WAP "lose by 1 less" Break-test mechanic to our stress-based combat-resolution system.
- **Grudgebearer** *(Dwarf Warriors unit identity rule)* — at the start of each game (during deployment, before the first turn), the Dwarf player **nominates one enemy unit as a recorded grudge**. Dwarf Warriors gain **Hatred against the nominated unit for the entire game** — not just the first round of combat (per standard Hatred), but **every round** in which they fight that unit, until the nominated unit is destroyed. The Book of Grudges made manifest at the unit level: a clan-warrior reads a name from the book before the battle begins, and that name does not leave their mind until the bearer of it is dead. Tactical decision at deployment: the Dwarf player picks the enemy unit they most want to grind down — could be a non-traditional foe (Bretonnian Knights, Empire Knights, Slann) where Ancestral Grudge wouldn't apply, or doubles down on a Greenskin/Skaven Hated target for sustained reroll-to-Hit across the whole game.

**Weapon profiles:**

- **Dwarf Axe** *(default, 1H — pairs with shield)* — 1" reach | **A 1** | S 3 | AP 0 | D 1 — every dwarf carries an axe; well-forged baseline weapon `[1H Blade]`
- **Great Weapon** *(option, free swap, 2H — replaces shield)* — 1" reach | **A 1** | S+2 (= S 5) | AP -2 | D 1 | Two-Handed — great axe, war-hammer, two-handed maul; master-crafted dwarf-quality, no -1 I penalty (unlike the Orc Great Choppa — dwarf craftsmen forge weighted weapons that swing true). Same profile as Empire Greatswords' Great Weapon `[2H]`

> **Lore notes:** Stats unchanged from WAP reference except **Res 9 → 10** per cross-faction +1 convention applied to disciplined factions. Dwarfs at Res 10 sit at the rank-and-file morale cap — parity with Saurus Warriors, reflecting the iron stoicism that defines dwarf identity. **M 3** unchanged (dwarfs are slow — explicit lore choice, no charge bonus, no march beyond what their short legs allow). **I 2** unchanged (slow reactions; dwarfs hit hard but aren't fast). **T 4** unchanged (dwarf hardiness, parity with Orcs and Saurus). **W 1** unchanged (rank-and-file durability). **WS 4** unchanged — every adult dwarf is a trained warrior. **A 1** baseline. **Stubborn moved to faction-wide** for Dwarfs (vs Empire's elite-only application) — captures the iconic dwarf stoicism that applies universally; Hammerers / Ironbreakers / Longbeards differentiate above the Stubborn baseline rather than gaining Stubborn at the elite tier. **Ancestral Grudge** translates WAP Hatred-(specific-keywords) directly: faction-wide effect targeting the four traditional dwarf foes; functions through our existing §8 Hatred special rule. **Resolute** translates WAP "Break test -1 CR loss" to "stress conversion -1" — same effective result in our morale system. **Grudgebearer** is the unit-specific identity rule, manifesting the Book of Grudges at the tactical level: nominate-at-deployment + permanent (whole-game) Hatred against the nominated target. Mechanically it does two things: (a) extends Hatred from first-round-only to all-rounds against the nominee, (b) adds a non-traditional-foe Hatred target the player can choose. Single trigger (deploy-nomination), permanent effect — low mental load. **Relentless** (WAP no-Res-test for marching near enemies) skipped — our system has no march Res tests to attach it to. **Heavy armour upgrade pricing:** +2 pts/model (rounded up from WAP +1.5). **Great weapon swap pricing:** free swap (trades shield slot for offense — net cost change is the lost shield value, ~+1 pt; pricing locks at default rather than +1 to keep the elite-tier price point clean). **Points 12 per model** — matches Empire Greatswords (12 pts: Stubborn + Professional Soldiers + great weapon default) at the elite-infantry price tier. Dwarf Warriors trade Greatswords' offensive output (S 5 AP -2 great weapon default, +1 I, Professional Soldiers always-on rerolls) for defensive durability (T 4 vs T 3, Res 10 vs Res 9, Ancestral Grudge + Grudgebearer for sustained reroll-to-Hit against the right targets). Same elite-infantry price tier, different identity profile.
>
> **Dwarf design philosophy** *(extracted for future Karaz Ankor drafts)*: slow (M 3) but tough (T 4), high WS (4) and Res (10 rank-and-file cap), faction-wide **Stubborn** + **Ancestral Grudge** + **Resolute** stack — the *patient anvil* identity, distinct from Greenskin "fight hard but unreliable" and High Elf "skill-and-discipline glass cannons." Heavy armour standard, master-crafted weapons. Elite units (**Hammerers**, **Ironbreakers**, **Longbeards**) layer Stubborn-irrelevant unit-specific rules on top — Hammerers add Killing Blow + heavy weapons, Ironbreakers add heavy plate / 3+ save baseline, Longbeards add extended grudge mechanics or +1 to-Wound vs Hated targets. **Rune magic** is its own deferred design space — a dwarf-specific magic-system pillar that would replace traditional spell lores; not addressed in this stress-test draft. The cross-faction reference points: Dwarf Warriors at 12 pts sits with High Elf Spearmen (10), Empire Greatswords (12), and below Saurus Warriors (22) — three different elite-infantry tiers across the four factions, three very different mechanical fingerprints (defensive-grudge / skill-discipline / stubborn-counterpunch / spawn-bred-cascade).

### Chaos Warriors *(Warriors of Chaos — Core, unmarked)*

> The elite of the Dark Gods' mortal followers — fully-armoured warriors who have survived enough battlefields to have caught the eye of Khorne, Nurgle, Slaanesh, or Tzeentch (or all four, in the case of those who serve Chaos Undivided). Each has fought hundreds of battles where lesser men would have died in the first; each has been twisted by chaos-exposure into something *more* than human. They wear master-crafted plate armour from the forges of the Chaos Wastes, wield weapons enchanted by daemonic patrons, and march under the banner of their Chaos Champion. Where Bretonnian Knights are nobles serving the Lady, Chaos Warriors are warlords serving the Ruinous Powers — same elite-warrior archetype, opposite moral pole. They do not panic; their devotion is too deep. They do not waver under terror; they have *seen* terror and made it their friend. **Will of Chaos** captures this — the chaos warrior ignores fear-based morale and steadies himself faster than other troops after losing combat. Drafted here as the unmarked baseline; **Marks of Chaos** (Khorne / Nurgle / Slaanesh / Tzeentch — adding Frenzy / +1 T / Stubborn+Imm Psych / Magic Resistance+Ward respectively) would be a future Chaos-faction expansion.

| 4 | 5 | 5 | 3 | 4 | 4 | 1 | 4 | 9 | 1 | 1 |

- **Keywords:** Chaos, Human, Infantry, Core, Mortal
- **Unit type:** Infantry
- **Unit size:** 10+
- **Points:** 20 per model
- **Natural Armour:** —
- **Equipment (default):** Hand weapon (chaos-forged sword/axe) + Heavy armour (combined save **4+**)
- **Equipment swap option (replaces hand weapon, pick at most one):**
  - **Additional hand weapon** (+2 pts/model) — second hand weapon, +1 A on weapon profile; cannot pair with shield
  - **Great weapon** (+4 pts/model) — 2H, no shield
  - **Polearm** (+3 pts/model) — 2" reach, may pair with shield
- **Worn armour additive:**
  - **Shield** (+2 pts/model) — combines with heavy armour for **3+** save (per §8 Armour Save Stacking); requires hand weapon or polearm (no shield with great weapon or additional hand weapon)
- **Command options:**
  - **Champion (Leader)** (+5 pts) — chosen of the unit
  - **Standard Bearer** (+10 pts, may take Magic Standard up to **50 pts** — Chaos Warriors get the elite banner budget, matching Knights and Greatswords)
  - **Musician** (+5 pts)

**Special rules:**
- **Will of Chaos** *(Chaos faction-wide identity rule)* — this unit is **Immune to Psychology** (per §8) AND may **reroll failed recovery rolls**. Centuries of devotion to the Dark Gods purges fear: the warrior shrugs off Terror and Fear, ignores stress from friendly units fleeing or being destroyed nearby, and steadies himself faster than any disciplined drill could teach. Combat wounds and direct spell damage still cause stress normally — the warrior is not unkillable, just unbreakable in spirit. Mechanically stronger than Empire Greatswords' Professional Soldiers (reroll recovery only) reflecting Chaos Warriors' elite-elite tier.

**Weapon profiles:**

- **Hand weapon (chaos-forged sword/axe)** *(default, 1H — pairs with shield)* — 1" reach | **A 2** | S 4 | AP 0 | D 1 — the A 2 reflects the unit's base attack profile; the chaos warrior swings twice in a single combat round, every blow at S 4 `[1H Blade]`
- **Pair of Hand Weapons (Additional)** *(option, 2× 1H — cannot pair with shield)* — 1" reach | **A 3** | S 4 | AP 0 | D 1 — second hand weapon adds +1 A to the base profile (A 2 → A 3); loses the shield slot `[Paired]`
- **Great Weapon** *(option, 2H — no shield)* — 1" reach | **A 2** | S+2 (= S 6) | AP -2 | D 1 | Two-Handed — chaos-forged great axe, war-hammer, or daemon-edged blade; master-crafted, no -1 I penalty (chaos craft is at least as good as dwarf craft) `[2H]`
- **Polearm** *(option, 1H — pairs with shield)* — 2" reach | **A 2** | S+1 (= S 5) | AP -1 | D 1 — chaos-bladed glaive, halberd, or partisan; the 2" reach lets the second rank stab past the first while the shield stays in hand `[1H Reach]`

On a **1**, the unit must declare a charge against the nearest visible enemy if able; if no charge possible, the unit instead gains **+1 stress** and may not Advance or March this Movement phase. The goblins poke each other with spears and lose grip on the spider's reins; the spider — Imm Psych — doesn't *care*, but the goblins panic and the unit's coordination breaks. Note: the Arachnarok itself is Imm Psych and unaffected by the stress; only the unit's *positional control* is disrupted by Animosity.
- **Immune to Psychology** *(per §8, Arachnarok-specific)* — the spider's animal mind ignores Fear, Terror, friendly-unit-fleeing stress triggers. Combat-wound stress still applies; the spider isn't unkillable, just fearless. **No Stubborn** — the Arachnarok doesn't *resist* in the disciplined sense; Imm Psych alone captures the animal-mind that doesn't break because it doesn't think to.
- **Poisoned Attacks** *(per §8, both spider AND crew)* — Arachnarok and Forest Goblin Crew melee + ranged attacks gain **+1 to-Wound**. The spider's venom drips from fangs and legs; the goblins coat their weapons (spear and shortbow) in milked spider-venom from the Arachnarok's own glands. Faction-flavour: Forest Goblins are the *poison-everything* sub-tribe of Greenskins.
- **Wall-crawler** *(Arachnarok identity rule)* — the Arachnarok ignores all Movement penalties from Difficult Terrain, Dangerous Terrain (no test required), and impassable-but-vertical features (cliffs, walls — the spider crawls). The Arachnarok cannot end its movement inside another model or impassable-horizontal terrain (water, lava, void). Equivalent to the Strider (any) special rule, scoped to terrain only.

**Weapon profiles:**

- **Spider Attacks** *(default, Arachnarok body)* — 1" reach | **A 8** | S 5 | AP -1 | D 1 | **Poisoned Attacks** — the spider's eight leg-strike-and-fang-bite attack pool resolved as a single attack stream. With Poisoned (+1 to-Wound), Arachnarok wounds T 4 on 3+ and T 6 on 4+ `[Natural]`
- **Venom Surge** *(special weapon — anti-large only)* — 1" reach | **A 1** | S 5 | AP -1 | **D D6** | **Poisoned Attacks** — once per Combat phase, when fighting an enemy unit with the **Monstrous Infantry**, **Monstrous Beast**, **Monstrous Cavalry**, **Monstrous Creature**, or **Monster** keyword, the Arachnarok may make this single Venom Surge attack **in addition to its 8 Spider Attacks**. The venom-fang strike scaled for large-frame prey: every successful unsaved wound from this attack causes **D6 wounds** (rolled fresh per wound, no minimum) — the Arachnarok injects half a body's worth of paralytic toxin in one targeted strike, killing large frames disproportionately. Useless against rank-and-file infantry (no MonInf+ keyword), devastating against Stegadon-class big creatures
- **Forest Goblin Shortbow (Crew, ranged)** *(default, Crew profile)* — 12" range | **A 1 each (×8 crew = A 8 total shots, or A 6 if Flinger / Spidershrine reduces crew to 6)** | S 3 | AP 0 | D 1 | **Poisoned Attacks** — venom-coated arrows; Forest Goblins shoot from the spider's back at any range within 12"
- **Forest Goblin Spear (Crew, melee)** *(default, Crew profile)* — 1" reach | **A 1 each (×8 crew, or ×6 if upgraded)** | S 3 | AP 0 | D 1 | **Poisoned Attacks** — venom-coated spear-tips; the goblins stab down at anything that closes with the spider `[1H Reach]`
- **Flinger** *(option, +25 pts — replaces 2 crew)* — 12-48" range, indirect-fire stone-thrower per §7.2. Hits at S 3 (or S 1 splash, S 3 direct hit) with Multiple Wounds (D3). Misfire: silent this turn

> **Lore notes:** Stats deliberately pushed past WAP per the design call that we're not slaves to WAP/8e/Old World/AoS stat conventions — the model is the largest in the cross-faction set, so the stats reflect that. **W 8 → 18** uplifted by 10 (vs my initial draft's W 12); the Arachnarok is genuinely larger than Stegadon (W 12), Bastiladon (W 10), Carnosaur (W 10), and Warsphinx (W 10) — placing it at the top of the wound-pool ladder by a wide margin. **M 7 → 8** for explicit speed (faster than Stegadon M 6, faster than most cavalry); the spider scuttles. **LiS 4 → 5** above Stegadon to reflect the model's footprint. **T 6** unchanged from WAP — chitin is strong but not stone (hence T 6 vs Warsphinx T 8). **Natural Armour 4+** kept from WAP — chitin is *biological armour*, distinct from the body's mass; the spider has T 6 (mass) AND a 4+ save (chitinous carapace) as separate defensive layers, unlike the Warsphinx where T 8 IS the stone (no separate save). **WS 4** unchanged. **S 5** unchanged. **I 4** unchanged (spider reflexes — fastest of the four monsters drafted in §18: Warsphinx I 1, Carnosaur I 3, Stegadon I 2, Arachnarok I 4). **A 8** on the body (matching WAP); the Venom Surge is a separate 1-attack weapon profile *in addition to* the 8 Spider Attacks rather than carved out of them — vs MonInf+ targets the Arachnarok strikes 9 times total per combat round. **Res 7** uses crew-level via Mixed Unit convention (the spider itself is Res 3 animal-mind; the crew handles morale at Res 6, but with the spider's Imm Psych the effective unit Res is whichever is higher and the spider's special rules set the morale floor). **No Stubborn** per design call — Imm Psych alone captures the animal-mind that doesn't break. **Wall-crawler** scoped to terrain via Strider (any) equivalent; biological climbing identity. **Venom Surge** consolidated into a separate weapon profile with 1 attack — cleaner mental load than "nominate one of the 8 attacks to be the surge"; the surge fires once per Combat phase when fighting MonInf+ targets, adding to the spider's normal 7 attacks. Multiple Wounds (D6) on top of Poisoned (+1 to-Wound) makes a single unsaved Venom Surge wound into D6 wounds — designed to crack Stegadon / Carnosaur / Warsphinx-class big creatures. **Catchweb Spidershrine** flagged as a Spider God magic-lore upgrade — full Spider God lore would be a Forest Goblin faction expansion, parallel to Geomancy / Cosmic Architecture / Lady. **Points 400** — uplifted from WAP 260 to reflect W 18 (50% durability above Stegadon), M 8 mobility, A 8 attack volume, Wall-crawler, and Venom Surge anti-monster scaling. Sits above all other §18 monsters as the apex Rare monster: Stegadon (275, T 6 W 12 NA 3+ + Skink shooting), Bastiladon (~325 max, T 7 W 10 NA 2+ + relic), Carnosaur (260 mount, T 5 W 10 NA 5+ + Bloodroar), Warsphinx (250, T 8 W 10 no save + Thundercrush). The Arachnarok is meaningfully larger and more durable than any of them — points and stats reflect that explicit "largest model in the comparison" framing.

> **Forest Goblin design philosophy** *(extracted for future Greenskin sub-tribe drafts)*: a sub-faction within Greenskins, distinct from Common Orcs (Loves a Scrap + Mighty/Boyz-Will-Be-Boyz) and Night Goblins (Hatred (Dwarfs) + Magic Mushrooms + likely faction-specific Bad-Moon-lore access). Forest Goblins emphasise **Poisoned Attacks across all weapons** (the spider-venom trade), **Wall-crawler movement via spider mounts**, and **Spider God magical access** (deferred lore). Goblin-baseline Animosity + low I + low Res + small frame remains; the sub-tribe identity layers spider-themed mechanics on top. Distinct identity from Common Orc (combat-strong-but-undisciplined) and Night Goblin (cowardly-but-hate-dwarfs); Forest Goblins are *poison-and-spiders*. **Spider God magic** would be a Forest-Goblin-specific lore (parallel to Geomancy / Cosmic Architecture / Lady) covering web-attacks, swarm-summoning, Venom-empowerment buffs, and possibly Wall-crawler-grants on friendlies.
