# Rat Ogres

> *"Rat Ogres are creatures born from the fusion of crossbreeding different types of horrific creatures with Skaven blood, and surgically augmenting what is left. They represent the very pinnacle of Clan Moulder ingenuity, a lethal killing-machine beast that combines the inhumane reflexes of a Skaven with the brawn and size of an Ogre."* The Rat Ogres are Clan Moulder's apex bio-engineered war-beasts — towering rat-things of stitched-together flesh and surgically-grafted muscle, **bereft of reason and nearly devoid of sanity**, *"utterly dependent upon their creators for any kind of mental faculty."* Without the constant supervision of Moulder's **Packmasters**, *"a Rat Ogre would wander aimlessly about the battlefield, only stopping to grab a quick snack or to rip the throat from any living thing that was unlucky enough to attract its mercifully short attention."* The fingerprint: a *lighter* Monstrous Infantry brute at the project's Mon Inf tier — **S 5 / T 4 / W 6** (smaller Mon Inf — below Trolls / Ogre Bulls / Kroxigor at W 7-8, reflecting the Rat Ogre's lighter rat-bio frame relative to full-Ogre or Troll mass) / **A 3 multi-claw-and-bite predator** / **NA 6+ bone-grafted bio-armour**, **Frenzy** (the bio-engineered killing-rage, default-state when the creature is fighting), **Fear** (a bone-stitched abomination on the battlefield), **conditional Stupidity** (the Packmaster dependency — without a Packmaster within 6" the Rat Ogre wanders aimlessly), and the **Skaven faction-wide rule stack** (Verminous Valour, Strength in Numbers, Backstabbing, Undisciplined — the bio-creature is Moulder-property and travels with the Skaven army; the faction-wide rules apply by attachment, not biology). The role: the Skaven army's heavy hitter — sent forward as a snarling, unstable Monstrous Infantry block to break enemy lines that the Clanrat wave cannot. The Rat Ogre is durable (W 7 with NA 6+) but unreliable (Stupid without a Packmaster, Frenzy lost on combat-loss, low Res 5). Lore-coherent: Clan Moulder builds them in numbers, and will *make more*.

## Special Rules

- **Undisciplined** *(per §8 Psychology)*. **-1 to Recovery rolls** (floor 1). The bio-engineered creature has no formation drill; recovery from stress is sluggish.
- **Fear** *(per §8 Psychology)*. At LiS 2, the Fear aura projects to **(6 + 2)" = 8"**. A bone-stitched abomination of rat-and-other-creature flesh is a primal terror on any battlefield. *Lore-canonical: "a lethal killing-machine beast" — enemies who see one charging through the Skaven line know exactly what comes next.*
- **Frenzy** *(per §8 Psychology)*. The bio-engineered killing-rage is the Rat Ogre's default combat state. The unit carries the **F4 fingerprint**: +1 A on the chosen weapon profile (typically Claws and Fangs → A 4 on the charge round), +1 MA, -1 MD, must charge nearest visible enemy if able, may not voluntarily disengage, must Follow Up on enemy Fall Back, must pursue, Imm Psych, suppresses Wavering / Broken state effects. *(Frenzy is **lost** on combat-loss per the standard §8 rule. A Rat Ogre that loses a combat round goes back to baseline rage-off — still Stupid (if no Packmaster), still Frenzied-once-it-re-engages, but the F4 surge is gone for the rest of the current engagement.)*
- **Beast Pack — Conditional Stupidity** *(Rat Ogre unit identity rule — the Packmaster dependency)*. The Rat Ogre has the **Stupidity** special rule (per §8) **except** while a friendly **Skaven Character** with the **Packmaster** or **Master Moulder** keyword is **within 6"** of any model in this unit (or joined to the unit). While a handler is present, the Rat Ogre is *directed* — the bio-creature's short attention span is anchored by the Packmaster's commands, and the unit functions as a normal Frenzied Mon Inf block. Without a handler within range, the Rat Ogre lapses into Stupidity: at the start of the unit's activation in the Movement phase, the unit must Charge, Advance toward the nearest visible enemy, or Advance aimlessly — *the bio-creature's short attention drifts off-piste*. Cannot March; cannot Rally; cannot make tactical movement of any kind. *(Forward-compatible with future Packmaster / Master Moulder character drafts. Until those characters exist, the Rat Ogre unit is always Stupid — the player accepts the unreliability as the Moulder-bio-tier identity tax.)*
  
  *(Lore-canonical: "Without the constant supervision of Moulder's Packmasters, a Rat Ogre would wander aimlessly about the battlefield." The 8e canon mechanic preserved: Packmaster within 6\" suppresses Stupidity; absent that, the bio-creature is uncontrolled.)*
- **Verminous Valour** *(Skaven faction-wide identity rule — by attachment, the Rat Ogre is Moulder property and travels with the Skaven army)*. **Mass-bravery / cowardice toggle, single threshold at half strength:**
  - While this unit has **more than half** its starting model count remaining, it **ignores the state effect of Shaken** (per §8).
  - While this unit is reduced to **half or fewer** of its starting model count, it accumulates **+1 additional stress** every time it would normally take stress.
  
  *(Per §1.5 Skaven faction-wide trait. Rat Ogres at 3-6 model unit size hit half-strength fast — a 3-model unit dropping one model immediately puts them in cowardice tier. Tactical: keep Rat Ogre packs intact, or commit them decisively.)*
- **Strength in Numbers** *(Skaven faction-wide identity rule)*. **+1 CR at ≥15 models; +2 CR at ≥25 models — model count only, formation-shape-independent.** *(Per §1.5. Almost never fires on Rat Ogre units — typical Mon Inf block size is 3-6 models, far below the 15-model threshold. Inherited at the faction level but mechanically inert for this unit in practice.)*
- **Backstabbing** *(Skaven faction-wide identity rule)*. When this unit is **Broken** (per §5), every friendly **Skaven** unit within **6"** of any model in this unit immediately gains **+1 stress** beyond the standard panic-cascade. *(Per §1.5. A Broken Rat Ogre unit signals "even the killing-machines are routing" to the rest of the Skaven army — the panic cascade hits hard.)*

## Options

**No equipment options.** The Rat Ogre is a bio-engineered killing-machine — no armour, no weapons beyond its grafted claws and fangs, no kit upgrades available. The Moulder Flesh-Shapers built it the way it is.

**No command options.** Rat Ogre packs do not carry banners, drums, or veteran champions. The unit is a Mon Inf brute formation, not a regimented infantry block. *(Packmasters and Master Moulders, when drafted, will be separate Hero / character units that may join or escort Rat Ogre packs — handled via the Beast Pack conditional Stupidity mechanic above.)*

## Weapon Profiles

### Close Combat

- **Claws and Fangs** *(natural, default — bio-grafted multi-claw multi-bite arsenal)* — 1" reach | **A 3** | S 5 | AP -1 | D 1 — the Rat Ogre strikes three times per Combat phase: massive grafted claws rake, surgically-augmented fangs bite, and the bio-engineered limb-mass slams the target down. **S 5** captures the Ogre-tier brawn (one above the standard Skaven baseline S 3 — the Rat Ogre's defining biological elevation). **AP -1** captures the claw-and-fang piercing — they're not the polished blades of an Empire weapon, but the bone-grafted hooks and bite-force tear through mail and light plate. **A 3** captures the multi-limb-multi-bite chaos of the bio-creature in melee. With **Frenzy +1 A** active (default state), the Rat Ogre delivers **A 4** at S 5 / AP -1 / D 1 on the charge round and subsequent rounds (until Frenzy is lost via combat-loss). `[Natural]`
