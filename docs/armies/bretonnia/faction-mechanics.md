# Faction Mechanics


#### Blessing of the Lady *(intrinsic universal trait — applies to Knights with Vows, Damsels of the Lady, Prophetesses of the Lady, including their mounts)*

Models with the Blessing of the Lady gain a **Ward Save** against incoming attacks:

- **6+ Ward Save** against attacks of **Strength 4 or lower**
- **5+ Ward Save** against attacks of **Strength 5 or higher**

The Lady's hand visibly intervenes against the heaviest blows — divine protection is most apparent when an ordinary wound would crush you. Weak attacks slip past with only a 6+ chance of intervention; strong attacks are markedly more likely to be turned aside. The Ward applies in melee, against ranged attacks, and against magical damage spells. It does **not** apply to:

- Crumble damage (Undead-only — irrelevant for Bretonnia)
- Automatic-effect spell damage that bypasses saves entirely
- Attacks framed as ignoring Ward Saves (Ward Piercing, see §8)

**Invocation.** The Blessing is invoked at the start of the battle, after both armies have deployed but before the first turn begins. It is automatically granted; no roll required. The Bretonnian player may decline the Blessing for narrative-scenario reasons (e.g., a fallen-from-grace force), but the default is "the Lady is with us."

**Loss conditions.** A unit / character that **flees from combat** (breaks, falls back beyond the unit's coherency, or is forced into involuntary withdrawal) **loses the Blessing for the remainder of the battle**. Once lost, it cannot be regained — the Lady does not bless the faithless. Additionally, a character (or the Champion of a unit of Knights) who **refuses an issued challenge** in a fight where they could have accepted likewise loses the Blessing. *(Note: the challenge mechanic is not formally drafted in the ruleset yet; this clause is forward-compatible — the Blessing-loss interaction triggers once challenges are designed. Until then, only the flee condition is active.)*

Pattern note: this is the **first Strength-variable Ward Save** in the ruleset. Captures lore via mechanical asymmetry — weak attacks get only 6+ (the hand isn't always visible); strong attacks get 5+ (the Lady's intervention is most pronounced when a knight's life is genuinely in danger). Pairs naturally with our high-Strength threats — Mighty Blow Tyrant, Great Cannon, Heavy Lance, etc., are exactly the things that should be slightly less devastating against the Lady-blessed.

#### Knight's Vow *(special rule — Knights of the Realm, Knights Errant, Pegasus Knights, Bretonnian Lord, Paladin)*

The standard chivalric vow taken by every Bretonnian knight upon investiture. Confers eligibility for the **Blessing of the Lady** (see above) — that is the *entire* mechanical content of the Knight's Vow. It is a marker, not an active rule.

Knight's Vow grants no Stubborn, no additional Res, no other effect — those are reserved for the higher vows (Crusader's, Questing, Grail). The Blessing IS the vow's reward.

Pattern: a clean eligibility marker, tier 1 of the vow ladder.

#### Crusader's Vow *(special rule — Bretonnian knights pledged to a holy crusade)*

The vow taken by Bretonnian knights who pledge their service to a specific holy crusade — typically the long campaign against the Chaos Wastes, the Undead realms of Sylvania, the Greenskin invasions of the Border Princes, or other long-running campaigns against the unholy. The Crusader's Vow is sworn before the Lady's altar and a designated war-banner; the knight commits to fighting the unholy until the campaign ends or his life does.

Confers:

- **Eligibility for the Blessing of the Lady** (see above)
- **Immunity to Panic-source stress** — the crusader has seen his fill of horrors and does not flinch from terrors that would unman a younger knight. Stress from Fear, Terror, friendly-unit-flees triggers, and other psychology-source events does not accumulate on this character or unit
- **Reroll failed recovery rolls** caused by psychology-source effects already in play. The crusader steadies himself faster than ordinary knights when fear takes hold (note: applies to recovery from psychology-source state effects; standard recovery rolls from non-psychology stress accumulate normally)
- **+1D6 charge distance roll, discard the lowest** — the crusader's righteous fury accelerates the charge. Roll an extra D6 alongside the normal charge distance dice; discard the lowest of all dice rolled, then add the remaining dice to M for the final charge distance

Pattern: vow ladder tier 2 *parallel branch* (the *crusader path*). Crusader's Vow and Questing Vow are *parallel* tier 2 vows — a knight chooses one or the other at investiture, not both. Crusader = anti-fear + charge fervour (the holy crusader's psychology hardening); Questing = Stubborn + great weapon (the questing knight's lance-renouncing discipline). Different mechanical signatures, equivalent tier.

#### Questing Vow *(special rule — Questing Knights, characters who have sworn the Quest)*

The vow taken by Bretonnian knights who set aside their domain and possessions to seek the Holy Grail. Confers:

- **Eligibility for the Blessing of the Lady** (see above)
- **Stubborn** (see §8) — the unit ignores the state effects of Shaken and Wavering. Their commitment to the Grail-quest transcends ordinary morale
- **The questing knight may not wield a Heavy Lance** — the lance is set aside as a renunciation of mortal vanity; the questing knight wields a great weapon instead. (Lance prohibition is intrinsic to the Questing Vow; characters with this vow have their Heavy Lance equipment options removed at list-build)

Pattern: vow ladder tier 2 *parallel branch* (the *questing path*). Parallel to Crusader's Vow rather than nested. Questing Knights and Questing-Vow characters trade lance access for permanent Stubborn protection, reflecting the knight-on-pilgrimage ethos.

#### Grail Vow *(special rule — Bretonnian knights who have personally completed the Quest)*

The apex chivalric vow, sworn only by knights who have **personally completed the Quest for the Grail** — encountered the Lady in person, drunk from her cup, and returned transformed. Grail Knights and Grail-Vow characters are exceptionally rare; their presence on the battlefield is itself a sign of the Lady's favour upon the army.

Confers:

- **Improved Blessing of the Lady** — the Ward Save tier improves to **5+ vs S ≤ 4, 4+ vs S ≥ 5** (one step better at each strength tier than the standard Knight's / Crusader's / Questing Vow Blessing of 6+/5+). The Lady's hand rests firmly on those who have completed her Quest
- **Stubborn** (see §8) — ignores the state effects of Shaken and Wavering, same as the Questing Vow
- **Immune to Psychology** — stress from Fear, Terror, Panic-source events, and any other psychology-source trigger does not accumulate. Already-accumulated stress is unaffected; only new psychology stress is blocked. Stronger than the Crusader's Vow's Immunity-to-Panic-source-stress: the Grail-touched knight is immune to *all* psychology, not just Panic-source

**Frenzy explicitly NOT granted.** The Grail-touched knight is an embodiment of *serene devotion*, not berserk fury. The apex chivalric character fights with calm, measured confidence under the Lady's protection, not with rage. Earlier drafts considered Frenzy as a Grail-tier rule (per some WAP editions); rejected because Frenzy's "loss of self-control" lore is fundamentally incompatible with the Grail-quest framing — the Knight has *seen* the Lady; he is not raging, he is *certain*.

Pattern: vow ladder tier 3 *apex*. The Grail Vow combines defensive elements from both parallel tier 2 branches (Stubborn from Questing, Psychology-immunity from Crusader's expanded form) plus its own unique improved Blessing. The chivalric ladder thus reads:

```
            Knight's Vow (tier 1: Blessing only)
                  ↓
        ┌─────────┴─────────┐
        ↓                   ↓
  Crusader's Vow        Questing Vow
  (tier 2: anti-fear,   (tier 2: Stubborn,
   charge bonus)         lance renounced)
        ↓                   ↓
        └─────────┬─────────┘
                  ↓
             Grail Vow
       (tier 3: improved Blessing,
        Stubborn, Immune Psychology)
```

A knight typically progresses Knight's Vow → (one of Crusader's / Questing) → Grail Vow over the course of a long career. Future Grail Knights units (when drafted in an expansion pass) will inherit this Vow as their default; the Bretonnian Lord and Paladin can take the Grail Vow as a list-build upgrade representing a personal completion of the Quest.

#### Lance Formation *(sub-state of Formed — Bretonnian mounted Knight units only)*

A Bretonnian Knight unit in **Formed** (per §6) **also** counts as being in **Lance Formation** if its model geometry matches a triangular wedge:

- **Row 1** contains **1 model** (the wedge tip)
- **Row 2** contains **2 models** behind row 1, with a slight lateral offset to either side
- **Row 3** contains **3 models** behind row 2
- Each subsequent row contains one more model than the row above
- All models face the same direction
- **Minimum 3 rows deep** — **6 models minimum** to qualify for Lance

Lance Formation is **no more sticky than Formed itself** — once the geometry is established, the unit counts as Lance until movement disrupts the wedge or casualties break the geometric pattern. There is no separate declaration or activation step; the formation is what it is, geometrically.

**While in Lance Formation, on the turn the unit completes a charge:**

- All melee weapons in the unit gain **+2" reach** for that combat round (Heavy Lance 2" → 4"; Hand weapon 1" → 3"; Polearm 2" → 4")
- All melee weapons in the unit gain **+1 AP** for that combat round *(Wedge Penetration — the wedge concentrates force into a piercing point, the lance bites deeper as the rear ranks push the tip forward)*

After the charge round, reach and AP revert to baseline. Lance Formation continues to count for any other rules that reference it (e.g., banner effects, magic items that interact with Lance), but the offensive bonuses fire only on the turn of the charge.

**All Formed defensive benefits apply** — Lance *is* Formed, not a replacement for it. +1 Res for stress thresholds (non-directional), charge resistance on the front arc (deny enemy +1 I), 2-CR absorb before stress — all apply normally while in Lance.

**Geometry note.** With +2" reach extension, knights in row 2 and row 3 can both strike at enemies in base contact with row 1 (the extended reach overcomes the rear-rank distance). A 6-model wedge thus produces all 6 weapon attacks on the charge round — 1 from the wedge tip + 2 from row 2 + 3 from row 3 — instead of the 1 front-rank attack a normal cavalry charge would generate. Larger wedges may have rear ranks (row 4, row 5, etc.) that still cannot reach even with the extension; the wedge geometry naturally caps strikes at the rows that fit within reach. The wedge maximises charge impact at the cost of fewer front-rank models in base contact (a 3-model-wide wedge produces only 1 front-rank attack post-charge, when reach reverts to baseline).

**Faction-specificity.** Lance Formation is **Bretonnia-only** even though Empire / Lizardmen / etc. also field heavy cavalry. The Bretonnian chivalric tradition produces Lance discipline; other factions' knights ride into combat in standard Formed but lack the trained wedge formation. (If a future faction develops a comparable tradition, Lance can be ported. The current ruleset reserves it.)
