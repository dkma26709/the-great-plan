# Armoury


The Lizardmen fight with stone, bone, and the living crystal of their homeland — celestite mined from the Black Pyramid, obsidian chipped from volcanic glass, wood older than civilisation. Canonical weapon profiles referenced by the unit entries below. A unit equipping a weapon from this armoury uses the profile exactly as written; unit-specific weapon variants are listed inline on the relevant profile.

(Generic **hand weapon** = 1" reach, AP 0, D1 — the implicit profile for any model not specifying otherwise.)

#### Celestite Blade

**1" reach | AP -1 | D1**

A short sword, hand-axe, or carving blade forged from celestite — a magical crystal mined from the Black Pyramid. Sharper than bronze, blessed by the Slann. Standard arming weapon for veteran Saurus and temple elite.

#### Celestite Spear

**2" reach | +1 I baseline | D1**

- **When the wielder's unit is charged in the front arc:** models using this weapon gain **+1 S** and **AP -1** for that Combat phase.
- **When targeting models with the Cavalry, Monstrous Cavalry, Warbeast, Monster, or Chariot keyword:** Damage is **2** instead of 1.

A wooden haft tipped with a chipped celestite spearhead. The +1 I baseline reflects the weapon's reach and point-first geometry — spear-wielders strike a half-beat before sword-wielders. A braced spear catches the weight of a charge and channels it into the point; the same weapon held overhand finds the softer flesh of horses, beasts, and monsters more reliably than the ranks of a drilled infantry block. Iconic of Saurus line infantry but wielded by any unit drilled in spearwork.

#### Celestite Halberd

**2" reach | +1 S | AP -1 | D1**

A polearm with a chipped celestite head, blessed by the Slann and carried by Temple Guard. A human would need both hands to wield it — a Saurus, with reptilian mass and muscle, holds one in one hand while still bracing a shield in the other. The extra reach lets the Temple Guard keep attackers at length while the shield covers their Slann. Iconic of the Slann's personal guard; not typically found outside the temple-cities.

#### Celestite Club

**1" reach | AP -1 | D1 | One-handed (may pair with shield)**

- **On charge** (first round of combat into which the wielder charged): +1 S | AP -2 | D2
- **Against targets with W 3+:** +1 Damage

A short, mass-heavy crushing club tipped and studded with chipped celestite — the counterpart to the piercing spear, preferred by Aggradon-mounted Crested Saurus who ride down tougher prey. Where the spear pierces, the club crushes through thick hide and bone alike. The W 3+ damage bonus captures the weapon's geometry: a heavy impact head does more against a heavy target than against a fragile one.

#### Obsidian Maul

**1" reach | AP 0 | D2 | Killing Blow (W ≤ 2, D3) — see §8**

A one-handed warclub studded with obsidian shards. Against plate or scale the stone only bruises, but a clean blow to an unarmoured skull is ruinous — the Killing Blow against small models represents this anti-infantry lethality.

#### Moonstone Javelin

**8" range | AP -1 | D1 | Quick to Fire | Poisoned Attacks (see §8)**

A short throwing-spear tipped with a chipped moonstone head, the shaft often wrapped in venom-soaked leather. Skink hunters carry several into battle — each blessed at the pyramid-temples before the march. Quick to Fire reflects the reflexive underhand cast skinks use on the move; the poison is the same pit-venom that coats blowpipe darts. Shared moonstone-armoury tradition with the Kroxigor Moonstone Club.

#### Skink Blowpipe

**12" range | AP 0 | D1 | Multiple Shots (2) | Quick to Fire | Poisoned Attacks (see §8) | 360° arc**

A hollow reed loaded with bone darts tipped in Skink-poison. Two quick puffs rather than one considered shot — accuracy suffers (per Multiple Shots (2)), but volume and venom do the work. The 360° arc reflects the skink's boneless agility — a skink can twist, arch, and fire in any direction without breaking stride. Quick to Fire captures the reflexive, breath-powered nature of the weapon: no reload, no bracing, just another dart to the lips.

#### Dartpipe

**18" range | AP 0 | D1 | Quick to Fire | Poisoned Attacks (see §8)**

A narrow-barrelled blowpipe with tighter tolerances than the common Skink Blowpipe, loaded with darts refined by the temple apothecaries for precision-hunting. Where a Skink Blowpipe is a volume-and-spray weapon, the Dartpipe is a precision weapon — single considered shot, longer range, same pit-venom on the dart. In chameleon-caste hands, with their BS 4, the Dartpipe reaches out to take the cannon crew, the sergeant, the wizard.

#### Celestite Dagger

**1" reach | AP 0 | D1 | Poisoned Attacks (see §8)**

A slender fang-like blade tipped with chipped celestite and coated in the same pit-venom as Dartpipe darts. Not a line weapon — a Chameleon Skink uses it only when the hunt has closed to arm's length, usually only once per quarry. Smaller and less substantive than the Saurus Celestite Blade; this is a personal weapon for a small hunter, not a drilled soldier's sword.

#### Skink Sling

**12" range | AP 0 | D1 | Quick to Fire**

A short woven-leather sling loaded with smooth river-stones. Not poisoned — Skinks reserve their pit-venom for blow-darts, where the poison's small dose can be delivered directly through broken skin. A stone thrown by sling lacks the delivery mechanism; it must rely on bruising mass alone. An older Skink tradition, less common in modern temple-hunts but still fielded by chameleon-caste hunters who prefer a shield in the off-hand to a volley of darts.

#### Spout Flames

**Flame Template | S4 | AP 0 | D1 | Flaming (see §8) | Slow to Fire (see §8) | Scatter (Artillery Die) (see §8)**

**Placement.** Place the Flame Template (per §7.4 Templates) with its narrow end touching the Salamander's base, the wide end pointing toward the intended target area. Roll a scatter die and an Artillery Die together per the Scatter (X) rule:
- **HIT face:** template lands exactly as placed. Resolve hits.
- **Arrow face + numeric Artillery Die (2/4/6/8/10):** template moves that many inches in the direction shown by the scatter die. Resolve hits on the final template position. No BS modifier applies (Salamanders have BS 0; the shot is the handlers' and the beast's combined instinct, not a marksman's BS-adjusted aim).
- **Artillery Die MISFIRE:** no flames emit. **D3 Skink Handlers from the firing unit are immediately removed as casualties** — the beast spasms, the gas ignites backward, and the nearest handlers are the first to burn. The Salamander itself takes no damage. A misfire counts as having attempted to shoot for Slow to Fire / move-and-shoot / turn-sequencing purposes.

**Each model under the final template takes 1 hit at the weapon profile** (S4, AP 0, D1, Flaming). Flaming interactions apply per §8 (no Regeneration saves, beast-keyword panic checks, etc.).

A living flame-thrower. The Salamander swallows a predatory amount of volatile spit-marsh gas, exhales a cone of hot fire, and the cone goes where the gas goes — which is not always where the handlers pointed. The Artillery Die captures this uncertainty: most of the time the flame reaches its target roughly as intended, sometimes it falls short or overshoots, occasionally the whole thing goes wrong in the beast's own throat.

#### Shoot Barbs

**18" range | S4 | AP -1 | D1 | Multiple Shots (Artillery Die) (see §8) | Rapid Fire (see §8)**

**Shots per Razordon.** Each Razordon firing Shoot Barbs rolls one Artillery Die (2/4/6/8/10/MISFIRE) to determine how many barbs it fires this Shooting phase:
- **2–10:** Razordon fires that many shots at BS 3 (handlers aim while the beast fires). Resolve each shot individually using normal hit rolls, wounds, and saves at the profile (S4, AP -1, D1). Move-and-shoot penalty applies normally (-1 to hit if the unit moved) unless a rule waives it (e.g., Instinctive Defence).
- **MISFIRE:** the Razordon's quill-spines misfire — a barb jams, the beast twists, a handler gets quilled. **No shots are fired this phase.** Additionally, **D3 Skink Handlers from the firing unit are immediately removed as casualties.** The Razordon itself takes no damage. A misfire still counts as having attempted to shoot for sequencing purposes.

**No template.** Shoot Barbs is a volley weapon — a spray of individual barbs rather than a cone of flame. It targets one unit at a time (the shooter's choice, within range + LoS), not an area.

The Razordon bristles with retractable quills that it can fire as a storm of barbed arrows under the handlers' direction. At volume the effect is like a repeating-crossbow battery; in short, the beast shoots like an organ gun, but the ammunition is its own spines regrown between battles.

#### Moonstone Club

**3" reach | Wide-Arc Swings (see below) | +2 S | AP -2 | D D3 | Two-handed (cannot combine with shield in melee)**

A huge fossil-stone maul wielded in both hands by Kroxigor — the head alone weighs more than a man, chipped and chiselled from a single slab of moonstone found in the deep pits. The extended reach lets a Kroxigor strike over the front rank of a friendly unit; the Strength bonus turns a S5 Kroxigor into a S7 engine of destruction, cleaving through armour and bone alike. Paired thematically with the smaller Skink Moonstone Javelin — shared stone-armoury tradition between the cold-blooded cousins.

**Wide-Arc Swings:** when a Kroxigor attacks with the Moonstone Club, its **A equals the number of enemy models in base contact within the Club's 3" reach in the Kroxigor's front arc**, **minimum A 2**, **maximum A 6**. Captures the lore-perfect sweeping motion of a Kroxigor swing — "easily reach over any intervening Skinks, allowing the reptilian giants to pulp more than their share of any foe" + "splatter a man-sized creature beyond all recognition." A 2 minimum: a Kroxigor focused on a single tough target still swings hard. A 6 maximum: the physical arc-radius of a swing has a practical upper bound — the Kroxigor pivots through a wide rotation but can't strike all 360° in a single motion. **D D3** captures multi-wound impact against larger foes (Carnosaur, Oldblood, multi-W cavalry) while averaging D 2 against rank-and-file.

#### Ancient Weapons

**1" reach | AP -1 | D1 | +1 Attack | Magical Attacks (see §8) | Ward Piercing (1) (see §8)**

Paired glaive-gauntlets or rune-carved greatblades, wielded by Kroxigor dedicated to the hunt of warded foes — daemons, vampires, champions bound in protective magic. The weapons are older than the spawning-pools; the runes etched into them are said to have been drawn by the Old Ones themselves. A Kroxigor wielding Ancient Weapons trades the sheer crushing power of the Moonstone Club for a faster, magic-hungry attack profile that strips ward saves and cuts through the unnatural.

#### Tepok Lance

**2" reach | One-handed (may pair with shield)**

- **On the turn the wielder's unit charged into combat (first round only):** +1 S | AP -2 | D2
- **Subsequent rounds of the same combat:** AP 0 | D1

Blessed at the Pyramid of Tepok by Slann consecration, these relic lances are the signature weapon of the Raptadon hunt-packs who serve the Old One of knowledge. In the instant of the Raptadon's charge, the lance emits a blinding burst of radiance — flesh incinerates, armour cleaves, and a single devastating strike carries the combined momentum of rider and beast. Once the charge has spent itself, the lance is merely a long pole: still reaching, but without the radiant edge of the first impact.

#### Light Lance

**2" reach | One-handed (may pair with shield)**

- **On charge** (first round of combat into which the wielder charged): +1 S | AP -1 | D1
- **Subsequent rounds:** AP 0 | D1

A short, balanced lance of wood and bone, commonly carried by lighter cavalry and medium-duty mounted units across many factions. The Lizardmen Light Lance is of simpler craft than a Tepok Lance or Celestite Lance — unblessed, unremarkable, effective. Carried by Horned One Riders and similar units that need a charge-impact weapon without the celestial-grade potency of a temple-consecrated lance.

#### Fireleech Bolas

**8" range | S4 | AP 0 | D1 | Flaming (see §8) | Quick to Fire (see §8)**

A thrown weapon: weighted cords bound with small fire-leech creatures from the deep pools — reptilian pests that spit and burn on impact. When the bolas wraps around a target, the leeches cook what they touch. Short range, high strength, always hot — a thrown anti-infantry weapon iconic of Terradon-borne Skink hunters. Flaming interactions apply per §8 (no Regeneration, beast-keyword panic).
