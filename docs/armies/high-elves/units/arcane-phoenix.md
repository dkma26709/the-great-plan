# Arcane Phoenix

> The most magical of the three Phoenix variants. "An Arcane Phoenix is a majestic and fearsome magical avian in the form of a giant raptor with a wingspan as great as any of the mighty war-eagles of the High Elves, cloaked in blazing feathers that can turn the sharpest arrow, their true power bound to their nature as a creature of spirit and fire — a living embodiment of the destructive and purging will of Aqshy, the Red Wind of Magic." Where the Flamespyre Phoenix is young and *physically* fire-aspected (Wildfire AoE), and the Frostheart Phoenix is aged and *cold-aspected* (Cold Aura debuff), the **Arcane Phoenix** is *magically* fire-aspected — its body burns with the *raw fire of the Winds of Magic itself*, not biological combustion. "In Elven lore, Arcane Phoenixes are the companions of Asuryan the Creator, king of the Elven Pantheon, and are harbingers of woe and bearers of hope in equal measure, drawn from Asuryan's side when the Winds of Magic howl through the mortal world." The lore signature is twofold: **Emberstorm** *(the magic-flame Fly-over vortex — when the Arcane Phoenix flies over enemy units, each takes a swirling magical-flame strike)* and **Fiery Rebirth** *(shared with the other Phoenix variants — when slain, may rise again from the rebirth-flame)*. Drafted here as the **Hero-tier Monster Mount profile** for High Elf Anointed of Asuryan characters (same canonical rider as the other Phoenix variants). Sister-pieces: Flamespyre Phoenix, Frostheart Phoenix. The High Elf battlefield read: where Flamespyre dominates static 6"-radius combat and Frostheart shuts down nearby enemies via heavy debuff, the Arcane Phoenix is the *mobile striker* — its Fly-over Emberstorm lets it pick off enemy formations across the battlefield, and its strongest Attunement to the Winds makes it the most magic-resilient of the three.

## Special Rules

- **Terror** *(Psychology — per §8)* — at LiS 4, the Terror aura projects to **(6 + 4)" = 10"**. The Arcane Phoenix's magical aura makes its mere presence terrifying to mortal soldiers — "harbingers of woe and bearers of hope."
- **Fly (8")** *(per §8)* — Arcane Phoenix wings of magic-flame; the Phoenix flies almost exclusively (M 1 ground stat is essentially flavour).
- **Magical Attacks** *(per §8 — Phoenix biology intrinsic)* — all of the Arcane Phoenix's attacks (Talons-and-Beak + Emberstorm) count as Magical Attacks. *The Arcane Phoenix is the most explicitly magical of the three Phoenix variants* — its biology is described as "a living embodiment of the destructive and purging will of Aqshy."
- **Emberstorm** *(Arcane Phoenix unit identity rule — Movement-phase magic-flame template drop)* — During this model's **Movement phase**, immediately after the Phoenix completes its move (Fly or otherwise), the controlling player may place a **small round template** *(3" radius per §7.4 templates)* centred on any point the Phoenix passed over during its move. **All models under the template** (friend or foe, per standard §7 template rules) take **1 hit at S 4 / AP -1 / D 1 / Flaming / Magical Attacks**.

  **Resolution:**
  - Hits are automatic (no to-Hit roll, no scatter — the Phoenix's magic-flame is directed via Aqshy's will, not aimed conventionally).
  - Standard wound allocation applies (each affected unit's player allocates wounds among its own models).
  - Armour saves, Ward saves, Regeneration all apply per standard.
  - The Phoenix may perform Emberstorm **once per turn**, during its Movement phase only.
  - **Emberstorm does NOT trigger on a Fly-charge** (or any charge move). The Phoenix must complete a *normal* Movement phase move — Fly, March, or Advance — to drop the template. The player chooses: charge into combat (no Emberstorm), OR fly past enemies and drop the template (Emberstorm, no charge that turn).
  - **The template may be placed on any single point along the Phoenix's path that turn**, including over the Phoenix's current position, over its starting position, or anywhere in between. The player picks the optimal drop location after the move is complete.

  **Friendly-fire note:** Standard §7 template rules apply — *all models under the template* are hit, friend or foe. The Arcane Phoenix's magic-flame doesn't discriminate. Tactical cost: the Arcane Phoenix-mounted Anointed must drop the template in a location that hits enemies *without* catching friendly units in the radius. Friendly units in tight formation behind enemy lines may be at risk.

  **Lore-direct:** "The Phoenix's spiralling dive whips the Winds of Magic into a swirling vortex of magical flame." The Phoenix's flight path *trails* magic-fire; the template represents the swirling vortex left behind at the point of maximum flame-concentration along the route. **Distinctive from sister Phoenix mechanics:** Flamespyre Wildfire is a passive 6" radius static aura (Combat phase); Frostheart Cold Aura is a passive 6" radius static debuff (always-on); Emberstorm is an *active*, *concentrated* (3" small template), *path-positional* drop. The Arcane player chooses *where* to concentrate the magic-flame, not *who* to spray-fire across.

- **Attuned to the Winds** *(Phoenix-shared identity rule — variable Ward save scaling with the Magic phase)* — At the start of each **Magic phase**, after both Power and Dispel pools are determined, roll a **D6**. The result determines the Arcane Phoenix's Ward Save for the rest of this turn:

  | D6 | Ward this turn |
  |---|---|
  | **1-2** | None (the winds are still — even the Arcane Phoenix's magic dims when Aqshy is quiet) |
  | **3-4** | **Ward 6+** |
  | **5** | **Ward 5+** |
  | **6** | **Ward 4+** |

  The Ward applies to all incoming wounds (melee, ranged, magic, template, breath). **Lore note for Arcane specifically:** the Arcane Phoenix is the most *direct* manifestation of the Winds of Magic among the three variants — its very biology is sustained by Aqshy. Mechanically the Attuned to the Winds variable Ward is the same across all three Phoenixes, but lore-wise it's strongest on the Arcane (the others have biological backups — Flamespyre's Wildfire deterrent and Frostheart's NA scales — that the Arcane lacks). Without the Ward, the Arcane is the most fragile of the three.

- **Fiery Rebirth** *(Arcane Phoenix unit identity rule — once-per-battle resurrection)* — Identical to the Flamespyre and Frostheart Phoenix's Fiery Rebirth rules. When this model is reduced to 0 W, **immediately roll a D6**. On a **4+**, the Phoenix returns at **half its starting Wounds (rounded up)** — W 7 → 4 W remaining. Stays at the model's current position. **If the rider was attached, the rider is removed from play permanently** (the rebirth-flame consumes the Anointed of Asuryan). Fiery Rebirth may trigger only **ONCE per battle**.
