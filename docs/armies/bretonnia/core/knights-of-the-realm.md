# Knights of the Realm


> The Bretonnian backbone — sworn knights of the realm, sons of every duchy and minor lord, tested and proven in their *errantry* and now invested with the Knight's Vow. Each carries a heavy lance, a heater shield bearing his coat of arms, full plate harness, and rides a barded warhorse from the duchy's stables. They charge in *lance formation* — the trained wedge that cleaves enemy lines, the lance-tip biting deepest at the moment of impact, the Lady's hand turning aside the worst the enemy can offer in return. Where Empire knights are paid professionals organised into orders, Bretonnian knights are *sworn vassals* riding in the name of their duke, of the king, and of the Lady of the Lake. The knight is everything; the peasant is incidental.

- **Keywords:** Bretonnia, Human, Cavalry, Core, Knights of the Realm
- **Unit type:** Cavalry
- **Unit size:** 5-12 (wider upper bound than Imperial Knights' 5-10 to accommodate Lance Formation depth — a 10-model wedge fills 4 rows; 12 models spill into a partial row 5)
- **Combined pool per model:** W 2 (rider 1 + mount 1). Attackers use highest WS (4) and highest T (3) per §1 *Rider and Mount Profiles*
- **Natural Armour:** —
- **Worn armour (default, fixed kit):** Heavy armour + Heavy Shield + barding (per §8 Armour Save Stacking, one step per source)
- **Combined save: 2+** (heavy armour 4+ → Heavy Shield 3+ → barding 2+)
- **Equipment (fixed kit, no swap options):** Heavy Lance + Heavy Shield + heavy armour + barding
- **Vow upgrade options (replace Knight's Vow at list-build):**
  - **Crusader's Vow** (+1 pt/model, whole-unit upgrade) — replaces Knight's Vow keyword. See §17 Faction Mechanics for full definition. Grants Immunity to Panic-source stress, reroll failed psychology-source recovery rolls, and +1D6 charge distance roll (discard lowest). Useful for KotR units committed to anti-Chaos / anti-Undead / anti-Greenskin crusade campaigns
  - *(Questing Vow not available — Questing Vow forbids Heavy Lance per §17, and KotR are lance-specialist units; a unit of knights who give up the lance becomes Questing Knights, drafted as a separate unit in §17 Special)*
  - *(Grail Vow not available at unit level — the Grail Vow is exceptionally rare; Grail-Vow knights typically appear as individual characters or future Grail Knights units rather than as a Knight's-Vow upgrade)*
- **Command options:**
  - **Leader** (+5 pts) — the unit's *Knight Gallant*; champion for Rally / challenge / Leader-snipe purposes
  - **Standard bearer** (+10 pts — Magic Standard up to 50 pts; Bretonnian Knights get the elite cavalry banner budget, matching Imperial Knights)
  - **Musician** (+5 pts) — +1 to recovery and Rally rolls (the unit's herald, signalling charges and rallies on horn)
- **Special rules:**
  - Disciplined (see §8)
  - **Knight's Vow** (see §17 Faction Mechanics) — Blessing-eligibility marker. No additional effect at this vow tier. Replace with Crusader's Vow at list-build (see Vow upgrade options above)
  - **Blessing of the Lady** (see §17 Faction Mechanics) — intrinsic via the Knight's Vow. Ward Save 6+ vs S ≤ 4, 5+ vs S ≥ 5, applying to rider and mount alike. Lost on flee from combat
  - **Lance Formation eligible** (see §17 Faction Mechanics) — counts as Lance whenever the unit's geometry forms a triangular wedge of 6+ models in 3+ rows; +2" reach + +1 AP on melee weapons during the charge round
  - **Purebred Warhorse** (see §8) — applies to the mount profile. The Warhorse does not suffer the -2 M penalty for being barded; KotR retain M 10 with full barding, unlike Imperial Knights' barded warhorses (M 8). Speed-preservation rule, no offensive effect

**Weapon profiles (A on weapons):**

- **Heavy Lance** *(default, 1H with shield — fixed kit, no swap)* — **2" reach | A 1 | on charge (first round): S+2 (= S 5), AP -2, D 2; subsequent rounds: S+0, AP 0, D 1** — the iconic Bretonnian shock weapon. With the Lance Formation +2" reach extension, Heavy Lance becomes 4" reach on the charge round, allowing rows 2 and 3 of the wedge to strike alongside the wedge tip `[1H Lance]`
- **Warhorse's Hooves and Bite** *(mount natural, each model)* — 1" reach | **A 1** | S 3 | AP 0 | D 1 — the mount contributes its own attack at the standard barded-warhorse profile `[Natural]`

> **Lore notes:** The defining Bretonnian Core unit. Stats mirror Imperial Knights (M 4 / WS 4 / S 3 / T 3 / I 3, Warhorse M 10 — the heavy-cavalry tier sits at M 10 across factions, matching Horned Ones) with **Res 9** per the §17 heft calibration note (Res 8 → 9 for chivalric knights — sworn nobles, drilled and faithful). **Combined save 2+** via heavy armour + shield + barding, identical to Imperial Knights.
>
> **Mechanical identity is faction-mechanic-driven, not unit-internal.** Knight's Vow grants eligibility, Blessing of the Lady provides the divine Ward, Lance Formation provides the offensive identity. No internal special rule beyond these markers — deliberately so. Compare Imperial Knights, which carry **Breakthrough Charge** as a unit-internal identity rule because Empire's cross-faction toolkit (Knightly Orders, Inner Circle, weapon swaps) provides its tactical flex, leaving an identity hole for the unit-internal layer to fill. Bretonnian KotR fill that hole at the **faction** layer (Lance + Blessing) instead of the **unit** layer. Adding a "Sworn to the Charge" rule on top would dilute.
>
> **Equipment is fixed — no weapon swap options.** Bretonnian knights are lance specialists; the lance is the chivalric weapon, the weapon of investiture, the weapon the Lady blessed. Swapping to great weapons or cavalry hammers belongs to the Questing Knights (who set aside the lance as part of their quest) or to Empire's tactical-flex template — not to the realm's standard knight. Reinforces the "Bretonnian knights are *one thing* and they do it well" identity, contrasted against Empire knights' multi-Order, multi-loadout flexibility.
>
> **Unit size 5-12.** A 6-model wedge is the minimum (1 + 2 + 3 = 6, three rows); a 10-model wedge is four rows (1 + 2 + 3 + 4); a 12-model wedge fills four rows plus a 2-model partial row 5 (1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + 2 = 12). Sizes that don't form a clean triangular number (7, 8, 9, 11) can still be Lance if the front 3+ rows match the pattern and the partial rear rank slots in along the wedge edge. Units of 5 cannot reach Lance (insufficient rows).
>
> **Points 35 per model** — +11 over Imperial Knights' 24. The premium covers three Bretonnia-exclusive identity layers: **Blessing of the Lady** (Strength-variable Ward 6+/5+ applying in melee, ranged, and magic — substantial defensive value across all phases, ~6 pts); **Lance Formation eligibility** (charge-conditional +2" reach + +1 AP, with the wedge geometry letting rows 2-3 strike alongside the wedge tip — ~5 pts); and **Purebred Warhorse** (M 10 preserved through barding while Imperial / Demigryph cavalry now sit at M 8 — speed becomes a *Bretonnia-exclusive* axis under the new §8 Barding rule, ~3 pts). Offsetting: KotR lose Imperial Knights' Breakthrough Charge identity (-1) and weapon-swap kit flexibility (-1, mostly aesthetic since most Imperial Knights take the lance anyway). Net premium: +11 over Imperial baseline.
>
> Vs Saurus Warriors at 22 pts (W 2 / T 4 / 4+ save / M 4 / Res 10 / Predatory Fighter / Stubborn): the +13 gap covers M 10 vs 4 (now uniquely Bretonnian among heavy cavalry), 2+ save vs 4+, Heavy Lance charge-spike, all three Bretonnian layers, minus the lost Predatory Fighter / Stubborn / Res 1 / T 1 / A baseline. Reasonable bridge between infantry-Core and cavalry-Core pricing.
>
> **Compare:**
> - **Imperial Knight (24 pts, M 8 barded)** — same rider stat baseline, Breakthrough Charge identity, Knightly Order + Inner Circle + weapon-swap toolkit. Tactically flexible, no Ward, slower under the §8 Barding rule
> - **Aggradon Lancer (120 pts)** — Monstrous Cavalry tier, S 5 mount attacks, Blood Frenzy commitment. Different scale; illustrates the "shock cavalry with irrevocable charge identity" pattern
> - **Demigryph Knight (80 pts, M 8 barded)** — Empire's MonstCav, Griffon-kin Instinct anti-MonstInf bonus. A tier above KotR on durability (W 4 vs W 2) and damage (Demigryph mount A 3 S 4 AP -1 vs Warhorse A 1 S 3 AP 0); also slowed by barding
>
> Bretonnian Lord (and Lord on Royal Pegasus), Paladin, Damsel, and Prophetess all attach naturally to KotR units (single-model character attachment per §1) and inherit the Blessing through their own vows / Lady's Chosen status, layering Res bonuses, magic protection, and casting from within the unit. KotR are the *frame* on which the Bretonnian list is built.
