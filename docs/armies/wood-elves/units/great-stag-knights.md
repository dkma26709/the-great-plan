# Great Stag Knights

> *"They are no longer truly the Elves they once were. The power of Kurnous flows through them as surely as it does through Orion."* The Great Stag Knights are the elite sub-tier of the **Wild Riders of Kurnous** — Orion's personal honour guard, each as aggressive and impulsive as the King of the Woods himself. Where the rank-and-file Wild Riders charge into battle on the **Steeds of Kurnous** (manifest aspects of the hunt — magical horse-spirits), the Great Stag Knights have been gifted the sacred **Great Stags** of Athel Loren as mounts: ancient, antlered war-beasts standing closer to a Demigryph or an Aggradon than a mortal warhorse, faster than any mounted beast on the field, with antlers that punch through plate as a longbow shaft punches through cloth. Kurnous's touch transforms both rider and mount alike — the elf forgoes mortal kinship and becomes *fey*, half-bound to the Forest Spirit, his blood burning with the rage of the Wild Hunt; the stag grows beyond natural size, its antlers wreathed in the green light of Athel Loren's primeval magic. When the Wild Hunt thunders out of the trees, the Great Stag Knights ride at its vanguard — antlers down, spears levelled, frenzied as the wolves at the heels of Orion himself. The unit fingerprint: an apex Asrai shock-cavalry unit at the **Forest Spirit / Asrai elf** hybrid identity tier, sitting at the **Monstrous Cavalry** tier alongside Demigryphs and Aggradon Lancers — at **W parity with Demigryphs** (mount W 3) but on the **lighter side** of the Mon Cav defensive spectrum (5+ save vs 2+). The Great Stag is *big and fast*, not *armoured and tough*; the unit's niche is the **charge**, and only the charge. Kurnous-touched riders carry **Forest Spirit** (Magical Attacks, Imm Psych, Ward 5+/6+, Fear, Forest Strider, Flammable) AND the cross-elf **Elven Grace** + **Hunter's Prowess**; **Frenzy** drives the rage; **Wild Hunt Charge** grants the rider **+4 Initiative** AND **Devastating Charge** (+1 A) on the turn of the charge — the Hunt outruns the world and strikes more often than mortal reflex allows; the Great Stag's antlers deliver **Antler Impact** (one Impact hit per model on the charge at S 6 / AP -2, resolved before the Combat phase) followed by the regular Antler Combat-phase strikes (now A 2 baseline, S 6 / AP -2 on charge). The trade-off: T 4 mount / T 3 rider, light armour (6+/5+ save), W 3 combined, **Flammable** Forest Spirit body. Apex glass-cannon Mon Cav.

## Equipment

## Special Rules

- **Forest Spirit** *(Forest Spirit sub-roster faction trait — see Dryads profile or FACTION.md for the full bundle text)*. The Kurnous-touched rider has crossed the threshold from mortal elf to half-bound forest-spirit; the Great Stag mount is likewise a creature of Athel Loren's magic. Both rider and mount inherit the Forest Spirit bundle: **Fear** (LiS 3 → 9" aura — Mon Cav LiS bumps the radius), **Magical Attacks** (rider's Asrai Spear and mount's Antlers count as Magical), **Flammable** (Flaming attacks reroll failed wounds — the great Forest Spirit weakness), **Forest Spirit Ward 5+ in forest / 6+ outside** (conditional Ward stacking after worn armour), **Forest Strider** (treats forest terrain as Open), **Immune to Psychology** (Athel Loren's will, not mortal sensibility).
- **Elven Grace** *(faction-wide elf species trait — see §8 Defence)*. Ward 6+ in melee against attacks made at the elf's Initiative or lower. At rider I 6 (apex elf Initiative), the Ward catches almost everything — only models striking at I 7+ (Hero / Lord-tier elves, certain apex cult-elite units) bypass. On charge round with Wild Hunt Charge active (rider I 10), the Ward catches every attack made against the rider. *(Stacks with Forest Spirit Ward via the standard Ward-save sequence; per §8 Ward Save Stacking, the better-of-the-two applies, not both serially.)* **Applies to the rider profile only** — the Great Stag mount does not inherit Elven Grace (mount is forest-beast biology, not elf).
- **Hunter's Prowess** *(Asrai faction-wide species trait — see §8 Combat)*. Reroll to-Hit 1s with ranged attacks. The Asrai marksmanship axis. *(Mostly inert on this unit — Great Stag Knights are shock cavalry, not horse-archers; they carry no bow by default. Inherited as faction-baseline; preserved for forward-compatibility if a future Hero / Lord upgrade adds a ranged weapon.)*
- **Asrai Way of War** *(Wood Elf faction-wide identity rule — see FACTION.md)*. While in **Loose** state (per §6), this unit gains the **benefits of the Skirmisher special rule** (per §8): **Coherency 2", +1 Movement, Fire on the move, Free pivots**. For Great Stag Knights, the +1 M (effective M 10 in Loose) and Free pivots are the load-bearing benefits — both accelerate the charge approach and give the unit unmatched mobility. The "Cannot Form" clause does NOT apply; the player may toggle between Formed (rank-discipline, command-aura access) and Loose (apex-Skirmisher mobility) each activation.
- **Frenzy** *(per §8 Psychology)*. The Wild Hunt rage flows through both rider and mount. The unit carries the **F4 fingerprint**: +1 Attack on a single chosen weapon profile (typically the Asrai Spear), +1 MA, -1 MD, must charge nearest visible enemy if able, may not voluntarily disengage, must Follow Up on enemy Fall Back, must pursue, Imm Psych, suppresses Wavering / Broken state effects. *(Frenzy is **lost** on combat-loss per the standard §8 rule — the Great Stag Knights are not Permanent Frenzy. Once knocked off the charge tempo by a strict combat-loss, the Hunt's surge subsides and the unit fights at baseline thereafter. This is the unit's *fragility tax*: charge hard, win hard — or lose Frenzy and revert to baseline shock cavalry.)*
- **Wild Hunt Charge** *(Great Stag Knight unit identity rule — captures Kurnous's outrunning the world; rider only)*. On the turn this unit successfully **charges**, the **rider profile** gains:
  - **+4 Initiative** on the charge round (rider I 6 → effective I 10). The Wild Hunt outruns mortal reflex; the rider's spear lands before all but the highest-Initiative defenders can react. Combined with the +1 I charge bonus from §4, the rider strikes at *effective I 11* on the charge round (though I caps practically at the strike-order top).
  - **Devastating Charge** *(per §8)* — the rider gains **+1 Attack** on the charge round. Combined with Frenzy's +1 A, the rider's Asrai Spear delivers **A 3** on the charge.

  *(Lore-canonical: the Wild Hunt is the rider's rage incarnate — Kurnous's gift speeds the rider's arm and lets the spear land thrice. Mount Initiative is unmodified: stag I 4 + 1 charge = effective I 5 strike order, so the antler-strike resolves mid-pack while the rider's spear pre-empts.)*
- **Antler Impact** *(Great Stag Knight unit identity rule — mount's charge contribution)*. On the turn this unit successfully **charges** into melee, before the Combat phase begins, resolve the following Impact attack (per §8 *Impact*):
  - **1 Impact hit per model** at **S 6** (mount S 5 + 1 charge) | **AP -2** (antler-tip piercing plate) | **D 1** | **Magical Attacks** *(per Forest Spirit)*
  - Resolved against the charged unit's models within Reach of any charging Great Stag in base contact.
  - Damage does not carry between models. Failed charges produce no Impact attacks.

  *(Lore: the Great Stag's antlers drive home with the full weight of the cavalry charge — the antler-impact moment hits **before** the Combat phase begins, dropping enemy front-rank models before the regular Antler and Spear strikes can land. Athel-Loren-wreathed antlers do not glance off plate. The Impact attack is **in addition to** the mount's regular Combat-phase Antler attacks (see Weapon Profiles); both fire on the charge round, with Impact resolving first in the Charges sub-phase per §7.2a, and the regular Antler attacks resolving in the Combat phase at the mount's strike step.)*

## Options

**Worn armour upgrade option:**
- **Shield** (+2 pts/model) → combined save **5+** (light armour 6+ + shield +1 step = 5+). The Asrai-craft heater shield, woven from bark and steel, often inlaid with the household's antler-rune. *(Shields are the only kit upgrade — the Kurnous-touched reject medium and heavy armour, and the spear is fixed kit. No barding on the mount.)*

**No weapon swap options.** The Asrai Spear is the sacred cavalry weapon of the Wild Hunt; Kurnous-touched riders do not carry alternative weapons. No bow option (Great Stag Knights are shock cavalry, not horse-archers — Glade Riders fill the mounted-archer slot).

**Command options:**
- **Wild Hunter (Leader)** (+5 pts) — Wild Rider veteran, longer-touched by Kurnous; sharper spear, longer antlers on the mount.
- **Standard Bearer** (+10 pts, may take Magic Standard up to 40 pts — Mon Cav elite cavalry banner budget). *Asrai cavalry banners are woven from Athel Loren spider-silk and antler-bone; specific Magic Standard options pending the Magic Standards subsystem.*
- **Musician** (+5 pts) — the Hunter's Horn-bearer; sounds the call of Kurnous, sets the Wild Hunt cadence.
