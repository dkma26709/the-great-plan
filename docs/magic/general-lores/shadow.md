# Lore of Shadow


> Stat manipulation, concealment, and misdirection. Where Death drains and Fire destroys, Shadow **warps reality** — stats waver, hits miss, units appear where they shouldn't be. The lore specialises in enemy stat reduction, -to-hit concealment on friendly units, minor displacement, and a narrow vein of initiative-test assassination. Flavour: silver and purple, mirrors, mist, whispered doubt. Not nihilistic like Death — Shadow is *trickster*. It wins fights the enemy didn't know they were having.

**Lore Attribute — Fading Step:** after this wizard successfully casts a Shadow spell, the caster may immediately make a **10" Fly move** (per §8 Fly — passes over models and terrain). The move is not a Charge and cannot end within 1" of an enemy unit, inside impassable terrain, or inside another model's base. Unlimited per turn — each successful cast is another fade. Shadow wizards are the most mobile casters in the game, flickering across the field between spells.

**Signature Spell — Melkoth's Mystifying Miasma**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 6 | 18" | Hex | Until start of next turn |

Caster chooses one of **WS, BS, I, or M**. Target enemy unit suffers **-D3** to that characteristic until start of next turn (minimum 1 — characteristics cannot drop below 1). Confusion fills their minds — swords miss their marks, legs falter, arrows fly wide. The workhorse Shadow cast — always available, flexible, softens any unit for what comes next.

#### Basic (Tier 1) — Lore Access 1+

**The Withering**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 8 | 18" | Hex | Until start of next turn |

Target enemy unit suffers **-1 T** (minimum 1) until start of next turn. The body forgets how to resist harm. Pairs viciously with any incoming damage — dropping T below an attacker's S turns grazing hits into wounds.

**Enfeebling Foe**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 8 | 18" | Hex | Until start of next turn |

Target enemy unit suffers **-1 S** (minimum 1) until start of next turn. The sword arm remembers it was tired; the mace weighs too much. Neuters a charging heavy-hitter — dropping a monstrous cavalry's S5 to S4 turns their impact into a scratch.

**Shroud of Shadow**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 7 | 18" | Augment | Until start of next turn |

Target friendly unit suffers **-1 to hit against it from ranged attacks** until start of next turn. A smoke of half-forms surrounds the unit — archers lose their marks. Distinct from Life's Leaf Fall (which is organic/living mist): this is pure shadow-illusion.

**Steed of Shadows**

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 8 | 12" | Augment, Instant |

Target **single friendly character** (not a unit, not a champion in a unit — solo models only) may immediately make a **12" Fly move**, ignoring terrain and models. The character cannot end within 1" of an enemy unit or inside impassable. Cannot declare a Charge as part of this move. The character fades into shadow and re-emerges somewhere useful. Paired with Fading Step, a Shadow wizard on foot can reposition over 20" in a single magic phase.

#### Intermediate (Tier 2) — Lore Access 2+

**Veil of Unreality**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 11 | 12" (aura) | Augment | Until start of next turn |

All friendly units with any model **within 12"** of the caster suffer **-1 to hit against them from ranged attacks** until start of next turn. A localised collapse of perception. Scales Shroud of Shadow up from single-unit to army-wide — protects the line, not the hero. Makes Shadow the premier anti-shooting lore. Strong-for-tier — the army-wide scope (vs Shroud's single target) justifies the CV ceiling.

**Pall of Hesitation**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 10 | 18" | Hex | Until start of next turn |

Target enemy unit gains **Always Strikes Last** (see §8) until start of next turn. The unit's strikes come a half-beat late, as if every swing is watched through smoked glass. Mechanically distinct from Miasma's numeric -D3 I — this is categorical, a unit with I10 still strikes last. Devastating on equal-I brawls where striking last loses the exchange before it starts.

**Shadow's Step**

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 9 | 18" | Augment, Instant |

Target friendly unit may immediately make a **D6" Fly move** (not a Charge, 1" rule, no impassable). The unit must end in a legal formation. Cannot be cast on a unit that has already declared a Charge this turn. A small unit-scale reposition — step a block out of a charge lane, slide a flank into threat range, fade a vulnerable unit behind cover. Shorter than Steed of Shadows (T1, character, 12") but unit-scale.

**Bewildering**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 10 | 18" | Hex | Until start of next turn |

Target enemy unit cannot benefit from **any newly-cast friendly augment spells, friendly Lore Attribute effects, or hostile-spell removal effects** until start of next turn. Existing buffs already on the unit when this spell resolves are unaffected; only effects that *would* land on the unit during the duration fail. The unit is fogged in a mirror-mist — their own wizards' blessings can't find them, and incoming Lore-attribute pulses miss the mark. Anti-magic hex — forces opposing wizards to choose less-protected targets, complementing Shadow's existing "-stat" approach. Single rule (block magic-targeting); single mental load.

#### Advanced (Tier 3) — Lore Access 3+

**Pendulum of Shadow** *(line template)*

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 12 | Special (line) | Direct Damage, Template |

The caster selects a direction. A narrow line **18"** long extends from the caster's base in that direction. Each **enemy model** touched by the line suffers **1 hit at S5, AP -2, damage D3**. **Magical Attacks.** Full line-template mechanics pending in §7.2. The lore's one damage tool — a scythe-swing that cuts through whatever formation stands in its path. Damage scales with how many models the line crosses: a tight 5-wide formation gets sliced down the centre; a lone character gets clipped once.

**Pit of Shades** *(large round template)*

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 14 | 18" | Direct Damage, Template |

Place a **large round template** anywhere within 18" of the caster; on initial placement, the template **scatters D6"** in a random direction (wider than Flame Storm/Purple Sun's D3 — Shadow magic is unreliable). Each model touched by the template must pass an **Initiative test** (roll a D6 — equal to or less than I passes). On a **failure**, that model suffers **1 wound with no armour save allowed** (ward saves permitted). **Magical Attacks.**

**Not Remains In Play** — this is a one-off strike, unlike Death's Purple Sun which persists. Thematic contrast with Purple Sun: Death targets T (the body gives up), Shadow targets I (the mind is too slow). High-I elves shrug it off; lumbering heavy infantry drops models in droves. Initiative can be softened first by Miasma (-D3 I) to turn a shrug into a massacre — the lore's core combo.

**Okkam's Mindrazor**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 13 | 18" | Augment | Until start of next turn |

Target friendly unit uses its **Res value instead of S** for wound rolls in melee combat until start of next turn. Armour and ward saves are unchanged; only the wound roll is boosted. The unit's blades strike with the weight of their will — minds sharpened into swords.

A wild swing: S3 peasants with Res 7 suddenly wound as if S7; a Loremaster character with Res 10 wounds on 2+ against almost anything. Rewards well-led, high-Res units (generals' regiments, veteran blocks) and makes an otherwise-average combat unit into a surgical cleaver for one brutal turn.

#### Master (Tier 4) — Lore Access 4+

**Penumbral Pendulum**

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 18 | Special (24" line) | Direct Damage, Template |

The caster selects a direction. A narrow line **24"** long extends from the caster's base in that direction. Each enemy model touched by the line must take an **Initiative test** (roll D6 — equal to or less than the model's I passes; otherwise fails). Each failing model suffers **1 wound, no armour save allowed; ward saves are permitted**. **Magical Attacks.** Full line-template mechanics pending in §7.2.

Combines Pendulum of Shadow's line template (Adv, S5 AP-2 D3 dice damage) with Pit of Shades' I-test damage mechanic (Adv, large round template) — but at Master scale: a 24" line of I-test damage cutting across the battlefield. Devastating against low-I formations that line up; clipping for high-I elves. The lore's longest-reach spell — the wizard sweeps a scythe of perception across the field. Honours the "no model-slay" rule via "1 wound, no armour, wards permitted."

**Cloak of Mirrors** *(RIP, aura)*

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 16 | 18" (self-radius) | Augment (aura), Remains In Play |

*Remains In Play.* While in play, all friendly units wholly within **18"** of the caster suffer **-1 to-Hit against all incoming attacks** (melee and ranged). The wizard wraps the army in mirror-shadow — every blow comes through illusion, finding empty space half the time.

Single rule. Scales Veil of Unreality (Int aura, -1 to-hit ranged only, until next turn) up to army-wide RIP including melee. Mirrors how the other lore Master Bs scale a lower-tier signature to army-wide-persistent (Reading of the Stars, Pha's Vigil, Pall of Doom, Mantle of the Beasts all follow this pattern). Caster has to commit a position near the line for the aura to cover the army's main body; RIP makes it dispellable.

> **Design note on Lore of Shadow.** Range identity is **mid-range** — between Death's intimate 12" and Heavens' far 24-36". The wizard's tricks reach across the field but don't summon from the sky. **18" baseline** for hexes, augments, and damage; **12" for self-aura concealment** (Veil of Unreality) and **character-mobility** (Steed of Shadows) where the caster is at the centre; **24" only for the Master line spell** (Penumbral Pendulum) — the lore's longest reach. CV calibration after this pass: Sig 6 (Miasma's flexibility kept reliable). Basic 7-8 with stat-debuff hexes (Withering, Enfeebling Foe) at the high end. Intermediate 9-11 with Veil of Unreality (army-wide aura) and Pall of Hesitation (categorical ASL) topping the band, plus the new Bewildering (anti-magic hex) at the same level. Advanced 12-14 with Pit of Shades' I-test template at the cap. Master 16-18: **Penumbral Pendulum** for apex line damage via I tests, **Cloak of Mirrors** for persistent army-wide concealment. Lore identity: Shadow remains the stat-manipulation, concealment, and trickster lore — every enemy debuff stacks viciously on the same target (Miasma -D3, Withering -1 T, Enfeebling -1 S, Pall of Hesitation ASL, Bewildering blocks counter-magic), and the wizard's own mobility is uncapped via the Fading Step Lore Attribute. Where Death drains, Shadow *makes the enemy's army work worse*. Lore Attribute (Fading Step) remains unchanged.
