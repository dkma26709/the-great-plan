# Saurus Warriors


> Spawned for war, drilled by instinct. These are not human soldiers who trained for a few years — they emerged from the spawning pools as fighters. WS-A 4 reflects that baseline martial ability; WS-D 3 captures the slower defensive instinct of the cold-blooded predator-warrior — the Saurus hits well but reacts a half-beat slow to incoming blows.

| M | WS-A | WS-D | BS | S | T | W | I | Res | LiS | US |
|---|----|----|----|---|---|---|---|----|-----|----|
| 4 | 4 | 3 | 0 | 4 | 4 | 2 | 2 | 10 | 1 | 2 |

- **Keywords:** Lizardmen, Saurus, Infantry, Core
- **Unit type:** Infantry
- **Unit size:** 10-30
- **Points:** 22 per model
- **Natural Armour:** 5+ (scaly skin)
- **Equipment (default):** Celestite Blade, shield
- **Equipment options:**
  - Celestite Spear + shield (free) — see Armoury
- **With shield:** combined save **4+** (Natural 5+ + shield +1 step, per §8 Armour Save Stacking)
- **Command options:**
  - **Spawn Leader** (champion, +10 pts) — acts as unit leader for Rally, challenges, and Leader-snipe purposes
  - **Standard bearer** (+10 pts)
  - **Musician** (+10 pts) — +1 to recovery and Rally rolls
- **Special rules:**
  - Stubborn (see §8)
  - Predatory Fighter (see §8)
  - **Pack Cohesion** — while this unit is in Formed formation and has 10 or more models, coherency checks triggered by casualties during the Combat phase are **deferred**. Coherency is checked and resolved only at the end of the Combat phase — until then, models may be removed and the remaining models continue fighting without penalty. If coherency is still broken at phase end, normal coherency-break consequences apply and the unit must rectify on its next activation. The spawn-brothers instinctively tighten their formation as casualties fall.

**Weapon profiles (A on weapons):**

- **Saurus Jaws** *(natural, always — front-rank only)* — **0.5" reach** | **A 1** | S 4 | AP 0 | D 1 — every Saurus snaps with its jaws when an enemy stands at base contact. The 0.5" reach means only models in actual base-to-base contact bite; second-rank spear-Saurus stab past their front-ranker but cannot reach the enemy with their teeth `[Natural]`
- **Celestite Blade** *(default, 1H)* — **A 2** — full profile in Armoury (1" | AP -1 | D 1) `[1H Blade]`
- **Celestite Spear** *(option, 1H)* — **A 2** — full profile in Armoury (2" | +1 I baseline | D 1, with anti-cavalry/monster D2 and anti-charge S+1 AP -1) `[1H Reach]`

> **Lore notes:** I2 is deliberate — Saurus are strong, tough, and skilled but not quick. They fight with cold, methodical precision. A2 baseline (up from WAP's 1, now on the weapon per A-on-weapons convention) reflects that in AoS these are 2-attack troops. US 2 reflects their physical mass — Saurus are substantially built and count on objectives accordingly. Natural Armour 5+ (scaly skin) improves to 4+ with shield; Saurus equipped with the Celestite Blade by default reflects their elite line-infantry status in the Lizardmen armies. Pack Cohesion captures the spawn-brother instinct — Saurus units stay functional deeper into a losing fight than human regiments would. **Saurus Jaws** at troop tier are a thematic identity flourish — every Saurus has a mouthful of teeth and uses them. The 0.5" reach is the mechanical hook: front-rank Saurus get a free bite when they engage, second-rank spear-Saurus don't (their teeth are too far back from the enemy). Character-tier jaws (Scar-Vet / Oldblood) remain distinctly nastier — A 2, S 5, AP -2, D D3 — preserving the tier gap. Pricing not adjusted in this pass; revisit during playtest if the bite proves to swing the unit's role. A-on-weapons convention applied (retroactive pass, ruleset update per project memory).
