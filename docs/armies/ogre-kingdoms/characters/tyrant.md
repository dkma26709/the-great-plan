# Tyrant


> The biggest, strongest, fattest Ogre in the tribe — won the title through single-combat dominance in the Maw-pit, devoured the previous Tyrant's broken body to claim authority. Rule extends across an entire Kingdom of the Mountains of Mourn. Tyrants accumulate boastful titles ("Skull-Cracker the Pitiless", "Greasus the Ravenous") — long lists of violent superlatives marking each kill that built their reign. They lead through brutal authority, not inspiration; subordinate Ogres follow because the Tyrant *can* personally crush them. Personal combat dominance is the Tyrant's whole proposition.

- **Special rules:**
  - **Apex Tyrant** *(identity rule, two-part)* — (a) **Crushing strikes:** the Tyrant's melee weapon attacks gain **Mighty Blow (1)** (see §8) — +1 Strength in the first round of each melee combat engagement. Captures the Tyrant's lore as the strongest Ogre alive: when his swing lands in the opening exchange, it crushes through anything. Hand weapon strikes at S 6 in round one (great weapon S 8). (b) **Brutal authority:** while the Tyrant is alive, friendly Ogre units that have any model within **12"** of the Tyrant **may not Fall Back** from a lost combat — they must hold the line and take **+1 stress** instead. Represents tribal might-makes-right: the Tyrant's presence physically threatens any Ogre considering retreat. Note: not an Ld bump; not Inspiring Presence; pure positional / behavioural enforcement via threat. **Predatory Fighter is reserved to Lizardmen units** per design call — Mighty Blow is the Ogre apex's combat-dominance signature instead

Represents the iconic Ogre headbutt; in melee, the Tyrant leads with the skull

> **Lore notes:** Stats per heft calibration — WS 6 / S 5 / T 5 / I 4 / A 5 reference unchanged. **W 6 → 10** (Lord-tier aggressive wound bump; **Tyrant is the Ogre Lord-MonstInf apex**, parity with the revised Kroxigor Ancient W 10 across factions for Lord-tier MonstInf characters). Tyrant W 10 sits well above Saurus Oldblood's W 7 — Ogres are physically larger than Saurus per lore, and at apex tier the gap is significant. **Ld 9 reference unchanged** (no +1 bump per Ogre heft note — even the apex Tyrant doesn't reach Ld 10; Ogres are brutal, not stoic). Combined 6+ save baseline (gut-plate); light armour 5+. **No heavy armour option** per Ogre roster ceiling (5+ across the faction; "fat guys with little armour"). **Apex Tyrant** is two-part by deliberate design: (a) **Mighty Blow (1)** captures personal melee dominance — the strongest Ogre alive in opening-exchange terms; +1 S in round one means hand weapon strikes at S 6 (great weapon S 8). (b) **No-Fall-Back-within-12"** captures might-makes-right tribal enforcement — Ogres near the Tyrant may not retreat, forced to take +1 stress instead. Crucially, this is *not* an Ld bump and *not* Inspiring Presence — Ogres aren't fighting harder because they trust the Tyrant; they're fighting because they fear him personally. Pattern: combat-axis + behavioural-axis identity rule, no morale-axis interaction. **Predatory Fighter is reserved to Lizardmen units** per design call (2026-04-25 session) — that's why the Tyrant gets Mighty Blow rather than the Saurus-style cascading-extras pattern. Cleaner faction-axis differentiation: Saurus apex = swarm-of-strikes, Ogre apex = single-crushing-blow. **Headbutt** is a Tyrant-exclusive weapon profile resolving once per melee round at **I 6** (revised from I 10 — still quick, but apex-Initiative units like Skink Chiefs / Chameleon Stalkers strike first). Compares to Saurus Oldblood (260 pts, NA 2+, Primeval Command 18" Ld aura): Tyrant **280 pts**, no NA (wears gut-plate — gear, not biology), no Ld aura (Ogre culture), W 10 vs Oldblood's W 7 (Ogres significantly bigger than Saurus at Lord tier). Different mechanical fingerprint, both at Lord-tier slot pricing. BSB option +25 per universal convention; though in lore the Bruiser is the more traditional BSB carrier (see Bruiser entry), the option is open to either Lord or Hero per our convention.
