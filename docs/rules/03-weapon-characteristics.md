# §3 Weapon Characteristics


Weapons have three properties independent of the wielder's stats:

### Armour Penetration (AP)

AP modifies the target's armour save.

| AP | Effect |
|----|--------|
| 0 | No modification |
| -1 | Save worsened by 1 |
| -2 | Save worsened by 2 |
| -3 | Save worsened by 3 |
| etc. | |

**Example:** A weapon with AP -2 against a target with a 3+ save means the target saves on 5+.

Ward saves are never modified by AP.

### Damage (D)

Each unsaved wound inflicts D damage, removing that many wounds from the target. Most basic infantry weapons are D1. Heavier weapons — great weapons, monstrous natural attacks, enchanted blades — deal D2 or more.

| D | Typical weapons |
|---|----------------|
| 1 | Hand weapons, spears, clubs, javelins, bows |
| 2 | Great weapons, halberds, monstrous infantry attacks, heavy cavalry lances |
| 3 | Monster natural weapons, siege weapons |
| D3/D6 | Variable — some devastating attacks roll for damage |

**Excess damage:** Damage that exceeds a model's remaining wounds is lost (does not carry over to the next model). A D3 weapon against W1 infantry wastes 2 damage per hit — this is intentional. Big weapons are designed for killing tough targets, not for mowing through chaff more efficiently.

> **Strength and AP are fully decoupled.** Strength affects the to-wound roll only; armour penetration is a weapon characteristic, independent of the wielder. A strong creature with blunt natural weapons wounds reliably but cannot force its way through plate; a weaker fighter with an enchanted blade cuts clean through but wounds less often.

### Weapon Profile Format

Weapons are described as: **Name** (Range/Reach, AP X, DY, special rules)

Examples:
- Celestite blade (1", AP -1, D1)
- Saurus spear (2", AP 0, D1, +1A on the charge)
- Kroxigor great maul (1", AP -2, D2)
- Carnosaur jaws (2", AP -3, D3)
- Skink javelin (8", AP 0, D1)
- Giant bow (36", AP -3, D3)
