# State Troops — Crossbowmen

> State Troops armed with the exotic crossbow — far less common than Handgunners or Empire Archers (auxiliary). Crossbow regiments emerged in the southern Empire from Tilean influence, where mercenary crossbow companies have been a fixture for centuries. Slower-firing than a bow but more powerful, and considerably easier to train than a handgun (a recruit can learn the basics in a week, where a handgun matchlock requires months). Drilled in volley-fire from a stationary position; the crossbow's reload demands the wielder stand still. Mostly fielded in lower numbers than Handgunners, often as a detachment supporting a Halberdier or Spearman main block.
