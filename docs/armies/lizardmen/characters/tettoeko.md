# Tetto'eko


> **Astromancer of Constellations** — Chief Astronomer of the Temple of the Eclipse in Tlaxtlan. Spawned in the first decades of the 24th century IC when the true moon obscured Morrslieb — an alignment once read as ill omen, now pronounced a sign of fortune by Tetto'eko's coming. Blessed by the Old One Tepok with unfathomable foresight; revered as a Slann, Temple Guard bowing low as his palanquin hovers across Tlaxtlan to attend to his many star-viewings. By his predictions, dire events have been averted before they were allowed to happen. At Quetza in 2489 IC he led an army to confront the Skaven when no Slann could be awakened, gave deployment orders shortly *before* the ratmen erupted from tunnels nearby, foretold their every move, and finally conjured a comet to fall upon the ruined city — shattering the tunnels beneath and crushing the hordes hiding within. It was Tetto'eko who pointed the great Chameleon Skink Oxyotl towards the hidden enclave of Skaven Assassins waiting to ambush Lord Mazdamundi. *It has been said that the Lizardmen have never lost a battle with Tetto'eko present.* Wields the Eye of the Old Ones (which sees into possible futures), the Stellar Staff (a mystical orrery that alters the path of comets), and is borne aloft on a Slann-gift Palanquin of Constellations. Yet even his foresight has limits: the Chaos Moon, Morrslieb, blocks his visions, and he cannot predict its motion.

- **Keywords:** Lizardmen, Saurian, Skink, Wizard, **Arcane Vassal**, Character, Lord, Named

- **Natural Armour:** 6+ (Skink baseline)

- **Equipment (intrinsic — cannot be removed or swapped):** Stellar Staff, Eye of the Old Ones, Palanquin of Constellations

- **Special rules:**
  - **Skink species package** — Disciplined, Cold-Blooded, Aquatic, Slippery (WS-A < WS-D split)
  - **Fly (8)** — via the Palanquin
  - **Wizard (Heavens, LA 4 — Master, Loremaster)** — see Magic Profile above
  - **Arcane Vassal** — friendly Slann may cast spells through Tetto'eko's model position
  - **Tepok's Foresight** *(identity rule — apex prediction)* — **once per phase**, Tetto'eko may force a re-roll of any single D6 rolled by either player anywhere on the battlefield. The re-rolled result stands. Unlike the generic Starseer's once-per-game / misread-risk Foresight, Tetto'eko's foresight is unfailing — there is no backlash. Lore-direct: "the Lizardmen have never lost a battle with Tetto'eko present" + "ability to shift the winds of fate his own way."
  - **Eye of the Old Ones** *(intrinsic — Ward 5+ already encoded above)* — additionally, at the start of every friendly turn, Tetto'eko may nominate one enemy unit on the battlefield. Until the end of that turn, friendly Lizardmen units **re-roll 1s to hit** in melee and ranged attacks against the nominated unit. Lore-direct: "as stellar alignments draw near, Tetto'eko can see opportunities for his forces to exploit."
  - **Comet of Cassandora** *(once per battle — apex stellar invocation)* — at any point in any friendly Magic phase, Tetto'eko may invoke the Comet without using a casting attempt or magic dice. Place a marker anywhere on the battlefield within **48"** of Tetto'eko. The Stellar Staff alters the comet's trajectory — Tetto'eko picks the precise landing point. At the **start of the next friendly turn**, the comet falls: every unit (friend or foe) within **D6"** of the marker suffers **2D6 hits at S 8 / AP -3 / D D3 / Magical / Flaming**. Lore-direct: at Quetza, Tetto'eko conjured a comet to fall upon the ruined city, shattering the tunnels beneath and crushing the Skaven hordes hiding within.
  - **Inscrutable to Morrslieb** *(narrative restriction)* — the Chaos Moon blocks Tetto'eko's foresight. Tepok's Foresight re-rolls have no effect when forcing a re-roll on a die roll made by a unit with the **Daemon (Tzeentch)** or **Skaven (Warpstone)** keyword. Lore-direct: "the Chaos Moon, Morrslieb, is able to block his visions."
  - **Reverence of a Slann** *(identity rule — apex Skink standing)* — friendly **Lizardmen** units within **18"** of Tetto'eko ignore the Cold-Blooded restriction "may re-roll **failed** Resolve tests" and instead re-roll **all** Resolve tests they take (taking the second result). Lore-direct: "even Temple Guard bow to him, given the same level of respect as a Slann."
  - **Astrological Premonition** *(deployment-phase rule)* — after both armies have completed deployment but before the first turn begins, Tetto'eko's controlling player may **redeploy up to D3+1 friendly units** anywhere within their deployment zone. Units redeployed via this rule may not be repositioned outside their original deployment zone. Lore-direct: "at Quetza, Tetto'eko gave orders to deploy shortly before the ratmen erupted from tunnels nearby."
  - **He Who Sees Daemons** *(army-build synergy — Oxyotl bond)* — if **Oxyotl** is in the army, Oxyotl's **Daemon-Sense** rule extends to also trigger when an enemy unit with the **Skaven** keyword arrives via reserves, ambush, deep strike, or any concealed deployment. Lore-direct: "it was Tetto'eko that pointed the great Chameleon Skink Oxyotl towards the hidden enclave of Skaven Assassins that was waiting to ambush Lord Mazdamundi."

> **Lore notes:** The Astromancer of Constellations — Chief Astronomer of the Temple of the Eclipse, blessed by Tepok with unfathomable foresight, given the same level of respect as a Slann. *It has been said that the Lizardmen have never lost a battle with Tetto'eko present.* **Loremaster Heavens** is the apex specialisation — every spell in the lore at every tier, the only Skink character with Loremaster access. **Tepok's Foresight** is once-per-phase, universal, no-backlash D6 re-roll — a massive upgrade over the generic Starseer's once-per-game / misread-risk version. **Comet of Cassandora** is the marquee invocation: place a marker within 48", call the comet, and at the start of next turn 2D6 S 8 / AP -3 hits land within D6" — captures the canonical Quetza miracle. **Reverence of a Slann** extends Cold-Blooded *all* Resolve rerolls (instead of just failed) to friendly Lizardmen within 18". **Astrological Premonition** lets the Lizardmen player redeploy D3+1 units after both armies finish — captures the lore-direct "gave deployment orders shortly *before* the ratmen erupted from tunnels nearby." **Inscrutable to Morrslieb** is the lore-direct caveat: Tepok's Foresight is denied against Daemon (Tzeentch) and Skaven (Warpstone) units. **He Who Sees Daemons** extends Oxyotl's Daemon-Sense to Skaven arrivals when both are in the same army — the army-build synergy that points Oxyotl at the hidden Skaven Assassin enclave.
