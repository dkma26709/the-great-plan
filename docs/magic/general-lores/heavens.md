# Lore of Heavens


> Celestial magic — the movement of stars, the fall of comets, the crack of lightning. Heavens wizards read the threads of fate and call down the sky. The lore's identity is twofold: fate manipulation (rerolls, hexes, foresight) and devastating anti-armour damage (Lightning Attacks).

**Lore Attribute — Astral Alignment:** A Heavens caster who is not in combat (no model within melee reach of an enemy) may reroll one die of their casting roll on their next cast this turn. The wizard reads the alignment of the stars and chooses which die to discard. Lost the moment they are engaged — you cannot contemplate the heavens with a blade swinging at your throat.

> **Damage spells from this lore have the Lightning Attacks special rule** (see §8 *Special Rules Glossary*) — ignoring armour saves entirely. This is inherent to any Heavens damage spell regardless of the lore attribute.

**Signature Spell — Harmonic Convergence**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 6 | 18" | Augment | Until start of next turn |

Target friendly unit may reroll all to-hit, to-wound, and armour save rolls of 1. The stars align — minor adjustments to fate that compound across an entire unit's worth of dice.

#### Basic (Tier 1) — Lore Access 1+

**Iceshard Blizzard**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 8 | 24" | Hex | Until start of next turn |

Target enemy unit suffers -1 to hit (melee and ranged) and +1 stress. A localised blizzard of ice shards — disorienting, blinding, and demoralising even if no one is killed. Basic tier but a strong-for-tier hex: combines a hit-rate debuff with a stress tick, so the CV reflects the doubled effect.

**Cerulean Shield**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 7 | 24" | Augment | Until start of next turn |

Target friendly unit gains Ward Save (5+) against ranged attacks. A shimmering celestial barrier deflects arrows and bolts — useless in melee, invaluable under fire. Range 24" reflects Heavens' long-reach identity — the wizard can extend the celestial barrier across a forward-pushed unit even from deep in the line.

**Wind Blast**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 8 | 24" | Hex | Instant |

Target enemy unit is pushed D6" directly away from the caster. If the unit contacts impassable terrain or another unit during the push, it suffers D6 S3 hits. A howling gale — not lethal in itself, but it disrupts positioning, can shatter formations, and punishes tightly packed armies. The CV reflects the disruption potential — pushed units lose charges, lose engagement, lose objective control.

**Urannon's Thunderbolt**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 9 | 30" | Magic Missile | Instant |

D6 S5 hits with Lightning Attacks (ignore armour saves). A single devastating bolt called down from the heavens — the signature damage spell of every Heavens wizard. Basic tier but high casting value: any Heavens caster can learn it, but landing it reliably demands real magical ability. Range 30" reflects Heavens' celestial reach — the bolt is summoned from the sky, not thrown from the wizard's hand, and can punish gunlines deep in the enemy deployment zone.

#### Intermediate (Tier 2) — Lore Access 2+

**Curse of the Midnight Wind**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 10 | 24" | Hex | Until start of next turn |

Target enemy unit must reroll all 6s on to-hit, to-wound, and armour save rolls. The inverse of Harmonic Convergence — where the stars align for your allies, they conspire against your enemies. Devastating against elite units that rely on every roll counting. Strong-for-tier hex: hits three axes simultaneously and disproportionately punishes high-WS / high-armour units, so the CV is held high in Intermediate.

**Portent of Far-Seeing**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 8 | Self | Augment | Until start of next turn |

The caster and all friendly units within 12" may reroll one failed dispel or recovery roll per turn. Foresight rippling outward — the wizard sees the threads and whispers guidance to those nearby.

**Storm of Cronos**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 11 | 18" | Direct Damage | Instant |

All enemy units within 18" of the caster suffer D3 S4 hits with Lightning Attacks (ignore armour saves). A ring of celestial lightning radiating outward from the wizard — punishes anything that gets too close, in any direction. The caster stands at the eye of the storm. Bubble damage hits every enemy unit in range simultaneously — the CV reflects that scaling against tightly-packed deployments. The 18" radius (rather than Heavens' usual longer reach) is the trade for that bubble effect.

#### Advanced (Tier 3) — Lore Access 3+

**Chain Lightning**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 12 | 30" | Direct Damage | Instant |

D6 S6 hits with Lightning Attacks (ignore armour saves) on the target unit. Then the lightning chains: on a 4+, the nearest enemy unit within 6" suffers the same hits. On a 5+, the next nearest. On a 6+, the next. Each link in the chain is a separate attack — the bolt arcs between metal, moisture, and flesh, seeking the path of least resistance. Range 30" (matching Urannon's Thunderbolt) — the bolt is summoned from sky to first target, so initial reach mirrors the lore's other lightning spells; subsequent chains are local to the strike.

**Comet of Casandora**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 13 | 36" | Summoning | Persistent |

Place a marker anywhere within range. At the start of each subsequent turn, roll D6: on 1-3 the comet grows (add +1 to the comet's Strength for the eventual impact, starting at S5); on 4-6 it strikes — all units within 2D6" of the marker suffer 2D6 hits at the comet's current Strength with Lightning Attacks (ignore armour saves). All units hit suffer +2 stress. The marker cannot be dispelled once placed — the comet is already falling. The longer it hangs in the sky, the worse the impact. Armies must scatter or gamble. The flagship Heavens spell — devastating area output, persistent threat, undispellable — held at the high end of Advanced CV.

**Sapphire Arch**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 13 | 24" | Conveyance | Special |

Remove a friendly unit from the battlefield entirely — it passes through a celestial gateway into the space between stars. At the start of any subsequent friendly Magic phase, the caster may place the unit anywhere on the table more than 9" from any enemy model. If the caster dies before the unit returns, the unit is lost — the arch collapses and the travellers are stranded between worlds. CV held high in Advanced — whole-unit teleport is among the strongest non-damage effects in the system, dictating engagement on the caster's terms.

#### Master (Tier 4) — Lore Access 4+

**Lightning Storm**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 18 | 24" (self-radius) | Direct Damage | Instant |

All enemy units with at least one model within **24"** of the caster suffer **D3 hits at S 6, AP -1, with Lightning Attacks** (ignore armour saves). The caster opens the sky over the battlefield and a sustained electrical storm descends — every raised banner, lifted spear, and metal helm becomes a target. Apex crowd-clear; punishes tightly-massed enemy lines and stacks devastatingly with Comet of Casandora if both land. Differs from Storm of Cronos by Strength (S 6 vs S 4), AP (-1 vs 0), and bubble size (24" vs 18"); the Master version is a meaningfully larger and more penetrating storm. CV 18 reflects that any number of enemy units take damage simultaneously — at scale, more wounds than any single-target damage spell in the lore.

**Reading of the Stars** *(RIP, aura)*

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 16 | 18" (self-radius) | Augment (aura), Remains In Play |

*Remains In Play.* While in play, all friendly units wholly within **18"** of the caster may **reroll their Charge distance roll** when declaring a charge. The wizard reads the constellations and tells her allies the moment to commit — the heavens themselves whisper where the charge will land, and the army gallops forward in step with fate. Single-rule capstone, but charge consistency at army-wide scale is decisive — failed charges leave units stranded in front of enemy lines, and a persistent charge-reroll aura turns shaky 9-10" charges into reliable engagement. RIP makes the spell dispellable on the opponent's turn; until dispelled, every friendly charge from inside the bubble is effectively guaranteed within reasonable distance bands. Caster has to commit a position near the line for the aura to cover the units that need it most.

> **Design note on Lore of Heavens.** Range identity is **far reach** — Heavens lightning is summoned from the sky rather than thrown from the wizard's hand, so damage spells push to 30" (Urannon's, Chain Lightning) and the iconic Comet sits at 36". Bubble effects (Storm of Cronos, Lightning Storm, Reading of the Stars) trade range for radius and must commit a position. CV calibration after this pass: Sig 6 (must be reliable), Basic 7-9 with strong-effect spells (Iceshard, Urannon's) at the top of band, Intermediate 8-11 with the multi-axis hexes (Curse of Midnight Wind, Storm of Cronos) reaching higher, Advanced 12-13 across the board, Master 16-18. Master tier offers a binary capstone choice: **Lightning Storm** for offensive-bubble apex, **Reading of the Stars** for army-wide charge reliability. A Master-tier Heavens caster ideally takes both — they cover the offensive and command axes respectively. Lore Attribute (Astral Alignment) remains unchanged.
