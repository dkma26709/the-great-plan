# Men-at-Arms


> The duke's household guard — paid retainers stationed at the castle, drawn from peasant stock but kept on year-round retainer and drilled by the duchy's serjeants in spear and shield. They wear the duke's livery and a chainmail hauberk from the castle armoury, fight under the duchy's banners, and hold the line while the knights manoeuvre. Men-at-Arms are not the levied serfs of Longbowmen or Bidowers — they are professional soldiers of common birth, the chivalric realm's standing infantry. Empire State Troops at the rank-and-file tier, but with the chivalric class barrier intact: Men-at-Arms cannot stand in formation with the Knights they serve.

- **Keywords:** Bretonnia, Human, Infantry, Core, Peasant
- **Unit type:** Infantry
- **Unit size:** 15-40 (peasant-block scale — larger than Empire State Troops to reflect the duchies' tradition of fielding the household guard in numbers)
- **Points:** 5 per model
- **Natural Armour:** —
- **Worn armour (default, standard kit):** Light armour (chainmail hauberk from the castle armoury) → 6+ save
- **Worn armour:** No upgrade options. Medium and heavy armour are reserved to the noble caste; Men-at-Arms wear what the duke's armoury issues.
- **Equipment (default):** Hand weapon + light armour
- **Equipment options (replace hand weapon, pick at most one):**
  - **Spear** (+1 pt/model — replaces hand weapon, pairs with shield) — 2" reach | A 1 | S 3 | AP 0 | D 1
  - **Polearm (Bretonnian Bill)** (+2 pt/model — replaces hand weapon, pairs with shield) — 2" reach | A 1 | S+1 (= S 4) | AP -1 | D 1 — billhook, glaive, or peasant halberd; same profile as Empire Halberd
- **Additive equipment:**
  - **Shield** (+1 pt/model) — combines with light armour for 5+ save (per §8 Armour Save Stacking)
- **Command options:**
  - **Leader** (+5 pts) — *Sergeant-at-Arms*; a senior household soldier, often the duke's veteran armsman or a trusted commoner-officer
  - **Standard bearer** (+10 pts — Magic Standard up to 25 pts; the duchy's heraldic banner)
  - **Musician** (+5 pts) — +1 to recovery and Rally rolls (drummer or hornman of the duchy's household)
  - **Grail Monk** (+20 pts) — single-model upgrade; part of the unit's command group. For **Peasant's Duty** purposes, the Grail Monk himself counts as a friendly Bretonnia character with the **Knight's Vow** keyword — the unit treats Peasant's Duty as **always-on** (Inspiring Presence (6") from the Monk's position within the unit) regardless of nearby knight characters. The aura ends if the Grail Monk is slain (removed as a casualty per normal rules); the unit then reverts to needing an external vow-character within 6" for Peasant's Duty to fire
- **Special rules:**
  - **Peasant's Duty** *(identity rule)* — While any model of this unit is within **6"** of a friendly Bretonnia character with the **Knight's Vow**, **Crusader's Vow**, **Questing Vow**, **Grail Vow**, or **Lady's Chosen** keyword, that character grants this unit the effects of **Inspiring Presence (6")** (see §8 Resolve and Command) — +1 Res for thresholds, can rally without a Leader, can rally in combat, and may reroll recovery / rally rolls. The aura passes *from outside the unit only*.
  - **Class Barrier** *(clause of Peasant's Duty)* — A Bretonnian character with the **Knight's Vow**, **Crusader's Vow**, **Questing Vow**, **Grail Vow**, or **Lady's Chosen** keyword standing within the coherency of a unit with Peasant's Duty does **not** gain the Character Attachment Exception's shielding benefit (§6) — the character can still be singled out by enemy shooting and hostile magic as if standing alone. Knights are too distinct (heraldry, livery, warhorse, chivalric profile) to disappear into a peasant block. Peasant's Duty's Inspiring Presence aura is unaffected — it passes from proximity, not from concealment

**Weapon profiles (A on weapons):**

- **Hand weapon** *(default, 1H — pairs with shield)* — 1" reach | **A 1** | S 3 | AP 0 | D 1 — peasant arming sword, axe, or mace from the castle armoury `[1H Blade]`
- **Spear** *(option, 1H with shield)* — 2" reach | **A 1** | S 3 | AP 0 | D 1 — cheap reach weapon, no inherent strength bonus `[1H Reach]`
- **Polearm (Bretonnian Bill)** *(option, 1H with shield)* — 2" reach | **A 1** | S+1 (= S 4) | AP -1 | D 1 — same profile as Empire Halberd; one-handed peasant polearm `[1H Reach]`

> **Lore notes:** **Drilled professional household guard, not levied chaff peasants.** WAP / Old World data confirms WS 3 / BS 3 / I 3 — full parity with Empire State Troops on combat skill, despite peasant social class. The §17 +1 Res calibration brings WAP Res 6 → **Res 7**, one step below Empire Halberdier's Res 8 — the gap captures Bretonnia's chivalric culture (the duke invests less in the commoner caste than the Empire invests in its city militia, so even the household guard is less drilled than Empire State Troops). **Light armour as standard kit** mirrors WAP — Men-at-Arms come with chainmail by default; medium / heavy armour are reserved to the noble caste.
>
> **Peasant's Duty as Inspiring Presence (6") aura.** The WAP rule literally reads "treat friendly Chivalrous Vow models as having Inspiring Presence (6)"; this draft preserves that translation directly using our existing §8 Inspiring Presence machinery. The full IP package — +1 Res threshold, rally without Leader, rally in combat, reroll recovery / rally — fires whenever a vow-bearing Bretonnia character is within 6". Stacks with the army General's standard Inspiring Presence (12") within its 12" range. The army general is typically a vow-bearer anyway, so the meaningful upgrade is when *any* vow character (not just the General) confers the aura at 6".
>
> **Class Barrier** is the second clause of Peasant's Duty, adapted from the WAP source: "Characters with the Peasant's Duty may not join units that have a Chivalrous Vow." Our system has no formal "join" mechanic — characters stand within unit coherency under the Character Attachment Exception (§6), gaining a shielding benefit (cannot be singled out by enemy shooting / hostile magic). The Class Barrier *revokes that benefit* for Bretonnian vow-bearers standing near peasant units: a Lord, Paladin, Damsel, or Prophetess can still position himself within a Men-at-Arms block's coherency to grant the Peasant's Duty Inspiring Presence aura, but he loses the cover the unit would normally provide. The trade-off is real — proximity (for the aura) costs survivability (the character becomes sniper-vulnerable). Captures the chivalric / commoner social separation as tactical exposure rather than as a hard ban.
>
> **No great weapon option** — Bretonnian peasants don't carry two-handed greatswords. That's noble-caste equipment (Questing Knights, with their Questing Vow allowing greatswords as the chivalric exception). Men-at-Arms equipment is hand-weapon-and-shield, spear-and-shield, or bill-and-shield. The Bretonnian Bill (polearm option) carries the same profile as Empire Halberd — same weapon, different cultural framing.
>
> **Points 5 per model** — +1 over WAP base 4 to cover light armour as standard kit and the §17 Res bump. Cheaper per-model than Empire Halberdier (6 pts) — Halberdier comes with a halberd default (real weapon) but no armour; Men-at-Arms come with light armour default but only a hand weapon. Comparable kit cost; different default tradeoff. Maximum kit = 5 base + 1 shield + 2 polearm = **8 pts/model** for a polearm-and-shield Men-at-Arms with 5+ combined save.
>
> **Grail Monk** *(+20 pts, single-model command-group upgrade)* — a wandering Grail-quest pilgrim attached to the unit, bolstering peasant faith from within. Mechanically the Grail Monk counts as a friendly Bretonnia character with the Knight's Vow keyword for **Peasant's Duty purposes only** — the unit gains Inspiring Presence (6") from inside its own ranks, always-on regardless of nearby knight characters. The +20 pt price (vs WAP's +5) reflects how much this upgrade is worth in list-building: it strips away the "manage knight-aura proximity" tactical constraint that *defines* Bretonnian peasant doctrine. At +5 pts it would be auto-include on every peasant block; at +20 it's a real anchor-block investment, taken on the unit you most want to hold ground while the knights manoeuvre elsewhere. The Monk does **not** grant the unit Blessing of the Lady (peasants remain commoners; the Lady protects the noble caste, not the levy) — only the Inspiring Presence aura passes through. Position the Monk in the rear rank to keep him out of casualty-removal range; once slain, the always-on aura ends. The Class Barrier rule does **not** apply to the Grail Monk — he is part of the unit, not a character standing within it from outside.
>
> **No Blessing of the Lady** per §17 — peasants are commoners, no vow, no divine protection.
