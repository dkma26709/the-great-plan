# Carnosaur


> The apex predator. Everything in Lustria learned to fear the Carnosaur before it learned anything else. Fast for its size, terrifyingly strong jaws, and a predator's instinct to go for the throat. Where the Stegadon is a wall, the Carnosaur is a spear — a surgical killer that happens to be enormous. Ridden into battle only by Saurus Oldbloods and Saurus Scar-Veterans, whose own apex-saurian nature is the one thing a Carnosaur respects.

> **Lore notes:** Carnosaur is deliberately the mirror of the Stegadon — both Lord-tier Monsters but different combat niches. Stegadon = mass, slow, armoured, howdah platform (W 12, T 7, NA 3+, M 6). Carnosaur = precision, fast, surgical killer (W 10, T 5, NA 5+, M 10). **Apex Predator** is the identity rule — the Carnosaur's bite is significantly better against Monster-tier or W 4+ targets, encoding the lore that these creatures specifically hunt big prey and disdain common infantry. **Pursuit Predator** forces the Carnosaur into relentless follow-ups — a strength (guaranteed pressure on broken enemies) and a weakness (can be baited into overcommitment). **Cornered Predator** bracket at W 1-3 adds Frenzy — a dying Carnosaur is more dangerous than a healthy one, narratively perfect for the tyrannosaur-trope. Points +260 on top of the rider — the Carnosaur is the most expensive character mount, reflecting the W 10 Monster durability plus the three-weapon apex-predator profile plus the Monster-keyword identity rules. Combined Saurus Oldblood (260) + Carnosaur (+260) = 520 pts before disciplines/upgrades, a genuinely elite centrepiece. A-on-weapons convention applied; Monster US=W convention applied; fits the retrofitted-unit convention consistently with Stegadon / Bastiladon / Troglodon / Arcanadon / Dread Saurian drafted earlier.
