# Wyvern

> Two-legged flying reptile, voracious cousin of the dragons, scaled and toothed and tipped with a venomous tail-sting. Wyverns are the most highly prized mounts within a Greenskin army — only the most powerful Warbosses or Great Shamans have the ability and unnatural bond to bind these cruel beasts to their will. "No Orc would be so foolish as to try to tame a fully grown Wyvern." Adventurous Night Goblins raid Wyvern nests to steal eggs, which are traded or sold to Orcs for a steep price; hand-rearing a Wyvern to adulthood is dangerous, slow work, and the resulting bond is fraught — the Wyvern obeys its Orc rider but hungers always for fresh meat, and "would sometimes land right in the middle of an enemy unit and feast upon their flesh in the droves while its rider hacks at the enemy that are still left." The lore signature is twofold: **Crash-Landing Feast** *(the iconic Wyvern charge — slamming down into the middle of an enemy formation, biting at whatever's nearest in the chaos before the main attack lands)* and the **Poisoned Tail Sting** *(the canon-classic Wyvern signature — a venomous barb that strikes past front ranks at 3" reach, wounding even the toughest target on Poisoned + 1)*. Drafted here as an **apex Greenskin flying Lord-mount** (single profile, Monster Mount keyword, points-adder format) — the iconic Orc Warboss on Wyvern is the natural Lord-on-Monster expression for the faction, parallel to Saurus Oldblood on Carnosaur for Lizardmen and Bretonnian Lord on Royal Hippogryph for Bretonnia. The Greenskin battlefield read: fly the Wyvern over a screen, declare the charge into a deep formation, Crash-Landing-Feast on the nearest neighbour unit, then watch the Warboss carve into the primary target while the Wyvern's Tail Sting reaches over the front rank to grab whichever character is hiding in the second.

## Special Rules

- **Terror** *(Psychology — see §8)* — at LiS 4, the Terror aura projects to **(6 + 4)" = 10"**. A two-legged scaled reptile with leathery wings, a tail tipped with venom, and a mouth full of fangs registers as terror on any battlefield — even Orcs find the fully-grown Wyvern intimidating (Night Goblin egg-raiders are the only Greenskins who'll go near a wild nest).
- **Fly (8")** *(per §8)* — the Wyvern moves 8" using its leathery wings, ignoring all terrain and intervening models. May Fly-charge: charge distance = 8" + D6 (Formed) or 8" + 2D6 (Loose — characters on monster mounts are typically Loose). Fly 8 is one tier below "true flyer" speeds (Fly 10-14) — the Wyvern is a heavy-bodied flapping flier, not a darting Pegasus or a soaring Dragon. Reflects the lore "voracious beasts" that **land** in the middle of enemy formations rather than skim over them.
- **Crash-Landing Feast** *(Wyvern unit identity rule — charge-only AoE bystander damage)* — When this model **successfully completes a charge**, immediately before resolving the main combat attacks against the charge target, the Wyvern may select **one enemy unit with at least one model within 1" of the charge target's footprint** (i.e., a unit adjacent to or in melee with the charge target). The Wyvern makes **D3 free Bite-and-Claws attacks** against the selected unit (use the Bite-and-Claws weapon profile below: S 5 / AP -1 / D 1 per attack — note **D 1** not D D3 for these bonus attacks, as the lore-frame is the Wyvern *biting* on landing, not pinning-and-crushing). Resolve these attacks fully (hit / wound / save) before the main charge attacks. The selected unit does **NOT** enter combat with the Wyvern from Crash-Landing Feast alone (no melee engagement, no retaliation, no CR contribution to the main combat); the wounds inflicted are simply applied per standard wound allocation, and stress from wounds taken accrues per §5 normally. If no enemy unit is within 1" of the charge target, Crash-Landing Feast does not trigger. **Lore frame:** "The Wyvern would sometimes land right in the middle of an enemy unit and feast upon their flesh in the droves while its rider hacks at the enemy that are still left." The crash-and-feed lands on whoever's nearest in the formation chaos.

## Weapon Profiles

### Close Combat

- **Bite-and-Claws** *(Wyvern natural — fanged maw + clawed forelimbs)* — **1" reach | A 3 | S 5 | AP -1 | D D3** — the primary Wyvern attack. Three crushing bites and claw-rakes per combat round. **S 5 / AP -1** wounds T 3 on 2+, T 4 on 3+, T 5 on 4+; AP -1 strips 6+ light armour entirely and 4+ heavy to 5+. **D D3** delivers 1-3 wounds per unsaved wound (average 2) — every successful bite is a meaningful damage spike. `[Natural]`
- **Poisoned Tail Sting** *(Wyvern natural — long venomous tail-barb)* — **3" reach | A 1 | S 4 | AP 0 | D 1 | Poisoned Attacks** (per §8 — reroll to-Wound... actually per §8 *Poisoned Attacks* gives **+1 to-Wound**) — the canon Wyvern signature. **3" reach** allows the sting to strike second-rank models or characters behind a bodyguard line (parallel to Carnosaur's Sweeping Tail, but front-arc here rather than rear). **A 1** — one precise strike per round, the tail uncoils and snaps. **S 4** is one step below the Wyvern's base S 5 (the venom does the killing work, not the tail-force itself). **Poisoned Attacks** — reflects the Wyvern's venomous-barb biology, +1 to-Wound. Against T 5 (e.g., Bretonnian Lord on Royal Hippogryph), the Tail Sting wounds on 4+ instead of 5+; against T 6 (e.g., Vampire Lord, Dwarf Lord on Shieldbearers), wounds on 5+ instead of 6+. The Sting is the Wyvern's *anti-character / anti-high-T-target* weapon — pick its target each Combat phase by allocating the 1 Tail attack to the toughest enemy in reach. `[Natural]`
