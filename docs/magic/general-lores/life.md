# Lore of Life


> Healing, growth, and the slow power of the wilds. Where Fire destroys and Light protects, Life **restores**. It is the only lore that mends physical wounds, the only lore that creates permanent terrain, and deliberately the quietest lore — one damage spell, zero debuffs, no morale effects. A Life wizard is not a battlefield killer. They are what keeps an army standing through a long war.

**Lore Attribute — Overgrowth:** when this wizard successfully casts a Life spell, one friendly unit within **12"** of the caster immediately restores **1 wound** (to a wounded model first; if all models are at full wounds, the excess is wasted). The magic's surplus life-force spills into the surrounding ground and nearby allies — every cast leaves the battlefield slightly more alive.

**Signature Spell — Regrowth**

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 7 | 18" | Augment, Instant |

Target friendly unit restores **D3 wounds**. Apply to wounded models first (restore lost W on multi-wound models); excess wounds bring back slain models if any (single-wound models return to the unit in base contact with the formation). Never restores more than was originally lost.

#### Basic (Tier 1) — Lore Access 1+

**Awakening of the Wood**

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 7 | 18" | Magic Missile |

**D6 hits at S4, AP -1, D1.** If any model in the target unit is inside a forest or wood terrain feature, the spell rolls **2D6 hits** instead of D6. The forest itself lashes out at those sheltered within it — sanctuary turns to predator. The lore's only damage spell.

**Flesh to Stone**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 6 | 18" | Augment | Until start of next turn |

Target friendly unit gains **+1 Toughness** until start of next turn. Skin hardens toward bark or stone — the body made firm against harm.

**Earth Blood**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 6 | 18" | Augment | Until start of next turn |

Target friendly unit gains **Regeneration (5+)** until start of next turn. The veins of the earth pulse through them — wounds close as they open.

**Shield of Thorns** *(RIP)*

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 7 | 18" | Augment, Remains In Play |

While this spell is in play, whenever an enemy model makes a melee attack against the target unit and the to-hit roll misses (modified or unmodified fail), the attacking model immediately suffers **1 hit at S3, AP 0, D1**. Every clumsy strike impales itself on the thorns that grow from the target's skin. Rewards agile defenders — the more enemies miss, the more they hurt themselves.

**Leaf Fall**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 7 | 18" | Augment | Until start of next turn |

Target friendly unit gains **-1 to hit against all incoming ranged attacks** until start of next turn. The air around them fills with drifting leaves, seeds, and living mist — the attacker's line breaks up before the shot lands. A cast-triggered defensive shield distinct from Loose formation's Dispersed: works regardless of the unit's arrangement.

#### Intermediate (Tier 2) — Lore Access 2+

**Throne of Vines** *(RIP)*

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 8 | Self | Augment, Remains In Play |

The caster is rooted to the ground — while this spell is in play, the caster **cannot move** (Advance, March, Charge, Wheel, voluntary Flee, or Elusive's reactive move all denied; Rally is permitted). In exchange, the caster gains **+1 to all Life casting rolls and +1 to all dispel attempts**. A deliberate trade — immobility for magical dominance.

**Choking Vines**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 7 | 24" | Hex | Until start of next turn |

Target enemy unit's **Movement is reduced by D3** (minimum M 1) until start of next turn. Phantom roots and briars tangle their feet.

**Awaken the Wild** *(Summoning)*

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 10 | 24" | Terrain, Summoning |

Place a **forest terrain feature of up to 6" diameter** within range. The placement may not overlap existing terrain or models at the moment of placement. The forest is treated as a standard forest for all rules — blocks line of sight, interacts with Strider, qualifies for Awakening of the Wood's in-wood bonus, etc. Per the Summoning rules (§7.3), this spell is **permanent after cast** and cannot be dispelled once the cast-turn reaction has passed. Reshapes the battlefield for the rest of the game.

**Cloak of Dain**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 9 | 18" | Augment | Until start of next turn |

Target friendly unit **ignores the first D3 hits** from any source (melee, ranged, Impact, spells) in **each phase** until start of next turn. Roll the D3 once when the spell is cast; the same number applies to each phase this turn (Shooting, Combat, and any others). The caster thickens the unit's living presence — the first strikes of each exchange slide off before any save is rolled. A damage-cap, not a save — distinct from wards.

#### Advanced (Tier 3) — Lore Access 3+

**The Bloom**

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 11 | Self | Augment, Instant |

Each friendly unit with any model within **12"** of the caster restores **D3 wounds** each. Apply as per Regrowth — wounded models first, then resurrect slain. A verdant surge across the army — the wizard becomes a moment of collective healing.

**Full Bloom**

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 11 | 18" | Augment, Instant |

Target friendly unit restores **D6+3 wounds**. Wounds restore to wounded models first, then resurrect slain models (returning to the unit in base contact with the formation). Never restores more than was lost. A single-target mass recovery — a crumbling hero's unit brought back from the brink, or a heavily-wounded monster character pulled out of mortal danger.

**Paranoth's Pathway** *(Conveyance)*

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 11 | 18" | Conveyance |

Remove a friendly unit from the battlefield — it passes through the living root-network of the world, dissolving into nearby growth. At the start of any subsequent friendly Magic phase (including the one immediately following the cast), the caster may place the unit anywhere on the battlefield more than **9" from any enemy model**. The unit arrives in a legal formation and **may not declare a charge on the turn it arrives**. If the caster dies before the unit returns, the unit is lost — the root-paths collapse and the travellers remain within the soil.

Uniquely Life-thematic — organic teleportation through the plant network of the world, distinct from Heavens' Sapphire Arch (which is a celestial gateway between stars). Pairs naturally with Awaken the Wild — you can emerge near or within a summoned forest.

> **Design note on Life.** Life is deliberately the *quietest* lore. One damage spell (Awakening of the Wood), no stat debuffs, no morale effects. What Life does that no other lore does: physical wound restoration, permanent terrain creation, and nature-themed utility (Cloak of Dain's damage cap, Leaf Fall's anti-shooting, Paranoth's organic teleport). A Life wizard keeps an army standing through sustained pressure rather than winning phases directly.

#### Master (Tier 4) — Lore Access 4+

**The Great Regrowth**

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 14 | 18" | Augment |

Target friendly unit:
- **Restore D3+3 slain models** to the unit (up to the unit's starting size). Restored models are placed in base contact with surviving models and must satisfy coherency and friendly-unit separation rules; any that cannot be legally placed are not restored (the spell has no effect beyond what can be positioned legally).
- Additionally, until the start of the caster's next Magic phase, all models in the target unit gain **Regeneration (5+)** (see §8).

For **characters or single models** (where the "restore slain models" clause cannot apply), the spell instead restores **D3 wounds** to the target's W pool. Tree-bark knits flesh, moss closes over scar tissue, the fallen stand up again. Life's capstone restoration — the ruined unit is rebuilt from the ground out, and the survivors are covered in thickening bark-skin for the coming turn.
