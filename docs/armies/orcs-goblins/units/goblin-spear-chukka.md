# Goblin Spear Chukka

> A wooden bolt thrower of crude Goblin construction, hauled onto the battlefield by a pair of Goblin engineers (or "the dim ones who didn't run") and aimed at whatever the unit Boss is pointing at. The Spear Chukka launches a long iron-tipped wooden bolt in a flat trajectory, capable of impaling the largest creatures and pinning multiple ranks of infantry with a single shot. **S 5 / AP -3 / D D3** — wounds T 4 on 3+, strips heavy armour, and inflicts multiple wounds per hit (devastating against Monsters, Heavy Cavalry, and Lord-tier characters). The trade-off is the usual Goblin engineering quality: 36" range with line-of-sight required (no indirect fire), and a meaningful Misfire rate from cracked timber, loose rope, and Goblin crew distraction. The Spear Chukka is the cheapest war machine in the Greenskin roster, a 40-pt anti-Monster sniper that a Greenskin Warboss fields in pairs to handle problems his Trolls and Boar Boyz can't reach. Goblin crew at BS 3 hits a stationary target on 4+; the Misfire-on-natural-1 risk means roughly 1-in-6 shots produces nothing or worse.

## Special Rules

- Undisciplined
- **War Machine** *(unit type — see §14 convention)* — single-profile unit with dual T. Ranged incoming: T 6. Melee incoming: T 3. W is a wound pool — wounds accumulate but do not reduce combat output, melee A, or fire rate. The Spear Chukka fires at full effectiveness from W=full all the way down to W=1; the engine goes silent only at W=0 (destroyed). Can be charged normally. Cannot itself Charge.
- **No Animosity** *(War Machine exception to the Greenskin-wide faction trait)*. The Spear Chukka does **not** roll Animosity. The faction-wide trait requires movement actions (force-charge nearest enemy on a 1-3 sub-roll) that the unit cannot perform — and the Crew, when not firing the machine, are too physically engaged with the loading-and-aiming process to squabble. The lore-fit framing: the Crew are *busy*, and Animosity is a *bored Greenskin* problem. The §8 Animosity rule's "applies to all Greenskin units" line is qualified here at the unit-profile level for the War-Machine sub-category specifically.
- **Crew attack in melee** — Crew fight with hand weapons at unified MA 2 MD 3, I 3, **A 2** baseline. Melee A is fixed and does not scale with remaining wounds. BS 3 (Goblin baseline) — affects to-hit rolls for the bolt thrower mechanic.
- **Stab in the Back** *(Goblin-typical, applies to Crew melee attacks only)*. +1 to Wound against rear-arc *(melee only)* or Shaken/Wavering targets. Does not apply to Bolt Thrower ranged shots (the Bolt Thrower hits whatever the player points it at, regardless of target's stress state — the rule is about Goblin opportunism, and a heavy bolt-thrower isn't opportunistic, it's heavy).
- **No Cowardly Numbers / No Loves a Scrap / No Mushroom Madness** *(War Machine exception)*. The Spear Chukka is a single-model unit with Crew embedded in the platform — Cowardly Numbers Stubborn / -1 Res clauses don't trigger meaningfully (the unit can never have 20+ models). Loves a Scrap is Orc-only. Mushroom Madness is Night Goblin sub-tribe; Spear Chukka Crew are Common Goblins by default.

## Options

**Worn armour options:** None.

**Crew upgrade options:**
- **Bigger Bolts** (+5 pts) — the Crew load oversized iron-tipped spears. The Spear Chukka's ranged profile becomes **S 6** instead of S 5 (wounds T 5 on 3+, T 6 on 4+), and gains the **Bolt Sticks Through** rule: on a successful hit and Wound, if the target unit has additional models *directly behind* the wounded model (in the same rank-and-file alignment), the bolt continues into the next model and deals **1 additional Wound at S 4 AP -2 D 1** to that next model. Repeats for as many models as are aligned, up to a maximum of D6 pierce-through hits in total. Captures the iconic "spear nails three Goblins in a row" lore moment.

**No command options.** War Machines do not take champions, standards, or musicians.

## Weapon Profiles

### Close Combat

- **Hand Weapon** *(Crew melee)* — 1" reach | **A 2** | S 3 | AP 0 | D 1 — fixed melee attack count, does not decrement with wounds `[1H Blade]`

### Ranged

**Bolt Thrower mechanic** *(new ranged weapon rule — Goblin Spear Chukka and similar direct-fire siege engines)*:

1. Declare a target model within the Spear Chukka's range (12-36") and forward 90° arc. The target model must be **visible to the Crew** (line-of-sight required; this is direct fire, not indirect).
2. Roll **D6 to hit** using the Crew's BS (3). Hits on **4+** (baseline 4+ with no modifiers; -1 for moving target, -1 for target in cover, etc., per standard §7 to-hit modifiers).
3. **On a natural to-hit roll of 1, the Spear Chukka Misfires.** No shot fired this phase. Roll **D6** on the Spear Chukka Misfire Table:
   - **1** — Catastrophic timber crack. Spear Chukka destroyed. The unit is removed (all remaining wounds lost).
   - **2-3** — Rope snapped (struck by snapping cord). Cannot fire next Shooting phase. **D3 wounds** inflicted on the unit (no Ward, no Armour).
   - **4-5** — Bolt jammed (loose timber, bolt misaligned). Cannot fire next Shooting phase. No wounds taken.
   - **6** — Close call (string slipped, shot wasted but no damage). Spear Chukka operable next phase; no wounds taken.
4. On a hit, resolve damage on the targeted model: **S 5 | AP -3 | D D3 (Multiple Wounds D3)** — the bolt punches through armour and inflicts D3 wounds in a single strike. The Multiple Wounds mechanic means each successful unsaved wound inflicts D3 wounds, with the standard MW cap — excess wounds beyond what the target can absorb are wasted. Lethal vs Monsters, Lords on Monstrous Mounts, and any multi-Wound target.
5. **Bolt Sticks Through** *(if Bigger Bolts upgrade taken)* — see Options.

**Per-Shooting-phase limit:** One shot per Shooting phase. Crew must be alive (W > 0). The Spear Chukka may not fire on the turn it has moved (any non-zero distance) — repositioning the machine takes the full phase.

- **Goblin Spear Chukka** *(ranged, direct-fire bolt thrower; Crew fire once per Shooting phase)* — **12-36" range** | **Direct Fire** (line-of-sight required) | **S 5** (or **S 6** with Bigger Bolts upgrade) | **AP -3** | **D D3 (Multiple Wounds D3)** | Misfire on natural to-hit 1 — see Bolt Thrower mechanic above for full resolution.
