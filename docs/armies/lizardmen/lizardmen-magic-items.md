# Lizardmen Magic Items


> The Lizardmen do not forge magic items in the human or chivalric sense — there are no rune-smiths, no enchanters' guilds, no celestite forge-houses producing weapons for the open market. Lizardmen items are **artefacts of the Old Ones** — celestite-blade weapons hammered into shape during the spawning of a particular Saurus, glyph-talismans inscribed at the Slann's instruction with prophecy that has not yet come to pass, plaques of jade carved with the cosmic geometry the Slann communes with at meditation. Each item is *singular*; each item exists for a specific pre-ordained purpose, awakened the moment its owner steps onto the battlefield where the Old Ones' designs require it. There is no second copy. The Lizardmen list is therefore *small but consequential* — what an Empire armoury would call "exquisite," the Slann would call "necessary."

Items follow the §11.5 framework: six categories, slot discipline (one item per category per character), Lord 100 / Sub-Lord 75 / Hero 50 budgets, item costs are flat and unscaled. Per-item restrictions ("Slann only," "Saurus character only," etc.) appear inline in the Effect column.

#### Magic Armour

| Item | Effect | Pts |
|---|---|---|
| **Sacred Stegadon Helm of Itza** | **Saurus character or Skink Chief / Skink High Chief, on foot only.** Counts as Light Armour (one step) for §8 Armour Save Stacking — combines with worn armour and shield per the standard rule. Grants the wearer **+1 T** and **Impact Hits (D3)** (see §8) on a successful charge (Impact Hits only fire when the wearer is on foot — a mounted character's mount-profile impact contribution is unaffected and does not stack). Fashioned from the bone-crest of a great Stegadon offered at the temple of Itza; the wearer charges with a head-down ram-strike | 40 |
| **Coronal Shield** | **Any character with a shield equipment option** (Saurus Oldblood / Scar-Veteran / Sunblood / Astrolith Bearer / Skink Chief / Skink High Chief / Skink Priest / Skink High Priest). Occupies the shield slot — replaces the character's mundane shield in play (per §11.5 magic-item precedence). Counts as Heavy Shield for §8 Armour Save Stacking (one step toward the 2+ cap). All enemy melee attacks targeting the bearer must **re-roll successful To Hit rolls**. The shield's celestite-mosaic catches the light and confounds the eye of the striker | 40 |

#### Talismans

| Item | Effect | Pts |
|---|---|---|
| **Aura of Quetzl** | The bearer gains a **Ward Save (4+)** against attacks of **Strength 5 or higher**. Lesser blows pass through unimpeded; Quetzl's hand turns aside only the killing strikes. (Strength-variable Ward pattern parallel to Bretonnian Blessing of the Lady, §17) | 30 |
| **Glyph Necklace** | All enemy melee attacks targeting the bearer suffer **-1 To Hit**. A string of jade discs carved with sigils of the Old Ones' geometry — the wearer's silhouette wavers in the eye of any who strikes at him | 35 |
| **Amulet of the Old Ones** | **Slann Mage-Priest only.** When this Slann would be reduced to 0 Wounds, instead the amulet activates: the Slann is **teleported to safety** — placed anywhere in the controlling player's deployment zone (more than 12" from any enemy, in a legal position), retaining **1 W** remaining. **Once per battle.** Captures the canonical "Slann are very rarely slain in combat — they magically teleport themselves out of danger before a killing blow is landed" detail | 20 |

#### Arcane Items

| Item | Effect | Pts |
|---|---|---|
| **Cupped Hands of the Old Ones** | **Slann Mage-Priest only. Once per battle.** The bearer may use this item in one of the following two ways (choice made at the moment the trigger occurs); once either effect has been used, the item is expended for the rest of the battle. (a) **Self-deflection** — when this Slann would suffer a Miscast (per §7.3), roll a D6: on a **2+** the Miscast is ignored entirely (the spell still resolves normally); on a 1 the Miscast occurs as normal. (b) **Reflection onto the enemy** — when an enemy Wizard within line of sight of this Slann suffers a Miscast, force that Wizard to reroll the Miscast Table result, taking the **worse** of the two outcomes. The Slann catches the chaos in cupped hands — either to spare himself, or to offer it back to the offending caster | 30 |
| **Diadem of Power** | **Wizard only.** Once per Magic phase, the bearer may shift **one die** from their personal Cast pool into their personal Dispel pool at any point during the phase. The shifted die is spent or lost with the rest of the pool at phase end (per §7.3 — unused dice are lost). A circlet of jade discs that holds the spent Winds for an instant longer, releasing them as the enemy's spell takes shape | 15 |

#### Enchanted Items

| Item | Effect | Pts |
|---|---|---|
| **The Cloak of Feathers** | **Skink character on foot only.** The cloak grants the wearer **Fly (8)** (see §8) and **Magic Resistance (1)** (see §8). Woven from the iridescent feathers of the Coatl, the serpent-bird emissaries of the Old Ones — the wearer is borne aloft on shimmering wings, half-shielded from hostile magic | 20 |
| **Carnosaur Pendant** | **Saurus Oldblood or Saurus Scar-Veteran only.** Once the bearer's attacks inflict one or more unsaved wounds in a combat phase, the bearer gains the **Frenzy** special rule (see §8) for the rest of the battle. This Frenzy **cannot be lost** — not by losing a combat round, nor by any other means that would ordinarily strip Frenzy. (The same biological-Frenzy mechanic active on Aggradon Lancers' mounts and the Wild Carnosaur's Bloodlust rule; per §1 Rider/Mount conventions, the rider's Frenzy state is independent of any mount's Frenzy state — both can be active simultaneously, each applying to its own profile's attacks.) A single tooth of a slain Carnosaur, blessed at the temple of the Old Ones — the wearer carries the predator's hunger | 10 |
