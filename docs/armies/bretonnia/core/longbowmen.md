# Longbowmen


> The duchy's longbow tradition — peasant levies trained from boyhood at the village butts, mustered for war and given a longbow and a quiver from the castle armoury. The Bretonnian longbow is not the Empire's gunpowder weapon, but it is *cheap*, *plentiful*, and rains arrows in volume from behind the duchy's lines. Longbowmen typically deploy in a second rank — Men-at-Arms in front, Longbowmen behind, knights on the wings — and loose volleys *over* the front rank's shoulders into approaching enemy formations. Less fashionable than Empire gunpowder, but a peasant boy with a bow costs the duke nothing — and at scale the difference is told in massed arrow-fall, not in a few crackling muskets.

- **Keywords:** Bretonnia, Human, Infantry, Core, Peasant
- **Unit type:** Infantry
- **Unit size:** 10-30
- **Points:** 7 per model
- **Natural Armour:** —
- **Worn armour (default):** — (village levies issued no body armour by default)
- **Equipment (default):** Hand weapon + Longbow
- **Worn armour options:**
  - **Light armour** (+0.5 pt/model) → 6+ save (a quilted jack or chainmail shirt from the castle armoury)
- **Additive equipment options:**
  - **Buckler** (+0.5 pt/model) → grants **Parry (6+)** in melee (see §8 Parry). Small enough not to interfere with longbow draw — Bretonnian buckler-and-bow is a known peasant kit, distinct from the larger shield carried by Men-at-Arms
  - **Braziers** (+0.5 pt/model, whole unit) → the unit's longbows gain **Flaming Attacks** for the duration of the battle. Pitch-soaked arrowheads ignited from a brazier or torch in the unit's deployment area
  - **Defensive Stakes** (+40 pts flat, whole-unit upgrade) — see special rules below
- **Command options:**
  - **Leader** (+5 pts) — *Bowmaster*; a senior peasant respected for his arrow-craft, often a ducal forester or a veteran of past musters
  - **Standard bearer** (+10 pts — Magic Standard up to 25 pts; the duchy's longbow-tradition banner)
  - **Musician** (+5 pts) — +1 to recovery and Rally rolls (drum or hornman of the village muster)
- **Special rules:**
  - **Peasant's Duty** *(identity rule)* — as Men-at-Arms; while any model is within **6"** of a friendly Bretonnia character with the **Knight's Vow**, **Crusader's Vow**, **Questing Vow**, **Grail Vow**, or **Lady's Chosen** keyword, that character grants this unit the effects of **Inspiring Presence (6")** (see §8 Resolve and Command). Aura passes from outside the unit only
  - **Class Barrier** *(clause of Peasant's Duty)* — a Bretonnian character with the **Knight's Vow / Questing Vow / Lady's Chosen / Crusader's Vow / Grail Vow** keyword standing within the coherency of this unit does **not** gain the Character Attachment Exception's shielding (§6) — the character can still be singled out by enemy shooting and hostile magic. Knights are too distinct to disappear into a peasant block; peasant proximity grants the aura but not concealment
  - **Defensive Stakes** *(option, +40 pts flat, whole-unit upgrade)* — when this unit deploys at the start of the game within its deployment zone, it places stake markers in a band immediately in front of every front-rank model. The staked area counts as **Dangerous Terrain (Stakes)** (see §6 Terrain — Dangerous Terrain) — each enemy model that **Marches into or Charges through** the staked band must take a Dangerous Terrain Test (D6, on a 1 the model takes 1 wound with no saves allowed). The Stakes terrain type means **Strider (Stakes)** units bypass the test; **Fly** likewise bypasses the stakes (the flier passes over the ground feature). **Advance** moves do not trigger the test — careful movement avoids the stakes. The stakes are removed (and the terrain feature ends) the moment this unit makes any **voluntary movement**. Stakes do not move with the unit. Reform-in-place does not count as movement; advancing, marching, charging, falling back, or fleeing all do

**Weapon profiles (A on weapons for melee; ranged weapons per §10 Armoury convention — no S listed for wielder-powered weapons):**

- **Hand weapon** *(melee, 1H — pairs with buckler if taken)* — 1" reach | **A 1** | S 3 | AP 0 | D 1 — peasant arming sword, hatchet, or knife from the castle armoury `[1H Blade]`
- **Longbow** *(default ranged)* — **30" range | AP 0 | D 1 | Volley Fire** (see §8) — the iconic Bretonnian peasant longbow. Range above the standard 24" bow tier, capturing the longbow's traditional reach advantage. S follows wielder (S 3); no AP. Volume over precision — Bretonnian doctrine is mass volley, not Hochland-style marksmanship. With **Braziers** taken, gains **Flaming Attacks**

> **Lore notes:** **Stats follow §17 peasant-levy calibration** — WS 2 / **BS 2** / S 3 / T 3 / I 3 (peasant levy baseline; village levy, not professional soldiery), **Res 6** (WAP 5 + 1 per §17 calibration; one step below Men-at-Arms Res 7, which captures the village-levy vs household-guard distinction within the peasant class). **WS 2** marks them as poor in melee — they are shooters, not fighters. **BS 2** captures "trained at the village butts but not professionally drilled" — they shoot in volume, not with precision. One BS step below Men-at-Arms (BS 3, drilled household guard) and Empire State Troops (BS 3, paid militia). The +1 Res bump from WAP 5 → 6 keeps them just functional in our stress system (Broken at 6 stress); without the Class Barrier's escort dependency they would be perpetual rout-risk.
>
> **Longbow at 30" range** captures the longbow's traditional reach vs the standard 24" bow tier (hunting-bow / shortbow / crossbow). Per the §10 Armoury / ranged convention, S is omitted for wielder-powered bows. **No AP** is the headline limitation — the bow rains arrows but cuts no armour. Makes Longbowmen good against light troops, weak against knights, Greatswords, or any armoured target. Their tactical value is *volume of fire from a deep formation*, not *armour-piercing precision*. Empire Handgunner (S 4 AP -2) is the comparison: handgun is anti-armour quality at 10 pts/model; longbow is cheap volume at 7 pts/model.
>
> **Volley Fire** is the longbow's defining keyword. Per the updated §8 Volley Fire rule, models in a deep-formation Longbowmen unit can shoot rear-rank arrows over their own front rank at -1 to hit. This makes a 3-deep Longbowmen block functional — front rank shoots clear, rear ranks volley over at -1 to hit. Pairs naturally with the Bretonnian battle-line: Men-at-Arms in front (or another Longbowmen unit in front), Longbowmen volleying behind. Without Volley Fire, only the front rank could meaningfully shoot — wasting deep formations.
>
> **Defensive Stakes** is the iconic Agincourt-style anti-cavalry mechanic: peasants plant sharpened stakes in front of their position before the battle, breaking enemy charges. Translated to our system as a **Dangerous Terrain (Stakes)** feature placed in front of the unit at deployment (per §6 Terrain). Per-model test: each enemy model that Marches into or Charges through the stakes rolls D6, on a 1 the model takes a wound with no saves allowed. A 6-model Knight unit charging through the stakes makes 6 tests, expecting ~1 wound; a 12-model Knight unit makes 12 tests, expecting ~2 wounds. Modest deterrent on its own; meaningful when stacked across multiple units charging the staked line, and especially valuable against Monsters / single-model Heroes (one model = one test, so a Hero charging through stakes has a 1-in-6 chance of suffering a wound that bypasses every save he has).
>
> **Pricing: +40 pts flat (whole-unit upgrade), not per-model.** Reflects the list-building investment framing — a Longbowmen block with stakes is committing to a fixed defensive position, sacrificing positional flexibility for the anti-cavalry shield. The flat cost discourages staking small (10-strong) units (4 pts/model effective) and encourages large staked blocks (1.3 pts/model effective at 30 strong) — lore-thematic, the bigger the peasant block, the wider the stake-line, the more arrows-per-stake-purchase. A 20-strong staked Longbowmen block becomes a 140 + 40 = 180-pt anchor, immobile but stake-defended. Stakes-as-terrain also forward-compatibilises with future factions: any unit type the player wants to give stakes (Wood Elves, Dwarfs) inherits the same +40 mechanic without redefinition.
>
> **Buckler** as a +0.5 pt option grants **Parry (6+)** — a small ward against melee attacks only, useful when the line collapses and the longbowmen are forced into hand-to-hand combat. Iconic for the Bretonnian "buckler-and-bow" peasant archer. Doesn't interact with the longbow's two-handed draw — the buckler is small enough to not interfere; the model can shoot with both hands on the bow, then fight with hand weapon and buckler in melee. Distinct from a full shield (which Men-at-Arms get): the buckler grants a Parry ward, not an armour-stacking step.
>
> **Braziers** at +0.5 pt/model give the longbow Flaming Attacks for the battle. Useful against Regeneration-keyword enemies (Trolls, Treemen) and Undead (some interpretations of Flaming hurt Undead extra; check the Undead keyword text in §8). Cheap upgrade with niche but real value — depending on opponent, taking Braziers is matchup-decisive.
>
> **Points 7 per model** — +1 over Men-at-Arms' 5 base, +1 over WAP's 6 (the systematic peasant +1 calibration). 3 pts cheaper than Empire Handgunner (10) — Handgun is S 4 AP -2 anti-armour vs Longbow S 3 AP 0 volume; the gap reflects the lore (Bretonnia's longbow is *quantity* and *cheap*, the Empire's handgun is *quality* and *expensive*). Per-model upgrades (light armour / buckler / Braziers) total +1.5 pts/model max; Defensive Stakes is a flat +40 pts whole-unit. A fully-kitted 20-strong block: 20 × 8.5 + 40 = **210 pts** (10.5 pts/model effective). At minimum (20 × 7 = 140 pts) Longbowmen are the cheap-shooting backbone; fully kitted with stakes they become a 200+ pt fixed-position anchor.
