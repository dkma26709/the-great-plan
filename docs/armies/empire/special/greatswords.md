# Greatswords


> Elite State Troops — the Empire's *standing* army, paid career soldiers rather than conscript militia. Drawn from veteran Halberdiers who've survived enough campaigns to be trusted with the two-handed greatsword. Stubborn, heavily armoured, Res 9 (exceptional for human infantry). In lore, Greatswords are held in reserve — State Troops hold the front, Handgunners thin the approach, Knights break the flank, and Greatswords counter-charge whatever's punched through the centre. When the line cracks, Greatswords are what the Elector Count sends in.

**Heft calibration:** WS 4 / I 4 inherited from WFB — Greatswords are genuinely trained soldiers, not militia. **Res 9 → 10** (pass 2, 2026-04-30): the State Troops baseline shifted to Res 9, and Greatswords sit one tier above (matching the Imperial Knight + Inner Circle elite-tier convention at Res 10). Captures the lore-canonical "exceptional for human infantry" framing — Greatswords are the Empire's pinnacle of mortal-veteran morale resilience. All other stats unchanged from reference.

Paid professionals who know how to regroup without command handholding. Supersedes the conditional State Troops rule on baseline Halberdiers / Handgunners — Greatswords have the trigger built in

> **Lore notes:** The Empire's *standing* army — paid career soldiers, drawn from veterans trusted with the two-handed greatsword. Held in reserve to counter-charge whatever's punched through the centre; when the line cracks, Greatswords are what the Elector Count sends in. Res 10 matches Imperial Knights and elite-tier characters — exceptional for human infantry, the pinnacle of mortal-veteran morale resilience. **Stubborn** + **Professional Soldiers** together make a genuine anvil-that-counter-punches: absorb sustained pressure (Stubborn ignores Shaken / Wavering), recover reliably (auto-rerolls regardless of General / BSB proximity — paid professionals don't need command handholding), and hit back with S 5 / AP -2 great weapons. The "Professional Soldiers" identity rule is the differentiator from rank-and-file State Troops, who need a friendly General / BSB within 6" for the same reroll.
