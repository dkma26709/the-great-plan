# Swordmasters of Hoeth

> Warrior-scholars of the White Tower. Cult-trained at Hoeth, the Asur god of wisdom — every Swordmaster has spent decades or centuries under the Loremasters, learning the thirty basic exercises of the order before progressing to forms that "would be impossible without decades of training and a mastery of one's own body." Founded by Phoenix King Bel-Korhadris to guard the Tower's scholars; weaponised by Aethis against the Cults of Excess and their Slaaneshi corruption — a secret war that continues to this day. When Ulthuan marches to war, none go more eagerly, "for only in war can they truly unleash the full extent of their deadly art." The Hoeth-forged ithilmar greatsword is balanced to perfect lightness despite being as long as an elf is tall, its edge never dulling, its enchantments enduring through helms and skulls alike. The cult vow: *On the last shore, a sword in my hand. Ulthuan shall never fall.* **MA / MD 7** and **I 7** mark the apex of non-character melee skill in the ruleset — Swordmasters are the best swordsmen alive, individually superior to almost anything they will face. **The Swordmaster identity is precision, not brute force.** A sword — even an ithilmar one — does not shred full plate; it *finds the gap*. The Hoeth-Forged Greatsword expresses this directly: low armour penetration (AP -1) but devastating depth (D 2) when the cut lands, and on the perfect strike (natural 6 to-Wound) **Touch of the Master** delivers 4 damage in a single blow — enough to one-shot a Lord-tier W ≤ 4 character. Lore-true: "the greatest masters of the order can slay a foe with but a touch, or kill with a single precisely-pitched whistle." The cult's combat fingerprint is the character / monster / elite-infantry hunter, not the plate-shredding grinder — the historical truth of the swordsman, expressed in mechanical form.

## Special Rules

- Disciplined
- **Elven Grace** *(faction-wide for elves — Ward 6+ in melee vs attacks at the elf's I or lower; see §8 Defence)*. At I 7, Swordmasters trigger the Ward against essentially everything in the ruleset — only apex-Initiative characters and a handful of monsters reach I 8+ and bypass it.
- **Martial Prowess** *(faction-wide for elves — reroll to-Hit 1s in melee; see §8 Combat)*. At MA 7, the unit is hitting on 2+ against most defenders, so Martial Prowess catches only the natural-1 fumbles; the gain is small but consistent.
- **Touch of the Master** *(Swordmasters unit identity rule)* — functions as **Killing Blow (Any, 4)** per §8. On an unmodified to-Wound roll of **6** in melee, the Swordmaster's attack deals **4 damage** instead of 2; excess damage does not carry between models (per §8 Killing Blow). The greatest masters can slay a foe with a touch, or kill with a single precisely-pitched whistle — the perfect strike finds the gap, drives through, and severs what cannot be re-knit. One-shots W ≤ 4 targets; meaningfully wounds anything bigger.
- **Swordmasters** *(Swordmasters of Hoeth unit identity rule)* — captures the apex cult discipline. Two effects:
  - **Blade-veil deflection.** Swordmasters gain **Ward Save (5+)** against unsaved wounds caused by **non-template ranged attacks** (arrows, bolts, bullets, javelins — anything resolved against a single-model target with line of sight that the elf can physically see in flight). The Ward does **not** apply against template attacks (cannonballs, mortar shells, breath weapons, flame templates, scatter weapons), against spell-source wounds (the §8 *Ward vs Spells* mechanic covers that ground separately), or against melee attacks (Elven Grace covers that ground). Lore-true: "blades blur and weave, knocking aside arrows in mid-flight" and the meditation ritual's "impenetrable web of ithilmar that swoops gracefully around the swordmaster." Per Ward Save Stacking (§8 Defence), this Ward stacks with other Ward sources under the standard step-up-to-4+-cap rule.
  - **No Initiative penalty from great weapons.** A Swordmaster suffers **no Initiative penalty** from wielding a great-weapon-class melee weapon. The Hoeth-forged greatsword is balanced to perfect lightness in cult-trained hands; the elf strikes at the bearer's full I 7 regardless of the weapon's class. *(Forward-compatibility clause: §8 imposes no great-weapon Initiative penalty by default; this entry ensures that if such a penalty is introduced in a future system pass, Swordmasters are exempt by their cult-training identity.)*

## Options

**Worn armour options:** Heavy armour is the cult kit; no upgrade or downgrade is offered. Hoeth-forged ceremonial plate is the canonical loadout — the cult identity does not bend to list-build preference.

**Command options:**
- **Bladelord (Leader)** (+4 pts) — cult champion, senior Swordmaster
- **Standard Bearer** (+8 pts, may take Magic Standard up to 50 pts) — elite banner budget, matching Greatswords / Knights tier
- **Musician** (+4 pts)

## Weapon Profiles

### Close Combat

- **Hoeth-Forged Greatsword** *(default, 2H — no shield)* — 2" reach | **A 2** | S+1 (= S 4) | AP -1 | D 2 | Magical Attacks — Two-Handed. The Hoeth-forged ithilmar greatsword: as long as an elf is tall, balanced to perfect lightness, enchantments "woven into the blade" carrying the **Magical Attacks** keyword per the explicit lore. **2" reach** lets the second rank strike past the first — even at greatsword scale, the cult fights in formation rather than as scattered duellists, lore-true to the regiment-of-Swordmasters that held Hathar Ford. **S+1** (rather than the standard great-weapon S+2) and **AP -1** (rather than the great-weapon's standard AP -2) together encode the historical truth that a sword — even an ithilmar one — does not shred plate: the cult finds the gap rather than forcing through. **D 2** reflects what happens when the precision cut *does* land — the meditation-trained strike goes deep, severing what the gap exposes. **A 2** is the cult-elite prolific-strike baseline (per FACTION.md elite-tier scaling rule #2). The mechanical fingerprint that emerges: low save-stripping, high per-wound depth, exceptional on the lucky-strike via Touch of the Master. `[2H Reach Magical]`
