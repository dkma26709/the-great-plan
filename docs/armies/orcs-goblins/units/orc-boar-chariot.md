# Orc Boar Chariot

> A heavy wooden cart bolted together by Orc engineers, pulled by two War Boars and crewed by two Orc Boyz with choppas. The chariot is built for one thing: ramming into the enemy line at full speed. On the charge, the combined mass of two charging Boars + the chariot's wheel-and-axle assembly delivers **D6 Impact hits at S 5 AP -1** — devastating against rank-and-file infantry, capable of cleaving holes in formed blocks before melee even begins. After the impact, the Crew swing choppas (2× S 4 AP -1) and the Boars bite tusks (2× S 4 AP -1) at standard cavalry-tier output. The chariot is fragile (W 4, T 5, NA 6+) and gets one good charge — sustained melee at lower wound counts loses effectiveness as the chariot creaks toward destruction. The Greenskin battlefield read: deploy 1-3 Boar Chariots as separate units, point them at the most rank-vulnerable enemy block, charge, deliver D6 Impact hits per chariot, and survive the return fire long enough to charge again next turn.

> **Stat-routing for Chariot Mixed Units (chariot-specific Crew-pattern variant — established this draft, 2026-05-19):**
> - The **Chariot Primary** carries only **T / W / Res / LiS / US** — the chariot frame is a durability container, not a combat actor. Its M / MA / MD / S / I are dashed.
> - The **War Boar Mount** carries the unit's **M (8)** — the Boars pull the chariot; the chariot has no independent movement. This is a variation on the standard Crew-pattern (where the supporting profile dashes M too) — for chariots only, the Mount's M is shown because the unit's movement IS the Mount's movement. The Mount also carries its own MA / MD / S / I for the Tusks weapon profile contribution.
> - The **Orc Crew** carries **MA / MD / BS / S / I** for the Crew's weapon profiles (Choppa or Spear) and for incoming-attack resolution (attackers roll to-hit against the Crew's MD 3). The Crew's M is dashed (they ride the chariot, not move independently).
> - **Incoming attacks** against this unit roll to-hit against the **Crew's MD 3** (the Crew is what defends), and to-wound against the **Chariot Primary's T 5** (the frame is what absorbs the wound).
> - **Wounds** go to the Chariot Primary's **W 4** pool. The Crew and Mount never die individually (full Crew-pattern wound priority — neither absorbs nor contributes wounds). Composition is fixed (2 Crew + 2 Boars per chariot, locked at deployment). If the Chariot Primary is destroyed (W 0), the entire unit is destroyed; Crew and Mounts go with it.
> - When the Wolf Chariot (#224) is drafted, this stat-routing variant should be promoted to a §1 *Chariot Crew-pattern* sub-section so both chariots reference the same rule.

## Special Rules

- Undisciplined
- **Animosity** *(Greenskin-wide faction trait — see §8 Psychology)*. D6 at start of each Movement phase when out of combat; on a 1, sub-roll D6 determines charge / self-hits at the Crew's S 4. *(The Chariot CAN charge — Animosity functions normally for this unit. The Crew Bickerin' 4-6 result resolves as Crew brawling with each other on the chariot deck, the Chariot takes D3 hits at S 4. The 'Ere We Go! 1-3 result forces a charge.)*
- **Loves a Scrap** *(Orc-specific faction trait — see §8 Psychology, applies to the Crew)*. Removes 1 stress per Combat phase engaged in melee; +1 A on a single weapon profile (Crew's Choppas, by default) if engaged at end of previous turn. Inherited from the Orc Crew's sub-species; the Boars and the Chariot platform don't benefit from this rule directly, but the Crew's stress relief contributes to the unit's overall morale state.
- **Chariot** *(unit type — see §8)*. Cannot March in Difficult/Dangerous Terrain. Flank/Rear Charge Vulnerability (+1 CR to chargers in flank/rear arc). Chariot Crew-pattern stat-routing variant per §8 (Mount provides M; Crew provides MA/MD/BS/S/I; Chariot Primary holds T/W/Res only). *(Promoted to §8 during the Goblin Wolf Chariot draft, 2026-05-19.)*
- **Boar Chariot Impact** *(Chariot identity rule — replaces the Tusker Charge from Orc Boar Boyz with a heavier chariot-specific Impact)*. On the turn this unit successfully charges into melee, before the Combat phase begins, resolve the following Impact effect (per §8 Impact — note that Impact "hits" are pre-determined hits with no to-hit roll required; the rolled count is the hit count directly):
  - **D6 hits per chariot** at **S 5** (mount S 4 + wheel-and-axle mass + 1) | **AP -1** (combined Boar tusks + chariot frame collision) | **D 1**
  - The hits are **automatic** — no to-hit roll. Roll D6 to determine the number of hits, then resolve to-wound and saves normally against the charged unit's models within Reach of the chariot in base contact.
  - Damage does not carry between models. Failed charges produce no Impact hits.
  - The chariot's Impact is significantly heavier than Orc Boar Boyz' Tusker Charge (D3 hits per model) because the combined mass of two charging Boars + the wheeled chariot frame produces a single concentrated impact rather than several distributed cavalry-tusker hits.

## Options

**No worn armour options.** The chariot is its own armour layer; no Crew worn armour available.

**Equipment swap options (Crew weapon, applies to both Crew identically):**
- **Spears** (+2 pts/chariot, both Crew) — Crew swap Choppas for Spears. 2" reach, S 4 AP 0 baseline (charge: S 5 AP -1 — same cavalry-charge bonus as Orc Boar Boyz Spear). May pair with Shield... wait, no shield option. Spears are 1H + may pair with shield in foot units, but Chariot Crew don't get a shield (no slot). Just a weapon swap.

**No command options.** Chariots are single-model units; no champion / standard / musician.
