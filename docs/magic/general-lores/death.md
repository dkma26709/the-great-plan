# Lore of Death


> Decay, fear, and the slow ebb of the soul. Where Life restores, Death **drains**. Where Light removes stress, Death **applies** it. The lore specialises in three things no other lore does: wound siphoning between enemy and ally, direct stress damage that bypasses combat entirely, and precision character attacks. Death is a psychic lore — cold whispers, grave-dust, the will giving up before the body. Its magic almost never projects as a missile; it settles, blankets, and drains.

**Lore Attribute — Presence of Doom:** whenever this wizard successfully casts a Death spell, roll a **D6**. On a **5+**, one enemy unit within **12"** of the caster immediately suffers **+1 stress**. No per-turn cap — the hand of death reaches further on some casts than others. Clean mirror of Light's Radiance (which removes stress from friends on cast).

**Signature Spell — Caress of Laniph**

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 6 | 12" | Direct Damage, Instant |

Target enemy **single model** (character, champion, or any solo model — not a rank-and-file model in a unit). The target suffers **D3 wounds with no armour save allowed** (ward saves permitted). The cheap sniper — Death's workmanlike spell, always available, threatens every exposed hero on the field. A character with decent Wounds and a ward save weathers one cast; a glass-cannon wizard opposite just died. Range 12" reflects Death's intimate identity — the wizard's drain reaches the target up close, not across the field.

#### Basic (Tier 1) — Lore Access 1+

**Spirit Leech**

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 9 | 12" | Hex, Instant |

Target enemy unit suffers **+D3 stress** immediately. Pure soul-drain — the nerve gives way before the body is ever touched. Combines viciously with the lore attribute: on a cast that triggers Presence of Doom, this spell can dump +D3+1 stress onto a single unit in one cast. Strong-for-tier — pure stress damage that bypasses combat entirely; the CV is held high in Basic, and the 12" range reinforces Death's intimate identity.

**Aspect of the Dreadknight**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 8 | 12" | Augment | Until start of next turn |

Target friendly unit gains the **Fear** keyword (see §8) until start of next turn. If the target already causes Fear, it upgrades to causing **Terror** for the duration instead. A grim silhouette settles over the unit — the enemy sees corpses even where there are soldiers.

**Siphon Life**

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 9 | 12" | Hex, Instant |

Target enemy unit suffers **D3 wounds with no armour save allowed** (ward saves permitted). Then **one friendly unit within 12"** of the caster restores the **same number of wounds** (wounded models first, per Regrowth rules). One life taken, one life given — roll the D3 once and apply to both sides. Mechanically unique: only Death transfers wounds this way. Strong-for-tier dual-effect spell (damage + heal) — the 12" range on both ends keeps the wizard committed to the front line for the drain to reach.

**Pall of Despair**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 9 | 12" | Hex | Until start of next turn |

Target enemy unit **cannot Rally** until start of next turn — any stress on the unit stays on it through the Rally sub-phase. Makes stress stick. Directly weaponises the lore attribute: Presence of Doom applies stress, Pall of Despair prevents its removal. One of the cleanest combos in the lore.

#### Intermediate (Tier 2) — Lore Access 2+

**Wind of Death**

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 10 | 24" | Magic Missile |

**D6 hits at S5, AP -2, D2.** The lore's only true magic missile — narrower but heavier than Fire's typical offerings (which favour 2D6 hits at lower damage). Rewards precision over spread. **Magical Attacks.** Range 24" is the lore's lone long-reach option — Wind of Death is a *physical projectile* of grave-cold rather than a drain, the one Death spell that doesn't require the wizard to be within whispering distance.

**Curse of Years** *(RIP)*

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 11 | 18" | Hex, Remains In Play |

On cast, target enemy unit suffers **-1 T** immediately. At the start of **each friendly Magic phase** the spell remains in play (including the turn it was cast), the Toughness penalty worsens by a further **-1** (no cap). When dispelled or the unit is destroyed, all penalties end. The flesh forgets how to function, a little more each day — a canny caster who can protect the spell from dispel dice will see the target unit crumble after three or four turns. Range 18" (vs Death's 12" baseline) reflects the RIP's persistent-targeting need — the wizard plants the curse from a slight distance, then maintains it across turns.

**Doom and Darkness**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 11 | 18" | Hex | Until start of next turn |

Target enemy unit treats its Ld as **-2 lower** for all stress threshold calculations until start of next turn (Shaken at Ld-6, Wavering at Ld-4, Broken at Ld-2). Ld for combat resolution modifiers and other Ld-dependent rules is unchanged — only the *stress thresholds* shift. A morale crusher that makes existing stress bite twice as hard without adding a single point.

**Echoing Doom**

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 11 | 12" (self-radius) | Hex, Instant |

All enemy units with at least one model within **12"** of the caster suffer **+D3 stress** immediately. Death's whisper reaches outward in a wave — every enemy nearby feels the chill of the grave. Multi-unit area-stress hex distinct from Spirit Leech (single-target +D3) and Pall of Doom (Master RIP per-Magic-phase aura): Echoing Doom is the *instant* burst, with the wizard pulsing dread out in all directions on a single cast. Strong-for-tier — the multi-unit scaling justifies CV 11 at the top of Intermediate.

#### Advanced (Tier 3) — Lore Access 3+

**Soulblight**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 13 | 12" | Hex | Until start of next turn |

Target enemy unit suffers **-1 S and -1 T** until start of next turn. The flesh remembers old failures; every sinew weakens. A phase-long neutering of an entire unit — often cast on the piece you intend to charge next turn. Strong-for-tier dual-stat hex; the 12" range commits the wizard to the front for the drain to land.

**Fate of Bjuna**

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 13 | 12" | Direct Damage, Instant |

Target enemy unit. **Each model** in the target unit must pass a **Toughness test** (roll a D6 — equal to or less than the model's T passes). On a failure, that model suffers **1 wound with no armour save allowed** (ward saves permitted). A blanket of grave-dust settles over the unit; the weaker flesh surrenders first. Scales naturally: a 20-strong T3 mob loses ~10 wounds, while a T5 monstrous regiment barely notices. Range 12" — Death's signature plague-blanket reaches one nearby unit, not across the field; the wizard must close to drain.

**Purple Sun of Xereus** *(RIP)*

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 14 | 18" | Direct Damage, Remains In Play, Template |

Place a **large round template** anywhere within 18" of the caster; on initial placement the template **scatters D3" in a random direction** (as Flame Storm). The spell remains in play until dispelled.

While in play, any model touched by the template — on initial placement, and at the start of **each friendly Magic phase** while the spell remains in play — must pass a **Toughness test**. On a failure, that model suffers **1 wound with no armour save allowed** (ward saves permitted).

At the start of each friendly Magic phase **after the cast-turn**, before resolving damage, the template moves **a scatter die's distance in a random direction**. Full template mechanics (partial coverage, placement rules, scatter die behaviour) will be formalised in §7.2 Shooting phase. **Magical Attacks.**

A slow drifting annihilation. Often wanders off-target — but while it sits on a unit, it grinds.

#### Master (Tier 4) — Lore Access 4+

**Walking Death**

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 18 | 12" (self-radius) | Direct Damage, Instant |

Each enemy **model** within **12"** of the caster must take a **Toughness test** (roll D6 — equal to or less than the model's T passes; otherwise fails). Each failing model suffers **1 wound, with no armour save allowed; ward saves are permitted**. Multi-wound models lose 1 wound; single-wound models go down. The caster radiates the chill of the grave — every nearby enemy tastes the earth, and the weak flesh fails first.

Per-*model* T test rather than per-unit — the bubble has a clear positional footprint; only models physically within 12" of the caster test, regardless of which unit they belong to. A unit with two models inside the bubble loses two T tests, not the entire unit's worth. Devastating against low-T armies that close on the wizard, far less effective against high-T (Ogres, Chaos Warriors, Lord-tier characters). Distinct from Dwellers Below (Life Master, S-test per-model in target unit) and from Fate of Bjuna (Death Adv, T-test on a single named target unit) — Walking Death is the *bubble* version, hitting any model that ventures into the wizard's drain-radius. The 12" intimate range matches Death's identity: the wizard does not call death across the field, she pulls it inward.

**Pall of Doom** *(RIP, aura)*

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 16 | 18" (self-radius) | Hex (aura), Remains In Play |

*Remains In Play.* At the start of **every Magic phase** (both the controlling player's and the opposing player's), all enemy units with at least one model within **18"** of the caster gain **+1 stress** each. The wizard becomes a beacon of doom — every magical pulse from the caster sends a shudder through nearby enemies, and the dread accumulates unrelenting until the spell is dispelled.

Triggers twice per game turn — while held, the bubble adds ~2 stress per turn to every enemy in range. Direct structural mirror of Pha's Vigil (Light Master B): where Pha's Vigil sheds stress from friendly units, Pall of Doom accumulates stress on enemies. The 18" radius (vs Death's 12" baseline) is the lone Death effect that reaches further than intimate range — RIP auras need to cover the army, not just the wizard's neighbours. RIP-dispellable; the dread breaks if the spell is countered.

> **Design note on Lore of Death.** Range identity is **intimate** — Death's whisper reaches close, the hand of the grave drains nearby. **12" is the baseline** across nearly every Death spell (Caress of Laniph, Spirit Leech, Aspect of the Dreadknight, Siphon Life, Pall of Despair, Soulblight, Fate of Bjuna, Echoing Doom radius, Walking Death radius); **18" is reserved for RIP placements and aura effects** (Curse of Years, Doom and Darkness, Purple Sun template placement, Pall of Doom radius); **24" is the lone outlier** for Wind of Death (the only physical projectile in the lore, a wind of grave-cold rather than a drain). Direct contrast with Heavens (24-36" far-reach) and Fire (mid-range at 18-24"). CV calibration after this pass: Sig 6 (narrow-targeting precision Caress kept reliable). Basic 8-9 — Death's Basics all sit at the strong end because the lore's identity is strength-via-bypass (no-save damage, stress, anti-recovery, healing-from-damage). Intermediate 10-11. Advanced 13-14 with Purple Sun's drifting RIP template at the cap. Master 16-18: **Walking Death** for apex per-model T-test bubble, **Pall of Doom** for persistent army-wide stress accumulation. Death remains the *loudest* morale lore — Presence of Doom, Spirit Leech, Pall of Despair, Doom and Darkness, Echoing Doom, and Pall of Doom all push the stress system directly. Damage is narrow (one magic missile, one single-model Direct Damage Sig, two unit-scale T-test spells, one bubble T-test Master) but characterised by inability to be saved against armour. Where Fire sprays hits and Heavens lands lightning, Death whispers — and what it touches simply stops living. Lore Attribute (Presence of Doom) remains unchanged.
