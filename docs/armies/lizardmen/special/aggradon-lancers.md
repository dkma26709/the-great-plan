# Aggradon Lancers


> Elite Crested Saurus on predatory saurian mounts. The Aggradons are frothing, bloodline-old reptiles that only a Saurus blessed by Itzl, master of beasts, can hope to tame — and even then, only until the first taste of blood. Once an Aggradon has killed, the rider is no longer steering: they are pointing. The rest is fury, fangs, and celestite. Riders mark their chosen mounts by attaching colourful feathers to the Aggradon's tail; once the bond between Saurus and beast forms, it is unbreakable.

  - **Locked Jaws** *(Saurus species rule — Crested Saurus rider)* — when a rider in this unit is removed as a casualty during the Combat phase, before removal the rider makes 1 final Saurus Jaws attack against the killing model (or closest enemy in base contact). Single attack, no Predatory Fighter cascade. The Saurus's death-bite — the Aggradon mount remains animate after the rider dies, but the dying rider gets one last bite in.
  - **Blood Frenzy** — once this unit's Aggradon mount attacks inflict one or more unsaved wounds in a combat phase, the unit gains the **Frenzy** special rule (see §8) for the rest of the battle. This Frenzy **cannot be lost** — not by losing a combat round, nor by any other means that would ordinarily strip Frenzy. The mount has tasted blood and will never stop chasing it. A guided missile, armed.

> **Lore notes:** The heavy Saurus cavalry Special slot — Aggradons are frothing, bloodline-old reptiles that only an Itzl-blessed Saurus can hope to tame, and even then only until the first taste of blood. Once the mount kills, the rider is no longer steering; they are pointing. **Blood Frenzy** is the identity rule — once an Aggradon's mount-bite draws blood, the unit gains Frenzy for the rest of the battle and *cannot lose it*. A guided missile, armed; the mount has tasted blood and will not stop chasing it. Replaces the legacy Cold One Riders cleanly — current-edition mounts, an identity that doesn't lean on a Stupidity-mitigation scaffold. Inherits Locked Jaws from the Saurus species; Tail Sweep deliberately *not* propagated (cavalry geometry — the rider's tail is too high above the ground).
