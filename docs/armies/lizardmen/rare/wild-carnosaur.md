# Wild Carnosaur


> The Carnosaur is an apex predator of Lustrian jungle and bone-strewn savannah, the largest pure-killer biology on the continent. Wild Carnosaurs roam outside the Spawning-Pool hierarchy — they answer to no Saurus chief and acknowledge no rider-bond. When one appears in a Lizardmen battle-line, it is because a **Slann Mage-Priest has reached out psychically and bound the beast to the army for this single engagement**: the Carnosaur is not tame, not trained, not loyal. It is *compelled* by Old-One-tier magic for as long as the Slann's concentration holds. Once the spell-leash slips, the Carnosaur reverts to predator instinct — and the hunt becomes whatever it sees first. **Pinned Prey** captures the iconic AoS predator pattern: claws lock the target down, jaws drive in for the killing crush. **Untamed (Slann-Bound)** captures the leash slipping when the Slann's attention is far afield.

*(WS-A 5 / WS-D 3 — apex predator's offensive skill paired with predictable, committed motion. The Carnosaur strikes with surgical hunter precision, but its lunges and bites are large telegraphed motions that a quick opponent can read and exploit.)*

- **Keywords:** Lizardmen, Saurian, Monster, Wild, Rare
- **Unit type:** Monster (single model, no rider)
- **Unit size:** 1
- **Points:** 224 per model
- **Natural Armour:** 5+ (predator hide — less armoured than howdah-saurians, this beast relies on speed and ferocity)
- **Ward Save:** —
- **No flying. No breath weapon. No regeneration.** The Wild Carnosaur's defensive ceiling is its W pool plus NA 5+; once the W is gone, the predator is gone. Vulnerable to high-S AP-heavy attacks, ranged attrition, and anti-monster magic. M 10 mobility partially mitigates by closing distance fast.

**Special rules:**
- **Cold-Blooded** *(Lizardmen faction-wide — applies)*
- **Predatory Fighter** *(Saurus faction-wide — applies; natural 6 to-hit cascades one extra attack at the same Initiative step, no further recursion)*
- **Terror** *(per §8)*
- **Apex Predator** *(unit identity rule — inherited from Carnosaur mount profile)* — the Carnosaur's Massive Jaws and Raking Claws gain **+1 to-Hit and +1 to-Wound** when targeting models with the **Monster**, **Monstrous Infantry**, **Monstrous Cavalry**, or **Warbeast** keyword. The lore-canonical instinct to hunt the biggest prey: devastating against peers and large beasts, no bonus against rank-and-file infantry or cavalry.
- **Bloodroar** *(unit identity rule — inherited)* — at the start of each Combat phase, every enemy unit with any model within **12"** of the Carnosaur immediately gains **+1 stress** (in addition to the Terror aura when relevant). Tyrannosaurid lung-power; the sound alone has broken formations.
- **Pursuit Predator** *(unit identity rule — inherited)* — when an enemy unit Falls Back from combat with the Carnosaur, the Carnosaur **must** follow up (no hold option). Additionally, if the Carnosaur **wipes out a unit in melee**, it may immediately make a free move of up to **6"** toward the nearest enemy unit. The kill instinct does not stop.
- **Bloodlust** *(Carnosaur identity rule)* — once this Carnosaur scores at least one unsaved wound in melee, it gains the **Frenzy** special rule (see §8) for the rest of the battle. The killing fury, once kindled, never abates — Frenzy cannot be lost by losing a combat round, by stress thresholds, or by any other rule that would normally strip Frenzy. Lore-direct: "having tasted blood, a Carnosaur is at its most deadly, for it enters a state of savage bloodlust — biting and tearing at any living creature."
- **Pinned Prey** *(Wild Carnosaur unit identity rule)* — when this model attacks in melee, **resolve Raking Claws attacks first**. If **both Raking Claws score hits against the same enemy model** (single-model unit, Character, Champion, or any specific rank-and-file model), that model is **pinned** — for the rest of this Combat phase, this Carnosaur's Massive Jaws attacks **against that pinned model** gain **+1 to-Hit and +1 Damage**. Effectively only matters against multi-W targets — the +1 Damage Jaws bonus is wasted on W 1 rank-and-file (a single hit kills regardless). Captures the AoS-iconic predator pattern: claws lock the prey, jaws drive in. Stacks with Apex Predator vs Monster + Pinned: Massive Jaws strike at +2 to-Hit (capped at 2+), +1 to-Wound, AP -3, D D3+1 — devastating but conditional on landing both Claws on the same model.
- **Untamed (Slann-Bound)** *(Wild Carnosaur identity rule)* — if there is **no friendly Slann model within 18"** of this Carnosaur at the start of the Movement phase, roll a D6. On a **1**, the Slann's psychic compulsion has slipped and the Carnosaur's predator instinct takes over:
  - If any enemy unit is within charge range and visible, the Carnosaur **must declare a charge against the nearest such enemy** this Movement phase.
  - If no charge is possible (no visible enemy within charge range), the Carnosaur instead gains **+1 stress** and may not Advance or March this Movement phase (it stalks restlessly, scanning for prey).
  
  While within **18" of a friendly Slann**, **no Untamed roll is required** — the Slann's psychic leash holds the Carnosaur fully bound. Reliable when the Slann is near, unpredictable when isolated. Mechanically reinforces the Slann + Wild-Carnosaur deployment pattern; the lore-framing of "Slann's will compels the beast" is enacted directly through the rule.
- **Locked Jaws** *(Lizardmen biology — soft inherit from Saurus species pass 2)* — if this Carnosaur is reduced to 0 W *during the Combat phase*, before being removed it makes **1 final Massive Jaws attack** against the model that scored the killing blow (or the closest enemy model in base contact, if the killing attacker is out of base contact). Single attack — no Predatory Fighter cascade on the death-bite, no Pinned Prey or Apex Predator interaction (the dying beast manages one last reflexive snap, not a calculated bite). Lore: severed reptilian heads keep snapping; even in death the apex predator's jaws still close on the killer.
- **Degrading** *(3-tier damage table, inherited)* — see table below

**Weapon profiles:**

- **Massive Jaws** *(natural, front arc)* — **2" reach | A 3 | S 7 | AP -3 | D D3** — primary predator weapon. On any unmodified **to-wound roll of 6** (before re-rolls or modifiers), the wound inflicts **D6 damage** instead of D3 — the jaws close fully and crush the target. Apex Predator and Pinned Prey bonuses apply against eligible targets per their respective rules. `[Natural]`
- **Raking Claws** *(natural, always-available)* — 1" reach | **A 2** | S 5 | AP -1 | D 1 — pinning strikes; the setup, not the kill. **Resolved first** in the Carnosaur's attack sequence to determine whether Pinned Prey triggers. `[Natural]`
- **Sweeping Tail** *(natural, rear arc only)* — 3" reach | **A D3** | S 5 | AP 0 | D 1 — reflex weapon, the tail sweeps whatever approaches from behind. `[Natural]`

*US is not in the damage table — US equals current remaining W directly.*

> **Lore notes:** The apex predator of Lustria roaming outside the Spawning-Pool hierarchy. A Wild Carnosaur in the line is on a Slann's psychic leash — compelled, not loyal. Pure offensive monster: M 7, S 7, AP -3 jaws, D D3 with crit-to-D6 — no flying, no breath weapon, no regeneration. **Pinned Prey** is the AoS-iconic predator pattern: Raking Claws resolve first, and if both hit the same model, Massive Jaws against that model gain +1 to-Hit and +1 Damage. Scoped to *model* not *unit* — the bonus only matters against multi-W targets (the +1 Damage is wasted on W 1 rank-and-file). **Apex Predator** drives the hunt-the-biggest instinct (+1 to-Hit / +1 to-Wound vs Monster / Monstrous keyword targets). **Untamed (Slann-Bound)** captures the leash slipping when isolated — without a friendly Slann within 18", a D6 misfire risks the Carnosaur charging the nearest visible enemy whether tactical or not. **Bloodlust** locks Frenzy in on the first unsaved wound — parallel to Aggradon Lancers, biology-consistent saurian fury. **Locked Jaws** is the soft Lizardmen-biology inherit — even in death, one final reflexive snap.
