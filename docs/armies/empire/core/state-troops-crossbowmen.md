# State Troops — Crossbowmen


> State Troops armed with the exotic crossbow — far less common than Handgunners or Empire Archers (auxiliary). Crossbow regiments emerged in the southern Empire from Tilean influence, where mercenary crossbow companies have been a fixture for centuries. Slower-firing than a bow but more powerful, and considerably easier to train than a handgun (a recruit can learn the basics in a week, where a handgun matchlock requires months). Drilled in volley-fire from a stationary position; the crossbow's reload demands the wielder stand still. Mostly fielded in lower numbers than Handgunners, often as a detachment supporting a Halberdier or Spearman main block.

> **Lore notes:** Crossbowmen sit between Halberdier (7 pts, melee-only) and Handgunner (11 pts, gunpowder volley) at **9 pts** — pricing reflects the Crossbow's ranged threat (S 4 AP -1 D 1, Move-or-Fire) being meaningful but below the Handgun's S 4 AP -2 with Slow to Fire. **30" range** is the longest Empire ranged weapon outside the Hochland Long Rifle (48", marksman) and the Cannon (48"+); reflects the crossbow's reach vs Handgun's 24". **Move or Fire** restricts Crossbowmen to static firing positions — they cannot reposition and shoot in the same turn, which encourages the WFB-canonical defensive deployment behind a main infantry block. Lore-distinctive identity: less common than Handgunners (lore: "the crossbow is not a popular weapon amongst men from the Empire, but there are always foreigners eager to be recruited into the Empire armies"); their presence flags Tilean influence in the army's recruitment pool. Stats baseline State Troops — Disciplined, Res 9, no special trait bumps. The Crossbow has no Stand-and-Shoot prevention (unlike the Handgun's Slow to Fire), so Crossbowmen *can* react to charges with a final volley before contact.
