# Ogre Kingdoms — Test Profiles


> **Design principle:** Cross-faction demonstration roster — second non-Seraphon faction after Empire. Where Empire stress-tested rank-and-flank human infantry, gunpowder, cavalry, war machines, and multi-Wind magic, Ogres stress-test the **Monstrous-Infantry-as-whole-army** archetype: small elite units (5-9 models per block), every model Kroxigor-adjacent in scale and pricing, faction-specific armour (gut-plate), a unique faction lore (Lore of the Great Maw — pending separate §11 draft), and the absence of inspirational-leadership tropes. See `cross-faction-reference-notes.md` at project root for design-prep research.

> **Heft calibration note for Ogres.** Ogres are **fat brutes** — wounds are the Ogre identity, more than Toughness or armour. Per design call (2026-04-25 session), **no artificial wound ceiling applies**; unit identity drives the wound count, not abstract tier caps. **Wounds across the roster:** Bulls W 3 → **6** (one above WFB reference; rank-and-file Bulls sit just below Kroxigor regular W 7 — Kroxigor are physically taller and muscle-bound, Bulls are tubby brutes at a slightly lower W tier), Ironguts → **6** (parity with Bulls; Ironguts' elite distinction expresses through **better save (5+ via gut-plate + light armour intrinsic)**, **better Res (8 vs 7)**, **mandatory great weapons**, and the **Bone Crackers identity rule** — gear and combat skill differentiate elite from rank-and-file, not wound count), Bruiser → **8** (Hero), Tyrant → **10** (Lord-combat apex), Slaughtermaster → **8** (Lord-caster; parity with Slann's W 8 — both are corpulent Lord-tier casters whose role is spiritual/magical, not personal warlord; the Tyrant's W 10 differentiates the *combat* Lord from the *spiritual* Lord). The **Kroxigor regular is revised to W 7 in §10** (rank-and-file Kroxigor remain physically bigger than rank-and-file Ogres in our scaling, the earlier framing restored after the W 6→7→6 calibration loop landed on Bulls W 6). The **Kroxigor Ancient** is W 10 in §10 (Hero-tier MonstInf apex, parity with the Tyrant). **Res stays at WFB reference values (no +1 bump)** — Ogres are brutal, not stoic; might-makes-right tribal culture doesn't translate to drilled-regiment morale resilience. WS / S / T / I / A close to reference. **Save ceiling 5+ across the roster** — no heavy armour anywhere; Ogre durability comes from **wounds + Toughness 4-5**, not armour. Compares to Saurus Oldblood's biological NA 2+: Ogres absorb hits with bulk; Saurus deflect them with hide.

> **Resolve note for Ogres.** Ogres do *not* lead like Empire. There is no innate Inspiring Presence on Ogre characters — the Tyrant does not buff allied Res through charisma. Authority in the Mountains of Mourn is established through single-combat dominance ("guts-out" fights in the Maw-pit, where the loser is consumed by the winner). The mechanical translation: Ogre character abilities operate on **combat axes** (extra attacks, no-fall-back enforcement, personal kills) rather than morale axes. **No Ogre character provides Res bumps, rerolls, or Inspiring Presence to other units.**

> **Predatory Fighter is reserved to Lizardmen.** Predatory Fighter (extra attacks on unmodified 6 to hit, see §8) is a **Saurus-caste signature mechanic** per design call (2026-04-25 session). Reserved to Lizardmen units (and very specific other cases where lore strongly justifies). Ogre characters do *not* have Predatory Fighter, even at apex tier — the Tyrant's personal combat dominance expresses through **Mighty Blow (1)** (+1 S in first round, see §8) plus his iconic **Headbutt** weapon profile.

> **Gut-plate convention.** Every Ogre wears a gut-plate — a strapped-on bronze chest-and-belly plate, often etched with tribal sigils. Mechanically, gut-plate functions as a **shield-equivalent** (one save step) but is **hands-free** — two-handed weapons preserve the gut-plate save (unlike Empire shields, which forbid two-handed weapons). Gut-plate is intrinsic to Ogre units and stacks with worn armour per §8 Armour Save Stacking, one step per source. Default Ogre with gut-plate alone = **6+** save; +light armour = **5+**. **No heavy armour anywhere in the Ogre roster.**
