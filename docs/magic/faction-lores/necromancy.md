# Lore of Necromancy


The dark magic of binding the dead. Vampires and Necromancers draw on Shyish (the Wind of Death) channelled through forbidden tomes (Liber Mortis, Nine Books of Nagash) to animate corpses, bend the slain to their will, and weave the necromantic web that holds Undead armies together. The full lore is pending separate drafting; one Signature spell is drafted now as the tactical anchor, since rank-and-file Undead (Skeletons, Zombies, Grave Guard) tactical viability depends on mid-game resurrection per §16's Crumble dynamic.

#### Signature: Invocation of Nehek

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 5 | 18" | Augment |

Targets one friendly **Undead** unit within range. The unit immediately recovers **D6 wounds** worth of slain models — restored to the unit's current model count (up to its starting size), positioned in coherency with surviving models. If the unit has no slain models to restore (i.e., the unit is at full starting strength), the spell instead **heals D3 wounds** distributed across surviving models in the unit (capped at each model's starting W; useful for character-tier Undead like a wounded Vampire Lord embedded in the unit, or for Wight characters carrying multiple W).

The signature spell of Necromancy and the **tactical anchor** of Vampire Counts armies. Without it, Skeletons / Zombies / Grave Guard accumulate stress and Crumble to nothing within 2-3 turns under sustained combat. With it, the army can absorb continuous damage as long as the Vampire / Necromancer lives. Pattern: parallels Lore of Life's Great Regrowth (model restoration / wound healing) but Necromancy's restoration is *grim* (raising new bones from the battlefield dead, not regrowing healthy life), and is *the* faction lifeline rather than a niche utility.

> **Design note on Necromancy (stub).** Lore is stubbed at Signature only in this initial pass — full Basic / Intermediate / Advanced / Master tiers are pending a dedicated §11 expansion pass. Reference WFB Necromancy includes **Vanhel's Danse Macabre** (movement augment), **Hellish Vigour** (combat buff for Undead), **Curse of Years** (DoT debuff), **Gaze of Nagash** (magic missile, Death-flavour), **Wind of Undeath** (large summon — raises new Skeleton / Zombie units mid-battle), and others — all to be drafted later. **Invocation of Nehek alone is enough to make Vampire Counts armies functional** out of the gate; the rest of the lore expands the magical toolkit when the lore expansion lands. The Vampire Lord and Necromancer ship with their lore-access mechanic and magic profiles locked, but the spell-list depth is intentionally limited until the expansion.
