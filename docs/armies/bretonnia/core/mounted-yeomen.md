# Mounted Yeomen

> The duchy's mounted scouts — peasant yeomen drawn from the duke's foresters, gamekeepers, and rural minor freemen, ridden out on common horses to screen the chivalric line, ride down stragglers, and harass the enemy approach. They are *not* knights — they wear no Vow, they bear no lance, they ride no Purebred destrier. They are peasants on horseback, given hand-me-down armour and a brace of throwing spears and sent ahead of the army to scout the line of march and snipe at enemy patrols. Where Bidowers are the army's *foot* skirmishers, Mounted Yeomen are its *mounted* skirmishers — faster, better-armed for melee than the Bidower's javelin-and-knife, but otherwise the same peasant tradition: harass, screen, fall back through the knight wings.

## Special Rules

- Undisciplined
- **Fast Cavalry** (see §8) — Vanguard (may deploy up to M outside the deployment zone), free pivots at any point during movement, Flee charge reaction (M + D6 directly away; the act of Fleeing costs **+4 stress** since Mounted Yeomen do not carry the Skirmisher keyword, per the revised §8 *Fast Cavalry* Flee clause). Fast Cavalry does **not** waive the standard -1 to-hit penalty for moving-and-shooting (per §7.4) — the package is a positioning advantage, not a shooting one
- **Peasant's Duty** *(identity rule)* — as Men-at-Arms; while any model is within **6"** of a friendly Bretonnia character with the **Knight's Vow**, **Crusader's Vow**, **Questing Vow**, **Grail Vow**, or **Lady's Chosen** keyword, that character grants this unit the effects of **Inspiring Presence (6")** (see §8 Resolve and Command). Aura passes from outside the unit only
- **Class Barrier** *(clause of Peasant's Duty)* — a Bretonnian character with the **Knight's Vow / Questing Vow / Lady's Chosen / Crusader's Vow / Grail Vow** keyword standing within the coherency of this unit does **not** gain the Character Attachment Exception's shielding (§6) — peasant proximity grants the aura but not concealment
- **Common Warhorse** *(mount profile note)* — the Mounted Yeoman's horse is the duchy's *common* warhorse, not the noble caste's Purebred destrier. **Purebred Warhorse does NOT apply** — the horse would take the standard barding M penalty if barded, but baseline Mounted Yeomen ride unbarded and so the penalty is irrelevant in practice
- **NOT Lance Formation eligible** — Lance Formation is the chivalric tradition, reserved for sworn knights. Peasant horsemen do not train the wedge; even if their geometry happens to match, the keyword does not trigger

## Options

**Worn armour options (additive, may take both):**
- **Light armour** (+0.5 pt/model) → 6+ save (a rough chainmail jack from the castle armoury)
- **Shield** (+0.5 pt/model) → combines with light armour for 5+ save (per §8 Armour Save Stacking); on its own, grants 6+ save

**Equipment options (melee — replace hand weapon, pick at most one):**
- **Spear** (+0.5 pt/model — replaces hand weapon, pairs with shield if taken) — see weapon profile below; 2" reach for the cavalry thrust

**Equipment options (ranged — replace throwing spears, pick at most one):**
- **Bow** (+0.5 pt/model — replaces Throwing Spears) — see weapon profile below; trades the throwing spear's per-shot impact for medium-range arrow-volume

**Command options:**
- **Leader** (+4 pts) — *Yeoman Master*; a senior peasant respected for his scout-craft, often a ducal forester or huntsman
- **Standard bearer** (+8 pts — Magic Standard up to 20 pts; the duchy's hunt-banner or village-muster banner)
- **Musician** (+4 pts) — +1 to recovery and Rally rolls (a mounted horn-blower relaying signals across the scattered patrol)

## Weapon Profiles

### Close Combat

- **Hand weapon** *(default, 1H — pairs with shield if taken)* — 1" reach | **A 1** | S 3 | AP 0 | D 1 — peasant arming sword, hatchet, or knife carried at the belt `[1H Blade]`
- **Spear** *(option, 1H — pairs with shield if taken)* — 2" reach | **A 1** | S 3 | AP 0 | D 1 — cheap thrust-spear; same profile as Men-at-Arms's spear `[1H Reach]`
- **Common Warhorse's Hooves and Bite** *(mount natural, each model)* — 1" reach | **A 1** | S 3 | AP 0 | D 1 — standard warhorse natural attack `[Natural]`

### Ranged

- **Throwing Spears** *(default ranged)* — **8" range | AP 0 | D 1 | Quick to Fire** (see §8) — a brace of short peasant javelins carried for forward harassment; S follows wielder (S 3). Quick to Fire allows Stand and Shoot reactions without the standard -1 to-hit penalty. Note: Quick to Fire does **not** waive the moving-and-shooting -1 (per §7.4) — the Yeoman who shoots after moving still takes the standard penalty, same as any other shooter
- **Bow** *(swap option, ranged — replaces Throwing Spears, +0.5 pt/model)* — **24" range | AP 0 | D 1** (see §8) — a peasant short bow; S follows wielder (S 3). Trades per-shot impact for medium-range volume. NOT Quick to Fire — Stand and Shoot reactions resolve at the standard -1 to-hit penalty
