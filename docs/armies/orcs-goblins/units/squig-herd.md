# Squig Herd

> A herd of Cave Squigs prodded along by Night Goblin Handlers. Each Squig is a smaller cousin of the bouncing Squig Hopper mount — same biology (massive jaws, S 5 / A 2 bite, no real intelligence) but on foot rather than under a rider, controlled by Handlers who poke them forward with pointed sticks. While the Handlers are alive, the herd moves at standard M 5 (controlled forward push). The moment all Handlers die, the Squigs lose direction entirely and revert to **Random Movement (3D6")** for the rest of the battle — bouncing in whichever direction the lead Squig was last facing, biting anything in their path including their own former handlers' friends. Mechanically a Mixed Unit (Handler-pattern per §1), with the Squigs as the Primary profile (W-bearing, damage-dealing) and the Handlers as the Supporting profile (absorb wounds first, control direction while alive). The army-list role: a cheap mid-tier brawl unit that delivers high-S volume damage in melee, with a build-in chaos clock — keep the Handlers alive and the herd is reliably aimed; lose them and the unit becomes an uncontrolled bouncing hazard that may or may not still help.

## Special Rules

- Undisciplined
- **Animosity** *(Greenskin-wide faction trait — see §8 Psychology)*. Rolled at unit level each Movement phase when out of combat. On a 1, use the **Mushroom Madness sub-table** (Night Goblin sub-tribe variant — see Night Goblins profile / §8 Animosity sub-tribe variants).
- **Stab in the Back** *(Goblin-typical, applies to Handler attacks only)*. The Goblin Handlers gain +1 to Wound when attacking rear-arc *(melee only)* or Shaken/Wavering targets. Does not apply to Squig Mouth attacks — Squigs are animals, not opportunistic Goblins.
- **Cowardly Numbers** *(Goblin-typical, unit-level)*. While this unit has 20+ models *(Squigs + Handlers counted together)*, it is **Stubborn** (per §8). Below half starting strength, -1 Res for the rest of the battle. *(Note: most Squig Herd units don't reach 20 models in practice; the Stubborn clause is rarely active. The -1 Res below-half clause is the live fragility tax.)*
- **No Loves a Scrap** *(Goblin sub-species — see §13)*.
- **Mixed Unit — Handler-pattern** *(per §1)*. Squigs are the Primary profile; Goblin Handlers are the Supporting profile. Wounds inflicted on the unit are applied to **Handlers first**. Only once all Handlers are removed may wounds be allocated to the Squigs. Handlers must remain within **2"** of a Squig at the end of any move; a Handler isolated from all Squigs is removed as a casualty. **If all Squigs are destroyed, any remaining Handlers are also removed and the unit is destroyed.**
- **Wild Squigs** *(Squig Herd unit identity rule — triggered when all Handlers die)*. The moment the **last Handler** in this unit is removed as a casualty (whether from melee, ranged, magic, or coherency loss), the remaining Squigs immediately gain **both Random Movement (3D6")** *(per §8)* **and Stupidity** *(per §8)* for the rest of the battle. The Squigs lose all standard M; cannot March, cannot Rally. **Direction interaction:** Stupidity's mandatory "Charge / Advance toward nearest visible enemy if able, otherwise Advance aimlessly" replaces the player's free pivot from Random Movement — the Squigs are pivoted *toward the nearest visible enemy* (or in a random direction if no enemy is visible, using the artillery die as the Mangler Squig precedent), and then 3D6" is rolled for the distance directly forward. If the rolled distance reaches an enemy unit in the path, the move resolves as a charge. *(Lore: without Handler direction, Cave Squigs revert to instinctive bouncing aggression — they bite what they land near and care nothing for friend or foe distinction. Stupidity captures the lack of Handler-imposed intelligence; Random Movement captures the uncontrolled bouncing tempo.)*

## Options

**No worn armour options.** Neither Squigs nor Handlers wear armour.

**No shield options.** Same.

**Unit composition upgrades:**
- **+2 Squigs + 1 Handler** (+17 pts/upgrade-step) — adds two Squigs and one Handler to the unit, maintaining the 2:1 ratio. May be taken any number of times within army-list points budget. Each upgrade-step is a single +17 pts increment (no partial steps).

**No champion / Standard / Musician options.** Squig Herd is a beast-handler swarm; no Boss, no Standard, no Musician. The Handlers are interchangeable rank-and-file controllers, not designated officers.
