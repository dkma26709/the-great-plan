# Saurus Warriors


> Spawned for war, drilled by instinct. These are not human soldiers who trained for a few years — they emerged from the spawning pools as fighters. WS-A 4 reflects that baseline martial ability; WS-D 3 captures the slower defensive instinct of the cold-blooded predator-warrior — the Saurus hits well but reacts a half-beat slow to incoming blows. Six-to-eight feet tall, broad-muscled, scales hardening with age; the Old Ones engineered them for a single purpose and a Saurus cohort fulfils that purpose with eerie, unspoken synchronicity. They register no emotion save savagery — and lesser troops that face them feel it.

- **Keywords:** Lizardmen, Saurus, Infantry, Core
- **Unit type:** Infantry
- **Unit size:** 10-30
- **Points:** 36 per model
- **Natural Armour:** 5+ (scaly skin)
- **Equipment (default):** Celestite Blade, Medium Shield
- **Equipment options:**
  - Celestite Spear + Medium Shield (free) — see Armoury
- **With shield:** combined save **4+** (Natural 5+ + Medium Shield +1 step, per §8 Armour Save Stacking)
- **Command options:**
  - **Spawn Leader** (champion, +10 pts) — acts as unit leader for Rally, challenges, and Leader-snipe purposes
  - **Standard bearer** (+10 pts)
  - **Musician** (+10 pts) — +1 to recovery and Rally rolls
- **Special rules:**
  - Disciplined (see §8)
  - Cold-Blooded (see §8)
  - Stubborn (see §8)
  - Predatory Fighter (see §8)
  - **Fear** *(Psychology — see §8)* — Saurus radiate Fear; their cohort silence, single-minded predator focus, and bared jaws unsettle any living creature within (6 + LiS)" = **7"**. Captures the lore-canonical "register no emotion save savagery" + the eerie unspoken synchronicity that lets the whole formation turn as one without a word.
  - **Pack Cohesion** — while this unit is in Formed formation and has 10 or more models, coherency checks triggered by casualties during the Combat phase are **deferred**. Coherency is checked and resolved only at the end of the Combat phase — until then, models may be removed and the remaining models continue fighting without penalty. If coherency is still broken at phase end, normal coherency-break consequences apply and the unit must rectify on its next activation. The spawn-brothers instinctively tighten their formation as casualties fall.
  - **Locked Jaws** *(unit identity rule)* — when a Saurus model in this unit is removed as a casualty *during the Combat phase*, before removal it makes **1 final Saurus Jaws attack** against the model that scored the killing blow (or the closest enemy model in base contact, if the killing attacker is out of base contact). Resolved at the Saurus's I (= 2). Single attack — no Predatory Fighter cascade on the death-bite. Lore: severed heads keep snapping for days; jaws lock onto flesh and have to be cut away from the victim — even in death, the Saurus's bite still goes in.

**Weapon profiles (A on weapons):**

- **Saurus Jaws** *(natural, always available)* — **0.5" reach** | **A 1** | S 4 | **AP -1** | D 1 — every Saurus snaps with its jaws when an enemy stands within reach. The 0.5" reach means only models in actual base-to-base contact bite; the cropped reach is the mechanical hook for restricting Jaws to engaged front-rank models. Lore: "the power of their crocodilian jaws is such that a vicious Saurus bite can sever a limb or crush steel" — AP -1 captures the crush-steel detail. Bites are highly infectious by lore; in our system this is treated as predator-flavour, not a separate Poisoned mechanic `[Natural]`
- **Tail Sweep** *(natural, rear-arc only)* — **1" reach** | **A 1** | S 4 | AP 0 | D 1 — heavy crocodilian tail. This Saurus makes 1 Tail Sweep attack against any enemy model in base contact in its **rear arc** — typical case: when the unit is engaged from the flank or rear by a charging or repositioning enemy. Resolved at the Saurus's I (= 2). The rear-arc restriction means Tail Sweep is silent in normal front-on engagements; it punishes flanking and surrounding the unit. Lore: "muscular tails can smash a man's ribcage" / "use them to knock or trip opponents" `[Natural]`
- **Celestite Blade** *(default, 1H)* — **A 2** — full profile in Armoury (1" | AP -1 | D 1) `[1H Blade]`
- **Celestite Spear** *(option, 1H)* — **A 2** — full profile in Armoury (2" | +1 I baseline | D 1, with anti-cavalry/monster D2 and anti-charge S+1 AP -1) `[1H Reach]`

> **Lore notes:** **W 2 → 3** revision (pass 2, 2026-04-29): the Old Ones engineered Saurus to fight as the apex Core of every Lizardmen army — broad-muscled bodies, scales that harden with age, lifespan effectively unbounded. The bump from W 2 to W 3 captures "spawn-bred apex" identity at the Infantry tier — Saurus rank-and-file are now the most durable Core unit in the ruleset, exceeded only by Monstrous Infantry (Kroxigor W 7, Ogre Bulls W 6). I 2 is deliberate — Saurus are strong, tough, and skilled but not quick. They fight with cold, methodical precision. A 2 baseline (up from WAP's 1, now on the weapon per A-on-weapons convention) reflects that in AoS these are 2-attack troops. US 2 reflects their physical mass — Saurus are substantially built and count on objectives accordingly. Natural Armour 5+ (scaly skin) improves to 4+ with Medium Shield (per §8 Shield Class baseline reference); Saurus equipped with the Celestite Blade by default reflects their elite line-infantry status in the Lizardmen armies. **Pack Cohesion** captures the spawn-brother instinct — Saurus units stay functional deeper into a losing fight than human regiments would. **Fear** (new in pass 2) captures the lore-canonical predator-warrior intimidation: enemies within 7" of a Saurus cohort take +1 stress per turn from the cohort's silent, emotionless advance. (Will of Chaos, Imm Psych, and Undead status all neutralise the Fear effect — Saurus's Fear is the right calibration vs lesser foes, not the elite-elite competitor.) **Locked Jaws** (new in pass 2) captures the iconic "even in death the bite still goes in" detail from Vincenzo Corenzo's logbook — a dying Saurus gets one final crocodile-strength chomp at the killer; mechanically a single-attack death-bite, no PF cascade. Pairs thematically with the now-AP -1 Saurus Jaws (lore: "can sever a limb or crush steel"). **Saurus Jaws AP -1** (was AP 0) — the lore-canonical jaw strength now lands; combined with Locked Jaws, the bite is a meaningful threat at troop tier. Bites are highly infectious in lore but in our system that's treated as predator-flavour confirming the species rather than a separate Poisoned mechanic — most predators' bites are infectious; the AP -1 + Locked Jaws does the lore work without bookkeeping. **Tail Sweep** (new in pass 2) — rear-arc-only natural attack at A 1 / S 4 / AP 0 / D 1 / 1" reach. Captures "muscular tails can smash a man's ribcage" / "knock or trip opponents." Strong flank-deterrent: surrounding a small group of Saurus becomes meaningfully dangerous since each surrounded Saurus generates 1 Tail Sweep per rear-arc enemy in addition to normal melee output. Character-tier jaws (Scar-Vet / Oldblood) remain distinctly nastier — A 2, S 5, AP -2, D D3 — preserving the tier gap. **Pricing 22 → 36** absorbs the W bump, AP -1 Jaws, Tail Sweep, Locked Jaws, and Fear additions; calibrates such that 10 Saurus at 360 pts vs ~equal-points Chaos lists puts unmarked Chaos at a clear deficit (Saurus as the spawn-bred apex Core), with **GW Chaos as the closest competitive baseline (~1.4-1.5:1 Saurus)** and **Marks of Chaos** (when drafted) restoring elite-elite parity. A-on-weapons convention applied (retroactive pass, ruleset update per project memory).
