# Grail Reliquae

> The duchy's holiest battlefield procession — the bones, weapon, or armour fragment of a fallen Grail Knight, carried through Bretonnia's war-camps in a wheeled wagon-shrine and surrounded by Battle Pilgrims, peasant zealots who have abandoned home and field to follow the relic. The Reliquae is the Lady's gift made physical: a Grail Knight's earthly remains, untouched by decay, still warm with the Lady's blessing. The Pilgrims who flock to its presence are devout but not raging; the relic *steadies* them rather than inflaming them, and through the relic's grace the Lady's Ward falls on peasants who would otherwise lack any Vow. Where Bretonnian peasants ordinarily serve under the Class Barrier of the chivalric order, the Pilgrims of the Grail Reliquae transcend their station for as long as the relic stands among them — not by becoming knights but by becoming the *Lady's own*.

## Special Rules

- Undisciplined
- **Reliquae Centrepiece** *(architecture — character-style attachment per §1 Character Attachment Exception, with peasant-context adjustments)* — the Reliquae cart attaches to this Battle Pilgrims unit at deployment (mandatory; cannot be fielded separately and cannot be removed). While attached:
  - The Reliquae has its own profile (T 5 / W 3, no attacks, no movement-of-its-own, no save). It moves with the unit
  - The Reliquae is **shielded by Pilgrim coherency** — like a character per §6 Character Attachment Exception, the Reliquae cannot be singled out by enemy shooting or hostile magic while at least one Battle Pilgrim is alive in the unit. **Class Barrier does NOT apply** to the Reliquae — the relic-cart is not a vow-bearing character; peasant proximity is its appropriate home, not an exception to it
  - Wounds inflicted on the unit allocate to **Battle Pilgrims first** under normal allocation; the Reliquae's W pool is depleted only by **Sniper-class effects targeting it directly**, by attacks specifically allocated to it once all Pilgrims are dead, or by area-effect spells / templates that touch the cart's base
  - **If the Reliquae's W reaches 0:** the relic is destroyed (a catastrophic moment for the army — the cart collapses, the relic shatters or is overrun). The unit loses both conferred bonuses (Blessing of the Lady, Recovery bonus) for the remainder of the battle. The unit continues as a regular Battle Pilgrims block — Peasant's Duty / Class Barrier remain, but the relic-specific rules end
  - **If all Battle Pilgrims are killed:** the unit is destroyed. The Reliquae is removed with the unit (it has no attacks, no movement, and cannot stand alone)
- **Blessing of the Lady** *(conferred by the Reliquae — the unique peasant-Blessing exception)* — while the Reliquae is alive, every Battle Pilgrim in the unit and the Reliquae itself gain a **Ward Save (6+ vs S ≤ 4, 5+ vs S ≥ 5)** per §17 Faction Mechanics *Blessing of the Lady*. This is the unique exception to the standard "peasants do not receive the Blessing" rule — the relic conveys the Lady's grace directly. **Lost on flee from combat** per the standard Blessing clause (the relic's blessing requires faithful stand, not retreat). Also lost if the Reliquae is destroyed (W 0)
- **Steadied by Faith** *(conferred by the Reliquae — Recovery bonus)* — while the Reliquae is alive, the host unit gains **+1 to Recovery rolls** at End of Turn (per §5 — the unit's D3 recovery becomes D3+1, floor still 1). The relic's calming presence steadies the Pilgrims' morale. Lost if the Reliquae is destroyed (W 0)
- **Peasant's Duty** *(identity rule)* — as Men-at-Arms; while any model is within **6"** of a friendly Bretonnia character with the **Knight's Vow**, **Crusader's Vow**, **Questing Vow**, **Grail Vow**, or **Lady's Chosen** keyword, that character grants this unit the effects of **Inspiring Presence (6")** (see §8 Resolve and Command). Aura passes from outside the unit only
- **Class Barrier** *(clause of Peasant's Duty)* — a Bretonnian vow-bearing or Lady's Chosen character standing within the coherency of this unit does **not** gain the Character Attachment Exception's shielding (§6). Knights cannot conceal themselves in the Pilgrim mob. (Note: the Reliquae itself is *not* a vow-bearer; it is the attached centrepiece, not a character, and is shielded normally per its own Reliquae Centrepiece rule above)
