# Kharibdyss

> Loathsome amalgam of hydra, kraken, and lamprey, dredged from the abyssal deeps off Naggaroth's coast by Black Ark fleets and goaded into battle with whip and chain. Where the War Hydra is *of the land* — a multi-headed serpent of the Blackspine Mountains, regenerating through fire and venom — the Kharibdyss is *of the deep* — a slimy, chitinous bulk adapted to oceanic pressure, "unstoppable on land," driven by abyssal hunger rather than serpentine cunning. "Smaller foes are plucked from the battlefield and swept into the Kharibdyss' maw by its crown of flailing tentacles; larger ones are entangled and held fast whilst razor-sharp teeth feast upon their flesh." It is a brutish, slow-witted creature, "and simply rampages wherever hunger leads it" — only the timely swipe of a Beastmaster's scourge keeps it from feasting on the Druchii host instead of the enemy. The lore signature is twofold: **Abyssal Howl** — a spine-chilling sub-vocal moan that carries below conscious hearing and amplifies the terror radiating from the beast's physical presence — and the dual-mode offence of **Cavernous Maw** *(short-reach, crushing strike — the razor-teeth feast on heavy targets)* paired with **Lashing Tentacles** *(long-reach, sweeping grab — the flailing crown plucks small foes whole into the maw)*. The Kharibdyss carries **no breath weapon** *(its lungs are abyssal, not pyric — it gulps brine and crushes prey, it does not exhale fire)* and **no regenerative biology** *(unlike the Hydra's headcount-driven regrowth, the Kharibdyss survives by raw chitinous bulk and the off-stage Beastmaster's lash)*. Fielded here as a **pure Monster** (single profile, no on-table Handlers — the lash-bond is implicit, parallel to the Hydra precedent; Beastmaster as on-table character deferred to a future Druchii Hero draft). The Naggarothi battlefield read: send the Kharibdyss into an elite-character formation, let the Howl bleed morale, then watch the Cavernous Maw crush whoever's at the front while the Tentacles reach over the ranks to pluck the champion or war-mage in the second rank.

## Special Rules

- **Terror** *(Psychology — see §8)* — at LiS 5, the Terror aura projects to **(6 + 5)" = 11"**. The Kharibdyss is a deep-sea horror dragged into the open air — its sheer alien biology, the tentacle-crown waving above the chitinous bulk, the multi-throated bellow, all of it registers as terror on any battlefield. Per §8, enemy units within 11" suffer +2 stress per turn while in range.
- **Abyssal Howl** *(Psychology — Kharibdyss unit identity rule — stress-aura amplifier)* — The Kharibdyss continuously emits a spine-chilling, sub-vocal moan carrying from its abyssal-adapted throat-cavities. Any enemy unit already within the Kharibdyss's **Terror radius** — i.e., within **11"** — suffers an **additional +1 stress per turn** on top of the +2 stress already imposed by Terror. **Net effect: enemy units within 11" of the Kharibdyss suffer +3 stress per turn while the beast lives.** The Howl is conditional on the Terror radius — outside 11" the moan is inaudible, inside it the soldier's spine-marrow registers it whether the conscious mind notices or not. **Psychology-tagged:** units with **Immune to Psychology** ignore the Howl's +1 stress *and* the Terror baseline (Frenzied Witch Elves, Chaos Warriors with Will of Chaos, Undead, and other Imm Psych units take no stress from the Kharibdyss's aura at all). The Howl does not stack with itself across multiple Kharibdyss (a unit within 11" of two Kharibdyss still suffers +3 stress, not +4) but it stacks with other-source Fear / Terror auras per the §8 stress-rule layering (the Howl's +1 is on top of whatever the dominant Psychology source resolves to, which under §8 is the Terror tier).
- **Cold-Blooded** *(per §8)* — crustacean-fish biology adapted to the dark, slow metabolism of the abyssal depths. Incoming stress is reduced by 1 (minimum 1) and Recovery rolls are reduced by 1 (minimum 1). The Kharibdyss takes longer to register threat *and* longer to shake it off — the reptilian-slow reaction-curve typical of cold-bodied predators.

## Weapon Profiles

### Close Combat

- **Cavernous Maw** *(natural concentrated bite)* — **1" reach** | **A 3** | **S 7** | **AP -3** | **D D3** — the "razor-sharp teeth feast upon their flesh" of the canon lore. Short reach (the maw sits centrally in the chitinous bulk, biting only what's directly in front), low attack count (three deliberate crushes per round), but devastating per-hit profile: **S 7** wounds T 5 on 3+, T 6 on 4+, T 7 on 5+; **AP -3** strips 3+ heavy and 2+ plate down to 5+ and 6+ respectively, ignoring most heavy infantry / cavalry saves entirely; **D D3** delivers 1-3 wounds per unsaved wound (average 2) — every successful bite is a meaningful damage spike on a multi-W target. The Maw is the Kharibdyss's *anti-elite / anti-character* weapon — concentrated punishment for whatever it bites. `[Natural]`
- **Lashing Tentacles** *(natural multi-tendril grab — long-reach sweeping crown)* — **3" reach** | **A 5** | **S 4** | **AP 0** | **D 1** | **Pluck** — the "crown of flailing tentacles" of the canon lore. Long-reach (the tendrils sweep 3" out from the body, striking past front ranks, around character bodyguards, into the second rank of a formation), high attack count (five sweeping grabs per round), modest per-hit profile: **S 4** wounds T 3 on 4+, T 4 on 5+; **AP 0** gives heavy armour its full effect. The Tentacles are the Kharibdyss's *anti-rank-and-file / character-grab* weapon — volume-not-quality against grunts, exceptional against single-base targets behind the front line. **3" reach interacts with §4 formation rules to permit attacking models in the second rank or characters behind a bodyguard line** — the tendrils reach over the front rank to grab whoever is hiding in the deeper formation. `[Natural]`

- **Pluck** *(Lashing Tentacles only — Kharibdyss unit identity rule)* — When the Lashing Tentacles attack a model whose individual base **US contribution is 1** (typical rank-and-file Infantry model, a single-base small model, an unmounted character or champion, a foot Hero or Lord — anything that contributes US 1 to its unit's total), each successful unsaved wound deals **6 Damage** instead of the weapon's baseline D 1. Normal saves apply. Excess damage does not carry between models per standard §8 conventions. The tentacle wraps around the model and drags it whole into the maw — even the heaviest plate armour offers no special defence against being plucked from the formation, but high-Toughness biology and Ward saves give some defence against the resulting crush-and-swallow. **The rule is consistent — it triggers on every successful wound vs a US-1 model, not on a natural-6 to-Hit or natural-6 to-Wound**: counterplay lives in armour, Ward, and the (T-vs-S 4) wound roll, not in trigger luck. Against US-1 W-1 targets the Pluck rule is functionally equivalent to D 1 (the model dies once, excess is wasted); against multi-W US-1 characters (W 3 Hero, W 5 Lord) the Pluck rule is devastating — a single unsaved tentacle wound delivers more damage than any other natural weapon in the codebase.
