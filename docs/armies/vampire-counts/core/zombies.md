# Zombies


> The crudest tier of Undead. Reanimated corpses in various stages of decay, no weapons beyond rotting fists, no armour, no coordination. Necromancers raise zombies as the lowest-effort animation — a horde of shuffling dead that overwhelms by mass and tirelessness rather than skill. Zombies are slower than Skeletons in lore, dimmer (the necromantic binding is loose, just enough to drive forward and bite). Where Skeletons are *trained* dead (former soldiers reanimated with their drilling intact), Zombies are *just dead* (any corpse will do).

**Points:** **3** per model

- **Keywords:** Vampire Counts, Undead, Zombie, Infantry, Core
- **Unit type:** Infantry
- **Unit size:** 10-40
- **Natural Armour:** —
- **Worn armour:** — (rotting flesh, no protection)
- **Combined save:** — (no save of any kind)
- **Equipment:** Rotting fists / improvised debris (no weapon options — Zombies are not given weapons)
- **Command options:** none (Zombies have no internal coordination — no leaders, no standards, no musicians)
- **Special rules:**
  - **Undead** (see §8)
  - **Numberless** *(identity rule)* — when **Invocation of Nehek** (or any Necromancy spell that restores Undead models) targets a Zombies unit, the spell restores **+2 wounds beyond the listed amount** (e.g., D6+2 wounds for Invocation of Nehek's D6 baseline). Represents zombies as the cheapest resurrection target — any corpse will do, and the necromancer can flood the table with shambling dead by a fraction of the effort required for a Skeleton

**Weapon profiles (A on weapons):**

- **Rotting Fists** *(natural, always)* — 1" reach | **A 1** | S 2 | AP 0 | D 1 — Zombies tear with hands, teeth, and broken bones; they do not wield weapons in any organised sense

> **Lore notes:** **Stats deliberately the worst in the entire ruleset** — WS 1, BS 0, S 2, I 1, Ld 4. Zombies are the cheapest, weakest, slowest-Crumbling rank-and-file in our roster. The +1 over Skeletons in fragility (Ld 4 vs Ld 5) means Zombies Crumble even faster than Skeletons under sustained pressure. The trade-off: 3 pts/model is the absolute floor; a 40-Zombie horde is **120 pts** of obstacle that the enemy must hack through to reach the Necromancer. Zombies are fodder *by design*. No equipment options — the lore is "anyone with a corpse can raise a Zombie; anyone with training can raise a Skeleton." No command options — Zombies don't have internal hierarchy. **A 1 / S 2 means Zombies almost never wound anything T 4+** without help (Vampire / Necromancer support spells like a Necromancy buff become essential). The unit is a *body* in both senses: a wound pool to absorb attacks and a footprint to deny terrain.
