# Pegasus Knights


> The skyborne knights of Bretonnia — sons of noble households whose dukes maintain a Pegasus stable, mounted on the winged warhorses of the Forest of Loren and the high passes of the Grey Mountains. The Pegasus is no mere mount: it is a magical creature, larger than a destrier, fiercer in the charge, and capable of flight. A duke who fields Pegasus Knights commands one of Bretonnia's elite traditions — the swooping aerial charge that breaks ranks, smashes flanks, or reaches enemies that ground cavalry cannot. Knights chosen for the Pegasus tradition train for years to master the winged mount; knight and Pegasus must trust each other absolutely, for a falling knight at altitude does not survive.

- **Keywords:** Bretonnia, Human, Cavalry, Special, Pegasus Knights
- **Unit type:** Cavalry (flying)
- **Unit size:** 3-10 (rarer than ground cavalry — pegasi are scarce magical beasts; few duchies maintain enough stables for full units)
- **Combined pool per model:** W 3 (rider 1 + mount 2). Attackers use highest WS (4) and highest T (4) per §1 *Rider and Mount Profiles*
- **Natural Armour:** —
- **Worn armour (default, fixed kit):** Heavy armour + Heavy Shield + barding (per §8 Armour Save Stacking, one step per source)
- **Combined save: 2+** (heavy armour 4+ → Heavy Shield 3+ → barding 2+)
- **Equipment (fixed kit, no swap options):** Heavy Lance + Heavy Shield + heavy armour + barding
- **Command options:**
  - **Leader** (+5 pts) — *Pegasus Lord*; the senior knight of the Pegasus stable, often the duke's airborne herald
  - **Standard bearer** (+10 pts — Magic Standard up to 50 pts; the duke's heraldic banner mounted on a Pegasus-borne pole)
  - **Musician** (+5 pts) — +1 to recovery and Rally rolls (the unit's herald, signalling charges and rallies on a sky-horn audible across the battlefield)
- **Special rules:**
  - Disciplined (see §8)
  - **Knight's Vow** (see §17 Faction Mechanics) — Blessing-eligibility marker. No additional effect at this vow tier
  - **Blessing of the Lady** (see §17 Faction Mechanics) — intrinsic via the Knight's Vow. Ward Save 6+ vs S ≤ 4, 5+ vs S ≥ 5, applying to rider and mount alike. Lost on flee from combat
  - **Fly (12")** (see §8) — applies to the mount profile. The unit may move 12" in fly mode each Movement phase, ignoring all terrain, intervening models (friendly or enemy), and elevation. Ground M (with barding penalty) is the alternate ground-mode movement value, used only if Fly is suspended for some reason. May Fly-charge: charge distance = 12" + D6 (Formed) or 12" + 2D6 (Loose). Impact attacks still trigger on a successful Fly-charge — velocity and mass are undiminished by lift
  - **NOT Lance Formation eligible** — Pegasus Knights operate in 3D aerial space, not the rigid ground wedge that the Bretonnian lance tradition requires. The Lance Formation discipline is reserved for ground cavalry (KotR, Knights Errant). Pegasus Knights' identity is aerial mobility, not wedge geometry
  - **NOT Purebred Warhorse** — Pegasus is a winged magical creature, not a Bretonnian destrier. Barding's -2 ground M penalty applies normally. The Fly value (12") is the operational movement mode and is unaffected by barding
  - **Diving Charge** *(identity rule)* — when this unit successfully completes a **Fly-charge**, each model in the unit inflicts **D3 Impact Hits at S 4, AP 0, D 1** against the charged enemy unit, resolved **before** melee combat begins. The Pegasus crashes into the enemy formation from above — body, hooves, and bulk strike before the lance work begins. Impact Hits resolve simultaneously across the unit (each Pegasus rolls D3 separately) and use the mount's S 4 (the dive's mass, not the lance's point). Resolves as standard Impact Hits per §4 — wounds rolled, saves taken, casualties allocated to the charged unit's models in reach. Only triggers on a Fly-charge (not a ground charge); requires the unit to actually be using Fly mode for the charge

**Weapon profiles (A on weapons):**

- **Heavy Lance** *(default, 1H with shield — fixed kit, no swap)* — **2" reach | A 1 | on charge (first round): S+2 (= S 5), AP -2, D 2; subsequent rounds: S+0, AP 0, D 1** — the chivalric shock weapon, identical profile to KotR's lance. Charge spike on the first round of combat after a successful charge; tails off thereafter `[1H Lance]`
- **Pegasus's Hooves and Bite** *(mount natural, each model)* — 1" reach | **A 2** | S 4 | AP 0 | D 1 — the mount contributes two attacks per Pegasus at S 4, the unit's *real* sustained damage output. The Pegasus is genuinely dangerous in close combat — bigger, more aggressive, and more bestial than a destrier `[Natural]`

> **Lore notes:** **Stat ladder above KotR on the rider, dramatically above on the mount.** Rider WS 4 / I 4 / Res 9 (per §17 chivalric +1 calibration; I 4 vs KotR's I 3 captures the airborne knight's training in fast aerial combat). Mount profile is the headline: **W 2 / T 4 / S 4 / A 2** vs the standard barded Warhorse's W 1 / T 3 / S 3 / A 1. The Pegasus is a real beast — heavier, more vicious, with twice the attacks at higher S. Combined unit T = 4 (mount's T, per §1 highest-T rule), making the unit much more durable against S 3 attacks (wounds on 5+ instead of 4+).
>
> **Combined W = 3 per model, save 2+, Fly 12".** Three layers of durability stacked: 3 W per model (rider 1 + mount 2), 2+ combined armour save, plus the Blessing of the Lady Ward (6+ vs S ≤ 4, 5+ vs S ≥ 5). A 6-strong unit has 18 wounds at T 4 with 2+ save and a Ward — genuinely tough to bring down. The Fly 12" mobility lets them choose engagements: drop on a war machine crew, harass shooters, charge a flank, retreat behind a building.
>
> **No Lance Formation eligibility.** Conscious design choice — Lance Formation is a ground discipline for KotR (and future Knights Errant), reflecting Bretonnia's chivalric wedge tradition. Pegasus Knights operate in three-dimensional space with much more positional freedom; the formal wedge formation doesn't apply lore-wise, and would be geometrically ambiguous for flying units. They retain the Bretonnian charge identity through the Heavy Lance + Knight's Vow + Blessing combination, but lose the wedge-multiplier that ground knights enjoy. A single-rank charge in our system means only the front rank fights — for a 6-strong Pegasus Knight unit charging in a 3-wide-2-deep formation, only 3 lance attacks resolve. This is a tactical limit that the Pegasus mount's higher A (2 vs 1) partially compensates for: the unit gets 12 mount attacks alongside 3 lance attacks on charge, instead of 6 mount + 6 lance from a KotR wedge.
>
> **No Purebred Warhorse.** The Pegasus is not a destrier. Barding's -2 M penalty applies to ground M (M 8 → M 6); however, the Fly value (10") is the keyword's X parameter and is independent of the mount's M stat per §8. So Pegasus Knights operate at Fly 10" in nearly every situation — the ground penalty is largely invisible. A future special-case rule could grant Pegasus Knights a Pegasus-equivalent of Purebred Warhorse if desired, but the current treatment is clean: ground M is for emergencies only.
>
> **Fly mechanics worth flagging.** Fly-charge distance = 12" + D6 (Formed) or 12" + 2D6 (Loose). Pegasus Knights typically charge in Loose formation to maximise charge distance — they're aerial harassers and swoopers, not rigid block cavalry. A Loose-formation Fly-charge averages 12" + 7 = 19" charge distance; with high rolls, 24" reach. This is enough to charge across most battlefields' deployment gaps in a single turn — Pegasus Knights are effectively *anywhere* by Turn 2.
>
> **Class Barrier applies normally** — Pegasus Knights are vow-bearers (Knight's Vow), so they may attach to other vow units (KotR, Questing Knights, Bretonnian Lord, Paladin), but cannot effectively attach to peasant units (Class Barrier revokes Character Attachment shielding for vow-bearing characters in peasant unit coherency). A Bretonnian Lord on Pegasus or Royal Pegasus mount, when attached to a Pegasus Knights unit, inherits all the unit's mobility and the unit's Blessing.
>
> **Points 100 per model** — +76 over Imperial Knight base 24 (which is the Empire ground-cavalry baseline). Premium covers: **Pegasus mount upgrade** (W 1 → 2, T 3 → 4, S 3 → 4, A 1 → 2, plus Fly 12" — substantial across multiple axes, ~30-40 pts of mount premium); **Blessing of the Lady** (~6 pts); **I bump** I 3 → I 4 (~1 pt); **Fly mobility advantage** at 12" — fast-fly tier, matching Ripperdactyl Riders' speed — as a discrete operational layer (~12-15 pts on top of the mount-stat upgrade); **Diving Charge identity rule** (~10 pts — D3 Impact Hits per Pegasus at S 4 on Fly-charge is a real charge-spike layer, stacking with lance and mount attacks for a three-layer dive). Vs WAP's 50 pts: +50 reflecting our system's fuller pricing of the W 2 mount via the Quadratic Wound Factor (§13), the operational value of Fly 12", and the Diving Charge unit-internal identity rule.
>
> **Compare:**
> - **KotR (35 pts)** — ground cavalry baseline, W 2 combined, 2+ save, Lance Formation eligible, Purebred Warhorse, Heavy Lance charge spike. Tactically grounded; superior wedge charge but no aerial mobility
> - **Demigryph Knight (80 pts)** — Empire MonstCav, W 4 combined, 2+ save (M 8 barded), Mount A 3 / S 4 / AP -1, Fear, Griffon-kin Instinct, Knightly Order menu. More wounds and meaner mount attacks than Pegasus, but ground-bound — no aerial reach, no Diving Charge. Pegasus 20 pts more for 1 less W combined but full Fly 12" mobility plus the dive-charge identity layer
> - **Imperial Pegasus / Griffon characters** (when drafted, future) — Empire's flying-character tier, mostly characters not units. Pegasus Knights are Bretonnia's *unit-tier* sky cavalry; Empire has no equivalent
>
> **Crusader's Vow option** (+2 pts/model whole-unit upgrade) — replaces Knight's Vow with Crusader's Vow per §17 Faction Mechanics. Grants Immunity to Panic-source stress, reroll failed psychology-source recovery rolls, and +1D6 charge distance roll (discard lowest). Useful for a Pegasus Knights unit committed to anti-Chaos / anti-Undead / anti-Greenskin crusade — the +1D6-discard-lowest charge bonus stacks meaningfully with the Pegasus Knights' already-fast Fly-charge (12" + 2D6 in Loose, now +1D6 discard lowest = 12" + 3D6 keep highest two). Pricing kept at the WAP value of +2 pts/model since the unit-level upgrade scales differently from the character-level vow upgrade (Pegasus Knights are a unit, not a character — every model in the unit gains the vow's benefits, so the per-model cost is lower than a single-character Lord/Paladin upgrade).
