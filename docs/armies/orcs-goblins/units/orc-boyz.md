# Orc Boyz

> The rank-and-file warrior of every Waaagh!. Common Orcs are large, brutish, and aggressive — physically similar in size to Saurus Warriors and Chaos Warriors, beaten by neither in the brawl-from-the-front role. The fungal-tinged Orc physiology gives them T 4 resilience and **W 2** (Pass-4 species-identity scaling, capturing fungal pain-tolerance), and at S 4 an Orc swing genuinely hurts. They wear basic furs and crude scrap-armour, wield whatever **choppa** they could find or steal — the iconic Orc weapon, any heavy edged thing they can hack with, cleaver-weight enough to bite through light armour (**AP -1** baseline) — and follow whichever Orc shouts loudest until a louder one comes along. Without immediate enemies to fight, an Orc unit's natural aggression turns inward — squabbling, brawling, sometimes breaking ranks to charge at random just to release tension. This is the famous **Animosity**: the cost of fielding Orcs is that you don't entirely command them. The flip side: an Orc unit *in* combat thrives on it. **Loves a Scrap** captures this — every round of melee actively bleeds stress off the unit, AND once the Boyz have been brawling for a turn the momentum makes them hit harder (sustained-combat +1 attack from round 2 onward). The Orc unit warms up to the fight; a player who keeps the Boyz engaged keeps them functional indefinitely AND increasingly dangerous.

## Equipment

- **Equipment (default):** Choppa (hand weapon)

## Special Rules

- Undisciplined
- **Animosity** *(Greenskin-wide faction trait — see §8 Psychology)*. D6 at start of each Movement phase when out of combat; on a 1, sub-roll D6 determines whether the unit charges nearest enemy or takes D3 self-hits at S 4.
- **Loves a Scrap** *(Orc-specific faction trait — see §8 Psychology)*. Removes 1 stress per Combat phase engaged in melee; +1 A on a single weapon profile if engaged at end of previous turn. **Goblins do not inherit this rule.**

## Options

## Weapon Profiles

### Close Combat

- **Choppa** *(default, 1H — may pair with shield)* — 1" reach | **A 1** | S 4 | **AP -1** | D 1 — any heavy edged thing the Orc can swing; cleaver-weight bites through light armour `[1H Blade]`
- **Pair of Choppas** *(Additional Choppa — 2× 1H — cannot pair with shield)* — 1" reach | **A 2** | S 4 | **AP -1** | D 1 — second hand weapon doubles the swings; loses the shield slot `[Paired]`
- **Spear** *(option, 1H — may pair with shield)* — 2" reach | **A 1** | S 4 | AP 0 | D 1 — improvised spear; the 2" reach lets the second rank stab past the first. No charge-defence bonus (unlike Celestite Spear) — Orcs are not drilled phalanx fighters. Trade-off vs Choppa: gain reach, lose AP `[1H Reach]`
- **Great Choppa** *(option, 2H — no shield)* — 1" reach | **A 1** | S+2 (= S 6) | **AP -1** | D 1 | **Two-Handed** | **-1 Initiative when used** — heavy two-handed cleaver; pure brute damage. The wielder swings high and hard, but the bulk of the weapon slows their reactions: -1 I makes Orc Great Choppa swings effective I 1 (base I 2 minus 1) — striking later than almost anything in the game. The +1 I from charging brings it back to I 2 on the charge turn, but on the receiving end of a charge or in a sustained brawl, the Great Choppa Orc takes hits before swinging back. AP held at -1 (matches base Choppa) — Orc design leans into bodies and attacks rather than super-AP weapon strikes; the Great Choppa's value is the +2 S, not deeper armour penetration `[2H]`
