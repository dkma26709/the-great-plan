# Field Trebuchet


> The duke's siege engine, dragged onto open battlefield — a counterweighted tower of timber and rope, manned by the duchy's stone-masons and quarry-foremen pressed into temporary service (Bretonnia has no formal corps of engineers — the village miller, the cathedral mason, and the duke's master-of-works between them assemble and crew the trebuchet on each campaign). It hurls boulders the size of barrels in high arching trajectories, dropping them on enemy formations from beyond the reach of arrows and shot. Slow to load, slow to aim, prone to misfire when the ropes are wet — but when the boulder lands, plate armour and stone walls alike are reduced to splinters and scree. The Trebuchet is the duke's answer to anything too large, too armoured, or too distant for the knights to charge.

**Field Trebuchet (platform + crew, unified profile):**

| M | WS-A | WS-D | BS | S | T (ranged) | T (melee) | W | I | Res | LiS | US |
|---|----|----|----|---|---|---|---|---|----|-----|----|
| 2 | 2 | 2 | 2 | 3 | **7** | **3** | **5** | 3 | 6 | 2 | 2 |

*The platform and its Peasant Crew share a single unified profile per the §14 War Machine convention. The unit has a **W 5 wound pool** — wounds accumulate on the machine as a whole; the crew is **not** individually removed and combat output stays at full capacity until W reaches 0 (catastrophic structural collapse, last surviving crew lost with it). Dual Toughness reflects what's being hit: **T 7 when targeted by ranged attacks** (boulders bounce off the timber frame and counterweight), **T 3 when targeted in melee** (attackers reach the Crew past the platform). Same identity tension as the Empire Great Cannon — devastating at range, vulnerable in melee, and all-or-nothing under focused targeting.*

**Points:** **110** for the Trebuchet (platform + Peasant Crew bundle, W 5 baseline). +5 for optional **Reinforced Crew** upgrade (raises W to 6 and melee A to 4 — represents an extra peasant pressed into service for loading and melee defence). +10 for the **Wall Warden** upgrade (see special rules).

- **Keywords:** Bretonnia, Human, **War Machine**, Rare, Peasant
- **Unit type:** **War Machine** (per §14 Empire Great Cannon convention) — single-profile unit with dual T. Ranged incoming: T 7. Melee incoming: T 3. W is a wound pool, not a crew count; combat output stays at baseline until W=0. Can be charged normally. Cannot itself Charge
- **Unit composition:** 1 Field Trebuchet (fixed platform with attached crew, W 5 baseline). Optional: **Reinforced Crew** upgrade (+5 pts, W 6 / melee A 4)
- **Natural Armour:** 6+ (ranged attacks only — the Trebuchet's timber frame and counterweight present a hardened surface; Crew have no save in melee)
- **Ward Save:** —
- **Equipment (Crew):** Hand weapons baseline
- **Command options:** none baseline (War Machines do not take champions, standards, or musicians) — *but see the **Wall Warden** upgrade below for an effective single-model crew leader*
- **Movement:** **M 2** — Crew may push the Trebuchet 2" per turn voluntarily. Cannot Charge. May pivot freely up to 180° during Movement phase. *(Note: real-world trebuchets were typically dismantled and reassembled rather than wheeled; for tabletop simplicity we treat the Trebuchet as slow-but-mobile, identical to the Great Cannon's M 2 convention.)*
- **Special rules:**
  - **War Machine** *(unit type — see §14)* — single-profile unit with dual T. Ranged incoming: T 7. Melee incoming: T 3. W is a wound pool — wounds accumulate but do not reduce combat output, melee A, or fire rate. The Trebuchet fires at full effectiveness from W=full all the way down to W=1; the engine goes silent only at W=0 (destroyed). Can be charged normally. Cannot itself Charge
  - **Crew attack in melee** — Crew fight with hand weapons at unified WS 2, I 3, **A 3** baseline (A 4 with Reinforced Crew upgrade). Melee A is fixed and does not scale with remaining wounds. Note WS 2 is one step worse than the Empire Great Cannon's Engineer Crew (WS 3) — peasant crew, not professional artillerists; melee defence is genuinely weak. BS 2 is similarly worse than Imperial Engineers' BS 3 — affects scatter resolution (Crew BS 3 → 2 means scatter penalty reduced by 1)
  - **Peasant's Duty** *(identity rule — same translation as Men-at-Arms / Longbowmen / Bidowers)* — while any model of this unit (the Crew) is within **6"** of a friendly Bretonnia character with the **Knight's Vow**, **Crusader's Vow**, **Questing Vow**, **Grail Vow**, or **Lady's Chosen** keyword, that character grants this unit the effects of **Inspiring Presence (6")** (see §8 Resolve and Command). Aura passes from outside the unit only
  - **Class Barrier** *(clause of Peasant's Duty)* — a Bretonnian vow-bearing character standing within the Trebuchet Crew's coherency does NOT gain Character Attachment Exception shielding. Knights cannot conceal themselves behind the peasant crew either
  - **Wall Warden upgrade** *(+10 pts, single-model crew leader upgrade)* — adds a Wall Warden to the crew, a master-of-works or veteran siege engineer who has overseen castle bombardments. The Wall Warden persists for the duration of the battle (until the Trebuchet is destroyed at W=0). While the Wall Warden's upgrade is active:
    - The unit's **WS** is treated as **3** (the Wall Warden raises the crew's melee competence)
    - The unit's **BS** is treated as **3** (the Wall Warden's professional eye improves scatter accuracy)
    - The unit's **Res** is treated as **7** (the Wall Warden's command experience steadies the levy)
    - The unit may **once per battle, reroll one Artillery Die** (any roll — for scatter distance, MISFIRE check, or the misfire-table D6 result)

**Weapon profiles:**

- **Field Trebuchet** *(ranged, indirect-fire stone-thrower; Crew fire once per Shooting phase)* — **12-60" range | Indirect Fire (see §8) | Small round template (3" diameter) | Scatter (Artillery Die) (see §8) | Multiple Wounds (D6) on direct hit** — fired via the **Stone Thrower** mechanic (below). One shot per Shooting phase. Crew must be alive (W > 0)
- **Hand weapon** *(Crew melee)* — 1" reach | **A 3** (or A 4 with Reinforced Crew) | S 3 | AP 0 | D 1 — fixed melee attack count, does not decrement with wounds `[1H Blade]`

**Stone Thrower mechanic** *(new ranged weapon rule — Field Trebuchet and similar template-firing siege engines)*:

1. Declare a target point within the Trebuchet's range (12-60") and forward 90° arc. The target point must be **within 12"** at minimum (the boulder cannot be lobbed shorter than that — it's a high arc; the counterweight requires distance to swing) and **within 60"** at maximum.
2. **No line of sight required** (Indirect Fire) — the Trebuchet may fire over hills, walls, friendly units, intervening enemy units, or any other obstruction. Target point must be visible *to the player* on the table; the Trebuchet does not need LiS.
3. Place the **3" round template** centred on the declared target point.
4. Roll the **Scatter Die** + **Artillery Die** for scatter (per §8 Scatter):
   - **HIT** — template lands exactly on the declared point
   - **Arrow** — template scatters the rolled Artillery Die distance in the indicated direction. Subtract the Crew's BS (3) from the scatter distance, minimum 0
5. Resolve hits on every model whose base is touched by the template:
   - **Direct hit (the model under the centre hole of the template)** — takes a hit at **S 10 | AP -3 | D D6 | Multiple Wounds** — the boulder smashes the centremost target. Multiple Wounds (D6) means a single failed save inflicts D6 wounds, capable of crushing Monsters and Lord-tier characters in a single shot
   - **Splash (other models touched by the template)** — each takes a hit at **S 3 | AP -1 | D 1** — debris and shrapnel from the boulder's impact. S 3 wounds T 3 on 4+, T 4 on 5+, T 5 on 6+, fails to wound T 7+ — splash is meaningful against unarmoured infantry but irrelevant against Monsters and heavy cavalry, where the central hit does the real work
6. **MISFIRE result on Artillery Die** — no shot fired this phase. Roll **D6** on the Trebuchet Misfire Table:
   - **1** — Counterweight catastrophic failure. Trebuchet destroyed. The unit is removed (all remaining wounds lost)
   - **2-3** — Frame damaged (struck by snapping ropes or falling timber). Cannot fire next Shooting phase. **D3 wounds** inflicted on the unit (no Ward, no Armour)
   - **4-5** — Trebuchet jammed (rope snarl, beam slipped from cradle). Cannot fire next Shooting phase. No wounds taken
   - **6** — Close call (release misaligned, shot wasted but no damage). Trebuchet operable next phase; no wounds taken

> **Lore notes:** **Second War Machine in the ruleset, after the Empire Great Cannon (§14).** Validates the §14 War Machine unit-type convention generalises across factions — single unified profile, W 5 wound pool with no decrement, dual T (ranged / melee), pushable but not chargeable. Where the Great Cannon is a **direct-fire bounce-line** weapon (declare direction, line lands, bounces), the Trebuchet is an **indirect-fire template** weapon (declare point, template lands, scatters). Different mechanical archetype within the same unit-type framework.
>
> **Indirect Fire is the headline upside.** No LOS required — the Trebuchet drops shots over hills, walls, intervening units. Tactically devastating: enemy can't hide behind terrain, can't break LOS by interposing screens. The Bretonnian player can position the Trebuchet behind friendly units (or even behind a hill) and bombard the enemy's deployment zone unseen. The 12" minimum range prevents anti-melee close defence (the Trebuchet cannot drop a boulder on a unit charging it within 12") — short-range engagement is the Trebuchet's structural weakness.
>
> **Peasant Crew is the structural downside.** WS 2 / **BS 2** / Res 6 (vs Empire Engineers' WS 3 / BS 3 / Res 8) — peasant crew is genuinely weaker in melee, scatter accuracy, and morale. A unit getting into melee with the Trebuchet has a much easier time vs Bretonnian peasants than vs Imperial Engineers. The Wall Warden upgrade (+10 pts) closes most of the gap (raises WS to 3, BS to 3, Res to 7, adds the Artillery Die reroll), but base Trebuchet without Wall Warden is genuinely fragile to melee assault and slightly less accurate on scatter.
>
> **Stone Thrower template mechanic** establishes the template-fire pattern for future templated weapons (Mortars, Bombards, Skink Salamander Spit-Fire if reframed). Direct hit at S 10 / AP -3 / D D6 / Multiple Wounds is brutally effective against any Monster, Lord, or armoured target. **Splash at S 3 / AP -1 / D 1** covers the surrounding area in shrapnel — wounds T 3 on 4+, T 4 on 5+, fails to wound T 7+. Useful against unarmoured infantry hordes (a 3" template covers ~6-8 infantry models in tight formation) but irrelevant against Monsters, heavy cavalry, and any T 5+ target — the splash is *area-denial against soft targets*, not *AOE damage against hardened ones*. The dual-strength profile (10 direct vs 3 splash) gives the Trebuchet two distinct tactical roles: precision boulder against priority targets, light area-denial shrapnel against blocks.
>
> **W 5 wound pool, no crew loss — the all-or-nothing tactical profile.** The Trebuchet operates at full output (single shot per Shooting phase, A 3 melee, full BS / WS / Res) until W reaches 0, at which point the machine collapses entirely. This decouples crew-attrition from output-degradation — less thematic than WAP's "as crew die, the gun fires worse," but mechanically cleaner and tactically distinctive. The Trebuchet at W 1 fires at the same effectiveness as W 5; the player who fails to remove the engine entirely gets full bombardment value. This creates a **first-turn priority** dynamic: if your artillery shoots first and lands wounds, you might silence the enemy's; if their artillery shoots first, yours dies. Mutual destruction at the artillery layer becomes a tactical layer in itself.
>
> **MISFIRE table** mirrors the Great Cannon's pattern under the new wound-pool convention — 1 destroys (machine removed), 2-3 damages with **D3 wounds inflicted on the unit** (no Ward, no Armour — internal damage), 4-5 jams without harm, 6 close call. The D3-wounds-from-MISFIRE-roll-2-3 result is meaningful — it can chain into a destroyed Trebuchet on subsequent rolls if the wound pool is already low. The Wall Warden's reroll-one-Artillery-Die ability is a meaningful safety net against MISFIRE — save it for the moment a MISFIRE result threatens, or for a critical scatter roll on a high-value target.
>
> **Wall Warden as the Trebuchet's "Engineer-equivalent."** Functionally the +10 upgrade closes most of the gap to Imperial Engineer crew quality — WS 3, BS 3, Res 7, Artillery Die reroll. The Wall Warden persists for the duration of the battle (no individual crew model removal under the new wound-pool convention) — once active, the upgrade's benefits last until the Trebuchet is destroyed. At +10 pts it brings the Trebuchet's effective price to 120, slightly above the Great Cannon's 110 base. The +10 over Cannon reflects the Trebuchet's headline advantage (Indirect Fire, no LOS required) outweighing the Cannon's headline advantage (Imperial Engineers reroll baseline + better crew baseline). Roughly equivalent unit at the upgraded tier.
>
> **Class Barrier applies** — a Bretonnian Lord cannot shelter from sniping by standing behind the Trebuchet Crew. Knights can be near (within 6" for Peasant's Duty aura) but lose Character Attachment shielding when within Crew coherency. Same restriction as all peasant units.
>
> **Points 110 base** — matches Empire Great Cannon's 110, both +10 over WAP's 100 baseline. The Trebuchet is intentionally priced at parity with the Cannon despite different mechanical profile (indirect template vs direct-line bounce, peasant crew vs engineer crew, Multiple Wounds D6 vs D D6). Wall Warden upgrade brings effective cost to 120 with Artillery Die reroll. **Reinforced Crew** (+5 pts) raises W to 6 and melee A to 4 — modest durability and melee defence boost. Maximum loadout: 110 + 5 (Reinforced Crew) + 10 (Wall Warden) = **125 pts** for a W 6 / A 4 / WS 3 / BS 3 / Res 7 Trebuchet with Artillery Die reroll — the top-tier siege configuration.
>
> **Compare:**
> - **Empire Great Cannon (110 pts)** — direct-fire bounce-line, S 10 AP -5 D D6 along the line, Engineer Crew (WS 3 / BS 3 / Res 8), Imperial Engineers reroll baseline. Better crew, requires LOS to target zone
> - **Field Trebuchet (110 pts base)** — indirect-fire template, S 10 (direct) / S 3 (splash), Peasant Crew (WS 2 / BS 2 / Res 6), Wall Warden upgrade for reroll. Worse crew, no LOS required
> - **Future faction artillery** (Mortar, Bombard, Skaven Warpfire Thrower, Dwarf Cannon) — extends naturally from the §14 War Machine + Stone Thrower / Bounce Line patterns
