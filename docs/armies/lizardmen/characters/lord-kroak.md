# Lord Kroak


> The Venerable Lord Kroak — Defender of Itza, eldest of the First Spawning, the most ancient and powerful of all the Slann. Mortally torn apart by Bloodthirsters at the Defence of Itza in -4450 IC, his spirit refused to concede defeat: it scoured the invaders with a divine light "like unto a second sun" and saved the First City. His Skink attendants gathered every scrap of his ruined body and wrapped it in resin-soaked bandages. Thus was created the first Relic Priest. His husk lies in hidden crypt at Itza, brought forth only in moments of greatest need. He was, without doubt, the strongest caster ever to live; in his half-state between life and death, that power is only a shadow of its former glory — yet still strong enough that Kroak could simply choose to be alive again, were he so inclined. He is, however, more often in a slumbering state.

*WS-A 0 / WS-D 0 — Kroak is a mummified husk on a palanquin, surrounded by spirits. He doesn't dodge; he doesn't dueling-strike; he doesn't even register the battle physically. Combat is something done **at** him. The spirits and intrinsic relics fight on his behalf via fixed-2+ to-hit weapon profiles, the same pattern as the generic Slann's Palanquin Lightning.*

- **Special rules:**
  - Cold-Blooded (see §8)
  - **Unbreakable** (see §8) — does not accumulate stress, cannot become Shaken / Wavering / Broken; includes Imm Psych. The spirit-bound husk is functionally undead-adjacent: stress, fear, panic, terror — all irrelevant to a being that already died. Lore-direct: Kroak's spirit "refused to let even death hinder him."
  - **Inspiring Presence (30")** — extended even beyond the Slann's 24"; Kroak's psychic projection covers an entire battle line
  - **Miscast Immunity** *(identity rule)* — Lord Kroak ignores all Miscasts. When he would suffer a miscast result on a casting roll (per §7.3 Miscasts), no miscast effect occurs — he simply cannot be backlashed by his own magic. Captures the lore-canonical apex-caster framing: "the strongest caster ever, when alive, and he could just choose to be alive again, such strong is he." Reality has stopped offering Kroak miscast consequences.
  - **Hypnotic Death Mask** *(intrinsic — Golden Death Mask)* — all attacks targeting Kroak in melee suffer **-1 to hit**. The mask's gem-eyes hold the gaze of any who look upon it.
  - **Mace of Malachite** *(intrinsic)* — magical weapons used in melee against Kroak have their AP reduced to 0 (capped). The Mace's residual enchantment negates the magic of weapons that strike at him.
  - **Glyph of Potec** *(intrinsic)* — Kroak's intrinsic weapon attacks (Mace, Serpent Standard, Guardian Spirits) deal **+1 Damage** against any unit with the **Undead** keyword.
  - **Amulet of Itza** *(intrinsic)* — Kroak gains **+1 to all Dispel rolls**.

> **Lore notes:** The Venerable Lord Kroak — Defender of Itza, the most ancient and powerful Slann ever to live, mortally torn apart by Bloodthirsters at the Defence of Itza in -4450 IC and refusing to concede defeat. His husk lies in resin-soaked bandages and is brought forth only in moments of greatest need. **WS-A 0 / WS-D 0** is lore-direct: the mummified husk doesn't fight, doesn't dodge — combat is something done *at* him, and the spirits and intrinsic relics fight back via fixed-2+ profiles. The strongest caster ever to live: **LA 4 Master across all 10 lores** with **SK 10** spells picked freely at list-build, DR 48". **Miscast Immunity** is the apex-caster identity — reality has stopped offering Kroak miscast consequences; lore-direct "could simply choose to be alive again, were he so inclined." **Unbreakable** replaces Stubborn — Kroak functions like undead in every meaningful way (mummified, spirit-bound, stress-immune, fear-immune); he just isn't *technically* Undead by keyword.
