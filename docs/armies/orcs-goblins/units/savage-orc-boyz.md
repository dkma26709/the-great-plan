# Savage Orc Boyz

> The frenzied-cult branch of the Orc sub-species. Savage Orcs are tribal Orcs who reject worn armour entirely — they paint their bodies in fungal-mineral pigments that channel a thin biological-ritual ward against incoming blows (**War-Paint — Ward 6+**), and they throw themselves into combat in a state of permanent battle-frenzy (**Frenzy**). They look the same as Common Boyz on the stat line — same S 4, T 4, W 2, MA 4, MD 3 — but they fight at the F4 fingerprint: +1 A, +1 MA, -1 MD, must charge, must follow up, no disengagement. The cross-faction parallel is the Druchii Witch Elves: glass-cannon attackers whose defence is *not* armour but a single layer of ritual / dodge that thinks it can keep the wielder alive long enough to swing first. Savage Orcs are the Orc-tier version — tougher than Witch Elves (T 4 / W 2 vs T 3 / W 1) but slower, less skilled, and more committed once the Frenzy locks in. They are the unit you take when you've decided combat is going to happen and you want the most attacks per point you can buy, even if they only swing for one or two rounds before the Frenzy breaks.

## Special Rules

- Undisciplined
- **Animosity** *(Greenskin-wide faction trait — see §8 Psychology)*. D6 at start of each Movement phase when out of combat; on a 1, sub-roll D6 determines charge / self-hits. *(Savage Orc manifestation: tribal posturing — chest-beating war-chants that overflow into actual brawls. Mechanically identical to Common Orc Animosity.)*
- **Loves a Scrap** *(Orc-specific faction trait — see §8 Psychology)*. Removes 1 stress per Combat phase engaged in melee; +1 A on a single weapon profile if engaged at end of previous turn.
- **Frenzy** *(Psychology — see §8)*. The Savage Orc is in permanent battle-rage from the moment the army deploys. F4 fingerprint: +1 A on a single weapon profile, +1 MA, -1 MD, must charge nearest visible enemy if able, no voluntary disengagement, must follow up + pursue, Immune to Psychology, suppresses state effects of Wavering and Broken. **Frenzy is lost permanently** when the unit loses a combat round (strict loss; ties don't count) or is forced to flee while Broken. Once lost, the unit reverts to baseline Boyz-tier combat output but retains War-Paint.
- **War-Paint** *(Savage Orc unit identity rule)*. Each model is daubed in fungal-mineral war-paint pigments that channel a thin biological-ritual ward against incoming blows. **The unit has a Ward Save of 6+** against every wound suffered (per §8 Ward Save — unmodified D6 roll against each wound that gets past Armour Save and Natural Armour). Always-on, no conditions, no degradation. Applies in melee, in ranged, in magic, in everything. Lore: the paint is the ritual; the body must be bare for the magic to take hold (see locked no-armour equipment below).

## Options

**No worn armour options.** Savage Orcs reject worn armour as a cultural identity — the War-Paint Ward requires bare painted skin. The unit cannot take Light Armour, Medium Armour, Heavy Armour, or any other worn-armour kit.

**No shield option.** The shield strap would cover the war-paint and disable the Ward. Savage Orcs fight two-handed or with paired weapons; the shield slot is locked out.

**Equipment options (pick one):**
- **Additional Choppa** (+2 pt/model) — second hand weapon; A 2 baseline before Frenzy and Loves a Scrap stacks
- **Spear** (+1 pt/model) — 2" reach; (note: shield-pairing clause on the Spear weapon profile is irrelevant — Savage Orcs have no shield option)
- **Great Choppa** (+2 pt/model) — replaces hand weapon, two-handed; S+2 (S 6), AP -1, -1 I when used

**Command options:**
- **Boss (Leader)** (+5 pts) — Savage Orc champion
- **Standard Bearer** (+10 pts, may take Magic Standard up to 25 pts)
- **Musician** (+5 pts)
