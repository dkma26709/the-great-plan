# Dwarf Warriors

> The clan-warriors of the dwarf holds — every adult male dwarf is one, trained in axe and shield from boyhood. They wear well-crafted armour (every dwarf can make his own), wield well-forged weapons (every weapon a tool used as a weapon when needed), and march in solid formation under their thane's banner. Dwarfs do not seek glory — they seek to **repay grudges**, to hold the line, to grind the enemy down through patience and superior craftsmanship. The Book of Grudges remembers every offense ever committed against dwarfkind across millennia, and a dwarf in battle is reading from that book in real time. **Ancestral Grudge** captures the faction-wide background hatred — Hatred reserved for the specific traditional foes (Greenskins, Skaven, Trolls, Beastmen) who have wronged dwarfkind through history. **Stubborn** is the iconic dwarf morale: they ignore the indignities of Shaken and Wavering, fighting at full effectiveness until they finally shatter. **Resolute** is the morale-floor on top — when the line falters, dwarfs hold a half-step longer than other races would. And **Grudgebearer** is the Book of Grudges made manifest at the unit level: a clan-warrior reads a name from the book before the battle begins, and that name does not leave their mind until the bearer of it is dead.

## Special Rules

- Disciplined
- **Stubborn** *(Dwarf faction-wide identity rule — see §8)* — Dwarf units ignore the state effects of **Shaken** (-1 to-hit) and **Wavering** (cannot-charge). Stress still accumulates normally and the **Broken** threshold still applies; the unit fights at full effectiveness until it finally shatters. Iconic dwarf stoicism — the underground city's iron resolve made manifest in every clan-warrior. Faction-wide for Dwarfs (vs Empire's Greatswords-only application) reflects WFB lore: every dwarf is stoic at heart, and Hammerers / Ironbreakers / Longbeards differentiate above this baseline via *other* rules (Killing Blow, heavy plate, etc.) rather than via Stubborn itself.
- **Ancestral Grudge** *(Dwarf faction-wide identity rule)* — Dwarf models gain **Hatred** (see §8) against units with the **Greenskin**, **Skaven**, **Troll**, or **Beastmen** keyword. Hatred lets the unit reroll failed to-Hit rolls in the first round of combat against Hated targets. The Book of Grudges made manifest as a faction-wide background — dwarfs strike harder against the races that have wronged them across millennia.
- **Resolute** *(Dwarf-wide faction trait — see §8 Psychology)*. -1 to combat-resolution-loss-to-stress conversion (minimum 0). The phalanx absorbs the indignity of losing slightly better than other races.
- **Magic Resistance (1)** *(Dwarf faction-wide species trait — see §8 and §1.5 Dwarfs species reference)* — Dwarfs are **naturally resistant to magic**. Enemy spells targeting or affecting any Dwarf unit suffer **-1 to the casting roll**. The dwarven body and soul reject the Winds; spells skip off them more readily than they would mortal humans. Faction-wide species baseline; every drafted Dwarf unit carries this rule (elite tiers may scale to MR 2 if lore warrants).
- **Grudgebearer** *(Dwarf Warriors unit identity rule)* — at the start of each game (during deployment, before the first turn), the Dwarf player **nominates one enemy unit as a recorded grudge**. Dwarf Warriors gain **Hatred against the nominated unit for the entire game** — not just the first round of combat (per standard Hatred), but **every round** in which they fight that unit, until the nominated unit is destroyed. The Book of Grudges made manifest at the unit level: a clan-warrior reads a name from the book before the battle begins, and that name does not leave their mind until the bearer of it is dead. Tactical decision at deployment: the Dwarf player picks the enemy unit they most want to grind down — could be a non-traditional foe (Bretonnian Knights, Empire Knights, Slann) where Ancestral Grudge wouldn't apply, or doubles down on a Greenskin/Skaven Hated target for sustained reroll-to-Hit across the whole game.
