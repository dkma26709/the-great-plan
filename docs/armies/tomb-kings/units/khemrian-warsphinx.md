# Khemrian Warsphinx

> Stone-bodied lion-sphinxes forged by Khemrian artisans in the elder days, then animated by Liche Priest necromancy when their kings rose from the sands of Nehekhara at the call of the Mortuary Cult. The Warsphinx walks on four massive paws, bears Tomb Guard riders in its howdah-shaped back, and ends battles with the **Thundercrush Attack** — a hammer-blow of stone limb that pulps any infantry unfortunate enough to be standing where it lands. Its hide is ancient sandstone, magically reinforced; its eyes glow with the fire of awakened tomb-magic. Where Vampire Counts undead are *dead bodies* puppeted by death-magic, Tomb Kings undead are often *constructs* — stone, bronze, gold, animated by the priests' rituals — and the Warsphinx is the apex of that construct tradition. **Stone Construct** captures the immutable monster-construct nature: no stress, no fear, no fall-back; it advances and crushes until destroyed. **Thundercrush Attack** is the iconic Warsphinx capability — a once-per-Combat-phase exchange of all normal attacks for one devastating stomp.

## Special Rules

- Disciplined
- **Undead** *(Tomb Kings faction-wide identity rule — abbreviated stress-test definition)* — Tomb Kings undead are **Immune to Psychology** (per §8), do not take stress from any morale source, cannot Fall Back from combat (they advance until destroyed), and are subject to a **Crumble** mechanic similar to Vampire Counts (when a Liche Priest or Tomb King anchor character within 12" is killed, Undead units in range take +1 stress per turn until restored — full Tomb Kings drafting would specify the anchor mechanic). Stress from combat wounds and direct spell damage still applies normally.
- **Terror** *(per §8)* — units charged by the Warsphinx, or that charge the Warsphinx, gain **+2 stress** on the turn the engagement happens (per standard Terror rule).
- **Magical Attacks (Tomb Guard Crew only)** *(per §8)* — the crew's weapon profile attacks count as Magical Attacks; the Warsphinx's body attacks are *not* Magical (the construct is animated, not enchanted-edge). Useful against Ethereal targets.
- **Killing Blow (Tomb Guard Crew only)** *(per §8)* — on natural to-Wound rolls of 6 against models with W 1, the Tomb Guard Crew slay the model outright (no save). Limited to the crew's attacks; the Warsphinx's body attacks do not have Killing Blow.
- **Stone Construct** *(Khemrian Warsphinx unit identity rule)* — the Warsphinx is a magically-animated stone construct. It gains: (a) **Imm to Killing Blow** (per §8 — stone has no joints to sever); (b) **Imm to Poisoned Attacks** as an incoming-rule (the construct has no flesh to poison; this Imm applies to the body, not to the crew); (c) **does not crumble while a Liche Priest model is on the battlefield** (the priest's animation magic sustains the construct directly, bypassing the standard Tomb Kings Crumble dynamic for as long as a priest lives).
- **Thundercrush Attack** *(Khemrian Warsphinx unit identity rule)* — once per Combat phase, the Warsphinx may **exchange all of its normal Maw/Claws attacks** for a single Thundercrush Attack. Resolution: roll one to-Hit against the highest MD among enemy models in base contact with the Warsphinx. If the hit lands, place the **small round template** anywhere touching the Warsphinx's base. Every model under the template that has the **Infantry**, **War Beast**, or **Swarm** keyword (friend or foe) suffers a single **S 3, AP 0, D 1** hit. The model under the template's centre instead suffers a single **S 9, AP -3, D D3** hit. Models with the **Cavalry**, **Monster**, **Monstrous Infantry**, **Monstrous Cavalry**, or **Chariot** keyword are unaffected — Thundercrush is anti-infantry-block by design; against monsters and large units, the Warsphinx fights with its normal Maw/Claws attacks. Stomp attacks (per §8 Stomp — if our system grants Stomp to large monsters) still resolve normally; Thundercrush is in addition to or instead of Maw/Claws, but does not replace Stomp.
