# Terrawings


> A flock of small reptilian flyers — the canopy's swift hunters, barely larger than a Skink's torso. Sharp-beaked, quick-winged, directed by Skink handlers as a mobile flurry of distraction. Not strong enough to break a line; sharp enough to ruin a cannon crew's day, foul a wizard mid-cast, or spoil a skilled swordsman's timing.

- **Keywords:** Lizardmen, War Beast, Core

- **No armour save** — too small and frail for meaningful protection
- **Equipment:** none (natural weapons)
- **Command:** none
- **Special rules:**
  - **Fly (12)** (see §8)
  - **Strider (Forest)** (see §8)
  - Skittish (see §8)
  - **Flock Disruption** — any enemy unit in base contact with this unit suffers **-1 WS** and **-1 BS** while the contact persists. Flapping wings, biting beaks, and constant movement distract combatants and foul aim — the enemy cannot concentrate on either sword or bow. Affects all enemy attack rolls from engaged models (melee from the contacted unit, and any ranged attack those models attempt).

> **Lore notes:** M2 on the ground reflects their awkward walking gait — they are creatures of the air, not the forest floor. Fly (12) is at the top of the flying scale; only a few Monsters exceed it. WS2 keeps them frail combatants in their own right; they do not *defeat* enemies, they *disrupt* them. Res3 means they buckle under the slightest pressure — 7 pts per base reflects both the disruption identity and the short expected lifespan. The combined -1 WS / -1 BS debuff from Flock Disruption is the identity rule: engage a Terrawings flock and you cannot fight cleanly against anything else. Cheap enough to be expendable; annoying enough that the enemy wants them gone *right now*.
