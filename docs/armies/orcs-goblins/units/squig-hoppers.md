# Squig Hoppers

> Night Goblins mounted on Cave Squigs — the most mechanically chaotic Cavalry unit in the Greenskin roster, and the most likely to end the battle by accident. Cave Squigs are pure aggression strapped to a leg-and-mouth biology: they bounce uncontrollably forward, biting anything they land near, and the Goblin "rider" (lashed to the Squig's back with rope) clings on and stabs at whatever he can reach without falling off. The unit has **no fixed Movement** — instead, **Random Movement (3D6")**: roll 3D6" each Movement phase, pivot once before the move, then bounce that distance directly forward. The roll can produce a 3" stumble or an 18" cross-table launch; the player has no control over which. Where Wolf Riders are *light cavalry* (M 9, Fast Cav, harass-and-retreat) and Spider Riders are *terrain-warfare cavalry* (M 7, Wall-crawler, poison + venom), Squig Hoppers are *anti-tactical cavalry* — they go where the dice say they go, hit what they land near, and contribute massive Squig-mouth damage if they get there. A pack of Squig Hoppers in the right position is devastating (S 5 / A 2 bite per Squig); in the wrong position they're cresting an enemy unit they were aimed three inches the other way from.

## Special Rules

- Undisciplined
- **Animosity** *(Greenskin-wide faction trait — see §8 Psychology)*. D6 at start of each Movement phase when out of combat; on a 1, **Night Goblin sub-tribe Mushroom Madness** sub-table applies (see Night Goblins profile / §8 Animosity sub-tribe variants).
- **Stab in the Back** *(Goblin-typical — see §13 *Goblins*)*. **Applies to both melee and ranged attacks.** +1 to Wound against any target being attacked in its rear arc *(melee only)* or currently Shaken or Wavering. Does not stack with itself.
- **Cowardly Numbers** *(Goblin-typical — see §13 *Goblins*)*. While this unit has 20+ models, it is Stubborn. Below half starting strength, -1 Res for the rest of the battle. *(Stubborn-clause functionally dormant at Cav unit sizes; the -1 Res clause is the active fragility tax.)*
- **No Loves a Scrap** *(Goblin sub-species — see §13)*.
- **Random Movement (3D6")** *(per §8)*. The unit has **no fixed M value**. Each Movement phase: pivot once (free), roll 3D6", move that distance directly forward. **Cannot March, Advance, or Rally** in the standard sense — the roll replaces all movement actions. **May charge** if the rolled distance would reach an enemy directly in front; the rolled distance replaces the standard M + D6 charge range. Stops 1" short of any friendly unit or impassable terrain it would otherwise contact. The Squig Hopper is **never Formed** — it lacks the discipline.
- **No Fast Cavalry rule.** Random Movement is the unit's mobility identity; the Fast Cavalry package (Vanguard, free pivots beyond the single Random-Movement pivot, Flee reaction) does **not** apply. The Squig bouncing IS the unit's mobility.

## Options

**Worn armour options:** Light armour (+1 pt/model) → rider 6+ save (no mount NA to stack with)

**Equipment swap options (replace Hand Weapon, pick at most one):**
- **Spear** (+1 pt/model) — 1H, 2" reach, replaces Hand Weapon. Useful for stabbing past the bouncing Squig's mouth and reaching whatever the Squig has landed near. *(No charge bonus — Random Movement does not produce a controlled charge in the cavalry-spear sense; the rider is along for the ride.)*

**No shield option.** The Goblin rider is lashed to the Squig; the off-hand grips the rope and the weapon. No room for a shield.

**No shortbow option.** Firing accurately from a bouncing Squig is impossible.

**Command options:**
- **Boss (Leader)** (+3 pts) — champion
- **Standard Bearer** (+6 pts, may take Magic Standard up to 25 pts)
- **Musician** (+3 pts)

## Weapon Profiles

### Close Combat

- **Hand Weapon** *(rider, default, 1H)* — 1" reach | **A 1** | S 3 | AP 0 | D 1 — short blade or club. The rider stabs at whatever the Squig has landed near while clinging on with the other hand. `[1H Blade]`
- **Spear** *(rider, option, 1H — replaces Hand Weapon)* — 2" reach | **A 1** | S 3 | AP 0 | D 1 — long jabbing spear; the 2" reach lets the rider hit past the Squig's mouth without leaning out and falling off. **No cavalry charge bonus** — Random Movement does not provide the controlled-spear-charge tempo of standard cavalry. `[1H Reach]`
- **Massive Mouth** *(mount, natural — always available)* — 1" reach | **A 2** | S 5 | AP 0 | D 1 — Cave Squig's enormous fanged maw bites twice per round. The A 2 reflects the Squig's two-stage chomp + lunge; S 5 captures the raw biological strength of the jaw. AP 0 — the Squig's teeth tear flesh but don't bite cleanly through plate armour. Cross-faction reference: vs Wolf Bite (A 1 S 3) the Squig Mouth puts out roughly 2.5× the damage; vs Spider Bite (A 2 S 3 Poisoned) the Squig Mouth has the same A but +2 S in exchange for no poison. `[Natural]`
