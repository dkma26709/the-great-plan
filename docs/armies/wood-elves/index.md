# Wood Elves


> The Asrai of Athel Loren. Descendants of the High Elves who chose to remain in the Old World and bonded across generations to the living forest. Biologically still *elves* — same long-lifespan slim physiology, same Elven Grace as the Asur and Druchii — but the cultural axis has warped sharply. Where Asur train for ten millennia in formation, where Druchii channel Khainite ritual into the killing blow, the Asrai *hunt*: forest-rangers, ambush-archers, kite-and-fade specialists. Their bond to Athel Loren is total — *time flows unevenly* within the forest's borders, the trees themselves take the field as Forest Spirits, and the Wild Hunt rides under Orion's banner when the Asrai go to war. *"Neither truly good nor evil"* — the Asrai pay homage to **Orion (King of the Wood, avatar of Kurnous the Hunter)** and **Ariel (Queen of the Wood, avatar of Isha the Mother)**, and the forest itself fights alongside them as a faction-anchor.

## Status

**Currently classed as §18 Cross-Faction Reference** — a single representative unit (Glade Guard) at present, with full-faction promotion deferred until the roster is broader. This document is the design reference for future Asrai unit drafts: it captures the philosophy, axes, and constraints that should hold across the faction so each unit's design loop starts from a shared baseline.

**Drafted units:** [Glade Guard](units/glade-guard.md), [Dryads](units/dryads.md), [Great Stag Knights](units/great-stag-knights.md).

## Cultural axis

Within the elf species, three sub-factions share biology but warp the cultural axis differently:

| Sub-faction | Cultural identity | MA / MD axis | Prowess channel | Recovery trait |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Asur** (High Elves) | Disciplined citizen-soldiers, ten-millennia drill, warrior-cults at elite tier | **MA ≈ MD** (disciplined-balanced) | Martial Prowess (melee accuracy) | **Disciplined** baseline |
| **Druchii** (Dark Elves) | Khainite raiders, frenzied bloodlust, slave-society militarism | **MA > MD** (frenzied-attacker) | Murderous Prowess (melee lethality) | Often Frenzy / cult-specific |
| **Asrai** (Wood Elves) | Forest-rangers, ambush-hunters, kite-and-fade | **MA ≤ MD** (skirmisher-evader) | **Hunter's Prowess (ranged accuracy)** | Neutral baseline (no Disciplined, no Undisciplined) |

The Asrai sit at the skirmisher-evader pole. Their elites do **not** scale offensively by stacking attacks (Druchii direction) or by formation drill (Asur direction) — they scale via *better archery*, *fluid evasion*, and *bond with the forest*. Asrai are the only elf sub-faction that channels prowess into the **ranged** axis; the bow is the faction's defining weapon, not the sword.

## Biological constraints

The shared elf-species ceiling applies to Asrai (per §13):

- **T 3 / W 1 at rank-and-file** — never raised by elf-cultural rules.
- **No Natural Armour** — Asrai protection comes from worn light armour (where worn at all), Elven Grace, Forest Stride, and fluid evasion. No intrinsic hide.
- **Hero-tier W cap 3, Lord-tier W cap 4-5** — same as Asur and Druchii.
- **No Monstrous Infantry tier (elf body)** — elf-bodied units stay slim. Asrai reach into Monster-class via *mounts* (Forest Dragons, Great Eagles) and via the **Forest Spirit sub-tier**, which is biologically distinct.

Asrai-specific cultural constraints:

- **No heavy armour** — Wood Elves disdain plate; their warfare is mobility-and-bow, not armour-and-charge. Light armour is the rank-and-file cap; cult-elite tiers (Eternal Guard, Wild Riders) may wear nothing heavier.
- **No gunpowder, no engineered war machines** — Asrai reject mortal-industrial technology. The closest faction equivalent is a Treeman — a *living* siege engine.
- **No formed-line combat tradition (mostly)** — Asrai are Skirmisher-keyword across most rank-and-file units. The **Glade Guard** are the lore-canonical exception (the deep-rank archer regiment), and **Eternal Guard** are the apex formed-line spear unit. Everything else (Wardancers, Waywatchers, Glade Riders) is Skirmisher.

## Faction-wide rules

All rank-and-file and most elite **Asrai elven** units carry the following at minimum (the rules are *biological* to the elf — Forest Spirits do **not** inherit them):

- **Elven Grace** (per §8 *Defence*) — Ward 6+ in melee vs attacks at the elf's I or lower. Cross-elf rule — shared with Asur and Druchii.
- **Hunter's Prowess** (per §8 *Combat*) — reroll to-Hit 1s with ranged attacks. **Asrai-specific.** The marksmanship axis of the elven prowess triad. Locked in 2026-05-15 with the Glade Guard draft.
- **Forest Stride** *(Asrai-distinct faction rule, working title — pending §8 lift)* — Wood Elves treat forest terrain as Open ground. No movement penalty, no Dangerous Terrain tests in dense undergrowth. The forest does not impede the forest-folk. Inline on unit profiles for now; will be lifted to §8 when the second Asrai unit is drafted (following the Resolute / Cold-Blooded promotion pattern).

**All Wood Elf-keyword units** (Asrai elves AND Forest Spirits, plus future Wood Elf cavalry and characters) carry the following faction-wide identity rule:

- **Asrai Way of War** *(Wood Elf faction-wide identity rule — added 2026-05-15)* — Wood Elves have been trained from earliest memory in the kite-and-fade discipline of the forest-warrior tradition (and Forest Spirits know the woodland equivalent intuitively). While in **Loose** state (per §6 *Formed, Loose, and Disordered*), this unit gains the **benefits of the Skirmisher** special rule (per §8): **Coherency 2", +1 Movement, Fire on the move, and Free pivots**. The Skirmisher rule's "Cannot Form" clause does **not** apply — Wood Elf units retain the option to adopt Formed state for rank discipline, command access, and depth-discipline. The rule is a *toggle*, not a permanent unit-class lock: the player chooses Formed or Loose at deployment and each subsequent activation, gaining the corresponding benefits. Captures the lore-canonical *"forest does not yield to those who march in step"* — the Asrai fight as the forest fights, fluid and dispersed when speed matters, formed and disciplined when the line must hold.

**Non-elf Asrai units** (Forest Spirits — Dryads, Tree Kin, Treemen; mounts — Forest Dragons, Great Eagles, Great Stags) carry the **Asrai** keyword (for faction-mechanic interactions) and inherit **Asrai Way of War**, but do **not** inherit the cross-elf biological rules (Elven Grace, Hunter's Prowess, Forest Stride). They carry their own species-appropriate identity rules (Dryads: Fear + Flammable + bark-NA + Forest Strider; Treemen: Magical + Forest Spirit; Great Stags: Frenzy on the charge; etc.).

## Elite-tier scaling rules

When scaling an Asrai elven unit above the rank-and-file baseline, the design ladder is:

1. **Increase BS first**, then MA / MD / I — the Asrai are *archers first*. Waywatchers reach BS 6, the apex sniper-archer.
2. **Increase A from 1 → 2** at cult-infantry tier. Wardancers and Wild Riders fit this pattern.
3. **Add one specialised offensive rule per cult** — Wardancers add Imm Fear/Terror + Frenzy-leaning dance-mechanics; Waywatchers add Scout + special arrows (Hagbane, Trueflight, etc.); Wild Riders add Wild Hunt mount-charge bonuses.
4. **Never increase T or W on the elf body.** Cap is biological.
5. **Light armour at most for rank-and-file**; cult-elite units (Wardancers) wear *no armour by ritual oath* (parallel to the Asur/Druchii cult exceptions).

The visible identity that should emerge: an Asrai elite is *more wild-bonded*, *better at archery*, or *deeper in the cult ritual* — not *more disciplined* or *more armoured*.

## Equipment patterns

- **Asrai Longbow** (30" / AP -1 / D 1, S follows wielder) — *the* apex longbow tradition in the codebase. Defines Glade Guard, Waywatchers, Glade Riders. AP -1 from elf-craft mithril arrow-tips; 30" range reflects the longbow's reach advantage; no S boost because the bow is wielder-powered (S 3 per the slim elf biology).
- **Magic Arrows** (Trueflight, Hagbane, Starfire, Swiftshiver, Moonfire) — unit-level upgrade options swapping the standard Asrai Longbow shot type. Each captures a distinct lore-flavour: Trueflight ignores to-Hit modifiers; Hagbane gains Poisoned; Starfire gains Flaming. Held as upgrade options on Glade Guard / Waywatcher / Glade Rider profiles; pending unified Magic Arrows subsystem treatment.
- **Asrai Spear** — Eternal Guard's signature weapon; the Asrai equivalent of an Asur Spear (1H reach 2"). Used in the King's-Guard formed-line role.
- **Paired Asrai Blades** — Wardancer cult kit; A 2 paired-weapon profile, no armour by ritual.
- **Light armour at most** — culturally; Asrai disdain heavy armour and plate. Most rank-and-file wear leather and cloth.
- **Hand weapon** — every Asrai carries a backup blade. Standard 1H profile.
- **No gunpowder, no war machines, no crossbows** — Asrai reject industrial weaponry. The bow is *the* ranged weapon.

## Magic

**Lore of Athel Loren** — Wood-Elf-exclusive faction lore drawing on the Weave of life and death flowing through the forest. Pending dedicated §11 drafting; expected to draw heavily from Lore of Life themes (regeneration, plant-summoning, healing) plus forest-specific spells (animating trees, calling the Wild Hunt, summoning Dryads onto the field).

**Spellweavers** (Lord-tier casters) and **Spellsingers** (Hero-tier) are the standard Wood Elf magical character. Magic is faction-anchor — Athel Loren itself contributes magical presence to any Wood Elf army on the field. **Ariel** is the apex Lord-caster (Isha-avatar, mistress of Athel Loren's magic).

- **Steeds of Isha** — Sisters of the Thorn's magical mount; apex Asrai magic cavalry.
- **Great Eagles** — shared with Asur; sacred birds of Athel Loren's high peaks. Fly + bird-of-prey rules pending.
- **Forest Dragons** — Athel-Loren-specific Dragon variant; apex Lord mount, magic-and-forest-bonded.
- **Orion** (named King-Lord, Kurnous-avatar) — apex Wood Elf Lord, Wild Hunt summoner.
- **Ariel** (named Queen-Lord, Isha-avatar) — apex Wood Elf caster-Lord, mistress of Athel Loren's magic.

| Comparator | Why it's useful |
|---|---|

Same 12 pts/model — same effective tier, different roles |
