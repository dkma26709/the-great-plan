# Lore of Shadow


> Stat manipulation, concealment, and misdirection. Where Death drains and Fire destroys, Shadow **warps reality** — stats waver, hits miss, units appear where they shouldn't be. The lore specialises in enemy stat reduction, -to-hit concealment on friendly units, minor displacement, and a narrow vein of initiative-test assassination. Flavour: silver and purple, mirrors, mist, whispered doubt. Not nihilistic like Death — Shadow is *trickster*. It wins fights the enemy didn't know they were having.

**Lore Attribute — Fading Step:** after this wizard successfully casts a Shadow spell, the caster may immediately make a **10" Fly move** (per §8 Fly — passes over models and terrain). The move is not a Charge and cannot end within 1" of an enemy unit, inside impassable terrain, or inside another model's base. Unlimited per turn — each successful cast is another fade. Shadow wizards are the most mobile casters in the game, flickering across the field between spells.

**Signature Spell — Melkoth's Mystifying Miasma**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 6 | 18" | Hex | Until start of next turn |

Caster chooses one of **WS, BS, I, or M**. Target enemy unit suffers **-D3** to that characteristic until start of next turn (minimum 1 — characteristics cannot drop below 1). Confusion fills their minds — swords miss their marks, legs falter, arrows fly wide. The workhorse Shadow cast — always available, flexible, softens any unit for what comes next.

#### Basic (Tier 1) — Lore Access 1+

**The Withering**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 7 | 18" | Hex | Until start of next turn |

Target enemy unit suffers **-1 T** (minimum 1) until start of next turn. The body forgets how to resist harm. Pairs viciously with any incoming damage — dropping T below an attacker's S turns grazing hits into wounds.

**Enfeebling Foe**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 7 | 18" | Hex | Until start of next turn |

Target enemy unit suffers **-1 S** (minimum 1) until start of next turn. The sword arm remembers it was tired; the mace weighs too much. Neuters a charging heavy-hitter — dropping a monstrous cavalry's S5 to S4 turns their impact into a scratch.

**Shroud of Shadow**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 6 | 18" | Augment | Until start of next turn |

Target friendly unit suffers **-1 to hit against it from ranged attacks** until start of next turn. A smoke of half-forms surrounds the unit — archers lose their marks. Distinct from Life's Leaf Fall (which is organic/living mist): this is pure shadow-illusion.

**Steed of Shadows**

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 7 | 12" | Augment, Instant |

Target **single friendly character** (not a unit, not a champion in a unit — solo models only) may immediately make a **12" Fly move**, ignoring terrain and models. The character cannot end within 1" of an enemy unit or inside impassable. Cannot declare a Charge as part of this move. The character fades into shadow and re-emerges somewhere useful. Paired with Fading Step, a Shadow wizard on foot can reposition over 20" in a single magic phase.

#### Intermediate (Tier 2) — Lore Access 2+

**Veil of Unreality**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 9 | 12" (aura) | Augment | Until start of next turn |

All friendly units with any model **within 12"** of the caster suffer **-1 to hit against them from ranged attacks** until start of next turn. A localised collapse of perception. Scales Shroud of Shadow up from single-unit to army-wide — protects the line, not the hero. Makes Shadow the premier anti-shooting lore.

**Pall of Hesitation**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 8 | 18" | Hex | Until start of next turn |

Target enemy unit gains **Always Strikes Last** (see §8) until start of next turn. The unit's strikes come a half-beat late, as if every swing is watched through smoked glass. Mechanically distinct from Miasma's numeric -D3 I — this is categorical, a unit with I10 still strikes last. Devastating on equal-I brawls where striking last loses the exchange before it starts.

**Shadow's Step**

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 8 | 18" | Augment, Instant |

Target friendly unit may immediately make a **D6" Fly move** (not a Charge, 1" rule, no impassable). The unit must end in a legal formation. Cannot be cast on a unit that has already declared a Charge this turn. A small unit-scale reposition — step a block out of a charge lane, slide a flank into threat range, fade a vulnerable unit behind cover. Shorter than Steed of Shadows (T1, character, 12") but unit-scale.

#### Advanced (Tier 3) — Lore Access 3+

**Pendulum of Shadow** *(line template)*

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 11 | Special (line) | Direct Damage, Template |

The caster selects a direction. A narrow line **18"** long extends from the caster's base in that direction. Each **enemy model** touched by the line suffers **1 hit at S5, AP -2, damage D3**. **Magical Attacks.** Full line-template mechanics pending in §7.2. The lore's one damage tool — a scythe-swing that cuts through whatever formation stands in its path. Damage scales with how many models the line crosses: a tight 5-wide formation gets sliced down the centre; a lone character gets clipped once.

**Pit of Shades** *(large round template)*

| CV | Range | Type |
|----|-------|------|
| 13 | 18" | Direct Damage, Template |

Place a **large round template** anywhere within 18" of the caster; on initial placement, the template **scatters D6"** in a random direction (wider than Flame Storm/Purple Sun's D3 — Shadow magic is unreliable). Each model touched by the template must pass an **Initiative test** (roll a D6 — equal to or less than I passes). On a **failure**, that model suffers **1 wound with no armour save allowed** (ward saves permitted). **Magical Attacks.**

**Not Remains In Play** — this is a one-off strike, unlike Death's Purple Sun which persists. Thematic contrast with Purple Sun: Death targets T (the body gives up), Shadow targets I (the mind is too slow). High-I elves shrug it off; lumbering heavy infantry drops models in droves. Initiative can be softened first by Miasma (-D3 I) to turn a shrug into a massacre — the lore's core combo.

**Okkam's Mindrazor**

| CV | Range | Type | Duration |
|----|-------|------|----------|
| 12 | 18" | Augment | Until start of next turn |

Target friendly unit uses its **Ld value instead of S** for wound rolls in melee combat until start of next turn. Armour and ward saves are unchanged; only the wound roll is boosted. The unit's blades strike with the weight of their will — minds sharpened into swords.

A wild swing: S3 peasants with Ld 7 suddenly wound as if S7; a Loremaster character with Ld 10 wounds on 2+ against almost anything. Rewards well-led, high-Ld units (generals' regiments, veteran blocks) and makes an otherwise-average combat unit into a surgical cleaver for one brutal turn.

> **Design note on Shadow.** Shadow is the *stat-manipulation* lore. Across the 11 spells, it can push -D3 on WS/BS/I/M (Miasma) and -1 flat on S/T (Withering, Enfeebling Foe), plus categorical ASL (Pall of Hesitation), plus I-test assassination (Pit of Shades). Concealment is carried by Shroud of Shadow + Veil of Unreality (friendly -1 to hit from ranged, single-unit and aura). Displacement is minor — three spells total: Fading Step (caster), Steed of Shadows (character), Shadow's Step (unit). One damage spell (Pendulum), one augment-capstone (Mindrazor). Where Death drains, Shadow *makes the enemy's army work worse* — an army debuffed by three Shadow hexes in sequence can barely function in the combat phase.
