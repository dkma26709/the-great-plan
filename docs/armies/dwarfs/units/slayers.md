# Slayers

> *"I am a Dwarf! My honour is my life and without it I am nothing. I shall become a Slayer. I shall seek redemption in the eyes of my ancestors. I shall become as death to my enemies, until I face he that takes my life and my shame."* — the Slayer Oath, sworn at the Shrine of Grimnir at Karak Kadrin, the **Slayer Keep**. Slayers are Dwarfs whose honour has been irreparably stained — by failure, by loss of family or treasure, by personal tragedy — and who have sworn to fight until killed in glorious combat. The pilgrimage to Karak Kadrin involves ritual head-shaving to a solitary orange crest stiffened with pig grease ("the larger the crest, the deadlier the Dwarf, for the longer he has lived and fought as the crest grows"), tattoos to the Ancestor God **Grimnir**, and the forsaking of all armour. **Death Oath** is the unit-defining identity: Slayers wield axes, seek death, and **cannot be lost from Frenzy** — once the Oath is sworn the Slayer fights at maximum berserk fury until killed. **Slayer Strike** captures the cult's monster-hunting reputation: most Slayers die in their first or second fight, but the survivors take title-names from their kills — Trollslayer, **Giant Slayer**, **Dragon Slayer**, **Daemon Slayer** — and become "psychopathically dangerous" warriors who specialise in felling the largest foes any battlefield can muster. The unit fingerprint: no save, no discipline, but apex Dwarf brawn channelled into berserker fury and a Killing Blow that triggers on Trolls / Giants / Monstrous Cavalry / Monsters — the Slayer chooses the biggest target on the field, charges in, and either kills it or dies trying. Most do the latter. The few who survive are *legendary*.

## Special Rules

- **Ancestral Grudge** *(Dwarf faction-wide species trait — see §8 Combat: Hatred)*. Hatred (Greenskin / Skaven / Troll / Beastmen). Reroll failed to-Hit in first round of combat against the four traditional Dwarf enemies. *(The Slayer's Oath layers onto this; an offending Greenskin / Skaven / Troll / Beastman warrior is the most natural target for redemption.)*
- **Resolute** *(Dwarf faction-wide species trait — see §8 Psychology)*. -1 to combat-resolution-loss-to-stress conversion (minimum 0). Slayers absorb the indignity of losing combat as well as any other Dwarf — though for them, losing is *less of a problem* than winning, since combat-loss is one step closer to the death they seek.
- **Magic Resistance (1)** *(Dwarf faction-wide species trait — see §8 Defence)*. -1 to enemy cast rolls targeting this unit. The dwarven body and soul reject the Winds. Slayers inherit it from the species biology; the Oath doesn't change the dwarvish resistance to Wind-magic.
- **Death Oath** *(Slayer Cult unit identity rule)* — This unit has the **Unbreakable** and **Frenzy** special rules (per §8). Additionally, the Slayer's Frenzy **is not lost** on combat-loss or by any other rule that would normally strip Frenzy from a unit — the Oath holds the rage until the Slayer is physically destroyed. *(Mechanically: Slayers never accumulate stress, cannot become Shaken / Wavering / Broken, are Immune to Psychology, and permanently carry the F4 fingerprint — +1 A on a chosen weapon profile, +1 MA, -1 MD, must charge nearest visible enemy if able, may not voluntarily disengage, must Follow Up on enemy Fall Back, must pursue. The Oath is binding from deployment to death; the Slayer fights at maximum berserk fury throughout, full stop.)*
- **Slayer Strike** *(Slayer Cult unit identity rule)* — captures the lore-iconic monster-hunting role:
  - **+1 to-Wound** when this unit's attacks target a model with the **Monstrous Infantry**, **Monstrous Cavalry**, **Warbeast**, or **Monster** keyword. Always-on conditional offensive boost; the Slayer's training and obsession make him *better at killing large things* than any other Dwarf.
  - **Killing Blow (Large keyword, 2)** — on an unmodified to-Wound roll of **6** against a model with the **Monstrous Infantry**, **Monstrous Cavalry**, **Warbeast**, or **Monster** keyword, the attack deals **2 damage** instead of 1. Excess damage does not carry between models (per §8 Killing Blow). The perfect strike that puts down a Troll, a Giant, or a Dragon.
  - Combined effect: against non-Large targets, the Slayer fights at baseline; against Large keyword targets, the Slayer wounds more easily AND has a 1/6 spike-damage chance. Lore-iconic monster-hunter cult mechanic.

**Slayers do NOT carry Disciplined or Stubborn** — the Death Oath supersedes the disciplined-anvil Dwarf identity. The Slayer Cult is the explicit Dwarf-faction exception to the cult-trained discipline that defines other Dwarf units.

## Options

**No equipment options.** The Slayer Oath forbids worn armour, shields, and ranged weapons. The paired axe loadout is the cult's canonical kit; no kit upgrades or downgrades are available.

**No worn armour options.** Per the Oath — Slayers fight bare-chested with only the orange crest and tattoos as their identification.

**Command options:**
- **Slayer Champion (Leader)** (+4 pts) — older Slayer with a larger crest; cult-veteran. Often a Giant Slayer (the title earned for killing a Giant).
- **Musician** (+4 pts) — Slayer drummer, drumming up the cult's death-march cadence.

*No Standard Bearer.* Slayers operate without banners — the cult is solitary by nature, and the only honour worth carrying is the kill-tally tattooed on the body.
