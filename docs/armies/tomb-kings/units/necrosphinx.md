# Necrosphinx

> Animate stone construct in the shape of a strange amalgamation of mythical beasts said to inhabit the Nehekharan Underworld — torso and face of a man, sphinx-lion lower body, vestigial scarab wings folded along its back, and gigantic scything blade-arms "that can sever the neck of a Dragon in a single slice." Where the Khemrian Warsphinx is a *siege-engine* — a four-paw stone hammer that crushes infantry blocks under its weight — the Necrosphinx is a *headsman* — a patient ground-stalker with surgical reach, bred (or rather, *carved*) by the Mortuary Cult specifically to find and execute the enemy commander on the battlefield. *The Necrosphinx's wings are ceremonial / decorative on the field — the stone body is too massive for priest-magic to sustain genuine flight under battle conditions; the wings unfurl when the Mortuary Cult performs its reawakening rituals and fold inert during combat. Mechanically: no Fly rule.* King Settra is said to have ordered the Mortuary Cult to reawaken the ancient Necrosphinxes; "after a week-long magical ceremony, a dozen Necrosphinxes broke the surface from the desert," and where they marched, no enemy character survived to lead the retreat. The lore signature is twofold: **Cleaving Blades with Killing Blow (Monster, D6)** — the scything blade-arms that decapitate apex prey, S 7 / AP -2 / D D3, modelled as a natural-to-Wound-6-on-Monster damage spike from D D3 to D 6; and **Soul Reaper** — at deployment the Necrosphinx is bound by priest-magic to one specific named enemy character, gaining re-roll to-Hit 1s against that character for the rest of the battle. The Necrosphinx is the Tomb Kings *anti-Lord / anti-Monster apex*, complementary to the Warsphinx's *anti-mass siege-engine* role. Drafted here at **W 10 apex parity** with the Warsphinx — same biological scale (stone-bodied, T 8, Res 10 Undead, no NA), heavier-bladed (S 7 vs Warsphinx body S 5, AP -2), more skilled (MA 5 vs MA 4), slower-swinging (I 2 — heavy stone blades take time to come around — vs Warsphinx I 1), and lacking the Warsphinx's Tomb Guard Crew (the Necrosphinx hunts alone — riders would only get in the way of the scything blade-arms). The Khemri battlefield read: deploy the Necrosphinx opposite the enemy's apex character or Lord-mount, mark them with Soul Reaper, march the construct forward under priest-magic protection across two-three turns, and end the duel on the first round of combat with a natural-to-Wound-6 Cleaving Blades strike — the patient executioner finally landing its blow.

## Special Rules

- **Disciplined** *(per §8)* — animated by Mortuary Cult ritual that imposes ordered priest-will on the construct; the Necrosphinx does not falter, does not panic, executes its orders with cold precision. +1 to Recovery rolls. *(Lore: the priest's binding is structured ritual, not chaos-frenzy.)*
- **Undead** *(Tomb Kings faction-wide identity rule — abbreviated stress-test definition)* — Tomb Kings undead are **Immune to Psychology** (per §8), do not take stress from any morale source, cannot Fall Back from combat (they advance until destroyed), and are subject to a **Crumble** mechanic similar to Vampire Counts (when a Liche Priest or Tomb King anchor character within 12" is killed, Undead units in range take +1 stress per turn until restored). Stress from combat wounds and direct spell damage still applies normally. *(See Khemrian Warsphinx profile for the precedent abbreviation; full Tomb Kings drafting would specify the anchor mechanic.)*
- **Terror** *(per §8)* — at LiS 5, the Terror aura projects to **(6 + 5)" = 11"**. A towering stone construct with gigantic scything blade-arms and the face of a long-dead pharaoh registers as terror on any battlefield. The size bump from LiS 4 to LiS 5 (vs initial draft) reflects the Necrosphinx as a properly *gigantic* construct — the canon "scything blades that sever the neck of a Dragon in a single slice" implies a model with reach and presence above mid-Monster tier.
- **Stone Construct** *(Khemrian and Necrosphinx-shared identity rule)* — the Necrosphinx is a magically-animated stone construct. It gains: (a) **Imm to Killing Blow (as incoming)** (per §8 — stone has no joints to sever, so enemy KB rules do not slay it outright; *the Necrosphinx's own outgoing Killing Blow on Cleaving Blades is unaffected*); (b) **Imm to Poisoned Attacks (as incoming)** (the construct has no flesh to poison; this Imm applies to the body); (c) **does not Crumble while a Liche Priest model is on the battlefield** (the priest's animation magic sustains the construct directly, bypassing the standard Tomb Kings Crumble dynamic for as long as a priest lives).
- **No Fly** — homebrew divergence from WAP canon (which gives the Necrosphinx Fly 9). The vestigial scarab wings are decorative / ceremonial on the battlefield; the stone body is too massive for sustained flight under combat conditions. The Necrosphinx walks at M 6 alongside the rest of the Tomb Kings host, lumbering toward its marked prey across multiple turns rather than skipping over screens. See `design.md` for the rationale.
- **Soul Reaper** *(Necrosphinx unit identity rule)* — At the start of the battle, immediately after deployment but before the first turn, the Necrosphinx's controlling player **names a single enemy character model** as the Necrosphinx's marked target ("marked for the Nehekharan underworld" per the priest-magic ritual). For the rest of the battle, this Necrosphinx's attacks against the marked character gain **re-roll to-Hit rolls of 1**. The Soul Reaper mark cannot be transferred or re-assigned mid-battle. If the marked character is removed from play (killed by the Necrosphinx or by any other source, banished, fled off the table, etc.), Soul Reaper's effect ends — the Necrosphinx may continue to attack other characters normally for the rest of the battle, but without the reroll bonus. The mark is a *priest-bound geas*; once the target is sundered, the binding is spent. *(Cross-reference: Killing Blow (Monster, D6) on the Cleaving Blades — the marked character is often a Lord-on-Monster or Hero-on-Monster, in which case Soul Reaper's reroll-hit-1s compounds with the KB(Monster) damage spike to produce a credible one-round-decapitation threat.)*

## Weapon Profiles

### Close Combat

- **Cleaving Blades** *(Necrosphinx natural — pair of gigantic scything blade-arms)* — **1" reach** | **A 4** | **S 7** | **AP -2** | **D D3** | **Killing Blow (Monster, D6)** — the iconic Necrosphinx weapon, "armed with gigantic, scything blades that can sever the neck of a Dragon in a single slice." Four scything strikes per Combat round, each carrying the apex bladework of the Mortuary Cult's design. **S 7** is the maximum body-strength tier and wounds even apex Monsters reliably — T 5 on 2+, T 6 on 3+, T 7 on 4+, T 8 on 5+; **AP -2** strips heavy armour (4+ heavy → 6+ effective, 3+ heavy → 5+ effective, 2+ plate → 4+ effective). **D D3** delivers 1-3 wounds per unsaved wound (average 2). **Killing Blow (Monster, D6)** — per §8, on an unmodified to-Wound roll of **6** against a model with the **Monster** keyword, this attack deals **D6 damage** instead of the baseline D D3 (mean ~3.5 vs ~2 — the damage spike is the decapitation, modelled directly). Excess damage does not carry between models per standard KB conventions. **Soul Reaper interaction:** if the model targeted is the Soul-Reaper-marked character (typically a Lord-on-Monster), reroll to-Hit rolls of 1 — compounding to a meaningfully-higher chance of landing the natural-to-Wound 6 that triggers the decapitation. **I 2 caveat:** the Necrosphinx strikes at I 2 — slower than most apex Monsters (Carnosaur I 3, Hydra I 3, Kharibdyss I 3) and most Lord characters (typically I 4-7). In a 1v1 duel the Necrosphinx **takes a round of return strikes before its first attack lands** — designed trade-off: heavy stone blades hit harder but swing slower. The W 10 / T 8 / no-NA-but-Imm-Poisoned biology buffers the strike-second cost; the S 7 + KB(Monster, D6) output exceeds most apex Monsters' damage per attack once it lands. `[Natural]`
