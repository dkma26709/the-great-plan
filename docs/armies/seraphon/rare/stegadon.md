# Stegadon


> A living siege engine. Bony plates thick enough to turn cannonballs, horns that can gore a knight and his horse in one thrust, and a howdah full of Skinks raining death from above. The Stegadon is the Lizardmen exception to the "no armour" identity — but even here, the protection is biological. Those dorsal plates grew, they weren't forged.

**Stegadon (Beast):**

| M | WS-A | WS-D | BS | S | T | W | I | Res | LiS | US |
|---|----|----|----|---|---|---|---|----|-----|----|
| 6 | 3 | 3 | 0 | 6 | 6 | 12 | 2 | 4 | 4 | =W |

*Stegadon US equals its **current remaining Wounds** (per the Monster-US convention — see §1). A full-strength Stegadon contributes US 12 to its unit and the battlefield; a heavily-wounded Stegadon contributes proportionally less. **Note: A (Attacks) is not a profile stat — each weapon carries its own Attack count** (see Weapon profiles below). Framework-wide pass to move A onto weapons is pending; Stegadon is the first profiled unit under the new convention.*

**Points:** **275** per Stegadon + 5 Skink Crew bundle

- **Keywords:** Lizardmen, Saurian, Monster, Rare (Stegadon); Lizardmen, Skink, Infantry, Rare (Crew)
- **Unit type:** Mixed Unit (Monster + Skink Infantry support, per §1)
- **Unit composition:** 1 Stegadon + **5 Skink Crew** (fixed ratio)
- **Natural Armour:** 3+ (armoured dorsal plates — the tankiest save in the roster; these plates genuinely turn cannon shot)
- **Ward Save:** — (none baseline)
- **Equipment:**
  - Stegadon: Goring Horns, Crushing Bulk, Armoured Tail (natural weapons — see profiles)
  - Howdah weapon (player picks one at list building): Giant Bow OR Giant Blowpipes (see weapon profiles)
  - Skink Crew: hand weapons (self-defence only in melee)
  - No shields
- **Command options:** none (single-beast unit)
- **Special rules:**
  - **Terror** (see §8) — causes Terror, not just Fear
  - Mixed Unit (see §1)
  - **Unstoppable Momentum** — this unit's Goring Horns charge strike resolves as **D3 hits per enemy model in melee reach**, instead of a normal single hit per attack. Not swinging — ploughing. The bow wave of a massive body hitting a formation at speed.
  - **Degrading** — performance degrades as wounds are lost. See damage table below.

> **Lore notes:** T 6, W 12, Save 3+ — extremely tanky, though T reduced by 1 during review to avoid overlap with Bastiladon which is the true "fortress" save-profile. The save is earned: those dorsal plates are near-impervious to normal weapons. Terror (not just Fear) reflects the sheer mass-effect presence — a Stegadon on the field is something the enemy reorganises around, not ignores. LiS 4 (not 5 as initially drafted) — Stegadons are big but not city-block big; a Monster in the LiS-4 tier. US = W remaining makes objective-scoring and targeting dynamic as the Stegadon takes damage, matching the Monster-scale presence feedback loop. Unstoppable Momentum D3-per-enemy-model on charge is the horn signature, scaled down to D2 then 1 per enemy model as the beast falters. Points at 275 include the 5 Skink Crew (previously 3 — corrected to reference count) bundled with the Stegadon beast, priced via §13 extended wound factor at the revised 0.5/W coefficient (W12 factor 7.48). Framework note: this is the first unit profiled under the **A-on-weapons convention** — A is no longer a stat on the profile line but lives on each weapon. Broader pass to convert the rest of the roster is pending.
