# Bretonnia Virtues of the Chivalric Knight


> Bretonnian Knights pursue *virtue* as the chivalric path's core discipline — qualities of soul cultivated through years of service, vow, and personal trial. Where magic items are *gifts of the Lady* (divine grace channelled into mortal craft), Virtues are *the Knight himself made better* — disciplines lived into being, not bestowed. A Knight under a Virtue is not improved by an artefact; he IS the improvement. Each Virtue is a distinct character-trait pick at list-build, layered on top of the Knight's vow tier (Knight's / Crusader's / Questing / Grail Vow) and equipment.

**Eligibility:** Knight characters only — **Bretonnian Lord** and **Paladin**. Damsels and Prophetesses (Lady's Chosen casters) do not take Virtues; their path is the Lady's voice, not the chivalric duel. Peasants do not take Virtues — they have no chivalric station.

**Virtue budget** *(separate from magic items budget)*:

- **Bretonnian Lord:** 50 pts
- **Paladin:** 25 pts

A character may take **multiple Virtues** within their budget. Per-character budget and per-character Virtue picks are independent of magic items — a character may carry magic items AND Virtues simultaneously, each drawn from its own budget pool.

**Rarity stacking — across the army:** Each Virtue carries its listed cost for the *first* character in the army to take it. If a *second* character takes the same Virtue, that character pays **double** the listed cost. A *third* character pays **triple**, and so on. Captures the lore: Virtues are rare honours, not common templates — a duchy's most senior knight may carry the Virtue of Heroism, but his deputy paladins do not casually wear the same. *(Practical effect at the 50/25 budget caps: most Virtues are effectively unique per army by budget pressure alone — a Lord taking Heroism for 35 pts leaves a Paladin's 25 pt budget unable to afford the doubled 70 pt cost; even cheap Virtues like Purity at 15 doubled to 30 exceed the Paladin's cap. The stacking rule is a formal layer reinforcing what budget caps already enforce in practice — but becomes meaningful once Virtue budgets expand or new character tiers appear.)*

#### The Six Virtues *(first pass)*

| Virtue | Effect | Pts |
|---|---|---|
| **Virtue of Heroism** | The Knight's melee attacks gain **Killing Blow (Any, D6)** (per §8) — on an unmodified wound roll of 6 against any target, the attack deals **D6 damage** instead of its normal Damage value. Excess damage does not carry between models. Works against any target including Monsters, Heroes, and Lord-tier characters. Captures the dragon-felling lore without binary "remove from play" mechanics. Items / rules granting immunity to Killing Blow (e.g. Sirienne's Locket) negate this effect | 35 |
| **Virtue of Stoicism** | The Knight gains the **Cold-Blooded** special rule (per §8 — incoming stress -1 minimum 1, recovery rolls -1 minimum 1). While the Knight is **attached to a unit** (per §1 Character Attachment Exception), that unit also gains Cold-Blooded for the duration of attachment. The Knight has stood at the Lady's altar through a night-vigil and ridden into battle without sleep — pressure means nothing to him; horror is just another report on the field | 25 |
| **Virtue of Audacity** | When the Knight makes melee attacks against an enemy model whose **base Strength** (the value on the profile, before any weapon, charge, or rule modifiers) is **higher than the Knight's own base Strength**, the Knight may **reroll failed To Hit rolls** and **failed To Wound rolls** against that target. For Rider/Mount combined models per §1, comparison uses the **higher S** of either profile (whichever is the more threatening attack source). The Knight rides at the towering Ogre, the great wyrm, the giant — fearless, his blade finds the gap that smaller knights can't see | 20 |
| **Virtue of Devotion** | The Knight gains a **Ward Save (2+) against wounds caused by enemy spells** (per §8 *Ward vs Spells*). Personal effect — applies only to wounds dealt to the Knight himself, not to the unit he is attached to, not to allies. The Knight's faith is the shield: hostile magic finds little purchase against one who has dedicated his soul to the Lady, though the bond is not absolute — even devotion may falter on a poor day | 20 |
| **Virtue of the Joust** | The Knight may **reroll failed To Hit rolls** when **either** of the following conditions is true: the Knight is attacking on the **charge round** (the round his unit completed a successful charge), OR the Knight is wielding a **Heavy Lance, Light Lance, or any Magic Weapon pinned to Heavy Lance / Light Lance** (mundane or magical, regardless of round). If both conditions are true, only one reroll source fires per die (standard one-reroll-per-die convention). The chivalric duel-tradition mechanically — the Knight's lance-craft is never out of practice | 20 |
| **Virtue of Purity** | The Knight's personal **Blessing of the Lady Ward improves by one step** (per §17 Faction Mechanics). Knight's Vow Blessing 6+/5+ → 5+/4+; Grail Vow Blessing 5+/4+ → 4+/4+ (Strength-variable improvement caps at 4+ via §8 Ward Save Stacking). Personal effect only — does NOT extend to mount, attached unit, or allies. The Knight has lived a life of austere chivalric devotion; the Lady's hand finds him more readily than other vow-bearers | 15 |

> **Lore notes:** First-pass Bretonnia Virtues — six Virtues drafted, eleven WAP-canonical Virtues deferred to a future expansion pass. The first pass selection prioritises Virtues that translate cleanly into our existing §8 Special Rules vocabulary and exercise different chivalric archetypes — Heroism (the dragon-felling apex), Stoicism (the disciplined elder), Audacity (the giant-slayer), Devotion (the unshakeable faithful), Joust (the lance-master), Purity (the saintly defender).
