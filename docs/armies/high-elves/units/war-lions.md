# War Lions

> Apex predators of the Chracian mountains, drawn into Asur service. Wild white lions of Chrace "stand as tall at the shoulder as a horse," and "a swipe of their claws is enough to shatter a spine" — they are amongst the fiercest creatures of the mountain kingdom, hunted in single combat as the rite of passage that turns a young Chracian Elf into a White Lion of the Phoenix Guard. The lions that march to war alongside Asur armies come from one of two paths. Most are **cub-raised** — taken as kits by veteran White Lion warriors and reared into "fierce but loyal war beasts," loyal to the regiment as the regiment is loyal to the pride. The rest are **mage-bound** — wild lions captured by Elven Wizards and held by Saphery-tier magical compulsion. The mage-bond is faster and riskier: "the white lions of Chrace are intelligent and patient beasts that are not easily controlled," and a bond that frays under battle pressure can turn the pride on the army that summoned it. Either way, what arrives on the field is a pride of pack-hunters: fast, fearless, devastating against soldiers on foot. **Pride Hunter** is the cult-of-Chrace bond expressed mechanically: the lions specialise in *infantry prey*. Against soldiers they read body language, isolate the weakest, and strike together. Against cavalry, monsters, or other beast prides, they remain competent threats — but the pack advantage is lost. Battlefield role: flanking strike force. Send them around the line, into the back of an infantry block, and watch the bone-breaker claws unmake whoever the cult sergeants point them at.

## Special Rules

- **Skirmishers** *(see §8)* — loose-formation pack, +1 M effective, Cannot Form, free pivots, Coherency 2". Lions hunt as a pride, not a phalanx.
- **Fear** *(Psychology — see §8)* — at LiS 2, the Fear aura projects to **(6 + 2)" = 8"**. A pride of horse-sized predators advancing on a soldier's position registers as terror — the lore-canonical "stand as tall at the shoulder as a horse, a swipe enough to shatter a spine" expression.
- **Pride Hunter** *(War Lions unit identity rule)* — when targeting a unit any of whose models carry the **Infantry** keyword, this unit's effective MA is **+1** in melee (MA 5 → 6). Pack-hunting bonus: the pride coordinates around infantry prey, exploiting openings that solo strikes don't find. Against units containing **no** Infantry models (Cavalry, Monstrous Cavalry, Warbeasts, Monsters, Chariots, Swarms, etc.), Pride Hunter does **not** apply — lions remain competent threats but lack the coordinated pack advantage against non-infantry prey.
- **Thick Pelt** *(War Lions unit identity rule)* — the dense white-fur pelt of the Chracian lion is unusually resistant to projectiles. This unit gains a **5+ armour save** against unsaved wounds caused by **ranged attacks** (anything resolved in the Shooting phase — arrows, bolts, bullets, javelins, templates, breath weapons, scatter weapons, magic missiles). **AP applies normally** — AP -1 reduces the save to 6+, AP -2 negates it. The pelt provides **no save in melee** — close-quarters strikes have leverage and force that arrows do not, finding gaps and gripping into the hide. *(Lore-stretch flagged in design.md: a pelt biologically resistant to arrows would also resist some melee, but the design framing scopes this rule strictly to ranged for mechanical distinctness.)*

## Options

**None.** The pride is a free-running pack — no Leader / Standard / Musician. Banners don't steer a beast and drums don't direct a hunt. The cub-raised handler-bond and mage-bound compulsion are off-stage; the pride that arrives on the table operates by predator instinct within the implicit handler's general direction.

- **Claws** *(natural, always available)* — 1" reach | **A 2** | S 5 | AP -1 | D 1 — the bone-breaker strike. Two claws per pride model per round, 1" reach allows engagement at the front rank or in skirmish-coherency-spread `[Natural]`
- **Jaws** *(natural, front-rank only)* — 0.5" reach | **A 1** | S 4 | AP 0 | D 1 — the killing bite once the strike has connected. Front-rank-only (parallel to Saurus Jaws) — lions in rear positions cannot bite over the front; the cropped reach is the mechanical hook. The pride alpha bites the throat; the wing lions claw the flanks `[Natural]`
