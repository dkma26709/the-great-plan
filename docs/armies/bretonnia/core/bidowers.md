# Bidowers


> The duchy's foragers, scouts, and forward harassers — peasant levies too poor or too few to muster a longbow, equipped instead with javelins or slings and sent ahead of the army to soften enemy lines. The name comes from the Old Breton *bidou* — "side-runner" — for the peasant who jogs alongside the knightly column, throws stones at scouting enemies, and disappears into the trees before retaliation. Bidowers are the army's *eyes and noise* — they harass, screen, and skirmish, and when the knights charge through, they fall back through the gaps and re-form behind the lines. Less prestigious than Longbowmen (who at least have proper bows), less well-equipped than Men-at-Arms (who at least have armour), but invaluable as a forward screen.

- **Keywords:** Bretonnia, Human, Infantry, Core, Peasant, Skirmisher
- **Unit type:** Infantry (Skirmisher)
- **Unit size:** 10-30 (loose-order skirmishers; smaller blocks fit the harassment role, larger blocks reflect the WAP 20+ horde tradition)
- **Points:** 6 per model
- **Natural Armour:** —
- **Worn armour (default):** — (village levies issued no body armour; Bidowers are too low-status to merit even a quilted jack)
- **Equipment (default):** Hand weapon + Javelin
- **Equipment options (replace javelin, pick at most one):**
  - **Sling** (+0.5 pt/model — replaces Javelin) — see weapon profile below; trades short-range thrown weight for medium-range stone-volume
- **Additive equipment options:**
  - **Buckler** (+0.5 pt/model) → grants **Parry (6+)** in melee (see §8 Parry). Small enough not to interfere with javelin or sling — Bidowers carry the buckler on the off-arm for melee defence after thrown ammunition is spent
- **Command options:**
  - **Leader** (+5 pts) — *Bidower Master*; a senior peasant skirmisher, often a ducal forester or a seasoned scout
  - **Musician** (+5 pts) — +1 to recovery and Rally rolls (a peasant horn-blower or runner who relays signals across the dispersed unit)
  - *(No Standard Bearer — skirmishers carry no banner. The Bidower lives by speed and scattered movement, not by the standard's anchor.)*
- **Special rules:**
  - **Skirmishers** (see §8) — coherency 2" instead of 1"; may shoot in the same turn it has marched (fire-on-the-move, no penalty); free pivots at any point during movement; cannot gain Formed benefits (Loose / Disordered states only)
  - **Peasant's Duty** *(identity rule)* — as Men-at-Arms; while any model is within **6"** of a friendly Bretonnia character with the **Knight's Vow**, **Crusader's Vow**, **Questing Vow**, **Grail Vow**, or **Lady's Chosen** keyword, that character grants this unit the effects of **Inspiring Presence (6")** (see §8 Resolve and Command). Aura passes from outside the unit only
  - **Class Barrier** *(clause of Peasant's Duty)* — a Bretonnian character with the **Knight's Vow / Questing Vow / Lady's Chosen / Crusader's Vow / Grail Vow** keyword standing within the coherency of this unit does **not** gain the Character Attachment Exception's shielding (§6) — same restriction as Men-at-Arms / Longbowmen

**Weapon profiles (A on weapons for melee; ranged weapons per §10 Armoury convention — no S listed for wielder-powered weapons):**

- **Hand weapon** *(melee, 1H — pairs with buckler if taken)* — 1" reach | **A 1** | S 3 | AP 0 | D 1 — peasant arming sword, knife, or hatchet from the castle armoury `[1H Blade]`
- **Javelin** *(default ranged)* — **8" range | AP 0 | D 1 | Quick to Fire** (see §8) — a short peasant throwing-spear; S follows wielder (S 3). Quick to Fire allows fire-on-the-move (already implicit via Skirmishers) and Stand and Shoot reactions without the standard -1 to-hit penalty
- **Sling** *(swap option, ranged — replaces Javelin, +0.5 pt/model)* — **18" range | AP 0 | D 1 | Quick to Fire** (see §8) — a leather sling and a pouch of river-stones; S follows wielder (S 3). Trades the javelin's heavier per-shot impact for medium-range stone-volume from a sustained position

> **Lore notes:** **Stats identical to Longbowmen** — WS 2 / BS 2 / Res 6, peasant levy baseline. The differentiator is the **Skirmisher** keyword, not the stats: Bidowers operate as loose-order foragers (2" coherency, fire-on-the-move, free pivots, no Formed) rather than rank-and-file Longbowmen. They are functionally interchangeable with Longbowmen on raw stats but tactically distinct — Bidowers go forward and harass; Longbowmen sit back and volley. **BS 2** captures the levy-peasant tier — village-trained but not professionally drilled; same as Longbowmen, one step below Men-at-Arms (BS 3).
>
> **Skirmisher type — first non-Skink in the ruleset.** Validates that the §8 Skirmishers special rule generalises across factions: peasant Bidowers operate identically to Skink Skirmishers in the Lizardmen list (loose coherency, no Formed, fire-on-the-move, free pivots). Captures the cross-faction "light infantry that cannot hold a line but can move fluidly and fire while moving" archetype.
>
> **No defensive options — no light armour, no Defensive Stakes.** Bidowers are *meant* to be fragile — their tactical role is harassment and forward screening, not holding a position. They lack even the Longbowmen's option to pay 0.5 pt for a chainmail jack; the Bidower goes to war in his work clothes. The buckler upgrade is the only defensive layer (Parry 6+ vs melee). If the player wants a defensive peasant unit, take Longbowmen with stakes; if they want a screening unit, take Bidowers.
>
> **Javelin vs Sling trade-off.** Javelin (8" range) is short — Bidowers must close to almost engagement distance to throw. Sling (18" range) keeps them at standoff. Most lists take the sling for the doubled range; javelin is a thematic option for a faster, more-aggressive Bidower unit that operates in the immediate forward zone (within 8" of enemy lines). Both weapons are S 3 AP 0 — neither penetrates armour; both rely on volume and harassment value rather than damage. Quick to Fire on both lets Bidowers harass on the move and respond to charges with Stand and Shoot at full BS.
>
> **No Standard Bearer.** Skirmishers don't carry banners — the standard's role is to anchor a Formed unit and confer combat resolution; a Bidower unit operates in dispersed Loose order and gains nothing from a standard. WAP also denies them the option. Saves 10 pts vs Longbowmen's typical Standard Bearer cost.
>
> **Peasant's Duty + Class Barrier apply normally.** Bidowers benefit from a vow-bearing Bretonnia character within 6" — the Inspiring Presence aura adds +1 Res threshold + reroll recovery / rally / etc. Tactically harder to enable than for Longbowmen, since Bidowers will typically be far forward of the knight character — but a Knight Errant unit in the same forward zone (within 6") can grant the aura while harassing.
>
> **Points 6 per model** — +1 over WAP's 5 (the systematic peasant +1 calibration). 1 pt cheaper per model than Longbowmen (7) — Longbowmen get the longer-range bow + access to Defensive Stakes / light armour / Braziers; Bidowers get only the Skirmishers package. The Skirmishers package is genuinely valuable (fire-on-the-move + free pivots + 2" coherency for spreading) but doesn't compensate for the longbow's 30" reach + AP options. Per-model upgrade max: 6 + 0.5 (sling) + 0.5 (buckler) = **7 pts/model** for a fully-kitted slinger-with-buckler Bidower. A 20-strong fully-kitted block: 20 × 7 = **140 pts** — same total as 20-strong bare Longbowmen (140 pts), with very different tactical profile (forward harassment vs back-rank volley).
>
> **No Blessing of the Lady** per §17 — peasants are commoners, no vow, no divine protection. Standard for all Peasant-keyword units.
