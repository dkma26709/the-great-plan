# Razordons


> Saurian beasts whose hide bristles with retractable quill-spines, tended by Skink Handlers who feed, aim, and keep them calm. Where a Salamander breathes fire, a Razordon fires a volley of barbed arrows from its own flanks — and when something charges a Razordon, the charger is greeted at mid-stride by a reflex storm of quills. The beast's natural defensive instinct is a hail of spines in any direction; the handlers exist mainly to point that hail where it will do most work.

**Points:** **80** per beast-bundle (1 Razordon + 3 Handlers)

- **Keywords:** Lizardmen, Warbeast, Special (Razordon); Lizardmen, Skink, Infantry, Special (Handler)
- **Unit type:** Mixed Unit (Warbeast + Skink Infantry support, per §1)
- **Unit composition:** 1-3 Razordons, each accompanied by **3 Skink Handlers** (fixed ratio)
- **Unit size range:** 4 models (1 beast + 3 handlers) to 12 models (3 beasts + 9 handlers)
- **Natural Armour:** 5+ (Razordon, bristled hide), 6+ (Handler)
- **Equipment:**
  - Razordon: Shoot Barbs (see Armoury) + natural claws and bite
  - Handler: hand weapon
  - No shields on either profile
- **Command options:**
  - **Handler Leader** (+5 pts) — designated Skink leader; replaces a Handler model (not added)
  - **Musician** (+5 pts) — replaces a Handler model
  - No Standard bearer
- **Special rules:**
  - Aquatic (see §8)
  - Skirmishers (see §8)
  - Fear (Razordons only)
  - Mixed Unit (see §1)
  - **Instinctive Defence** — when this unit is charged by an enemy, each Razordon **must Stand and Shoot** with its Shoot Barbs in response, if able (the unit has not already shot that turn, the charging unit is a valid target, and so on — standard §7.4 Stand and Shoot requirements). Additionally, Stand-and-Shoot attacks made by Razordons:
    - Gain **Quick to Fire** (no -1 to hit penalty from the Stand-and-Shoot reaction)
    - May **reroll the Artillery Die** for the shot count (including rerolling a MISFIRE result — a rerolled MISFIRE stands)
    - The unit cannot voluntarily decline the Stand and Shoot; it is reflexive, not a choice. The beast's defensive instinct is a storm of quills.
  - **Aimless** — when this unit's Handler count drops to zero, all remaining Razordon models in the unit immediately gain the **Stupidity** special rule (see §8) for the rest of the battle. Without handlers to focus them, the beasts stop aiming; they fire, charge, and crash forward without purpose. Not Frenzied (that is Salamander's feral theme) — *aimless*, distracted, lost in the beast's own instinct.

> **Lore notes:** Razordons are the thematic twin of Salamanders — same W5 Warbeast beast with M 8 and A 3, same 3-Handler fixed support, same bundle price (80 pts). The distinction is *how* they fight at range and *what* they become when their handlers die. Salamanders = template + Frenzy-on-handler-loss; Razordons = volley + Stupidity-on-handler-loss. Instinctive Defence makes Razordons genuinely hard to charge — the charger must weigh the cost of an Artillery-Die-worth of BS 3 AP -1 barbs as the price of contact, and a rerollable Artillery Die means the big numbers (8 or 10 shots) come up more often. Shoot Barbs with **Rapid Fire** means the volley ignores the -1 to-hit penalty that Multiple Shots usually imposes — the beast fires while the crew reloads, always some quills in flight. Aimless is the slower, less-dramatic failure mode (compared to Unleashed Flame's Frenzy) — the unit becomes a liability rather than a loose weapon, and the beasts wander forward firing haphazardly at whatever's in front of them. Shoot Barbs uses BS 3 (handler-directed) where Spout Flames needs no BS (template); the two weapons have different sensitivities to handler-pool depletion and different scaling against armoured vs. unarmoured targets.
