# Goblin Wolf Chariot

> A smaller, lighter chariot built by Common Goblin engineers (or stolen from somewhere and re-pegged together), pulled by two Giant Wolves and crewed by two Common Goblin scouts. Where the Orc Boar Chariot is a heavy ramming shock vehicle, the Wolf Chariot is a fast, opportunistic harassment platform — M 9 from the Wolves (the fastest base-M in the Greenskin roster), lighter Impact on the charge (D3 hits at S 4 AP 0), and a fragile frame (W 3 / T 4 / no NA). The Wolf Chariot's job is *positioning*: race across the flank, charge an isolated vulnerable target, deliver Impact + Goblin Crew Choppa strikes, and hope the chariot survives the return fire long enough to retreat. Cheap enough at 35 pts to be fielded in pairs or trios as a flanking screen.

## Special Rules

- Undisciplined
- **Animosity** *(Greenskin-wide faction trait — see §8 Psychology)*. D6 at start of each Movement phase when out of combat; on a 1, sub-roll D6 on the standard sub-table. The Wolf Chariot CAN Charge, so Animosity functions normally (force-charge or D3 self-hits at Crew S 3).
- **Stab in the Back** *(Goblin-typical — see §13 *Goblins*, applies to Crew and Mount melee+ranged attacks)*. +1 to Wound against rear-arc *(melee only)* or Shaken/Wavering targets. Does not apply to the Chariot Primary's Impact (the chariot's wheel-and-axle collision isn't "opportunistic" — it just rolls over whatever it hits).
- **Cowardly Numbers** *(Goblin-typical — dormant at chariot unit size)*. Stubborn-while-20+ clause cannot trigger (single-model unit). The -1 Res below-half-strength clause cannot trigger either (the chariot is either alive or destroyed; no partial unit). Functionally inactive for this unit.
- **No Loves a Scrap** *(Goblin sub-species)*.
- **Chariot** *(unit type — see §8)*. Cannot March in Difficult/Dangerous Terrain. Flank/Rear Charge Vulnerability (+1 CR to chargers in flank/rear arc). Chariot stat-routing variant per §8 (Mount provides M; Crew provides MA/MD/BS/S/I + defensive MD; Chariot Primary holds T/W/Res).
- **Wolf Chariot Impact** *(Wolf Chariot identity rule)*. On the turn this unit successfully charges into melee, before the Combat phase begins, resolve the following Impact (per §8 Impact — hits are pre-determined, no to-hit roll required):
  - **D3 auto-hits per chariot** at **S 4** (mount S 3 + chariot mass + 1) | **AP 0** | **D 1**
  - The hits are **automatic** — no to-hit roll. Roll D3 to determine the number of hits, then resolve to-wound and saves normally against the charged unit's models within Reach of the chariot in base contact.
  - Damage does not carry between models. Failed charges produce no Impact hits.
  - Lighter than the Orc Boar Chariot's Wheel-and-Axle Mass (D6 hits at S 5 AP -1) — the Wolves carry less mass than War Boars and lack tusks, so the impact bruises and tramples rather than crushes and pierces.
