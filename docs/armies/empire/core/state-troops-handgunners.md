# State Troops — Handgunners


> State Troops of the handgun-wielding variety. Empire-innovated gunpowder infantry — the matchlock, shoulder-braced muzzle-loader that changed Warhammer warfare. Handgunners train at the Nuln shooting ranges and the Imperial Engineers' guild. Not snipers — massed volley troops. Slow to reload, but when the volley lands, armour matters less.

Same stat baseline as Halberdiers. State Troops share the human-infantry profile; what differentiates them is equipment.

- **Keywords:** Empire, Human, Infantry, Core, State Troops
- **Unit type:** Infantry (ranks, not Skirmishers)
- **Unit size:** 10-20
- **Points:** 10 per model
- **Natural Armour:** —
- **Equipment (default):** Hand weapon + Handgun
- **Worn armour options:** Light armour (+0.5 pt/model) → 6+ save (no shield — Handgun is Two-Handed)
- **Equipment replace-Handgun options** (pick one swap at list-build):
  - **Brace of Pistols** (+0 pt, free swap) — twin pistols, short range, rapid double-shot
  - **Repeater Handgun** (+5 pt/model) — multi-barrel overheating volley
  - **Hochland Long Rifle** (+10 pt/model, **max 1 per unit** — designated marksman) — precision long-range shot
- **Command options:**
  - **Leader** (+5 pts)
  - **Standard bearer** (+10 pts, Magic Standard up to 25 pts)
  - **Musician** (+5 pts)
- **Special rules:**
  - **State Troops** — as Halberdiers: reroll recovery / rally rolls while within 6" of General or BSB

**Weapon profiles (A on weapons for melee; ranged weapons use Armoury convention — no A listed, BS from shooter's profile):**

- **Handgun** *(default ranged)* — **24" range | S 4 | AP -2 | D 1 | Slow to Fire (see §8) | Two-Handed** — matchlock powder weapon. Slow to Fire prevents Stand-and-Shoot reactions. S 4 AP -2 reliably wounds heavy infantry (T 4 on 3+, cuts 4+ armour to 6+). The Empire's mainstay firearm
- **Brace of Pistols** *(swap option, 0 pts)* — 12" range | S 4 | AP -2 | D 1 | **Multiple Shots (2)** | Quick to Fire | One-Handed each — twin pistols, trades range for rapid double-shot and no Slow to Fire. Multiple Shots (2) imposes the standard -1 to hit penalty (two guns, no aim)
- **Repeater Handgun** *(swap option, +5/model)* — 18" range | S 4 | AP -1 | D 1 | **Multiple Shots (3)** | Slow to Fire | Two-Handed — overheating multi-barrel; accepts the Multiple Shots penalty for volume
- **Hochland Long Rifle** *(swap option, +10/model, max 1 per unit)* — **48" range** | S 4 | AP -2 | D 1 | **Sniper** (see §8, Chameleon Stalker entry) | Slow to Fire | Two-Handed — marksman's rifle; may target character / lone models anywhere within range
- **Hand weapon** *(melee profile — baseline kit)* — 1" reach | **A 1** | S 3 | AP 0 | D 1 — Handgunners carry hand weapon + handgun as dual-kit baseline (per reference equipment); the hand weapon is their actual melee profile, not a disarm fallback `[1H Blade]`

> **Lore notes:** Points 10 reflects the Handgun's S 4 AP -2 over a Halberdier's melee-only kit at 6. Reference pricing had Handgunners at 8 vs Halberdier 5 (3-pt premium); our system at 10 vs 6 (4-pt premium) tightens this slightly — our Handgun with Slow to Fire and AP -2 is a real threat to Monster-scale profiles (wounds Stegadon T 6 on 5+, cuts 3+ save to 5+). Three replacement-weapon options give the unit tactical flex: pistols for close-range skirmish, repeaters for volume against infantry, Hochland for the one-shot character-snipe. Two-Handed designation on the Handgun forbids shields — consistent with our A-on-weapons two-handed convention. Hochland Long Rifle tests our Sniper rule on a non-Seraphon unit; first cross-faction use of the mechanic drafted for the Chameleon Stalker.
