### To Wound — Strength vs Toughness

| S vs T | Roll needed |
|--------|-------------|
| S > T by 2 or more | 2+ |
| S > T by 1 | 3+ |
| S = T | 4+ |
| S < T by 1 | 5+ |
| S < T by 2 or more | 6+ |

Each point of difference shifts the roll by 1, capped at 2+ and 6+. Simple, linear, every point of S and T matters.

### Characteristic Tests

Some rules call for a model to **test** a characteristic — "take a Toughness test", "test Initiative at -2". Test-based effects resolve against the body itself, not against armour or weapon skill; spells are the primary current users (Toughness tests in the Lore of Death, Initiative tests in the Lore of Shadow).

**Procedure:** roll a **D6** for the testing model. A roll **equal to or less than** the (modified) characteristic **passes**; anything higher **fails**. The calling rule states the consequence of failure — typically wounds, often with restricted saves.

- **A natural 6 always fails and a natural 1 always passes**, regardless of the characteristic's value or any modifier. No model is immune to a test, and no test is hopeless.
- **Modifiers adjust the characteristic, never the die.** "Test Toughness at -2" means a T5 model tests as T3, passing on 1–3. The natural-6 / natural-1 rule reads the raw die face.
- **Per-model.** Each affected model tests individually, unless the calling rule says otherwise.
- **Combined models (rider and mount):** test the **highest** relevant characteristic across the model's profiles, mirroring the §1 to-wound convention. A profile showing **—** for the tested characteristic does not test on its own; effects route to the unit's primary profile per §1 *Mixed Unit*.
- **Resolve is never tested.** There are no Res tests anywhere in the system (per §5) — a rule calling for a characteristic test always names a physical stat (S, T, I, M).
