# Executioners of Har Ganeth

> Anointed warriors of Khaine, drawn from the elite guards of Har Ganeth — the Dark Elf city that serves as the spiritual centre of Khaine's worship. The cult was forged in the aftermath of a great Druchii victory over the High Elves: the guards of Har Ganeth led thousands of captives to the temple summit and beheaded them with full ceremony, and from that day "executions became a regular feature in the society of Har Ganeth." Every Executioner spends half their waking day as a city sentry and the other half practising their blade — decades to perfect a single chosen strike, judging precisely "the angle of the blow and how the blade might be deflected or otherwise cheated by splinters of bone." The lore-defining trait: *"a fully-trained Executioner knows the way to kill any creature with but a single blow, whether by decapitation, disembowelment or a single thrust through the heart."* Where Witch Elves are the **frenzied** Khainite cult — wild blood-lust, ecstatic violence — the Executioners are the **disciplined** Khainite cult: dispassionate killers who take pride in dispatching their foes with the minimum of effort. They are not frenetic butchers; they are heartless ritualists who see their role as sacred. **Khaine's Stroke** is the cult signature: the *practised* killing-blow that triggers more often than other-cult strikes (5+ to-Wound, not 6) but deals only baseline D 1 damage when it does not trigger, and D3 damage when it does — sometimes a clean decapitation, sometimes a glancing partial cut, sometimes a flesh-wound that bleeds the foe over the round. The **Draich** is the ceremonial Khainite executioner blade: a two-handed great-axe variant forged by the wielder under the temple armourers' supervision, "able to cleave through armour, flesh and bone with but a single, flawlessly placed strike." As Executioners charge they cry their praise of Khaine in "a piercing wail that chills the blood of all who hear it" — the cult terror that announces the City of Executioners.

## Special Rules

- Disciplined
- **Elven Grace** *(faction-wide for elves — Ward 6+ in melee vs attacks at the elf's I or lower; see §8 Defence)*. At I 7, Executioners trigger the Ward against essentially everything in the ruleset — only apex-Initiative characters and a handful of monsters reach I 8+ and bypass it.
- **Murderous Prowess** *(faction-wide for Druchii — reroll to-Wound 1s in melee; see §8 Combat)*. The Druchii lethality axis. At S 5 vs T 3-4 (most rank-and-file infantry) the Executioner wounds on 2+ or 3+, so the reroll catches the rare natural-1 fumble — modest contribution. Against higher-T targets (T 4 elite or T 5 Saurian) the reroll matters more.
- **Hatred (High Elves)** *(see §8)* — Executioners gain Hatred (reroll failed to-Hit in first round of combat) against units with the **High Elf** keyword. The millennia-old kin-grudge expressed mechanically. Shared with Witch Elves and all Khainite cults.
- **Fear** *(Psychology — see §8)* — at LiS 1, the Fear aura projects to **(6 + 1)" = 7"**. The piercing Khainite wail "chills the blood of all who hear it"; the cult terror that announces the City of Executioners. The unit registers as terror to enemies within range.
- **Khaine's Stroke** *(Executioners unit identity rule)* — functions as **Killing Blow (Any)** per §8, with **two modifications**:
  1. **Trigger on natural 5+ to-Wound** (instead of the standard 6 only). Lore: every fully-trained Executioner has spent decades perfecting the practised strike; the lethal cut comes more reliably than other-cult work.
  2. **Damage is D3 instead of fixed.** Lore: the killing blow can be a clean decapitation (3 damage), a disembowelling cut (2), or a precision thrust that wounds-with-bleed (1). The variable damage captures the cult's diverse methods — "decapitation, disembowelment, or a single thrust through the heart."

  *Excess damage does not carry between models (per §8 Killing Blow). Roll the D3 only when the wound roll triggers Khaine's Stroke (natural 5+); roll separately per wound that triggers, not pool-wise.*

## Options

**Worn armour options:** Heavy armour is the cult kit; no upgrade or downgrade is offered. Khainite ceremonial plate is the canonical loadout — the cult identity does not bend to list-build preference.

**Command options:**
- **Draich-Master (Leader)** (+4 pts) — senior Executioner, often a candidate for promotion to the Bloodcleave or Crimson Draichs cabals
- **Standard Bearer** (+8 pts, may take Magic Standard up to 50 pts) — elite banner budget, matching Black Guard / cult-elite tier
- **Musician** (+4 pts) — drums of Khaine, accompanying the cult wail

## Weapon Profiles

### Close Combat

- **Draich** *(default, 2H — no shield)* — 1" reach | **A 2** | S+2 (= S 5) | AP -2 | D 1 — Two-Handed. The ceremonial Khainite executioner blade, forged by its wielder under the supervision of Khaine's temple armourers. Typically a double-handed great-axe variant, but some Executioners prefer a slender sword or heavy axe-blade depending on their chosen killing method — the Draich is *form-variable, function-fixed*. **S+2** (the standard great-weapon class — vs Asur Swordmasters' precision-cleaver S+1) reflects the Druchii brute-force vs Asur precision: the Druchii does not "find the gap," he *makes one* by force. **AP -2** (vs the Asur S+1 AP -1) — the heavier blade shreds armour where the precision-cleaver finds purchase. **D 1 baseline** — the standard strike wounds and cuts but does not in itself sever; the depth-of-cut comes only when Khaine's Stroke triggers (natural 5+ to-Wound for variable D3). This is the cult's *signature spike-or-nothing* damage profile: most cuts are clean-but-shallow, and the practised killing-blow is the killing-blow. **1" reach** (vs Asur Greatsword's 2") — the Draich is a chopper, not a polearm; close-quarters work. No Magical Attacks — the Khainite ritual is sacred but the blade is not magic-keyword. `[2H]`
